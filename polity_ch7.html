🔑 7th Constitutional Amendment, 1956

  • ✦ Allowed one person to be Governor of 2+ states
  • ✦ Removed Part VII of the Constitution
  • ✦ Allowed one High Court for multiple states or UTs

📋 No Impeachment of Governor — Why?

Because the Governor serves at the pleasure of the President (Art. 156). The President can remove the Governor anytime. No special procedure needed → no impeachment provision exists for Governor.

Contrast: President has impeachment (Art. 61) — special majority in both Houses, signatures of 1/4 members to initiate, borrowed from USA Constitution.

Pardoning Power — President vs Governor
Art. 72 (President) vs Art. 161 (Governor)

Types of Pardoning Power (Both President & Governor)

  • Pardon — Complete forgiveness, removes conviction entirely
  • Commute — Converts punishment to lighter form (e.g. death → life)
  • Remission — Reduces quantum of punishment without changing nature
  • Respite — Gives a lesser sentence in special circumstances (e.g. pregnancy)
  • Reprieve — Temporary stay of execution of sentence

Article 74 — Council of Ministers
The Binding Advice Saga: 42nd → 44th Amendment

Core Rule: Art. 74

There shall be a Council of Ministers with the Prime Minister at the head to aid and advise the President. The President shall act in accordance with such advice.

The Amendment Timeline — Binding Advice

42nd CA 1976

Indira Gandhi's move: Made President's acceptance of CoM advice completely binding. President has zero discretion.

44th CA 1978

Correction: Gave President one power — can send advice back for reconsideration ONCE. But reconsidered advice is binding again.

Current position: President can return once → must accept second time.

24th CA 1971

Also by Indira Gandhi: President must give assent to Constitutional Amendment Bills. Cannot use veto power on them.

Article 75 — Six Clauses Decoded
The most important article for PM & CoM
  • Clause 1(A)
    Appointment
    President appoints PM, and other ministers are appointed by President on the advice of PM.
    Draupadi Murmu appoints Modi ji → Modi ji decides who else becomes minister.
  • Clause 1(B)
    91st CA 2003
    Council strength capped at 15% of Lok Sabha members (including PM). Added by 91st Amendment, 2003.
    Also: Anyone disqualified on grounds of defection (10th Schedule / 52nd CA 1985) cannot become minister.
  • Clause 2
    Individual Resp.
    Ministers hold office during pleasure of President → Individually responsible to the President.
    BUT President can't remove them on his own — only on PM's advice.
  • Clause 3
    Collective Resp.
    CoM is collectively responsible to the Lok Sabha (House of the People).
    If PM resigns or dies → entire CoM dissolves.
    If any minister disagrees with a cabinet decision → must resign.
    They are answerable to Lok Sabha — not Rajya Sabha.
  • Clause 4
    Oath
    President administers oath to ministers (of office and secrecy).
  • Clause 5
    6-Month Rule
    If a minister is not a member of either House for 6 consecutive months → ceases to be minister.
    Within 6 months must get elected to Lok Sabha or Rajya Sabha.
    Example: S. Jaishankar was made minister → sent to Rajya Sabha via Gujarat.
  • Clause 6
    Salary
    Salaries and allowances of ministers determined by Parliament.
🧠 Remember the 6 Clauses

A-D-I-C-O-S (very rough):
Appointment → Defection + 15% cap → Individual responsibility → Collective responsibility → Oath → Six months + Salary

Better image: Think of a MINISTER'S CAREER in sequence: First he's Appointed (after passing Defection check + 15% space exists) → he's individually responsible to President while collectively responsible to Lok Sabha → he takes an Oath → he must win a seat in 6 months → his Salary is fixed by Parliament.

Articles 77 & 78 — Business & PM Duties
The linkage role of the Prime Minister

Art. 77 — Conduct of Business

All business of the Government of India is conducted in the name of the President. All orders, instruments bear the President's name/signature.

Art. 78 — Duties of Prime Minister

PM acts as the communication bridge between the Council of Ministers and the President:

  • Communicate all decisions of CoM to President
  • If President asks — furnish information about any single minister's decision that has NOT been considered by the full CoM
  • If President asks — submit the matter to the full CoM for consideration

"PM is the Lynchpin of the Government" — Jawaharlal Nehru

CM & State CoM — Art. 163–167

All provisions of Art. 74–78 apply to CM & State CoM. Just:

  • Replace President → Governor
  • Replace Prime Minister → Chief Minister
  • Replace Parliament → State Legislature
  • Replace Lok Sabha → Legislative Assembly (Vidhan Sabha)
  • Add 89 to Union articles: 74 → 163, 75 → 164, 78 → 167
Attorney General of India
Article 76 — 4 Clauses

Who is he? The highest law officer of India — legal advisor and lawyer for the Government of India in all courts.

  • Clause 1
    Appointed by the President.
    Qualification: Should be qualified to be a Judge of the Supreme Court:
    • HC judge for 5 years, OR
    • HC advocate for 10 years, OR
    • Distinguished jurist in President's opinion (e.g. eminent law professor)
    ⚠️ Trap: NOT "qualified as SC judge" literally — means has the same qualifications that would make one eligible to become SC judge.
  • Clause 2
    AG's duty: Advise Government of India on legal matters + perform duties assigned by President/Constitution.
  • Clause 3
    Right of audience in all courts of India — can appear in any court, special privilege, no restrictions.
  • Clause 4
    Holds office during pleasure of the President (Doctrine of Pleasure).
    Usually holds for ~3 years. No fixed term mentioned in Constitution.

Key Special Facts

  • Private practice allowed — AG is NOT barred from private practice (unlike government servants)
  • But CANNOT hold brief against Government of India
  • Solicitor General assists AG — but this post is NOT mentioned in Constitution (statutory, not constitutional)
  • Current AG: R. Venkataramani (verify for updates)
  • First AG: M.C. Setalvad
  • Current Solicitor General: Tushar Mehta (verify for updates)

🧠 Chapter Memory Palace
Walk through a Government Building in your mind

Imagine you are visiting a Government Building. You enter through the gate, walk through several floors, each room holding a key concept. Walk through it every time you revise.

GATE
🚪 The Security Guard (Art. 152)

He checks your ID and says: "This building is the STATE — not that 12-article state, the real state like UP, MP." This is Chapter 2 of Part VI — Art. 152, the only article, defining "State" for Part VI.

FLOOR 1
🏛️ Reception Desk — The Governor (Art. 153–161)

The receptionist is the GOVERNOR. No election — he was appointed by the President's letter (Art. 155). He can cover 2 desks (2 states) since 7th CA 1956. His job contract says "5 years" but the President can fire him anytime (Doctrine of Pleasure, Art. 156). He's 35+ years old and Indian (Art. 157). He can't be an MP or MLA (Art. 158). His oath was given by the HC Chief Justice (Art. 159).

FLOOR 2
⚖️ Pardoning Room (Art. 161)

The Governor sits here granting mercy to prisoners. But there's a big LOCKED BOX labelled "DEATH PENALTY" and another "COURT MARTIAL" — he has no key to those. Only the President has the key (Art. 72). He can open all the other boxes: Pardon, Commute, Remit, Respite, Reprieve.

FLOOR 3
👔 PM's Office (Art. 74–75)

The PM sits here as real boss. On his desk: a STAMP (he advises; President rubber-stamps). A 42nd Amendment paper says "Advice is BINDING" and a 44th says "Can return once." His office has a HEADCOUNT board: "15% of Lok Sabha only" (91st CA 2003). Each minister's file has two stickers — "Individual: President" and "Collective: Lok Sabha." A calendar shows "6 MONTHS" to get elected or lose the job.

FLOOR 4
📞 Telephone Room — Art. 78 (PM Duties)

The PM sits on a telephone connecting two people — the CoM on one end and the President on the other. He is the LYNCHPIN (Nehru's word) — the bolt that holds the machine together. If he quits, the whole government falls.

FLOOR 5
⚖️ Legal Department — AG (Art. 76)

The ATTORNEY GENERAL has a bag with Supreme Court-level qualifications (5yr HC judge / 10yr HC advocate / distinguished jurist). He can walk into ANY court in India freely (right of audience). His contract says "Pleasure of President" but he still takes private cases — just not against the Government of India. First occupant: M.C. Setalvad.

MIRROR
🪞 The State Mirror

Everything you saw in this building — hold it up to a MIRROR. The reflection is the STATE government. Governor instead of President. CM instead of PM. Advocate General instead of Attorney General. Add 89 to all the article numbers (74→163, 75→164, 76→165, 78→167).

⚠️ Exam Traps
What toppers know that average students don't
🪤 TRAP 1: Governor's Qualification

President's qualification = Citizen of India + 35 years + qualified to be member of Lok Sabha.
Governor's qualification = Citizen of India + 35 years ONLY. NO Legislative Assembly qualification needed.
Examiners LOVE asking this difference.

🪤 TRAP 2: "Qualified to be appointed as SC/HC Judge"

AG must be "qualified to be a SC judge" — this does NOT mean he IS or WAS a SC judge. It means he has the qualifications (HC judge 5yrs OR HC advocate 10yrs OR distinguished jurist). Don't confuse "qualified to be" with "is."

🪤 TRAP 3: Death Sentence Pardon

Both President AND Governor can grant pardons. But ONLY the President can pardon death sentences. If a question says "Governor can pardon death sentence" — it is FALSE.

🪤 TRAP 4: 42nd vs 44th Amendment

42nd (1976): Made advice completely binding. 44th (1978): Gave President power to return advice ONCE for reconsideration — but reconsidered advice is still binding.
Don't confuse: 44th did NOT remove binding nature — it just added one-time reconsideration.

🪤 TRAP 5: 91st Amendment Cap

Council of Ministers strength cap = 15% of Lok Sabha members. This INCLUDES the PM (not excludes). Exam may ask "excluding PM" as wrong option.

🪤 TRAP 6: Solicitor General

Solicitor General assists the AG but is NOT a constitutional position — not mentioned in the Constitution. Examiners may try to trick you into saying it's a constitutional body.

🪤 TRAP 7: Oath — Who Administers to Whom

President → oath by CJI (Supreme Court Chief Justice)
Vice President → oath by President
Governor → oath by Chief Justice of High Court (NOT CJI)
This is a favourite trap — many confuse Governor's oath administrator.

🪤 TRAP 8: 7th Constitutional Amendment

7th CA (1956) did THREE things: (1) One person can be Governor of 2+ states, (2) Removed Part VII, (3) One HC for multiple states/UTs. Exam may ask any one and present others as wrong options.

🪤 TRAP 9: Minister Classification in Constitution

Cabinet Minister, MoS (IC), MoS, Deputy Minister — this hierarchy is NOT mentioned in the Constitution anywhere. It is a conventional/practical arrangement. Exam trap: "Which is NOT mentioned in Constitution" — answer: this classification.

🪤 TRAP 10: Collective Responsibility to Lok Sabha NOT Rajya Sabha

The Council of Ministers is collectively responsible to the Lok Sabha only, not Rajya Sabha or Parliament. The PM need not be a member of Lok Sabha (can be Rajya Sabha), but CoM answers to Lok Sabha.

❓ Active Recall — Test Yourself
Click Reveal to check your answer
1. Who appoints the Governor? Through what process?
The President appoints the Governor. There is NO election — direct appointment by Presidential warrant (Art. 155).
2. What is the Doctrine of Pleasure in context of the Governor?
The Governor holds office during the pleasure of the President (Art. 156). The President can remove the Governor at any time without following any specific procedure. No reason needs to be given.
3. Who administers the oath to the Governor?
Chief Justice of the High Court of that state (Art. 159). NOT the CJI (that's for President).
4. What are the TWO things the Governor CANNOT do that the President CAN do in pardoning power?
(1) Cannot pardon a death sentence. (2) Cannot suspend, remit, or commute sentences of court martial (military courts). These two powers are exclusively Presidential.
5. What did the 7th Constitutional Amendment of 1956 do? (All 3 points)
1. Allowed one person to be appointed Governor of two or more states simultaneously.
2. Repealed/removed Part VII of the Constitution.
3. Allowed one High Court to have jurisdiction over multiple states or union territories.
6. Fill in the blank: CoM strength cannot exceed ___% of total members of ___. Added by ___th Amendment of year ___.
15% of total members of Lok Sabha. Added by 91st Constitutional Amendment of 2003. This includes the PM.
7. What did the 42nd Amendment do to the President's obligation to follow CoM advice? How did the 44th Amendment change it?
42nd CA (1976): Made it completely binding — President MUST accept CoM advice, no option to return.
44th CA (1978): President can now return the advice ONCE for reconsideration, but the reconsidered advice coming back is binding.
8. What happens if a minister is not a member of any House of Parliament for 6 consecutive months?
He ceases to be a minister (Art. 75, Clause 5). Within 6 months of becoming a minister, one must get elected to either Lok Sabha or Rajya Sabha. This route was used for S. Jaishankar via Rajya Sabha from Gujarat.
9. Name the first Attorney General of India.
M.C. Setalvad (M.C. Selvad) was the first Attorney General of India.
10. Is the classification of Cabinet Minister / MoS / MoS-IC / Deputy Minister found in the Constitution?
NO. This hierarchy is NOT mentioned anywhere in the Constitution. It is a conventional/practical arrangement based on political practice.
11. Who called the PM the "Lynchpin of the Government"?
Jawaharlal Nehru called the Prime Minister the "Lynchpin of the Government" — referring to the PM's role as the link between the CoM and the President (Art. 78).
12. Can the Attorney General take private cases while serving? One restriction?
YES, the AG can take private cases — private practice is NOT barred. One restriction: cannot hold a brief (fight a case) against the Government of India.
13. Reverse: Article 78 deals with what?
Article 78 deals with the Duties of the Prime Minister — specifically, his duty to act as a communication link between the Council of Ministers and the President.
14. What is the Article number for: Conduct of Business of Government of a State?
Article 166 (= Art. 77 + 89). Art. 77 = Conduct of Business of Government of India. Add 89 → 166 = State equivalent.
