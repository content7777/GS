CHAPTER 1 · POLITY

Making of the Constitution

The story of how a newly independent nation of 350 million people wrote a single governing document — in 2 years, 11 months, and 17 days.

Big Picture

This chapter is about WHO demanded a Constituent Assembly, WHO rejected it and WHY, HOW it was structured, and WHAT it produced. Every fact here links to a political drama of independence. Understanding the drama = remembering the facts forever.

The Core Story in 3 Lines:

Indians demanded a body to write their own constitution → British stalled with half-measures (Dominion Status offers) → Cabinet Mission Plan finally worked → Constituent Assembly met, fought, and produced the world's longest constitution.

Chapter Memory Palace

Imagine walking through a grand Parliament Building under construction. Each room holds a memory.

MN Roy stands at the gate with a placard: "We need our own Constitution!" He is first. Remember: MN Roy = 1934 = Gate (First demand).

🏛️

📜

British receptionist offers three gifts: August Offer (1940) — REJECTED, Cripps Mission (1942) — REJECTED, Cabinet Mission (1946) — ACCEPTED. First two gifts were fake (Dominion Status), third was real enough.

🗓️

First meeting of Constituent Assembly. Sachchidanand Sinha (oldest man) sits in the temporary chair. Rajendra Prasad takes the permanent steering wheel.

📋

Ambedkar's room is the most important (Drafting Committee). Nehru's rooms have "Union" on the door. Patel's room has "Minorities" written. Rajendra Prasad owns the entire building.

✍️

Prem Bihari Narayan Raizad writes English in beautiful calligraphy (for free!). Vasant Krishna Vaidya writes Hindi. Nandlal Bose decorates the pages.

Constitution comes into force. Republic Day. Why 26th Jan? Because 26 Jan 1930 was the first independence day celebration (Lahore Session, 1929). Sacred date → chosen deliberately.

CHAPTER 1 · LAYER 1

Demand for Constituent Assembly

🔥 Must Know

Four people/bodies made the demand. Learn them in chronological order.

The Demand Timeline

1934
MN Roy (Manindranath Roy) — First ever demand for a Constituent Assembly. He was a Marxist thinker. Remember: Roy = Roy = Royal First.
1935
Indian National Congress officially placed the demand. Congress formalized what Roy started.
1936
Jawaharlal Nehru placed the demand at the Lucknow Session of Congress. Location matters for MCQs!
1940
August Offer — Viceroy Linlithgow gave Britain's first formal response. REJECTED — offered Dominion Status, not Purna Swaraj.
1942
Cripps Mission arrived. REJECTED — again offered Dominion Status. Congress wanted full independence.
1945–46
Cabinet Mission Plan — Formed in 1945, came to India in 1946. ACCEPTED. Finally paved way for a real Constituent Assembly.

Why Were August Offer & Cripps Mission Rejected?

August Offer + Cripps Mission → offered Dominion Status → Congress had declared Purna Swaraj (1929 Lahore) as goal → REJECTED

What is Dominion Status?

India gets power to make its own laws, BUT remains under the British Crown. Like a tenant who furnishes their own room but the landlord still owns the building. India wanted to OWN the building.

Purna Swaraj = Complete independence. No British Crown overhead.

Why Was Cabinet Mission Accepted?

⭐ Important
PartyWhy Accepted?
CongressNo explicit mention of Dominion Status. Path to full independence was implied.
Muslim LeagueGrouping of Provinces (Group A, B, C) — Jinnah thought he could carve Pakistan from Group B & C (Muslim majority areas).
Memory Hook
Jinnah saw the grouping as a back door to Pakistan. When Nehru said grouping is NOT compulsory, Jinnah slammed the door — called Direct Action Day (Aug 16, 1946), and the plan collapsed for the League. Eventually Pakistan was created separately.

Cabinet Mission — 3 Members

Came from Britain in 1946. Three people:

  • Pathik Lawrence (Pethick-Lawrence)
  • Alexander
  • Stafford Cripps (same person as Cripps Mission 1942)
Memory Hook
LAC — Lawrence, Alexander, Cripps. Like a lacquer coat on the constitution — they gave India its final polished path.

CHAPTER 1 · LAYER 1

British Offers — Quick Comparison

OfferYearBy WhomKey FeatureResult
August Offer 1940 Linlithgow (Viceroy) Dominion Status Rejected
Cripps Mission 1942 Stafford Cripps Dominion Status Rejected
Cabinet Mission 1946 Lawrence + Alexander + Cripps Grouping of Provinces (A, B, C) Accepted
⚠ EXAM TRAP

Stafford Cripps appears in BOTH the 1942 Cripps Mission AND the 1946 Cabinet Mission. Examiners love this confusion. Don't say Cabinet Mission = only Cripps. It had 3 members.

Why Did Muslim League Accept, Then Reject Cabinet Mission?

May 1946
Cabinet Mission arrives. Jinnah accepts — sees grouping (Group B = Muslim-majority NW, Group C = Muslim-majority NE) as a path to Pakistan.
July 1946
Nehru states that grouping is NOT compulsory — provinces can opt out. Jinnah is furious.
Aug 16, 1946
Muslim League calls Direct Action Day. Violence erupts. Cabinet Mission Plan effectively collapses for the League.
1947
Pakistan created as a separate country. Muslim League makes its own constitution.

Interim Government

Formed on 2nd September 1946. This was a transitional government before full independence. (Details covered in Lok Sabha chapter.)

Sardar Patel — Father of Indian Unity

➕ Good to Know

  • National Unity Day celebrated on 31 October (his birthday)
  • Also chaired Advisory Committee on Fundamental Rights, Minorities, and Tribal & Excluded Areas

What Else Did the Constituent Assembly Do?

The Constituent Assembly was not just a constitution-making body. It also:

  • Made laws for the country (Parliament function)
  • Adopted the National Flag (22 July 1947)
  • Ratified Commonwealth membership (May 1949)
  • Adopted National Anthem and National Song (24 Jan 1950)
  • Elected India's first President (Rajendra Prasad, 24 Jan 1950)

What is the Commonwealth?

Countries that were once British colonies. Britain is also a member. India joined voluntarily. India can leave anytime it wants. Currently still a member.

Provisions Implemented on 26 Nov 1949 (Not Waiting for 26 Jan 1950)

🔥 Must Know
  • Provisions related to Citizenship
  • Provisions related to Elections
  • Provisions related to Provisional Parliament

Why immediately? The country couldn't function without knowing who is a citizen, who can vote, and who governs provisionally.

MEMORY SYSTEM

Smart Memory Hooks

Hook 1 — The Demand Chain

Story
Picture a chain with 4 links: Roy's ring (1934) → Congress badge (1935) → Nehru's Lucknow scarf (1936) → British offers (1940, 1942, 1946). Roy starts, Congress continues, Nehru amplifies at Lucknow, British finally relent (partially).

Hook 2 — Rejected/Accepted Formula

Formula
If it says "Dominion Status" → REJECTED. Congress wanted full freedom (Purna Swaraj declared 1929 Lahore). August Offer = Dominion → Rejected. Cripps = Dominion → Rejected. Cabinet Mission never said "Dominion" explicitly → Accepted.

Hook 3 — 389 = 296 + 93

Math Hook
British India: 292 (from 11 governor provinces) + 4 (from 4 chief commissioner provinces) = 296.
Princely States: 93.
Total: 389. After partition, only 299 remained active.

Hook 4 — Ambedkar's Drafting Committee = MAGIC 7

Story
Seven samurai of the constitution. Ambedkar is the leader. Two samurai got injured mid-way (Mitter → Madhav Rao; Khaitan → TT Krishnamachari) and were replaced. But the team finished the job.

Hook 5 — 26 Nov vs 26 Jan

Mnemonic
Nov = Written, Jan = Born. Like an author writes a book (Nov 26 = written/adopted), and the book is PUBLISHED/released on Jan 26 (enforced). Writing ≠ publishing.

Hook 6 — The Steering Wheel (Rajendra Prasad)

Visual
Rajendra Prasad holds a steering wheel. He steers: Rules of Procedure, Finance, National Flag Committee, the whole Constituent Assembly. Wherever you see "head of house" or "president of assembly," the steering is Rajendra Prasad's.

Hook 7 — Prem Bihari = Free Calligrapher

Story
Prem Bihari was so in love (Prem = love in Hindi) with the nation that he wrote the entire English constitution for FREE. His only condition: write his name on each page. Selfless patriot with a personal branding request.

Hook 8 — Why 26 January?

Story
Nehru at the 1929 Lahore Session said: "Let's celebrate January 26th — it's holy because in 1930 we declared our first Purna Swaraj Day." Like how you choose an auspicious day for a housewarming — India chose a sacred day to "move in" to the new constitutional house.

EXAM STRATEGY

⚠ Exam Traps & Confusions

These are the exact points where marks are lost. Study each one carefully.

⚠ TRAP 1 — MN Roy vs M.N. Roy

MN Roy = Manindranath Roy. He made the demand in 1934. Not to be confused with Ram Manohar Lohia or any other "Roy." He was the FIRST to demand a Constituent Assembly.

⚠ TRAP 2 — V.T. vs T.T. Krishnamachari

V.T. Krishnamachari = Vice President of the Constituent Assembly. T.T. Krishnamachari = Member of the Drafting Committee (replaced D.P. Khaitan). Two different people, similar names. Exam loves this.

⚠ TRAP 3 — Cripps in Two Places

Stafford Cripps led the 1942 Cripps Mission AND was one of the three members of the 1946 Cabinet Mission. Don't confuse Cabinet Mission as "just Cripps" — it was Lawrence + Alexander + Cripps.

⚠ TRAP 4 — 26 Nov vs 26 Jan

26 November 1949 = Constitution Day (Samvidhan Diwas) = Constitution ADOPTED. 26 January 1950 = Republic Day = Constitution ENFORCED/IMPLEMENTED. Two events, two dates. Never mix them.

⚠ TRAP 5 — 389 vs 299

The Constituent Assembly was planned for 389 members. After Muslim League boycott and partition, only 299 seats remained for the working assembly. The constitution was made by 299, not 389.

⚠ TRAP 6 — Drafting Committee Chairman vs Chief Draftsman

Ambedkar = Chairman of the Drafting Committee. S.N. Mukherjee = Chief Draftsman (the person who actually drafted/wrote the legal text). Different roles!

⚠ TRAP 7 — Ambedkar's Original Constituency

Ambedkar was first elected from Bengal Constituency. After partition, this became part of East Pakistan. He was then re-elected from Bombay Constituency. Trick question: "Ambedkar was always from Bombay" — FALSE.

⚠ TRAP 8 — Time Taken: 17 or 18 days?

Constitution took 2 years, 11 months, and 17 days. Some sources say 18 days. Both may appear in options. The most commonly accepted answer is 17 days.

⚠ TRAP 9 — National Unity Day

National Unity Day = 31 October (Sardar Patel's birthday). NOT Independence Day, NOT Republic Day. Patel unified princely states → Unity Day on his birthday.

⚠ TRAP 10 — Sachchidanand Sinha's Role

Sachchidanand Sinha was the Temporary (Provisional) President of the first session on 9 Dec 1946 — because he was the oldest member. He was NOT the permanent president. Rajendra Prasad was elected permanent president.

⚠ TRAP 11 — 13 Minor vs 14 Minor

You may see "13 minor" or "14 minor" committees in different sources. For exam purposes: 8 Major is the definitive number. Minor is contested — go with 13 if forced to choose.

One-Liner Quiz Questions

Q1. The first person to demand a Constituent Assembly was ___________.
MN Roy (Manindranath Roy) — in 1934
Q2. Why was the Cripps Mission (1942) rejected by Congress?
It offered Dominion Status, but Congress had declared Purna Swaraj (complete independence) as its goal in the 1929 Lahore Session.
Q3. Why did Jinnah initially accept the Cabinet Mission Plan?
It had a provision for "Grouping of Provinces" (Group A, B, C). Jinnah thought he could use Muslim-majority Group B and C to eventually form Pakistan.
Q4. How were members of the Constituent Assembly selected?
Indirectly — members of Provincial Assemblies elected CA members (like Rajya Sabha). Princely State reps were nominated by their rulers. One seat per 10 lakh population.
Q5. V.T. Krishnamachari vs T.T. Krishnamachari — who was what?
V.T. Krishnamachari = Vice President of Constituent Assembly. T.T. Krishnamachari = Member of Drafting Committee (replaced D.P. Khaitan). TWO DIFFERENT PEOPLE.
Q6. Why was the Constitution enforced on 26 January 1950, not on 26 November 1949?
26 January was historically significant — it was the date of the first "Purna Swaraj" declaration (Independence Day celebration) in 1930, after the 1929 Lahore Session under Nehru. It was considered a sacred/auspicious date.
Q7. Which THREE provisions were implemented immediately on 26 Nov 1949 (without waiting for 26 Jan 1950)?
1. Citizenship provisions, 2. Election provisions, 3. Provisional Parliament provisions. The country couldn't function without knowing who is a citizen and who governs.
Q8. Ambedkar was replaced from which constituency and then stood from where?
Originally elected from Bengal Constituency. After partition, his area became East Pakistan. He was then elected from Bombay Constituency.
Q9. Who decorated the Constitution, and who wrote the calligraphy?
Decoration: Nandlal Bose + Beohar Rammanohar Sinha. English calligraphy: Prem Bihari Narayan Raizad (for free). Hindi calligraphy: Vasant Krishna Vaidya.
Q10. Nehru placed the demand for CA at which session of Congress?
Lucknow Session of Congress — 1936.
