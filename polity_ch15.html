Constitutional Amendments

All important amendments + procedure. Engineered for 20–100 revisions. Built to be unforgettable.

🌐

Big Picture

This chapter is the story of how India's Constitution evolves. Every amendment is a reaction — to a court ruling, a political crisis, a social need, or an international agreement. Understanding the WHY behind each amendment makes the numbers unforgettable. Think of amendments like chapters of Indian political history, not random numbers.

🧠

Chapter Memory Palace — The Constitutional Railway

Imagine a train journey. Each station = an era of amendments. Walk through it mentally every time you revise.

1
🚉 Station NEHRU (1951–56): Land & States

Nehru is at the platform handing over land to farmers (1st Amend). He then reshuffles the carriage labels from A/B/C/D to proper state names (7th Amend). He signs a paper with Pakistan's PM Noon to hand over Berubari (9th Amend).

2
🚉 Station INTEGRATION (1960–74): Bringing India Together

Dadra joins (10th), Goa kicks out Portuguese with Operation Vijay (12th/14th), Nagaland gets a VIP badge 371A (13th), Sindhi language boards the train (21st), HC judges get 2 extra years (15th), Sikkim sits in the waiting room as Associate State (35th).

3
🚉 Station INDIRA (1971–76): Power Plays

Indira fires the privy purse kings (26th), Parliament fights back against Golaknath and wins via 24th, adds 31C shield for DPSPs (25th). Then Emergency hits — 42nd is a STORM: preamble changes, duties added, President caged, states lose subjects.

4
🚉 Station JANATA REPAIR (1978): Fixing the Storm

44th amendment: cage broken (President can send advice back once), Right to Property thrown off the train (now just a legal right at Article 300A), "Armed Rebellion" replaces "Internal Disturbance", Special Majority needed for Emergency now.

5
🚉 Station DEMOCRACY (1985–92): People Power

52nd: Anti-defection law added (10th Schedule). 61st: Voting age drops from 21 → 18. 65th: SC/ST gets multi-member commission. 69th: Delhi gets Legislative Assembly as NCT. 71st: Konkani, Nepali, Manipuri board the 8th schedule train.

6
🚉 Station GRASSROOTS (1992): 73rd & 74th

Two giant stations: Panchayati Raj (Part IX, Art 243–243O, 11th Schedule) and Municipalities (Part IXA, Art 243P–243ZG, 12th Schedule). Democracy reaches the village.

7
🚉 Station MODERN INDIA (2002–2023): Rights & Reforms

86th: Education becomes FR (Art 21A). 89th: SC & ST get separate commissions (338 & 338A). 91st: Cabinet size capped at 15%. 92nd: Bodo, Dogri, Maithili, Santhali join 8th schedule. 99th: NJAC fails in court. 100th: Bangladesh land deal. 101st: GST arrives. 103rd: EWS 10% quota. 104th: Anglo-Indian nomination ends. 106th: Women get 33% seats (not yet active).

Amendment Procedure — Article 368

Source: Borrowed from South Africa | Article: 368 | Part: XX | KC Wheare called this procedure "Vice but rarely found" (quasi-rigid)

Simple Majority

  • 2nd Schedule, 3rd Schedule
  • Rules of procedure of Parliament
  • Citizenship provisions
  • Creating/abolishing Legislative Council
  • New states (Art 2 & 3) — NOT under Art 368
  • Flexible part of Constitution

Special Majority

  • Majority of total membership of House
  • PLUS 2/3 of members present & voting
  • (Whichever is higher prevails)
  • Fundamental Rights
  • DPSPs
  • Most provisions of Constitution

Special + State Ratification

  • Special majority in Parliament
  • PLUS simple majority in ≥ 14 states (half of 28)
  • Supreme Court / High Court provisions
  • President's election
  • 7th Schedule
  • Article 368 itself
  • State representation in Parliament
Key Rule Detail
Bill introduction Can be introduced in either Lok Sabha or Rajya Sabha (NOT State Legislature)
Joint sitting? NO — Joint sitting not allowed for Constitutional Amendment Bills (also not for Money Bills)
President's prior recommendation? NOT required (unlike Money Bills & Art. 3 bills)
President's assent? MANDATORY — President CANNOT use veto power (made mandatory by 24th Amendment)
Joint sitting provision Article 108 (borrowed from Australia)
🧠 MEMORY HOOK — Special Majority Calculation

Rajya Sabha has 245 members. Special majority = BOTH conditions must be met: (1) majority of 245 = 123+, AND (2) 2/3 of those present & voting. WHICHEVER number comes out BIGGER — that's your special majority threshold. It's a safety net with double locks.

Important Amendments — Complete Notes

1st
Land Reforms & 9th Schedule 🔥 Must Know
1951 | Jawaharlal Nehru
  • Added 9th Schedule to Constitution — laws inside it cannot be judicially reviewed (if added pre-1973)
  • Added Article 15(4) — reservation for backward classes not violative of Art 15 [Champakam Durai Rajan Case → Tamil Nadu]
  • Added Article 19(3) & 19(6) — additional reasonable restrictions on freedom of speech & occupation
  • Purpose: protect land reform laws from courts. Landlords were taking govt to court
  • IR Coelho Case: 9th Schedule laws added after 1973 can be reviewed by courts
5th
President's Time Limit Power
For state reorganization bills (Art 2 & 3)
  • President can now prescribe a time limit for state legislatures to give their recommendation on state-related bills
  • State legislature's recommendation is NOT binding on Parliament
7th
State Reorganisation 🔥 Must Know
1956
  • Abolished A, B, C, D grouping of states — removed Part VII of Constitution
  • Created 14 States & 6 Union Territories
  • Allowed common High Court for two or more states (e.g., Maharashtra & Goa share a HC)
  • Allowed common Governor for two or more states
9th
Nehru-Noon Agreement — Berubari ⭐ Important
1960
  • Implemented Nehru-Noon Agreement with Pakistan (PM Firoz Khan Noon)
  • Transferred Berubari Union to Pakistan
  • Berubari Case (1960): SC said you can TAKE territory by simple majority (Art 4), but to GIVE territory you need a Constitutional Amendment
  • Bangladesh at that time = East Pakistan
10th
Dadra & Nagar Haveli incorporated as UT
1961
  • Dadra and Nagar Haveli became part of Indian Union as a Union Territory
  • Ruled by Portuguese earlier. Operation Vijay (1961) removed them.
12th
Goa, Daman & Diu as UT ⭐ Important
1962
  • Incorporated Goa, Daman & Diu as Union Territory
  • Goa was NOT yet a state — it became full state only via 56th Amendment (1987)
  • Daman & Diu still remains a UT (now merged with Dadra & Nagar Haveli)
  • Portuguese expelled by Operation Vijay (1961) — "Portuguese: first to come, last to go"
13th
Nagaland — Article 371A
1962
  • Added Article 371A — gave special status/provisions to Nagaland
14th
Puducherry into Indian Union
  • Incorporated Puducherry into Indian Union (was under French rule)
  • Enabled UTs of Himachal, Manipur, Tripura, Goa, Daman-Diu, Puducherry to have Legislature & Council of Ministers
15th
HC Judge Retirement Age: 60 → 62
  • Increased retirement age of High Court judges from 60 to 62
  • Supreme Court judges retire at 65 (unchanged)
  • No minimum age prescribed for SC/HC judges
19th
Election Tribunals Abolished 🔥 Exam Trap!
1966
  • Abolished Election Tribunals
  • ⚠️ TRAP: Students often confuse this with Privy Purse abolition — Privy Purse was 26th, NOT 19th!
  • This came up in NTPC exam!
21st
Sindhi added to 8th Schedule ⭐ Important
  • Added Sindhi to the 8th Schedule
  • First language to be added after the original 14 (Constitution had 14 languages originally)
  • 8th Schedule now has 22 languages total
24th
Parliament CAN Amend FRs + President Must Give Assent 🔥 Must Know
1971
  • Trigger: Golaknath Case — SC said Parliament CANNOT amend Fundamental Rights
  • Parliament's reaction: 24th Amendment declared Parliament HAS power to amend any FR
  • Made it mandatory for President to give assent to Constitutional Amendment Bills — no veto power (cannot return, withhold, or pocket)
25th
Article 31C — DPSP Shield
1971
  • Added Article 31C
  • Implementing DPSPs of Art 39(B) & 39(C) shall NOT be violative of Art 14, 19, and 31
  • Also said judiciary cannot review — but Kesavananda Bharati Case (1973) struck down this judicial review bar
26th
Privy Purse Abolished 🔥 Must Know
1971 | Indira Gandhi
  • Privy Purse = annual payment given to princely state rulers to convince them to join India (Sardar Patel's deal)
  • Indira Gandhi abolished this payment system in 1971
  • ⚠️ TRAP: NTPC had a question where the answer "Privy Purse" was placed under 19th Amendment — WRONG! It was the 26th Amendment!
35th & 36th
Sikkim — From Guest to Full Member ⭐ Important
35th: 1974 | 36th: 1975
  • 35th Amendment (1974): Sikkim given status of Associate State (via Article 2A)
  • 36th Amendment (1975): Sikkim made full-fledged state, Article 2A removed
  • Earlier ruled by the Chogyaal dynasty
42nd
"Mini Constitution" 🔥 Must Know — Biggest Amendment
1976 | Indira Gandhi (Emergency Era)
  • Emergency declared in 1975 (grounds: internal disturbance), President = Fakhruddin Ali Ahmed
  • Preamble: Added 3 words — SOCIALIST, SECULAR, INTEGRITY
  • 4 new DPSPs added: Art 39F (children's protection), 39A (free legal aid), 43A (worker participation in industry mgmt), 48A (environment protection)
  • Fundamental Duties added — Part IVA, Article 51A (earlier NO fundamental duties existed)
  • Made President bound by Cabinet advice (no power to return advice)
  • Provided for Administrative Tribunals — Part XIV-A added
  • Transferred 5 subjects: Education, Forest, Weights & Measures, Justice Admin, Wild Animals from State List → Concurrent List
  • Union List: 97 → 100 | State List: 66 → 61 | Concurrent: 47 → 52
  • Extended 42nd's DPSP shield from only Art 39B/C to ALL DPSPs
44th
Undoing the Emergency Damage 🔥 Must Know
1978 | Janata Government
  • "Cabinet" word added to the Constitution for the first time
  • President can now return Cabinet advice ONCE (but must accept it the second time)
  • Right to Property removed from Fundamental Rights (Part III) → moved to Article 300A (Part XII) as a mere legal right
  • Emergency now requires Special Majority (not simple majority)
  • "Internal Disturbance" replaced by "Armed Rebellion" in Art 352 (grounds for National Emergency)
  • Restored fundamental rights that were curtailed during Emergency
  • Note: Art 300A legal rights → writs issued by High Court (HC has wider jurisdiction than SC on this)
52nd
Anti-Defection Law — 10th Schedule 🔥 Must Know
1985
  • Added 10th Schedule — Anti-Defection Law
  • Defection = leaving your party to join another
  • Independent member joining any party = disqualified
  • Nominated member (Rajya Sabha) joining any party after 6 months = disqualified
  • Exception (91st Amend): If ≥ 2/3rd of party members leave together = called MERGER (not split), defection doesn't apply
  • Disqualification decided by Presiding Officer (Speaker for LS, Chairman for RS)
  • Presiding Officer's decision is subject to judicial review
56th
Goa becomes Full State
1987
  • Goa granted full statehood (was UT since 12th Amendment, 1962)
  • Daman & Diu remained a separate UT
61st
Voting Age 21 → 18 🔥 Must Know
1988
  • Reduced voting age from 21 to 18
  • After this, Election Commission of India became a multi-member body for the first time
65th
SC/ST Commission becomes Multi-Member
  • Earlier: Single special officer for SC/ST
  • 65th: Converted into multi-member National Commission for SC/ST
  • ⚠️ Don't confuse with 89th: 89th came later and SPLIT this into two separate commissions
69th
Delhi = National Capital Territory 🔥 Must Know
1991
  • Delhi became National Capital Territory (NCT) with Legislative Assembly
  • Added Article 239AA & 239AB
  • Only 3 UTs with Legislature: Delhi, Puducherry, Jammu & Kashmir
  • Union can make laws on Land, Police, Public Order for Delhi (even though these are State List subjects)
  • Delhi's Council of Ministers capped at 10% of Legislative Assembly (by 69th Amend, not 91st)
71st
Konkani, Nepali, Manipuri added to 8th Schedule
1992
  • Added Konkani, Nepali, Manipuri (KNM) to 8th Schedule
  • Memory: "KoNeMa joined the schedule in 1992"
73rd & 74th
Panchayati Raj & Municipalities 🔥 Must Know
1992
  • 73rd: Added Part IX (Art 243 to 243O) + 11th Schedule (29 subjects) — Panchayati Raj (three-tier system mandatory)
  • 74th: Added Part IXA (Art 243P to 243ZG) + 12th Schedule — Municipalities (Urban Local Bodies)
  • Women get 1/3 reservation in Panchayat & Nagarpalika
86th
Right to Education — Article 21A 🔥 Must Know
2002
  • Added Article 21A — Free & Compulsory Education for children 6-14 years = Fundamental Right
  • Added duty in Article 51A for parents/guardians to educate their children
  • Changed Article 45 (DPSP): Earlier it had right to education → now it covers early childhood care for 0-6 years
89th
SC & ST Commissions Separated
2003
  • Split the combined SC/ST commission into two:
  • Article 338: National Commission for Scheduled Castes (also looks after Anglo-Indians)
  • Article 338A: National Commission for Scheduled Tribes
  • OBC Commission was statutory (not constitutional) — got constitutional status via 102nd Amendment (2018) → Article 338B
91st
Cabinet Size Capped at 15% 🔥 Must Know
2003
  • Added Art 75(1A): Council of Ministers at Centre cannot exceed 15% of Lok Sabha
  • Added Art 164(1A): State Council of Ministers cannot exceed 15% of State Legislative Assembly (min 12 ministers)
  • Delhi exception: 10% cap (via 69th Amendment)
  • Also added Art 75(1B): Member disqualified for defection also disqualified from becoming a Minister
  • Merger exception: if ≥ 2/3 members leave a party together = MERGER, not defection
92nd
Bodo, Dogri, Maithili, Santhali added to 8th Schedule ⭐ Important
2003
  • Added Bodo, Dogri, Maithili, Santhali to 8th Schedule
  • Memory: "BDMS joined in 2003"
  • 8th Schedule now has 22 languages total (original 14 + 8 added via 21st, 71st, 92nd)
93rd
Reservation in Private Educational Institutions
  • Provided for reservation in private unaided educational institutions
95th
SC/ST + Anglo-Indian Reservation Extended
2009
  • Extended SC/ST seat reservation in Lok Sabha (Art 330) and Legislative Assemblies (Art 332) for 10 more years
  • Also extended Anglo-Indian nomination for 10 years
  • In 2019: SC/ST reservation extended again BUT Anglo-Indian nomination ENDED (104th Amendment)
96th
Oriya → Odia (Name Change Only!) 🔥 Trap!
  • Changed name spelling from "Oriya" to "Odia" in 8th Schedule
  • ⚠️ NO new language was added — only spelling change!
97th
Cooperative Societies — Constitutional Status ⭐ Important
2012
  • Added Part IXB (Art 243ZH to 243ZT) — deals with Cooperative Societies
  • Added right to form cooperative society in Art 19(1)(c)
  • Added Art 43B (DPSP): State shall promote cooperative societies
99th
NJAC — Declared Unconstitutional! 🔥 Must Know
2014 (declared unconstitutional 2015)
  • Replaced Collegium System with NJAC (National Judicial Appointments Commission)
  • Collegium System: CJI + 4 senior judges recommend judges to President
  • Supreme Court in the Four Judges Case declared 99th Amendment unconstitutional
  • Collegium System was restored
  • No woman has been CJI till date
100th
Land Exchange with Bangladesh ⭐ Important
2015
  • Exchange of enclave territories with Bangladesh under Land Boundary Agreement
  • Similar to 9th Amendment (Nehru-Noon/Berubari) — giving territory requires a constitutional amendment (Berubari case principle)
  • Taking territory = simple majority (Art 4). Giving territory = amendment needed
101st
GST — Goods & Services Tax 🔥 Must Know
2016
  • Introduced GST — required Special Majority + ratification from at least half of states (14 states)
  • Why state ratification? GST changed the states' share of taxes — federal structure affected
  • State ratification requires only simple majority (not special)
102nd
OBC Commission gets Constitutional Status
2018
  • OBC Commission was statutory (by law) — now got constitutional status
  • Article 338B: National Commission for Backward Classes
  • Order: SC (338) → ST (338A) → OBC (338B)
103rd
EWS — 10% Reservation 🔥 Must Know
2019
  • Provided 10% reservation for Economically Weaker Sections (EWS)
104th
Anglo-Indian Nomination Ended ⭐ Important
2019
  • Ended nomination of Anglo-Indians in Lok Sabha (Art 331 — 2 members) and State Assemblies (Art 333 — 1 member)
  • SC/ST reservation in Lok Sabha & Assemblies extended for 10 more years (till 2030)
  • Anglo-Indian nomination had been extended every 10 years since independence — now permanently ended
105th
States can maintain own OBC Lists
  • Restored power of state governments to identify and maintain their own OBC lists (socially and educationally backward classes)
106th
33% Women Reservation — Nari Shakti Vandan 🔥 Must Know
2023
  • Reserved 33% seats for women in Lok Sabha and State Legislative Assemblies
  • ⚠️ NOT in Rajya Sabha or Vidhan Parishad
  • ⚠️ NOT yet implemented — will be implemented after next Census + Delimitation Commission
  • Currently women have reservation only in Panchayat & Nagarpalika (1/3 = 33.3%)

Exam Traps — What Students Always Get Wrong

⚠️ TRAP 1: Privy Purse vs Election Tribunals

19th Amendment (1966) abolished Election Tribunals — NOT Privy Purse!

26th Amendment (1971) abolished Privy Purse — NOT 19th!

📌 NTPC exam had a question where Privy Purse was given as an option for 19th Amendment. Many fell for it.

⚠️ TRAP 2: Goa's Amendment History
  • 12th Amendment (1962) → Goa incorporated as UT
  • 56th Amendment (1987) → Goa became full State
  • Daman & Diu remained a separate UT (now merged with Dadra & Nagar Haveli)
⚠️ TRAP 3: SC/ST Commission — 65th vs 89th
  • 65th Amendment → Single officer CONVERTED to multi-member commission
  • 89th Amendment (2003) → The one commission SPLIT into TWO: 338 (SC) and 338A (ST)
⚠️ TRAP 4: Simple vs Special Majority Confusion
  • Simple majority = more than 50% of members present & voting (normal bills)
  • Special majority = majority of TOTAL membership AND 2/3 of present & voting (whichever is higher)
  • Simple majority provisions are NOT counted as amendments under Article 368
⚠️ TRAP 5: Joint Sitting — Which Bills Are Excluded?
  • Joint Sitting (Art 108, borrowed from Australia) is NOT allowed for:
  • 1. Constitutional Amendment Bills
  • 2. Money Bills
  • These are the two exceptions — know both!
⚠️ TRAP 6: 96th Amendment — No New Language!
  • 96th Amendment only changed "Oriya" → "Odia" (spelling only)
  • No new language was added by the 96th Amendment
  • 8th Schedule has 22 languages (additions by 21st, 71st, 92nd)
⚠️ TRAP 7: 106th Amendment (Women's Reservation) — NOT YET ACTIVE
  • 106th Amendment reserved 33% for women in LS & State Assemblies
  • NOT in Rajya Sabha or Vidhan Parishad
  • Will be implemented ONLY AFTER: Census + Delimitation Commission
  • Currently women have reservation ONLY in Panchayat & Nagarpalika (1/3)
⚠️ TRAP 8: 99th Amendment — NJAC Declared Unconstitutional
  • 99th Amendment created NJAC to replace collegium system
  • Supreme Court (Four Judges Case) struck it down — unconstitutional
  • Collegium system was RESTORED (CJI + 4 senior judges recommend to President)
⚠️ TRAP 9: Which Amendment Made President's Assent Mandatory?
  • Answer: 24th Amendment — NOT 42nd!
  • 42nd made President bound by Cabinet advice
  • 44th allowed President to return Cabinet advice ONCE
⚠️ TRAP 10: "Internal Disturbance" vs "Armed Rebellion"
  • Original Art 352: "War, External Aggression, Internal Disturbance"
  • 44th Amendment: "Internal Disturbance" REPLACED by "Armed Rebellion"
  • The 42nd Amendment had Internal Disturbance — Indira used it to justify Emergency
⚠️ TRAP 11: Right to Property — Article Number
  • Right to Property was a Fundamental Right under Art 31 (Part III)
  • 44th Amendment (1978) removed it from Part III
  • Now it's a Legal Right under Art 300A (Part XII)
  • Writs for legal rights issued by HIGH COURT (not SC) — HC has wider jurisdiction here
⚠️ TRAP 12: Sikkim — 35th vs 36th
  • 35th Amendment (1974): Sikkim = Associate State (Article 2A added)
  • 36th Amendment (1975): Sikkim = Full State (Article 2A REMOVED)
  • Ruled by Chogyaal dynasty before joining India
⚠️ TRAP 13: Delhi's Council of Ministers Cap
  • All states: 15% of Legislative Assembly (91st Amendment)
  • Delhi: 10% of Delhi Assembly (69th Amendment — different amendment!)
⚠️ TRAP 14: Wakf Amendment ≠ Constitutional Amendment
  • Wakf Amendment Act (Umeed Act 2025) is NOT a Constitutional Amendment
  • Don't call it 107th Constitutional Amendment
  • It replaced the Wakf Act 1995 — it's a regular parliamentary act

🔥 Q1. From which country did India borrow the Constitutional Amendment procedure?
Tap to reveal
South Africa | Article 368 | Part XX
🔥 Q2. What is Special Majority? How is it different from Simple Majority?
Tap to reveal
Special Majority = (1) Majority of TOTAL membership of House + (2) 2/3 of members present & voting. Whichever number is higher = the threshold. Simple majority = just more than 50% of present & voting.
🔥 Q3. Name 5 things added by the 42nd Amendment (Mini Constitution).
Tap to reveal
1. Preamble: Socialist, Secular, Integrity | 2. Fundamental Duties (Art 51A, Part IVA) | 3. 4 new DPSPs (39F, 39A, 43A, 48A) | 4. Transferred Education/Forest/etc to Concurrent List | 5. President bound by Cabinet advice | 6. Administrative Tribunals (Part XIV-A)
🔥 Q4. What did the 44th Amendment do to 'Right to Property'?
Tap to reveal
Removed it from Fundamental Rights (Part III). Made it a Legal Right under Article 300A (Part XII). High Court (not SC) issues writs for legal rights.
⭐ Q5. What was abolished by the 19th Amendment (1966)?
Tap to reveal
ELECTION TRIBUNALS — NOT Privy Purse! Privy Purse was abolished by 26th Amendment (1971).
⭐ Q6. What is the 'Merger Exception' in Anti-Defection Law?
Tap to reveal
If 2/3 or more members of a political party leave together, it is called a MERGER (not split/defection). Anti-defection law does NOT apply to mergers. (Added by 91st Amendment)
🔥 Q7. Which amendment added 'Socialist', 'Secular', and 'Integrity' to the Preamble?
Tap to reveal
42nd Amendment (1976) — during Emergency period, President was Fakhruddin Ali Ahmed.
⭐ Q8. How many languages are in the 8th Schedule? Which amendments added them?
Tap to reveal
22 languages. Original = 14. Added by: 21st (Sindhi) + 71st/1992 (Konkani, Nepali, Manipuri) + 92nd/2003 (Bodo, Dogri, Maithili, Santhali). 96th = only Oriya → Odia (spelling change, NO new language).
🔥 Q9. What happened to NJAC? Which case? Which amendment?
Tap to reveal
99th Amendment created NJAC to replace Collegium System. Supreme Court declared 99th Amendment UNCONSTITUTIONAL in the Four Judges Case. Collegium System was RESTORED.
🔥 Q10. Fill in: SC Commission = Art ___, ST Commission = Art ___, OBC Commission = Art ___
Tap to reveal
SC = Art 338 | ST = Art 338A | OBC = Art 338B (102nd Amendment, 2018). SC Commission also covers Anglo-Indians.
⭐ Q11. What does 'Internal Disturbance' have to do with Constitutional Amendments?
Tap to reveal
42nd Amendment: Indira Gandhi justified Emergency on grounds of "Internal Disturbance" (Art 352). 44th Amendment (1978) REPLACED "Internal Disturbance" with "Armed Rebellion" to prevent misuse.
⭐ Q12. Goa was incorporated as UT by which amendment? When did it become a full state?
Tap to reveal
12th Amendment (1962) → UT. 56th Amendment (1987) → Full State. Portuguese expelled by Operation Vijay (1961). "Portuguese = first to come, last to go from India."
🔥 Q13. Which amendment deals with Panchayati Raj? What part and schedule was added?
Tap to reveal
73rd Amendment (1992). Added Part IX (Articles 243 to 243O) and 11th Schedule (29 subjects). Three-tier system mandatory. Women get 1/3 reservation.
⭐ Q14. What is the ONLY bill type where President is BOUND to give assent?
Tap to reveal
Constitutional Amendment Bills (made mandatory by 24th Amendment). For all other bills, President has some veto options. For CA Bills: no return, no withholding, no pocket veto.
🔥 Q15. 106th Amendment (Women 33%) — what are the 3 key limitations to remember?
Tap to reveal
1. NOT in Rajya Sabha or Vidhan Parishad — only Lok Sabha + State Assemblies. 2. NOT yet active/implemented. 3. Will be activated only after next Census AND Delimitation Commission formation.
➕ Q16. What is the Collegium System? How many judges?
Tap to reveal
CJI + 4 senior-most judges of SC recommend judges to President. President is bound by their advice. Emerged from Second Judges Case. No woman has been CJI of India yet.
⭐ Q17. Which amendment made Delhi a National Capital Territory? What special restrictions apply?
Tap to reveal
69th Amendment (1991). Added Art 239AA and 239AB. Delhi gets Legislative Assembly. BUT Union (Centre) can make laws on Land, Police, and Public Order (State List subjects) for Delhi. Delhi Cabinet max = 10% of Assembly.
🔥 Q18. Which amendment is called "Mini Constitution" and why?
Tap to reveal
42nd Amendment (1976). Called Mini Constitution because it made the most changes to the Constitution: changed Preamble, added Fundamental Duties, added DPSPs, shifted subjects to Concurrent List, caged the President, gave power to administrative tribunals, etc.
⭐ Q19. Reverse Question: The word "Cabinet" appears in the Constitution. Which amendment added it?
Tap to reveal
44th Amendment (1978). Before this, the word "Cabinet" did not appear anywhere in the Constitution.
➕ Q20. What does KC Wheare say about the Indian Constitution's amendment procedure?
Tap to reveal
KC Wheare called the Indian amendment procedure "Vice but rarely found" (meaning rigid but with a flexible element). He also called Indian federalism "Quasi-Federal."
