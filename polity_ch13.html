Big Picture
What this chapter is really about

This chapter is the evolutionary story of Indian governance — from a private trading company in the 17th century to a fully independent democratic republic in 1947. Each Act is a stepping stone: the British gradually tightened control over the Company, then transferred power from the Company to the Crown, and finally handed sovereignty to India.


Think of it as 3 phases:
Phase 1 (1773–1833): British regulates and absorbs East India Company
Phase 2 (1858–1935): Direct Crown Rule — building towards democracy
Phase 3 (1947): Independence and partition

Chapter Memory Palace
Walk through this train journey every time you revise
🚂 The Company-to-Crown Express (1773 → 1947)

Imagine a train journey from the East India Company's headquarters to India's freedom. Each station is an Act.


🚉 Station 1 — 1773: You board at "Regulate Station". Warren Hastings is the first conductor (Governor General). The Supreme Court is the first-class cabin. 4 executive members sit around him.


🚉 Station 2 — 1784: "Pitt's Divide Station". The train splits in two — one half (commercial) goes to Court of Directors, the other half (administrative) goes to the new Board of Control. Half law system. Also, the carriage is now labelled "British Possession."


🚉 Station 3 — 1813: "Monopoly Break Station". The tea compartment and China compartment still have locks. But all other compartments are now open to all traders. Missionaries board the train with ₹1 lakh bags.


🚉 Station 4 — 1833: "St. Helena Mega-Station" (remember the island near Africa). The Governor General of Bengal gets a giant crown: now Governor General of India. Macaulay becomes the head of the law library car. Company closes its trading shop completely — only an administrative body now.


🚉 Station 5 — 1853: "Open Exam Station". They announce open exams for civil services — but just attempt, not actually do it. Legislature and Executive get separate compartments for the first time.


🚉 Station 6 — 1858: "Crown Takes Over Station". A new conductor called Viceroy Canning boards. Board of Control and Court of Directors are thrown off. A Secretary of State with 15 assistants now controls the train from London.


🚉 Station 7 — 1861: "Decentralize Station". Power flows back to provinces. Portfolio bags are distributed (ministers get departments). Indians nominated to legislative council — Raja of Banaras boards!


🚉 Station 8 — 1909: "Morley-Minto Communal Station". Council size explodes: 16 → 60. Satyendra Prasad Sinha enters the Executive compartment. But wait — Muslims are told they have their OWN separate coach now. Separate electorate begins.


🚉 Station 9 — 1919: "Montagu-Chelmsford Diarchy Station". Train splits at provincial level: Reserved and Transferred subjects. First direct elections! High Commissioner office opens in London. 3 of 6 executive members are now Indian.


🚉 Station 10 — 1935: "Big Reform Station". Diarchy moves to Centre; provinces get autonomy. RBI is born. Federal Court opens. Burma separates. Three lists appear (Federal, Provincial, Concurrent). 15-member Secretary of State council dissolved.


🏁 Final Station — 1947: "Independence Platform". British rule ends. India + Pakistan created. Viceroy post abolished. Secretary of State's office abolished. Mountbatten = last British Governor General. C. Rajagopalachari = first and only Indian Governor General.

Complete Revision Notes — Act by Act
Every fact preserved. Every exam point tagged.
1773 Regulating Act
Regulating Act of 1773 🔥 Must Know
  • Governor of Bengal → Governor General of Bengal. Warren Hastings = 1st GG of Bengal 🔥
  • Warren Hastings was also the last Governor of Bengal (dual fact trap!) 🔥
  • Executive Council of 4 members created to assist GG
  • Bombay and Madras Presidencies made subordinate to Bengal ⭐
  • Established Supreme Court at Calcutta in 1774 — Chief Justice + 3 judges. First CJ: Elijah Impey 🔥
  • Servants of Company prohibited from taking bribes
  • Court of Directors required to report on revenue, civil & military affairs
💡 First Act by which Parliament started controlling Company. Beginning of Legislative control.
1781 Amending Act
Amending Act of 1781 ➕ Good to Know
  • Brought to correct defects in Regulating Act 1773 — not independently important
1784 Pitt's India Act
Pitt's India Act, 1784 🔥 Must Know
  • Called "Double Government" or "Half Law System" 🔥
  • Court of Directors → Commercial Affairs (C = Commercial = Court)
  • Board of Control (new) → Administrative Affairs
  • For the first time, Company's territories called "British Possessions in India" (not Company possessions) 🔥
  • Separated commercial and administrative functions of the Company
💡 Why "Half"? Because both Court of Directors AND Board of Control existed — power was split, not unified.
1793 Charter Act
Charter Act of 1793 ⭐ Important
  • Extended Company's trade monopoly for another 20 years ⭐
  • Commander-in-Chief was NOT a member of the Governor General's Council
1813 Charter Act
Charter Act of 1813 🔥 Must Know
  • Ended Company's trade monopoly (EXCEPT: tea trade + trade with China) 🔥
  • Asserted sovereignty of British Crown over Company's territories in India ⭐
  • Allowed Christian missionaries to come to India
  • Set aside ₹1 lakh for Indian education (Christian missionaries to spend)
  • Local governments authorized to impose taxes on individuals
1833 Charter Act / St. Helena Act
Charter Act of 1833 — Also called St. Helena Act 🔥 Must Know
  • St. Helena = island near Africa transferred to East India Company (that's why the name) 🔥
  • Governor General of Bengal → Governor General of India (centralization complete!) 🔥
  • Lord William Bentinck = 1st Governor General of India
  • Bombay and Madras Governors deprived of legislative powers — extreme centralization
  • Provided for one Law Member in GG's Council
  • First Law Commission Chairman: Thomas Babington Macaulay 🔥
  • East India Company became purely administrative — ended as commercial body
  • Last monopoly (tea + China trade) also ended
  • ATTEMPTED (not introduced!) open competition for civil services 🔥
⚠ TRAP: 1833 ATTEMPTED open competition. 1853 INTRODUCED it. Don't confuse!
1853 Charter Act
Charter Act of 1853 🔥 Must Know
  • Introduced open competition for Civil Services (ICS) 🔥
  • Separated legislative and administrative functions of GG's Council 🔥
  • Added 6 new members to GG's Council as Legislative Councillors (4 exec + 6 legis = legislature)
  • Extended Company's rule but period not specified (clear hint that rule could end anytime)
  • Introduced local representation in Indian Legislative Council (4 of 6 from local govts)
1858 Govt. of India Act
Government of India Act, 1858 — Act for Good Governance of India 🔥 Must Know
  • East India Company abolished. Reason: Revolt of 1857 🔥
  • Direct Crown Rule established — Queen Victoria was the Queen
  • Governor General → Viceroy. First Viceroy: Lord Canning 🔥
  • Ended Double Government — Board of Control + Court of Directors BOTH abolished 🔥
  • Created new office: Secretary of State for India — a British Member of Parliament
  • Secretary of State assisted by a 15-member Council of India ⭐
1861 Indian Councils Act
Indian Councils Act, 1861 🔥 Must Know
  • Decentralization started (reverse of 1833's extreme centralization) ⭐
  • Viceroy nominated: Raja of Banaras, Maharaja of Patiala, Sir Dinkar Rao to Legislative Council
  • New legislative councils for Bengal, North-Western Province and Punjab
  • Recognition of Portfolio System for the first time 🔥
  • Portfolio system = specific department allotted to a specific minister
1909 Indian Councils Act
Indian Councils Act, 1909 — Morley-Minto Reforms 🔥 Must Know
  • Morley = Secretary of State; Minto = Viceroy — hence "Morley-Minto"
  • Increased Legislative Council size from 16 → 60 members 🔥
  • Official majority in Central; Non-official majority in Provincial ⭐
  • Members allowed to ask supplementary questions and move resolutions on budget
  • Indians associated with Viceroy's Executive Council — Satyendra Prasad Sinha = first Indian 🔥
  • Introduced SEPARATE ELECTORATE for Muslims 🔥
  • Minto known as "Father of Communal Electorate" in India 🔥
1919 Govt. of India Act
Government of India Act, 1919 — Montagu-Chelmsford Reforms 🔥 Must Know
  • Montagu = Secretary of State; Chelmsford = Viceroy
  • Divided subjects into Central and Provincial (not three lists yet)
  • Further divided Provincial subjects into Reserved and Transferred — this is DIARCHY 🔥
  • Diarchy introduced in Provinces (not Centre)
  • Started Bicameral Legislature at centre for the first time ⭐
  • Introduced direct elections for the first time ⭐
  • 3 out of 6 Viceroy's Executive Council members to be Indians ⭐
  • Created office of High Commissioner for India in London
  • Provided for establishment of Public Service Commission (set up in 1926)
  • Extended separate electorate to Sikhs, Indian Christians, Anglo-Indians and Europeans ⭐
1935 Govt. of India Act
Government of India Act, 1935 🔥 Must Know
  • Diarchy abolished from provinces → Provincial Autonomy given 🔥
  • Diarchy introduced at Centre (shifted from provinces)
  • Provinces now got Bicameral Legislature (6 out of 11 provinces) ⭐
  • Proposed All India Federation — but never came into existence 🔥
  • Adult franchise extended — but only 14% of population could vote
  • Reserve Bank of India established 🔥
  • Federal Public Service Commission + Provincial PSC + Joint PSC established
  • Federal Court established in 1937 (ended on 26 Jan 1950 when Supreme Court born) 🔥
  • Burma (Myanmar) separated from India through this Act 🔥
  • Powers divided into three lists: Federal, Provincial, Concurrent 🔥
  • Abolished 15-member Council of Secretary of State ⭐
💡 Largest part of our Constitution was borrowed from this Act! Major structural framework.
1947 Independence Act
Indian Independence Act, 1947 🔥 Must Know
  • Ended British rule — India declared independent and sovereign 🔥
  • Partition of India and Pakistan — both given right to secede from British Commonwealth
  • India ratified British Commonwealth membership in May 1949 by Constituent Assembly
  • Abolished office of Viceroy 🔥
  • Abolished office of Secretary of State for India (15-member Council abolished in 1935) 🔥
  • Princely states given choice: join India, join Pakistan, or remain independent
  • ~562 princely states — 559 joined; 3 agitated: Junagadh, Hyderabad, J&K 🔥
  • Junagadh → Plebiscite; Hyderabad → Police Action; J&K → Instrument of Accession (Hari Singh)
  • Last British Governor General: Mountbatten 🔥
  • First & only Indian Governor General: C. Rajagopalachari (Rajaji) 🔥

Smart Memory Hooks
Vivid associations that survive 6 months of forgetting
1773 Regulating Act
🎬 "Warren Hastings, THE FIRST CONDUCTOR" — He's both the LAST Governor AND FIRST Governor General (a double role actor). Supreme Court was built in 1774 — one year AFTER the Act. Chief Justice Elijah Impey = EI (East India) → his initials match the company!
1784 Pitt's India Act
🍕 "PITT splits the pizza" — One half (C for Cheese = Commercial = Court of Directors). Other half (A for Admin = Board of Control). The pizza box is now stamped "British Property" — not the chef's (Company's) pizza!
1833 St. Helena Act
🏝 "HELENA near AFRICA" — Napoleon died on St. Helena island! Remember St. Helena = 1833. MacAulay is in his MAC (laptop) writing laws. GG of Bengal gets promoted to GG of INDIA = biggest promotion ever. Company closes its shop — only HR department left.
1833 vs 1853 — THE BIG TRAP
🎯 "1833 ATTEMPTED a marathon but didn't finish. 1853 ACTUALLY COMPLETED the marathon." — Open competition for Civil Services: 1833 = tried, failed. 1853 = implemented. Never mix these up!
1858 — Crown Takes Over
👑 "CANNING was the CANNON that fired 1857" — The Revolt of 1857 was the REASON. Canning = first Viceroy. Secretary of State = British MP who sits in London controlling India. His 15 secretaries assist him.
1909 Morley-Minto
🕌 "MINTO gave MUSLIMS their own MINT" — Separate electorate = Muslims get their own coin (mint = separate). Minto = Father of Communal Electorate. 16 → 60 members = quadrupled! Satyendra Prasad Sinha breaks the glass ceiling.
1919 — Diarchy Provinces
🎭 "DIARCHY = Double Drama in Provinces" — Reserved (kept by British) + Transferred (given to Indians). MC reforms = Montagu-Chelmsford. 3 of 6 Executive = half-Indians entering the room. High Commissioner = India's first embassy in London!
1935 — The Switcher
🔄 "1935 SWAPPED everything 1919 did" — Provinces had Diarchy → removed. Centre gets Diarchy → added. Provinces had no bicameralism → added (6/11). Centre had bicameralism → kept. RBI born. Burma leaves. Federal Court opens. The big SWAP Act!
1947 — The 3 Stubborn States
😤 "Junagadh VOTED, Hyderabad got KICKED, Kashmir SIGNED" — Junagadh: Plebiscite (people voted). Hyderabad: Police Action (Operation Polo). J&K: Instrument of Accession signed by Hari Singh. 3 different methods for 3 stubborn states!
Governor General vs Viceroy
🎩 "Company man = GG. Crown man = Viceroy." Under Company → Governors General. Under Crown (post 1858) → Viceroys. After Independence → Governor General again (briefly). Last British = Mountbatten. First+Only Indian = Rajaji (C. Rajagopalachari).

Concept Connections
How facts link to each other and to modern Constitution
1833 Centralization → 1861 Decentralization → 1919 Further Decentralization
1833 stripped all power from Bombay and Madras → 1861 began restoring power (decentralization starts) → 1919 officially divided Central and Provincial subjects → 1935 gave full Provincial Autonomy. Power flowed out from centre, then back to states — like Indian Constitution's federal structure today.
Diarchy 1919 → Abolished 1935 (Provinces) → Born at Centre 1935
Diarchy = Reserved (British) + Transferred (Indian ministers). 1919 put it in provinces. 1935 removed it from provinces (gave them autonomy) but introduced it at Centre. Like a hot potato being passed from provinces to centre.
1909 Separate Electorate → 1919 Extended → Communal Award 1932 → Abolished 1947
The communal electorate that Minto started for Muslims in 1909 was a seed of future communal divide. Extended to more communities in 1919, deepened communal politics, and ultimately contributed to the demand for Pakistan by 1947.
1853 Civil Services → UPSC Today
1853 introduced open competition for civil services → 1919 Act said to establish Public Service Commission → 1926 Central PSC actually set up → 1935 renamed to Federal PSC → After independence became UPSC (Article 315). Straight lineage from 1853 to today's UPSC!
1935 Three Lists → Article 246 + Seventh Schedule
1935 Act had Federal List, Provincial List, Concurrent List. Our Constitution's Seventh Schedule (Union List, State List, Concurrent List) is directly borrowed from this. Concurrent List borrowed from Australia's Constitution. Current counts: Union 100, State 61, Concurrent (ask in comments!).
1937 Federal Court → Article 374 + Supreme Court (28 Jan 1950)
Federal Court of 1935 Act started functioning in 1937. When India became republic on 26 Jan 1950, Supreme Court was established. Federal Court ended 26 Jan 1950; SC started functioning 28 Jan 1950. Article 374 transferred all pending cases of Federal Court to Supreme Court.
1858 Secretary of State → 1935 15-Member Council Abolished → 1947 Secretary of State Abolished
1858 created Secretary of State + 15-member Council. 1935 abolished the 15-member Council. 1947 abolished the Secretary of State's office itself. Three-step demolition of the British India administration structure.

Exam Traps
What toppers know that others get wrong
⚠ CRITICAL TRAPS — READ BEFORE EXAM
Warren Hastings was BOTH the last Governor of Bengal AND the first Governor General of Bengal. Two titles, one person. Don't answer as if they're different people.
1833 ATTEMPTED open competition for civil services. 1853 INTRODUCED it. The word "attempted" vs "introduced" is the exam trap. 1833 = tried and failed. 1853 = actually done.
Pitt's Act 1784 = "Double Government" NOT "Dual Government" — Diarchy = Dual Government (1919). Double Government = 1784. Don't confuse Diarchy with Double Government!
Supreme Court was established in 1774 (one year after the Regulating Act 1773). The Act came in 1773, Court came in 1774. Questions may say "established by Regulating Act 1773 in 1773" — WRONG! It says established IN 1774.
1st Governor General of India ≠ 1st Governor General of Bengal. Bengal GG: Warren Hastings (1773). India GG: Lord William Bentinck (1833). Two different acts, two different people.
Diarchy was introduced in PROVINCES by 1919 Act, NOT at the centre. And abolished from provinces by 1935 Act, which gave diarchy to the CENTRE. This flip is a favourite exam question.
Bicameral Legislature at Centre: introduced by 1919. Bicameral Legislature in Provinces: introduced by 1935 (only 6 of 11 provinces, not all).
All India Federation (1935) was proposed but NEVER came into existence. Don't say "1935 established All India Federation."
Federal Court (1937–1950) ≠ Supreme Court. Federal Court ended exactly when SC was established. Questions may try to say Federal Court became SC — not quite, it ended and SC was newly constituted.
Act Names: 1909 and 1861 are "Indian Councils Act" (not Govt. of India Act). 1858, 1919, 1935 are "Government of India Act." Many students confuse these.
Minto is Father of Communal Electorate (not Morley). Morley was Secretary of State, Minto was Viceroy. The separate electorate idea originated with Minto's support for Muslim League demands.
C. Rajagopalachari was FIRST AND ONLY Indian Governor General. After him, the post was abolished. Do NOT confuse with first President (Rajendra Prasad). Two different positions!
Burma (Myanmar) was separated by 1935 Act, not by the Independence Act of 1947. This is a common confusion — Burma got separated before Indian independence.
Princely State methods: Junagadh = Plebiscite (not accession). Hyderabad = Police Action. J&K = Instrument of Accession signed by Maharaja Hari Singh. These 3 methods are frequently asked.

Active Recall Section
Click to reveal answers — test yourself every revision
What were the THREE things Regulating Act 1773 created for the first time? +
1. Governor General of Bengal (Warren Hastings) → 2. Executive Council of 4 members → 3. Supreme Court at Calcutta (1774). Also: Bombay and Madras made subordinate to Bengal.
Pitt's India Act 1784: Why is it called "Double Government"? Who handled what? +
Two bodies ran India simultaneously: Court of Directors (Commercial affairs) + Board of Control (Administrative affairs). Neither had full control — hence "double" or "half-law" system. First time company territories called "British Possessions."
What is the difference between 1833 and 1853 Charter Acts regarding Civil Services? +
1833 = ATTEMPTED to introduce open competition for civil services (just an attempt, not implemented). 1853 = ACTUALLY INTRODUCED open competition for civil services (ICS). This is the most common exam trap in this chapter!
Fill in the blank: 1773 made Warren Hastings GG of ___. 1833 made Lord Bentinck GG of ___. +
1773 → Governor General of BENGAL. 1833 → Governor General of INDIA. The distinction matters — GG of Bengal had authority only over Bengal, Bihar, Orissa. GG of India had authority over ALL of British India.
Diarchy: In which year was it introduced and where? In which year was it removed from there? +
1919 Act introduced Diarchy in PROVINCES (Reserved + Transferred subjects). 1935 Act abolished Diarchy from provinces (gave provincial autonomy) but introduced Diarchy at the CENTRE. Big swap!
Name all 3 "troublesome" princely states and HOW each was integrated. +
1. JUNAGADH → Plebiscite (people voted to join India, ruler wanted Pakistan). 2. HYDERABAD → Police Action / Operation Polo (military force by Sardar Patel). 3. JAMMU & KASHMIR → Instrument of Accession signed by Maharaja Hari Singh after Pakistani tribal invasion.
Who abolished the Council of 15 members of the Secretary of State? Who abolished the Secretary of State's office itself? +
15-member Council: Abolished by Government of India Act, 1935. Secretary of State's office itself: Abolished by Indian Independence Act, 1947. Two separate acts, two separate steps!
Rapid-fire: What did the Indian Councils Act of 1909 change the Legislative Council size from and to? +
From 16 members → To 60 members. Nearly 4x increase! Plus, Satyendra Prasad Sinha became first Indian in Viceroy's Executive Council. Plus separate electorate for Muslims introduced.
Which Act established RBI? Federal Court? Central Public Service Commission? +
RBI → Government of India Act, 1935. Federal Court → Government of India Act, 1935 (started functioning 1937). Central PSC → Provided by 1919 Act; actually set up in 1926.
How did India ratify its Commonwealth membership? When? +
India ratified membership of the British Commonwealth in May 1949 through the Constituent Assembly. The 1947 Independence Act gave India the right to secede from the Commonwealth, but India chose to stay (as a republic, which was a new concept for the Commonwealth at the time).
