Post-Mauryan Dynasties

Shunga · Kanva · Satavahana · Chedi · Indo-Greeks · Sakas · Parthians · Kushans — the 300-year turbulence that reshaped India after Mauryan collapse.

High Frequency Multi-Shift Questions CGL Mains 2024 Topic
Layer 1 · Big Picture

What This Chapter Is Really About

After Ashoka's death (~232 BC) the Mauryan Empire collapsed. What followed was a 300-year power vacuum filled by Indian dynasties (Shunga, Kanva, Satavahana, Chedi) and Central Asian invaders (Indo-Greeks → Sakas → Parthians → Kushans). The chapter is really a story of India's political fragmentation and cultural synthesis — foreigners kept invading but kept getting absorbed.

🎯 The Domino Chain
BC 185 → Shunga → Kanva → Satavahana (Indian kingdoms)
2nd century BC → Indo-Greeks → Sakas → Parthians → Kushans (Central Asian wave)
Key insight: The Great Wall of China forced Central Asians westward — into India.

Layer 1 · Chapter Memory Palace

🧠 The Ruined Palace Journey

🏛 Your Memory Palace: A Crumbling Palace Taken Over Room by Room
Imagine walking through Ashoka's abandoned palace in Pataliputra. Each room has been seized by a new dynasty. Walk room to room and everything comes back.
01
The Throne Room (Shunga) — You enter and see Pushyamitra, a general, sitting on the throne dripping with blood. He just killed the last Mauryan king (Brihadrath). He's wearing a Shiva pendant. The room has a big sign: "VIDISHA, MP." His son Agnimitra is flirting with Malavika in the corner (Kalidas's play). Patanjali the grammarian is doing Ashvamedha Yagya in the courtyard outside.
02
The Servants' Quarters (Kanva) — Small room, servants have taken over. A servant named Vasudev killed Devbhooti (last Shunga) and sits here. The room sign reads: "Pataliputra." A calendar on the wall says "BC is almost over."
03
The Grand Hall (Satavahana) — Huge hall, Maharashtra-style décor. Gautamiputra Satkarni stands tall after defeating Saka Nahapana. His mother wrote his praise on the wall (Nasik Inscription). Lead coins are scattered on the floor. Buddhist cave paintings cover the walls (Ajanta). Ajanta and Amaravati stupas visible through the window.
04
The Side Chamber (Chedi) — Small chamber, Kalinga decor. Kharavela sits in Jain monk robes. An elephant engraving on the wall = Hathigumpha Inscription, Bhubaneswar.
05
The Treasury (Indo-Greeks) — You see gold coins everywhere with the KING'S FACE on them — first time ever! Menander/Milind is talking with Buddhist monk Nagasena. Greek columns with Shiva idols — assimilation! The coins glow with Hellenistic art patterns.
06
The Military Wing (Sakas) — Five doors, five branches. A general (Satrapa) is sitting on the throne, not the king's son. Vikramaditya Parmar storms in at 57 BC and defeats them — founds Vikram Samvat. Rudradaman I is fixing a old lake (Sudarshan) in Gujarat.
07
The Chapel (Parthians) — Tiny room, barely noticeable. Only one thing here: Saint Thomas (disciple of Jesus) visited during Gondophanes.
08
The Grand Audience Hall (Kushans) — The biggest room. Kanishka sits on the gold throne — the purest gold coins ever minted surround him. He controls the Silk Route between China and Central Asia. Fourth Buddhist Council is happening in the corner. Peshawar is written above the entrance (was Purushpura). The calendar shows 78 AD = Shaka Samvat starts.
09
The Art Gallery (Mathura vs Gandhara) — Split room. Left side: red sandstone Buddha with glowing halo ring, made by Kushans, proudly Indian. Right side: Greek-influenced grey sandstone Buddha, halo barely there, Hellenistic face. One room, two artistic worlds.

🔥 Art Schools — Mathura vs Gandhara

🟡 Mathura School

  • Developed by Kushans
  • Indigenous — born in India
  • Covers Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism
  • Clear, well-developed Halo (ring) behind Buddha
  • Made of Red Sandstone
  • North-central India (Mathura region)

🔵 Gandhara School

  • Developed by the Greeks
  • Based mainly on Buddhism
  • Halo present but not well developed
  • Made of Grey Sandstone
  • NW India (Gandhara region)
  • Hellenistic art influence

Smart Memory Hooks

⚔ The Double Assassination Rule
Shunga dynasty: general killed king → new dynasty
Kanva dynasty: general killed king → new dynasty
Pattern: when a general kills a king, a new dynasty is born. Recognize this pattern and you'll never confuse who ended which dynasty.
💰 The Gold Coin Progression
First gold coins: Indo-Greeks (also first coin with king's image)
Purest/largest gold coins: Kanishka (Kushans)
Lead coins: Satavahana
Dinar (gold): Vasudev Kushan
Think: Greeks introduced gold → Kanishka perfected it → Satavahana went cheap (lead)
📅 The Samvat Confusion Buster
Vikram Samvat: 57 BC — Vikramaditya Parmar defeated Sakas
Shaka Samvat: 78 AD — Kanishka (Kushan) ascended throne
"Shaka" Samvat started by a KUSHAN (not a Saka!) — because Indians called all foreigners "Shakas"
🔥 This is an EXTREME exam trap.
🗿 Stone Color for Art Schools
Mathura = RED sandstone (Indian/warm/earthy)
Gandhara = GREY sandstone (Greek/cool/foreign)
Mathura is warm like India, Gandhara is cool grey like Greece.
🏛 City Name Change
Peshawar's original name = Purushpura
Kushans made it their capital. Remember: Puru-shpura → Peshawar (P to P, ancient to modern)

Layer 1 · Exam Traps

⚠ Exam Traps

🔴 Trap 1 — Shaka Samvat is NOT from Sakas!
Shaka Samvat (78 AD) was started by Kanishka who was a Kushan, not a Saka. Indians called all foreigners "Shakas" — hence the name. Do NOT write "started by Sakas."
🔴 Trap 2 — Vikram Samvat vs Shaka Samvat
Vikram Samvat: 57 BC — Vikramaditya Parmar (defeat of Sakas)
Shaka Samvat: 78 AD — Kanishka (accession)
Students confuse these. Remember: Vikram = BC (earlier), Shaka = AD (later by ~135 years)
🔴 Trap 3 — Who started Ajanta Caves?
Ajanta Caves were started by Satavahanas, not completed by them alone. Many rulers contributed. Don't say only one dynasty built Ajanta.
🔴 Trap 4 — Rudradaman I (Saka) vs Rudradaman (Satavahana context)
Rudradaman I is a SAKA ruler who defeated Satavahana ruler Vashishtha Pulwami. Students confuse Rudradaman as a Satavahana ruler. He's their enemy!
🔴 Trap 5 — "First Gold Coins" vs "Purest Gold Coins"
First to introduce gold coins: Indo-Greeks
Purest/largest collection of gold coins: Kanishka (Kushans)
Both are different facts. Exam may try to swap them.
🔴 Trap 6 — Mathura vs Gandhara (stone color)
Mathura = Red sandstone (not grey)
Gandhara = Grey sandstone (not red)
Also: Halo is clear in Mathura; barely visible in Gandhara.
🔴 Trap 7 — "Second Ashoka" vs "First Ashoka"
Kanishka = Second Ashoka (Kushan dynasty). Ashoka himself is the first. Students sometimes write Kanishka is the "Ashoka of Kushan" vaguely — it must be "Second Ashoka."
🔴 Trap 8 — Satavahana Society: Patriarchal or Matrilineal?
Answer: BOTH. Patriarchal (sons inherit throne), Matrilineal (names include mother's name). Exam may give only one as the answer — both aspects are correct.
🔴 Trap 9 — Sudarshan Lake: Who built vs who repaired?
Built by: Pushyagupta Vaishya (under Chandragupta Maurya)
Repaired by: Rudradaman I (Saka)
Source: Junagadh Rock Inscription, Girnar Hills, Gujarat
🔴 Trap 10 — Kushan ruler order (CGL Mains 2024!)
1st: Kujula Kadphises | 2nd: Vima Kadphises | 3rd: Kanishka
Exam asked this in BOTH SHIFTS of CGL Mains 2024. Do NOT mix up the order.
