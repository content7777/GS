Chapter Overview

Big Picture

What is this chapter really about?

19th–20th century India was broken. Sati, child marriage, caste discrimination, purdah, illiteracy. A set of extraordinary reformers — some religious, some social, some both — rose to fight these evils. They formed sabhas, samajes, missions, and societies. Governors legislated. The British both helped and exploited.

In SSC CGL, this chapter generates questions on who formed what, when, and where — especially the year + organisation + founder triplet.

The 3 Waves

  • Wave 1 — Bengal Renaissance (Ram Mohan Roy, Brahmo Samaj, 1828+) — monotheism, anti-Sati, widow remarriage
  • Wave 2 — Maharashtra Reform (Phule, Ranade, Gokhale, Karve) — caste upliftment, women's education
  • Wave 3 — South India + Muslim Reform (Vivekananda, Syed Ahmed Khan, Narayan Guru, Periyar)

Key Pairs to Master

Exam loves pairing reformer + governor:

  • Ram Mohan Roy + William Bentinck → Sati abolished 1829
  • Vidyasagar + Dalhousie → Widow Remarriage Act 1856
  • Macaulay + Bentinck → English education 1835
  • Wood's Dispatch + Dalhousie → 1854 education
Chapter Memory Palace

🧠 The Reform Train of India

Imagine a long train moving from Bengal → Maharashtra → South India → North India. Each compartment has reformers doing their work. Walk through the train mentally to recall everything.

🚂 COMPARTMENT 1 — Bengal (The Fire Compartment)

1
Ram Mohan Roy sits at the door waving a flag that says "NO IDOLS, ONE GOD". He formed Atmiya Sabha (1814) → became Brahmo Samaj (1828). A king called him Raja (Akbar II gave title). Outside the window: a bonfire of Sati pyres being extinguished (1829, Bentinck signs the law).
2
Devendra Nath Tagore (Rabindranath's father) runs a printing press inside — printing Tattwabodhini Patrika. He merges his Tattwabodhini Sabha with Brahmo Samaj.
3
Keshav Chandra Sen storms out at a station in 1866, shouting "I'm taking Brahmo Samaj of India with me!" (split 1866). Devendra is left waving from Adi Brahmo Samaj window. Keshav later disgraces himself — marries minor daughter to a Hindu king.
4
Radhakant Dev looks furious, trying to pull the train back (Dharma Sabha 1830 — revivalist, anti-reform).
5
Derozio, a young firebrand teacher, rallies students from Hindu College — Young Bengal Movement (1830). But the train doesn't stop because it didn't reach masses.

🚂 COMPARTMENT 2 — Maharashtra (The Factory Compartment)

6
Jyotirao Phule (a Mali/gardener) plants seeds in the floor of the train — Satyashodhak Samaj (1873). His wife Savitribai teaches women. Books scattered: Gulamgiri, Sarvajanik Satyadharma (all in Marathi).
7
Paramhans Mandali (1849, Dadaba Pandurang) and Prarthana Samaj (1867, Atmaram Pandurang) are two prayer groups chanting "one God, no idols" — same ideology as Brahmo Samaj but in Maharashtra.
8
DK Karve sits with widows, teaching them. He founded SNDT Women's University (1916). And worked on widow remarriage (Vidhwa Vivah Punar Utkaasi Mandal was by Vishnu Shastri Pandit 1866 — different!)
9
Gokhale stands reading with a book labelled "Servants of India Society 1905" — Guru of both Gandhi and Jinnah. He also joined Prarthana Samaj in 1870 (as MG Ranade did).

🚂 COMPARTMENT 3 — Arya Samaj (The Vedas Compartment)

10
Swami Dayanand Saraswati (born Mool Shankar) stands holding a copy of Satyarth Prakash, shouting "BACK TO VEDAS! INDIA FOR INDIANS!" Formed Arya Samaj 1875 in Bombay. HQ moved to Lahore. DAV College opened 1886.
11
The train carries people being "purified" back into Hinduism — the Shuddhi Movement. (Reverse conversion programme for those who left Hinduism.)

🚂 COMPARTMENT 4 — Vivekananda's Rocket (The Chicago Compartment)

12
Narendranath Dutta (original name of Vivekananda) stands on the roof. Born 12 Jan 1863 (National Youth Day). Chicago speech 1893. Formed Ramakrishna Mission 1897 in memory of guru Ramakrishna Paramhans (real name: Gadadhar Chattopadhyay). Vivekananda's rock memorial is in Tamil Nadu. Died 4 July 1902, age 39.

🚂 COMPARTMENT 5 — South + Muslim Reform (Last Carriages)

13
Periyar (EV Ramaswamy Nayakar) in Tamil Nadu carriage — Self Respect Movement. Narayan Guru in Kerala carriage — SNDP Movement for Ezhava community. Theosophical Society train platform at Adyar, Madras (1882).
14
Sir Syed Ahmed Khan in the last carriage with a Quran in one hand and a science book in the other — Aligarh Movement 1875. Anglo-Oriental College → Aligarh Muslim University (1920). Book: Tahzibul Akhlaq.

Complete Revision Notes

📚 Topic-by-Topic Notes

1. Abolition of Sati

  • Abolished 1829 by an Act passed by William Bentinck
  • Bentinck was then Governor-General of Bengal (NOT India — became GoG of India in 1833)
  • Main reformer: Raja Ram Mohan Roy
  • 🔥 RAM MOHAN ROY = Reform efforts | BENTINCK = Administrative sanction

2. Widow Remarriage

  • Widow Remarriage Act 1856 — During Dalhousie, efforts of Ishwar Chandra Vidyasagar
  • Vidhwa Vivah Punar Utkaasi Mandal — Vishnu Shastri Pandit (1866)
  • Vidhwa Vivah Tejak Mandal — MG Ranade (1861)
  • DK Karve also worked for widow remarriage

3. Child Marriage

  • BM Malabari — champion against child marriage
  • Age of Consent Act 1891 — marriage below 12 prohibited
  • Sharda Act 1930 — boys min 18, girls min 14
  • Current — boys 21, girls 18
  • Indian National Social Conference (Ranade + Raghunath Rao, 1887) — pledge movement against child marriage

4. Education Reforms

  • Macaulay's Minute 1835 — English education, downward filtration theory, under Bentinck. Macaulay = Father of English Education in India. Also chaired 1st Law Commission (formed 1835 under Act of 1833)
  • Wood's Dispatch 1854 — "Magna Carta of Indian Education." Under Dalhousie. Rejected downward filtration. English at higher level, vernacular at lower level.
  • First Hindu College 1791, Banaras — Jonathan Duncan (Ram Mohan Roy had minor role)
  • Hindu College 1817, Calcutta — David Hare (Ram Mohan Roy in founding committee)
  • SNDT Women's University 1916 — DK Karve (First women's university in India)

5. Raja Ram Mohan Roy — Bengal

  • Known as Father of Indian Renaissance
  • Atmiya Sabha 1814 → Brahmo Samaj 1828
  • Title "Raja" given by Akbar II
  • Opposed: polytheism + idol worship (the two key things!)
  • Promoted: monotheism
  • Books: Gift to Monotheism, Precepts of Jesus, Sambad Kaumudi, Mirat-ul-Akhbar

6. Brahmo Samaj — Splits & Family Tree

  • 1828: Brahmo Samaj (Ram Mohan Roy)
  • 1839: Devendra Nath Tagore forms Tattwabodhini Sabha → later merges with Brahmo Samaj
  • 1866 SPLIT: Keshav Chandra Sen takes → Brahmo Samaj of India. Devendra Nath keeps → Adi Brahmo Samaj
  • Veda Samaj 1864, Madras = "Brahmo Samaj of South India" — Keshav Chandra Sen + K. Shridharlu Naidu
  • Keshav Chandra Sen controversy: married minor daughter to a Hindu king (caused further split)

7. Maharashtra Movements

  • Paramhans Mandali 1849 — Dadaba Pandurang + Mehtaji Durgaram. Same ideals as Brahmo Samaj (monotheism, anti-idol).
  • Prarthana Samaj 1867 — Atmaram Pandurang. MG Ranade joined 1870.
  • Satyashodhak Samaj 1873 — Jyotirao/Jyotiba Phule. For lower castes. From Mali community. Wife Savitribai = India's 1st female teacher. Books: Gulamgiri, Sarvajanik Satyadharma (Marathi).
  • Servants of India Society 1905 — Gopal Krishna Gokhale. Guru of Gandhi and Jinnah.

8. Arya Samaj

  • Founded 1875 by Swami Dayanand Saraswati (original name: Mool Shankar)
  • First unit: Bombay. HQ: Lahore.
  • Slogans: "Back to Vedas" / "India for Indians"
  • Book: Satyarth Prakash (NOT Satya Prakash — that's by Karson Das Mulji)
  • DAV College 1886, Lahore. Split 1893: College Party (modern) vs Mahatma Party (traditional + vegetarian)
  • Shuddhi Movement — reconversion of people back to Hinduism

9. Ramakrishna Mission

  • Founded 1897 by Swami Vivekananda (original: Narendranath Dutta)
  • Born: 12 January 1863 → National Youth Day. (International Youth Day = 12 August)
  • Died: 4 July 1902, age 39
  • Guru: Ramakrishna Paramhans (real: Gadadhar Chattopadhyay)
  • Books: Rajayoga, Karmayoga
  • Chicago speech: 1893
  • Rock memorial: Tamil Nadu (Kanyakumari)

10. Theosophical Society

  • Founded 1875 by HP Blavatsky + MS Olcott
  • Initially HQ: New York, USA
  • HQ moved to Adyar, Madras in 1882
  • Later president: Annie Besant

11. Aligarh Movement

  • Founded 1875 by Sir Syed Ahmed Khan
  • Muhammadan Anglo-Oriental (MAO) College 1875 → became AMU in 1920
  • Focused on Muslims: Quran + Science education
  • Book: Tahzibul Akhlaq
  • Wahabi Movement started by Syed Ahmed Barelvi (not Syed Ahmed Khan)
  • Deoband Movement 1868 — Nanotovi and Gangohi in Deoband, UP
  • Faraizi Movement 1818 — Haji Shariatullah

12. South India + Dalit Movements

  • Self Respect Movement (Tamil Nadu) — EV Ramaswamy Nayakar (Periyar)
  • SNDP Movement (Kerala) — Narayan Guru, for Ezhava community upliftment. Also called Alu Virupam Movement.
  • Justice Movement 1917 — CN Mudaliar, TM Nair, P Tyagaraja
  • TK Madhavan — Temple Entry Movement 1924 (South)
  • Vakkom Satyagraha 1924 — KP Keshav
  • Satnam Movement 1820, Chhattisgarh — Guru Ghasidas (for leather workers)

13. Ambedkar's Movements

  • Bahishkrit Hitkarini Sabha 1924, Bombay — Ambedkar
  • Mahad Satyagraha 1927 — against Chaturvarna. Manusmriti burned.
  • Temple Entry 1927 — started in North
  • Kalaram Temple Entry 1930, Nasik — Ambedkar
  • Ambedkar Jayanti: 14 April

Smart Memory Hooks

🧲 Memory Hooks That Stick

HOOK 1 — The BENT WINDOW (Bentinck + Widow)

Imagine a BENT WINDOW (= Bentinck). Ram Mohan Roy throws a burning sati torch through the bent window in 1829. The governor signs the law. Same Bentinck sees Macaulay sitting inside writing English textbooks in 1835. One governor, two reforms: 1829 + 1835.

HOOK 2 — DALHOUSIE's Widow Gets a DISPATCH

Picture Dalhousie handing a widow a piece of paper (a dispatch) — it says "You can remarry" (1856 Widow Remarriage Act). A postman delivers another dispatch from Wood: "Magna Carta of Education" (Wood's Dispatch 1854). Dalhousie = 1854 + 1856.

HOOK 3 — MOOL SHANKAR meets SWAMI D

A boy named Mool Shankar stares at his mother's corpse as it moves — he realises a rat ran under the idol. "This idol does nothing!" He runs away, becomes Swami Dayanand Saraswati, yells "Back to Vedas!" and writes Satyarth Prakash. Born again as reformer — Arya Samaj 1875.

HOOK 4 — NARENDRANATH's January Birthday

Narendra is born in January, cold winter, everyone is young and energetic — 12 January = National Youth Day. He goes to Chicago in the summer of '93 and gives a speech so powerful the whole world knows India. Dies young, 39, on 4 July (American Independence Day — irony!).

HOOK 5 — 1875 Triple Launch Year

1875 is the "Reform Olympics Year" — THREE things launched: Arya Samaj (Dayanand), Theosophical Society (Blavatsky+Olcott), Aligarh Movement + MAO College (Syed Ahmed Khan). Remember: 1875 = Arya + Theo + Aligarh.

HOOK 6 — PHULE'S GARDEN TRUTH

Jyotiba Phule (Mali = gardener) plants seeds of truth (Satyashodhak Samaj = Truth-Seekers' Society, 1873). His wife Savitribai is the first female teacher, watering the seeds. Books are fruits: Gulamgiri (Slavery), Sarvajanik Satyadharma. 1873 → the garden was planted 2 years before Arya Samaj bloomed.

HOOK 7 — BM MALABARI's Double Act

Malabari fought against child marriage → Age of Consent Act 1891. Then 17 years later, started Seva Sadan 1908 for women's welfare. Same man, two acts, 17 years apart. BM = Both Married-age fight AND Seva Sadan.

HOOK 8 — The Hindu College Twins

There are TWO Hindu Colleges: Twin A born 1791 in Banaras (Jonathan Duncan) and Twin B born 1817 in Calcutta (David Hare). Ram Mohan Roy is the godfather of Twin B — not the parent. Don't mix them up!

Concept Connections

🔗 Cause → Effect Chains

Ram Mohan Roy campaigns → Bentinck passes Sati Act 1829 → Social legitimacy for reform
Vidyasagar lobbies → Widow Remarriage Act 1856 (Dalhousie) → Vidhwa Vivah associations formed
Macaulay's downward filtration → rejected by → Wood's Dispatch 1854 → Vernacular + higher English ed.
Christian missionaries 1813 → forced conversions → Arya Samaj Shuddhi Movement → Reconversions back to Hinduism
Brahmo Samaj (Bengal) → inspired → Prarthana Samaj + Paramhans Mandali (Maharashtra) → inspired → Veda Samaj (South India, 1864)
Atmiya Sabha 1814 → evolved into → Brahmo Samaj 1828 → split 1866 into → Adi B.S. + B.S. of India

Reform vs Revivalism — KEY DISTINCTION

Reform (Brahmo Samaj etc.) = End evil customs, adopt new thinking
Revivalism (Dharma Sabha) = Return to old/ancient customs

Arya Samaj = Both! Revival of Vedas but reform of social evils.

Exam Traps

⚠️ Exam Traps — What Students Always Get Wrong

TRAP 1 — Bentinck: Bengal vs India
  • When Sati was abolished (1829) → Bentinck was Governor-General of BENGAL
  • Bentinck became Governor-General of INDIA only in 1833
  • Exam may say: "Sati was abolished when Bentinck was Governor-General of India" → ❌ WRONG
TRAP 2 — Two Hindu Colleges
  • 1791, Banaras — Jonathan Duncan (Ram Mohan Roy had minor role)
  • 1817, Calcutta (Thak) — David Hare (Ram Mohan Roy in founding committee)
  • Exam swaps the years and founders. Know both cold.
TRAP 3 — Satyarth Prakash vs Satya Prakash
  • Satyarth Prakash → Swami Dayanand Saraswati (Arya Samaj)
  • Satya Prakash → Karson Das Mulji
  • Exam will give you "Satya Prakash" and ask who wrote it → Not Dayanand!
TRAP 4 — Theosophical Society Origin
  • Founded 1875 in New York, USA — NOT India
  • Came to India (Adyar, Madras) only in 1882
  • Annie Besant came LATER as president — she didn't found it
  • Founders: HP Blavatsky + MS Olcott (not Annie Besant!)
TRAP 5 — 1875 Year: Three Different Movements
  • 1875 = Arya Samaj (Dayanand) + Theosophical Society (Blavatsky) + Aligarh Movement (Syed Ahmed Khan)
  • Exam will say "Aligarh Movement 1876" or "Arya Samaj 1876" → Wrong, all three are 1875
TRAP 6 — Brahmo Samaj Split: Who got What?
  • Keshav Chandra Sen = Brahmo Samaj of India
  • Devendra Nath Tagore = Adi Brahmo Samaj
  • Exam reverses these. Remember: Keshav was the "new" radical one → "Brahmo Samaj of India" (newer India)
TRAP 7 — Widow Remarriage: Two Mandals
  • 1861: Vidhwa Vivah Tejak Mandal — MG Ranade
  • 1866: Vidhwa Vivah Punar Utkaasi Mandal — Vishnu Shastri Pandit
  • Ranade came EARLIER (1861). Vishnu Shastri came LATER (1866). Don't mix dates.
  • DK Karve also worked on widow remarriage — but SNDT (1916) is his main contribution
TRAP 8 — Mool Shankar vs Narendranath vs Gadadhar
  • Mool Shankar = original name of Swami Dayanand Saraswati
  • Narendranath Dutta = original name of Swami Vivekananda
  • Gadadhar Chattopadhyay = original name of Ramakrishna Paramhans
  • Exam will ask original names. Don't confuse the three!
TRAP 9 — National vs International Youth Day
  • 12 January = National Youth Day (Vivekananda's birthday)
  • 12 August = International Youth Day (UN designation)
  • Both are "12" — different months. Exam swaps August and January.
TRAP 10 — Wahabi vs Deoband vs Faraizi vs Aligarh
  • Faraizi 1818 — Haji Shariatullah (Bengal)
  • Wahabi — Syed Ahmed Barelvi (not Syed Ahmed Khan!)
  • Deoband 1868 — Nanotovi + Gangohi (UP)
  • Aligarh 1875 — Sir Syed Ahmed Khan
  • Wahabi founder (Barelvi) ≠ Aligarh founder (Khan). Both "Syed Ahmed" — different people!
TRAP 11 — Dharma Sabha is REVIVALIST, not Reform
  • Dharma Sabha (Radhakant Dev, 1830) was formed to OPPOSE Brahmo Samaj
  • It wanted to preserve old customs — revivalist, NOT reformist
  • Exam may trick: "Radhakant Dev formed Dharma Sabha to reform society" → ❌
TRAP 12 — BM Malabari did BOTH things
  • BM Malabari worked against child marriage → Age of Consent Act 1891
  • BM Malabari ALSO founded Seva Sadan 1908
  • Exam may test either and you may think different people did these
