Emergence of Gandhi

A complete high-retention revision system for SSC CGL — from Gandhi's birth to the Swaraj Party. Designed to make you remember every fact under exam pressure.

🔥 Must Know ⭐ Important ➕ Good to Know
Chapter Overview
🌅 Big Picture

This chapter covers Gandhi's arrival on the Indian political stage — from his birth in 1869 and work in South Africa, to his first movements in India (1917–18), then the national-level agitations (Rowlatt, Khilafat, Non-Cooperation), and the aftermath (Swaraj Party, 1922–24).

The central story: Gandhi turns three local successes into national mass movements, and the British respond with repression (Jallianwala Bagh) — which only galvanizes the freedom struggle further.

Retention Framework
🏛️ Chapter Memory Palace

Imagine walking through a grand Railway Station. Each platform is a phase of Gandhi's life. Walk platform by platform to recall everything.

01
Platform 1 — Birth Platform (Porbandar)
A tiny baby is born 2 October 1869 in a house made of por-bandars (pots of butter). His mother Putlibai is making butter. His father Karamchand is the station master. Gokhale, the political mentor, waves a flag on the platform.
02
Platform 2 — South Africa Express (1893–1914)
Gandhi boards a train to South Africa. He sets up a Natal Indian Congress (1894), reads the newspaper Indian Opinion (1903), sleeps at Phoenix Farm (1904), and tends a Tolstoy Farm (1910).
03
Platform 3 — India Arrival Gate (9 Jan 1915)
Gandhi steps off the train on 9 January 1915. There's a big sign: "Pravasi Bharatiya Divas". His guru Gokhale tells him to travel India for a year before doing anything.
04
Platform 4 — Three Fights Train (1917–18)
A train with three coaches arrives: Coach 1 = Champaran (indigo farmers, 1917). Coach 2 = Ahmedabad Mill Strike (plague bonus, 1918). Coach 3 = Kheda Satyagraha (drought + tax, 1918 — Sardar Patel drives this one).
05
Platform 5 — Black Law Platform (1919)
A black engine labeled Rowlatt Act / Kala Kanoon roars in. Gandhi launches Satyagraha on 6 April 1919. At a garden (Jallianwala Bagh), General Dyer locks the gate and fires. Udham Singh vows revenge — he kills Michael O'Dyer in London in 1940.
06
Platform 6 — Unity Express (1920–22)
Two trains merge: Khilafat Movement + Non-Cooperation Movement. Tilak Swaraj Fund is the ticket counter (created 1921, Tilak died 1 Aug 1920). The Chauri Chaura fire (4 Feb 1922) derails the train — Gandhi pulls the emergency brake.
07
Platform 7 — Split & Swaraj (1922–24)
At Gaya Station (Dec 1922), the Congress crowd splits. CR Das and Motilal Nehru walk to Platform 7B and form the Swaraj Party. CR Das = President, Motilal = Secretary. Gandhi gets off at Belgaum 1924 — the only Congress session he ever presided over.
Complete Revision Notes
📅 Master Timeline
🔥 Must Know — Basic Facts

Full name: Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi | Born: 2 Oct 1869, Porbandar, Gujarat | Mother: Putlibai Gandhi | Father: Karamchand Gandhi | Political Guru: Gopal Krishna Gokhale (also Jinnah's political guru)

1894
Gandhi establishes Natal Indian Congress in South Africa. 🔥
1903
Starts newspaper Indian Opinion in South Africa.
1904
Sets up Phoenix Farm in South Africa.
1910
Establishes Tolstoy Farm in South Africa.
9 Jan 1915
Gandhi returns to India. This day is celebrated as Pravasi Bharatiya Divas (NRI Day). 🔥
1916
Gandhi's first public appearance in India — at the inauguration of BHU (Banaras Hindu University), founded by Madan Mohan Malviya. ⭐
1917
Champaran Satyagraha — Bihar. Rajkumar Shukla invites Gandhi. Issue: Tinkathiya System (forced indigo cultivation on 3/20th part of land). 🔥
→ First Civil Disobedience Movement in India
→ Recall: Indigo Revolt 1859 (Nadia, W.Bengal) led by Digambar Biswas; play Neeldarpan by Deen Bandhu Mitra
1918
Ahmedabad Mill Strike — Anusuya Sarabhai invited Gandhi. Mill owners removed plague bonus. Workers demanded wage rise. Gandhi's first hunger strike. 🔥

Kheda Satyagraha — Gujarat. Drought → crop failure → farmers can't pay tax. British refused exemption. Gandhi + Sardar Patel lead. British suspend tax for 1 year. First Non-Cooperation Movement. 🔥
1919 (Apr)
Rowlatt Act / Anarchical and Revolutionary Crimes Act — Named after Sydney Rowlatt. Gandhi calls it Kala Kanoon / Black Act. Allowed 2-year imprisonment without trial, no appeal, no lawyer. 🔥
→ Permanent replacement of Defence of India Act 1915

Gandhi launches Rowlatt Satyagraha on 6 April 1919.
Saifuddin Kitchlu and Dr. Satyapal arrested on 9 April 1919.
13 Apr 1919
Jallianwala Bagh Massacre — Amritsar. Baisakhi gathering + protest against Kitchlu/Satyapal arrest. General Reginald EM Dyer ordered firing. Only exit gate locked. 🔥

Udham Singh (under name Ram Mohammad Singh Azad) kills Michael O'Dyer in London in 1940 at Caxton Hall.
Rabindranath Tagore returns Knighthood title. Gandhi returns Kaiser-i-Hind title.
British set up Hunter Commission (1919) to investigate — Dyer's action called unjustified, but no fine; Indian Indemnity Act passed (called Whitewashing Act). ⭐
1919
Indian National Liberation Federation founded by Surendranath Banerjee.
National Liberation Federation founded by TB Sapru (Tej Bahadur Sapru) and MR Jayakar. (Different organizations — don't confuse!) ⭐
1919–1920
Khilafat Movement — Ottoman Empire defeated in WW1. Caliph removed from power → Muslim anger worldwide → India's response = Khilafat Movement.
Led by Ali Brothers: Maulana Mohammad Ali & Shaukat Ali.
All India Khilafat Conference held in Delhi, November 1919. Gandhi leads it. 🔥
1920
Non-Cooperation Movement launched by Gandhi. Merged with Khilafat Movement. 🔥
1 Aug 1920: Bal Gangadhar Tilak dies.
Nagpur Session of Congress (Dec 1920): NCM endorsed. Congress commits to extra-constitutional mass struggle. Congress Working Committee of 15 members formed. 🔥
Big leaders resign after Nagpur: Annie Besant, Jinnah, Bipin Chandra Pal.
Boycott of councils, foreign goods, British institutions. New universities set up: Gujarat Vidyapeeth, Bihar Vidyapeeth, Jamia Millia.
1921
Tilak Swaraj Fund created in memory of Tilak.
Malabar/Mappila Revolt (1920–21) — Initially Congress-supported. But Muslim peasants (Moplahs) turned against Hindu landlords. Congress withdrew support. ⭐
4 Feb 1922
Chauri Chaura Incident — Gorakhpur, UP. Mob burns police station; policemen die. Gandhi withdraws Non-Cooperation Movement. Approved in Bardoli Session of Congress. 🔥
Dec 1922
Gaya Session of Congress — Congress split into No-Changers and Pro-Changers.
No-Changers: Rajendra Prasad, Vallabhbhai Patel, MA Ansari → focused on constructive work.
Pro-Changers: CR Das, Motilal Nehru, Ajmal Khan → formed Swaraj Party (All India Khilafat Swaraj Party) in 1923. 🔥
CR Das = first President | Motilal Nehru = first Secretary.
1924
Gandhi presides over Belgaum Session of Congress — the only session Gandhi ever presided over. 🔥
Gandhi also accepts and supports the Swaraj Party at this point.
1928
Swaraj Party defeats Public Safety Bill and Trade Disputes Bill in councils. ⭐
Retention Boosters
🧲 Smart Memory Hooks
🧈
Porbandar Birth

Imagine "Por-Ban-DAR" = a poor band playing on the dar (door). Gandhi born here, very poor setting. 2 Oct 1869. Also India's National Non-Violence Day / Gandhi Jayanti.

🌱
South Africa Sequence

N-I-P-T: Natal Congress 1894 → Indian Opinion 1903 → Phoenix Farm 1904 → Tolstoy Farm 1910. Spells NIPT — Natal Indian Paper Phoenix Tolstoy.

✈️
Return to India

Gandhi lands on 9 January → January 9 = NRI day. Think: J-9 = Joins 9 India. "January 9th is when the Non-Resident Indian came home."

🌾
Champaran → First Civil Disobedience

Rajkumar Shukla calls Gandhi → think "Shukla = White Sugar = Indigo's opposite" → about Indigo (blue dye). 3/20th land = Tinkathiya. Champaran CGL exam appeared in Mains 2024!

🏭
Ahmedabad = First Hunger Strike

Think: Mill workers hungry → Gandhi also goes hungry. Both starving. Anusuya Sarabhai = "Sarah Bhai" = Sister Sarah called Gandhi as a brother. Mill owners removed plague bonus.

🌧️
Kheda = Patel's First

Kheda Satyagraha = Kheda → Khedut (farmer in Gujarati). Drought + tax = Sardar Patel steps up. This is Sardar Patel's first big national action — he's "protecting the farmers like a Sardar (leader/chief)."

⚫
Rowlatt Act = Black Law

Sydney Rowlatt wrote the Black Act. Picture a black-robed judge named Rowlatt who jails you for 2 years without asking anything. No appeal, no lawyer, no trial. Gandhi: "This is Kala Kanoon!"

🔥
Jallianwala Bagh Numbers

Date: 13-4-1919. Event: Baisakhi (13 April every year). General: Reginald Dyer = "RD sharma but more evil." Revenge: Udham Singh = "Udham (noise/rebellion) against injustice."

🕌
Ali Brothers

Two Alis: Mohammad Ali + Shaukat Ali. Think: Ali Ali like a chant in the mosque. Together they formed Khilafat Committee. Khilafat = Caliph's protection.

🚂
Chauri Chaura

Chauri Chaura = "Chori (theft) + Chaura (wide road)" → violent mob "stole peace" from Gandhi's movement. 4 Feb 1922 → Gandhi hits the emergency brake on NCM. 22 policemen burned.

⚖️
Swaraj Party Leaders

CR Das + Motilal Nehru. Think: Captain Roadrunner (CR Das) = President. Motilal = "Moti (pearl) + Lal (red)" = Secretary. "The Pearl-Red Secretary runs after the Captain-President."

🏅
Belgaum — Only Session Gandhi Presided

Think: Bel-GAUM = Bell Game. Gandhi rang the bell at Belgaum in 1924. Only once in his life did Gandhi chair the Congress session. Ask yourself: "Which session? Belgaum. Which year? 1924."

Common Mistakes
⚠️ Exam Traps
⚠ Two Hunter Commissions!

Hunter Education Commission 1881 (Lord Ripon's era) = about education.
Hunter Commission 1919 = to investigate Jallianwala Bagh massacre. Examiners LOVE this confusion!

⚠ Reginald Dyer vs Michael O'Dyer

Reginald Dyer = General who ORDERED firing at Jallianwala Bagh.
Michael O'Dyer = Lieutenant Governor who SUPPORTED Dyer's act. He was killed by Udham Singh in 1940.
Reginald Dyer was already dead by then. Examiners ask both!

⚠ Indian National Liberation Federation vs National Liberation Federation

Indian National Liberation Federation → Founded by Surendranath Banerjee (1919).
National Liberation Federation → Founded by TB Sapru + MR Jayakar. Two different organizations!

⚠ Chauri Chaura Date

Some sources say 5th February. Your teacher says 4th February 1922 — go with this. Note the place: Gorakhpur, UP, not just "UP".

⚠ First Civil Disobedience vs First Non-Cooperation

Champaran 1917 = First Civil Disobedience Movement.
Kheda 1918 = First Non-Cooperation Movement.
Students often mix these labels up!

⚠ Nagpur Session (Dec 1920) vs Gaya Session (Dec 1922)

Nagpur Session 1920: NCM endorsed, CWC of 15 members formed, Congress commits to extra-constitutional struggle, leaders like Jinnah/Annie Besant resign.
Gaya Session 1922: No-Changers vs Pro-Changers split, Swaraj Party born.

⚠ Belgaum 1924 — "only" session presided by Gandhi

Many students forget the word "only". Gandhi presided over exactly ONE Congress session — Belgaum, 1924. The question may say "How many Congress sessions did Gandhi preside over?" — Answer: ONE.

⚠ Swaraj Party — Who was what?

CR Das = first President of Swaraj Party.
Motilal Nehru = first Secretary.
Don't reverse them!

⚠ Separate Electorate vs Reservation

Reservation (today): A Dalit can stand in elections, but anyone can vote.
Separate Electorate (1909 Morley-Minto): Only Muslims can stand AND only Muslims can vote in Muslim seats. Much stronger division! This was first given in Indian Councils Act 1909.

⚠ Kaiser-i-Hind vs Knighthood

Rabindranath Tagore returned Knighthood title after Jallianwala Bagh.
Gandhi returned Kaiser-i-Hind title (given for supporting British during Boer Wars in South Africa).

⚠ Pravasi Bharatiya Divas Date

Gandhi returned on 9 January 1915. That's why Pravasi Bharatiya Divas = January 9th every year. Don't confuse with Gandhi Jayanti (2 Oct).

Active Recall
🎯 Examiner's Favourite Questions

Click each question to reveal the answer. Test yourself honestly!

Q1
Gandhi's first public appearance in India was at the inauguration of which institution?
▼
BHU — Banaras Hindu University in 1916, founded by Madan Mohan Malviya.
Q2
Which movement is called the 'First Civil Disobedience Movement' of India?
▼
Champaran Satyagraha (1917). About the Tinkathiya system (forced indigo cultivation on 3/20th of land).
Q3
Who ordered the Jallianwala Bagh firing? Who was killed by Udham Singh? Are they the same person?
▼
General Reginald EM Dyer ordered the firing. Udham Singh killed Michael O'Dyer (Lieutenant Governor who supported the massacre). They are NOT the same — Reginald Dyer had already died.
Q4
Which is the ONLY Congress session presided over by Mahatma Gandhi?
▼
Belgaum Session, 1924.
Q5
Who were the first President and Secretary of the Swaraj Party?
▼
President: CR Das. Secretary: Motilal Nehru. Formed at Gaya Session 1922, officially in 1923.
Q6
Why did Gandhi withdraw the Non-Cooperation Movement?
▼
Because of the Chauri Chaura Incident (4 Feb 1922, Gorakhpur) — a mob burned a police station killing several policemen, violating Gandhi's principle of non-violence.
Q7
Who was Gandhi's political guru? Whose political guru was he also?
▼
Gopal Krishna Gokhale was Gandhi's political guru. He was also the political guru of Jinnah.
Q8
Pravasi Bharatiya Divas is observed on which date and why?
▼
9 January — because Gandhi returned to India on 9 January 1915.
Q9
Rowlatt Act is also known as?
▼
Anarchical and Revolutionary Crimes Act. Gandhi called it Kala Kanoon / Black Act. Proposed by Sydney Rowlatt.
Q10
Which Congress session endorsed the Non-Cooperation Movement? What else happened there?
▼
Nagpur Session 1920. Also: Congress committed to extra-constitutional struggle, Congress Working Committee of 15 members formed, Jinnah/Annie Besant/Bipin Chandra Pal resigned.
Q11
What was the Khilafat Movement about? What had it to do with Gandhi?
▼
Ottoman Empire's Caliph was removed after WW1 → Indian Muslims protested. Gandhi saw it as an opportunity for Hindu-Muslim unity and merged it with the Non-Cooperation Movement (1920).
Q12
Fill: Kheda Satyagraha (1918) is called the first _____ movement. It was related to _____ in a drought year. Sardar _____ was the key leader.
▼
Non-Cooperation movement. Related to tax collection (farmers couldn't pay taxes due to drought). Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel.
Q13
Udham Singh killed Michael O'Dyer in which building, in which year?
▼
Caxton Hall, London, 1940. Udham Singh used the alias Ram Mohammad Singh Azad.
Q14
ASSERTION: Rabindranath Tagore returned his knighthood after Jallianwala Bagh. REASON: Gandhi returned his Kaiser-i-Hind title for the same reason.
▼
Both assertions are correct. Tagore returned Knighthood; Gandhi returned Kaiser-i-Hind (which was given to him for supporting British in Boer Wars). Both did this as protest against the massacre.
