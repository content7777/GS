🎯 What This Chapter Is Really About

This chapter answers one fundamental question: How and why do things move? — and what happens when we push or pull them.

It moves from measuring motion (distance, displacement, speed, velocity, acceleration) → to types of motion (uniform, circular, rectilinear) → to Newton's laws that govern all motion in the universe → to momentum and impulse which are the consequences.

🚂 The UTSAV Express — A Train Ride Through Physics

Imagine you are boarding a train called "UTSAV Express" at a station. Each compartment holds one concept. Walk through it mentally.

🚉
Platform (Scalar/Vector) — The platform has two lanes: a SLOW lane (no direction → Scalar: speed, distance, time) and a FAST lane with arrows (direction matters → Vector: velocity, displacement). Remember: S for Scalar = Speed; V for Vector = Velocity
1
Compartment 1 — Distance vs Displacement — A boy runs around the entire train (20 laps = Distance). But his START and END point are the same, so Displacement = ZERO. Picture a dog chasing its own tail — all that effort, zero displacement!
2
Compartment 2 — Speed & Velocity — The speedometer on the train shows speed. But the train's GPS (with direction) shows velocity. Speed = distance/time (scalar). Velocity = displacement/time (vector). Unit = m/s for both. Multiply by 5/18 to convert km/h → m/s.
3
Compartment 3 — Acceleration — The driver presses the accelerator = velocity changes. Rate of change of velocity = Acceleration. If he brakes = Negative acceleration = Deceleration. Unit = m/s². Think: Pressing pedal = +a, Pressing brake = -a
4
Compartment 4 — Circular Carriage (Uniform Circular Motion) — A ceiling fan is spinning in this compartment. The fan blade moves at constant SPEED but its direction keeps changing → so velocity changes → acceleration exists → directed toward the center = Centripetal acceleration = v²/r. Force = mv²/r.
5
Compartment 5 — The UTSAV Equation Compartment — Three equations are written on the wall: (1) v = u + at, (2) s = ut + ½at², (3) v² - u² = 2as. UTSAV: U=initial velocity, T=time, S=distance, A=acceleration, V=final velocity. For vertical: a→g, s→h
6
Compartment 6 — Force Room — Two people: one TOUCHING the wall (contact force: friction, spring, muscle) and one MAGNET floating (non-contact: gravity, magnetic, electrostatic). Conservative forces = non-contact + spring. Non-conservative = friction.
7
Compartment 7 — Newton's Throne Room — Newton sits on a throne with 3 laws written: Law 1 = "I won't move unless you force me" (Inertia). Law 2 = "Force = how hard you push × how heavy I am" (F=ma). Law 3 = "Whatever you do to me, I'll do back equally" (Action-Reaction).
🏁
Final Station — Momentum & Impulse — A heavy truck (high momentum = mv) collides gently. But a quick SLAP (short time, large force = Impulse). Conservation of momentum: what you had before collision = what you have after. Rocket = 3rd law + momentum conservation.

📐 Scalar & Vector Quantities
Scalar vs Vector
🔥 Must Know Foundation of all physics questions

📏 SCALAR Quantities

  • Only magnitude — no direction
  • Scalar → Speed (S for S!)
  • Distance
  • Time
  • Mass
  • Energy, Work, Power
  • Temperature

🧭 VECTOR Quantities

  • Magnitude + Direction
  • Vector → Velocity (V for V!)
  • Displacement
  • Force
  • Acceleration
  • Momentum
  • Weight

🧠 Memory Hook: S–S–V–V Rule

S → S    V → V

Scalar → Speed  |  Vector → Velocity

Time is scalar: "9 PM" — you never say "9 PM in the eastward direction!" Direction makes no sense for time. But velocity = "10 m/s NORTH" — direction is essential.

📍 Distance & Displacement
Distance vs Displacement
🔥 Must Know Circular motion question is a favourite!

📏 Distance

  • Total path length covered
  • Scalar quantity
  • Always positive (≥ 0)
  • A→B→A = 20 km (if AB = 10 km)

📍 Displacement

  • Shortest distance from start to end point
  • Vector quantity
  • Can be zero, positive, or negative
  • A→B→A = 0 (back at start!)

🎯 The Famous Circular Motion Trap Question

A child rides a bicycle in a circle of radius = 10 m. One complete rotation takes 1 minute. What is displacement after 150 seconds?

1
150 sec ÷ 60 sec = 2.5 rotations. After 2 full rotations → displacement = 0. Remaining = 0.5 rotation = half circle
2
After half circle, child is at the diametrically opposite point. Shortest distance between start & that point = Diameter
✅
Displacement = Diameter = 2 × radius = 2 × 10 = 20 meters
🧠 Rule: For ANY number of complete rotations → displacement = 0. Only the remainder matters. If remainder = half rotation → displacement = diameter (2r).

🔑 Key Rules to Remember

n complete circles → Displacement = 0
½ circle → Displacement = Diameter (2r)
¼ circle → Displacement = r√2 (diagonal)
Distance is always the actual path traveled → never zero if moving

🚀 Acceleration
Acceleration & Deceleration
⭐ Important Core for numericals

Definition

Rate of change of velocity per unit time.

a = Δv / t = (v − u) / t
SI Unit: m/s²  |  Vector quantity

Positive Acceleration

  • Velocity increases with time
  • Pressing gas pedal
  • Speeding up
  • a is positive

Negative Acceleration (Deceleration / Retardation)

  • Velocity decreases with time
  • Pressing brakes
  • Slowing down
  • a is negative

💥 Force & Its Types
Force & Its Classification
⭐ Important Classification tree is exam favourite

Definition

Force is that which can produce change in velocity or change in direction of motion.

SI Unit = Newton (N)
F = ma  |  1 Newton = 1 kg × 1 m/s²

🌳 Force Classification Tree

1. Based on Contact

Contact Forces

  • Objects must be touching
  • Frictional force
  • Spring force
  • Muscular force
  • Normal force

Non-Contact Forces

  • Objects not touching
  • Gravitational force
  • Magnetic force
  • Electrostatic force

2. Based on Path Independence (Work Done)

Conservative Forces

  • Work done independent of path
  • Work done = 0 in closed path
  • All non-contact forces
  • Exception: Spring force (contact but conservative!)

Non-Conservative Forces

  • Work done depends on path
  • Work done ≠ 0 in closed path
  • Frictional force
  • Air resistance
🧠 Memory: "All non-contact + Spring = Conservative. Friction = Non-conservative."
Exception alert: Spring force is CONTACT but CONSERVATIVE!

3. Balanced vs Unbalanced

Balanced Force

  • Net force = Zero
  • Forces cancel each other
  • Object stays in same state (rest or constant velocity)
  • Example: Book on table

Unbalanced Force

  • Net force ≠ Zero
  • Forces don't cancel
  • Object accelerates or decelerates
  • 30N − 10N = 20N net force
⚖️ Newton's 3 Laws of Motion
Newton's 3 Laws of Motion
🔥 Must Know Every exam has at least 1-2 questions from here
1

Law of Inertia

An object at rest stays at rest, an object in motion stays in motion — unless acted upon by an external force.

📌 Inertia = Resistance to change in state | Inertia ∝ Mass

Types of Inertia:
A
Inertia of Rest: Dry fruits fall when tree is shaken (fruits stay, tree moves). Passenger thrown backward when vehicle suddenly starts.
B
Inertia of Motion: Passenger thrown forward when vehicle suddenly brakes. Coin flipped upward falls back in same place (bus/train).
🧠 Hook: Bigger mass = Harder to stop truck vs cycle = Higher inertia. Truck has more inertia of rest AND motion!
2

Law of Force & Acceleration

F = ma
Force = Mass × Acceleration

Acceleration is directly proportional to force and inversely proportional to mass.

Derivation from Momentum:
F = Δp/t = m(v−u)/t = m×a
This is where the momentum formula comes from!
3

Law of Action-Reaction

For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.
SituationActionReaction
RocketGases pushed downwardRocket pushed upward
Gun firedBullet pushed forwardGun recoils backward
WalkingFoot pushes ground backGround pushes you forward
SwimmingHands push water backWater pushes you forward
🧠 Hook: "Do good and you'll get good" — whatever you give, you receive equal and opposite!

🚀 Conservation of Momentum Law

m₁u₁ + m₂u₂ = m₁v₁ + m₂v₂
Total momentum before collision = Total momentum after collision (no external force)
📌
Sticking together (perfectly inelastic): m₁u₁ + m₂u₂ = (m₁+m₂)v — they share one final velocity
🚂
Example: Girl (moving) jumps on stationary cart → combine masses, find common velocity using conservation
🚀
Rocket: Works on conservation of momentum + Newton's 3rd law

👋 Impulse — The "Slap" Concept

Force applied for a very short time = Impulse.
Like a slap — hand-to-cheek contact is just milliseconds, but the force is large.
Impulse = F × t = Change in momentum (Δp)

Why airbags save lives? They increase time of collision → reduces force (F = Δp/t, same Δp, more t = less F)
⚠️ Exam Traps — Don't Lose Marks Here!
⚠️ Exam Traps
These are the exact points where students lose marks. Study these separately before every exam.
🪤
Trap 1: Uniform Circular Motion — Speed vs Velocity
Students say "velocity is constant in uniform circular motion." WRONG! Speed is constant, velocity is NOT (direction changes). Centripetal acceleration exists precisely because velocity changes.
🪤
Trap 2: Circular Motion Displacement = Diameter, NOT radius
After half rotation, displacement = Diameter (2r), not radius (r). Students confuse and write r.
🪤
Trap 3: Area of Velocity-Time Graph
Students say area of v-t graph = velocity or speed. NO! Area of v-t graph = Displacement. Slope of v-t graph = acceleration.
🪤
Trap 4: Spring Force is Conservative (not non-conservative)
Spring force is a CONTACT force, but it is CONSERVATIVE (exception!). Students assume all contact forces = non-conservative. Only friction is non-conservative in common forces.
🪤
Trap 5: Free Fall — Initial Velocity
In free fall, u (initial velocity) = 0. Students use some other value. If thrown upward, at the highest point, v (final velocity) = 0.
🪤
Trap 6: Displacement can be ZERO even if distance is not
Moving in a circle and returning to start → Distance = 2πr × n laps, but Displacement = 0. This confuses many.
🪤
Trap 7: Escape Velocity = 11.2 km/s (not m/s, not km/h)
The unit matters. 11.2 km/s. Watch the unit in MCQs — they may give 11.2 in wrong units as distractors.
🪤
Trap 8: km/h to m/s Conversion Direction
To go from km/h → m/s: multiply by 5/18. To go from m/s → km/h: multiply by 18/5. Students often flip these.
🪤
Trap 9: Momentum SI Unit = kg·m/s (not kg·m/s²)
kg·m/s² = Newton (unit of force). Momentum (p) = kg·m/s. Easy to confuse under pressure.
🪤
Trap 10: Newton's 1st Law vs 3rd Law for Rockets
Rockets work on Newton's 3rd Law (Action-Reaction) AND conservation of momentum. Some students say 2nd law.
❓ Active Recall Quiz
Active Recall Quiz
Test yourself! Click options to check. Force retrieval before re-reading.
Q1. In uniform circular motion, which quantity remains constant?
Speed remains constant. Velocity changes because direction changes continuously. Acceleration (centripetal) = v²/r, its direction keeps changing toward center, so only its magnitude is constant — but speed is the classic exam answer.
Q2. A ball is thrown upward. At the highest point, which of the following is true?
At the highest point, velocity = 0 (ball momentarily at rest). But gravity still acts, so acceleration = g = 9.8 m/s² downward. Gravity never switches off!
Q3. The area under a velocity-time graph gives:
Area of v-t graph = Displacement. Slope of v-t graph = Acceleration. Slope of d-t (distance-time) graph = Speed. These three facts are all exam-tested.
Q4. Which force is a contact force yet conservative in nature?
Spring force is a contact force (spring must touch the object) but it is CONSERVATIVE (work done is independent of path). This is the key exception. All non-contact forces (gravity, magnetic, electrostatic) are conservative too.
Q5. The escape velocity from Earth's surface is:
Escape velocity = 11.2 km/s. The unit is km/s (kilometers per second) — not m/s or km/h. This is deliberately tested with wrong units as distractors!
