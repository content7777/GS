Hormones & Plant Movement

SSC CGL Biology | Complete Retention System | Last Lecture of Biology

🌐 What This Chapter Is Really About

The body and plants are like smart organizations. They need managers (hormones) to send messages and coordinate work. This chapter maps out who the managers are, what they control, where they live, and what goes wrong when they fail. The second half covers how plants move — not randomly, but in response to signals.

🏛️ Chapter Memory Palace

Walk through this hospital in your mind. Each room = one gland.

The Hormone Hospital — Your Memory Story

You are a patient walking through a grand Hormone Hospital. As you enter each room, you meet the gland that lives there. It shows you its hormone, tells you what goes wrong when it fails, and sends you to the next room. Walk through this hospital every time you revise.

🦋

Room 1 — The Butterfly Room (Neck Corridor)

A giant butterfly sits in the corridor of the hospital. This is the thyroid gland — butterfly-shaped, in your neck. It hands you a bottle labelled Thyroxine (metabolism fuel). If you drop it, you get goiter. If it runs out, hypothyroidism → cretinism. If too much spills, hyperthyroidism.

🧂

Room 2 — The Calcium Vault (Behind the Butterfly)

Parathyroid glands hide behind the butterfly. They guard a vault of calcium — making sure blood calcium is never too high or too low.

🥋

Room 3 — The Training Dojo (Behind the Sternum)

Thymus is a dojo behind the sternum bone, in front of the heart. Young WBCs (T-lymphocytes) come here to be trained. After puberty, the dojo shrinks.

🎩

Room 4 — The Hat Room (Kidneys Floor)

Adrenal glands sit like caps on top of both kidneys. When there's danger or excitement, they throw out the fight-or-flight hormone — adrenaline. They also release cortisol (stress). Deficiency = Addison's disease.

🧠

Room 5 — The Brain HQ (Top Floor)

Hypothalamus is the CEO — makes hormones but doesn't release them. It sends them downstairs to Pituitary (the secretary/master gland) which releases everything. Pituitary has two wings: anterior (makes its own: Growth Hormone + Prolactin) and posterior (releases hypothalamus orders: ADH + Oxytocin).

🍃

Room 6 — The Pea Garden (Corner of Brain)

Pineal gland — tiny as a pea, sits in the brain. It manages your sleep clock by releasing melatonin. No melatonin = broken sleep.

🍬

Room 7 — The Sugar Factory (Abdomen)

Pancreas is a mixed gland. Its alpha cells make glucagon (glycogen → glucose = glycogenolysis). Beta cells make insulin (glucose → glycogen = glycogenesis). Insulin shortage = Diabetes mellitus.

🌿

Room 8 — The Garden Wing (Plant Section)

5 hormones in the garden: Auxin (tip, bends toward light), Gibberellin (germination + elongation), Cytokinin (cell division), ABA (stress, kills growth), Ethylene (ripens fruit — same gas used in metal welding!).

🔬 Glands 101

The foundation — understand this and everything else makes sense.

The Great Split: Endocrine vs Exocrine

🟢 Endocrine Glands

  • Release Hormones
  • Released directly into blood
  • No duct needed → Ductless glands
  • Examples: Thyroid, Pituitary, Adrenal

🔵 Exocrine Glands

  • Release Enzymes
  • Released through ducts
  • Have ducts → NOT ductless
  • Largest overall gland = Liver (exocrine)

🌟 Mixed Gland — Pancreas

Does both! Acts as endocrine (releases insulin, glucagon into blood) AND as exocrine (releases pancreatic juice through duct). 🔥 Must Know

Memory Hook: Duct or No Duct?

Endocrine = Into blood directly. Think: endocrine goes inside without any middle-man (no duct). Exocrine = has extra equipment (a duct).

Memory Hook: Plant Hormone Story

A plant is like a company. Auxin (the tip manager) tells the team which way to grow. Gibberellin is the growth consultant who starts new projects (germination). Cytokinin is HR — keeps hiring new cells. ABA is the crisis manager — when there's stress (drought), he says "Stop everything, close the doors (stomata)!" And Ethylene is the retirement notice — when the fruit is ready, it signals "We are done here, time to ripen and fall."

⚠ TRAP: Auxin is NOT the hormone that makes plant grow toward light directly

Auxin actually MOVES AWAY from light. It collects on the shaded side. That side grows MORE. So the plant bends TOWARD the light. The examiner will try to trick you by saying "Auxin moves toward light" — that is FALSE.

🌱 Plant Movement

Tropic (with growth) vs Nastic (without growth)

🌱 Tropic Movements (Growth-based)

Movement that involves growth. The plant physically grows toward or away from a stimulus. Permanent change in direction.

🌀 Nastic Movements (Non-growth)

Movement that does NOT involve growth. Plant moves in response to stimulus but doesn't grow. Reversible.

Tropic Movements

TypeStimulusPositive (toward)Negative (away)
Phototropism Light (Photo) Stem (grows toward sun) Root (grows away from sun)
Hydrotropism Water (Hydro) Root (grows toward water) Shoot (grows away from water)
Geotropism Gravity (Geo) Root (grows with gravity = downward) Shoot (grows against gravity = upward)
Chemotropism Chemical Pollen tube grows toward ovule (positive chemotropism) Away from chemicals
Memory Hook: Roots vs Shoots Rule

Roots are ALWAYS positive for things underground (water ✅, gravity ✅) and NEGATIVE for light. Shoots are the OPPOSITE. Roots want water and gravity. Shoots want light and freedom from gravity. Simple as that.

How Phototropism Works (Auxin Mechanism)

Step-by-Step: Why plants bend toward light

Sunlight hits one side of stem
↓
Auxin moves AWAY from light → collects on shaded side
↓
More auxin on shaded side → MORE growth on that side
↓
Plant BENDS toward light (toward sun)

Nastic Movements

TypeStimulusExampleGrowth?
Photonastic Light Sunflower tracking the sun daily ❌ No growth
Thigmonastic Touch (Thigmo = touch) Mimosa pudica (touch-me-not) wilts on touch; Venus flytrap closes on insect ❌ No growth

🪤 Venus Flytrap — Carnivorous Plant

An insect lands on its leaves → leaves snap shut → plant digests the insect. This is thigmonastic movement. The plant is carnivorous (meat-eating). No growth occurs — just mechanical movement.

🌻 Sunflower's Daily Movement

Sunflower follows the sun across the sky during the day. This is photonastic movement — NOT phototropism because there is no growth involved, just daily reversible movement.

🧠 Smart Memory Hooks

Vivid associations designed to survive forgetting for 6+ months.

Hook 1: Endocrine vs Exocrine

ENdocrine → ENters blood (ductless, no middleman). EXocrine → EXits through a duct (like an exit door). Enzyme comes out the door. Hormone jumps straight into the river (bloodstream).

Hook 2: Thyroid — The Butterfly That Eats Iodine

Imagine a butterfly sitting in your throat eating iodine salt. When it eats enough, it makes thyroxine. No iodine → starving butterfly → GOITER (the butterfly swells up). Too little thyroxine → CRETINISM (baby butterfly, stunted). Too much → HYPERTHYROIDISM (butterfly on caffeine).

Hook 3: Adrenal — The Cap on the Kidney

Picture two kidneys wearing police caps. Whenever there's an emergency (police situation), the cap releases ADRENALINE. Your heart races, you breathe fast — FIGHT or FLIGHT. The cap also has a stress alarm (CORTISOL). If the alarm breaks → Addison's disease.

Hook 4: Pituitary — The Master Butler

The Hypothalamus is the king who makes orders (hormones) but never delivers them himself. The Pituitary is his butler who runs through the blood delivering everything. The butler also has his own gifts (GH, Prolactin) in his FRONT pocket (anterior). The hypothalamus's royal orders go in the BACK pocket (posterior) — ADH and Oxytocin.

Hook 5: Insulin vs Glucagon — The Seesaw

Imagine a seesaw labelled SUGAR. INSULIN pushes sugar DOWN (glucose → glycogen, stored). GLUCAGON lifts sugar UP (glycogen → glucose, released). If INSULIN is missing → sugar stays HIGH → DIABETES MELLITUS. If GLUCAGON is missing → sugar crashes too low.

Hook 6: Gigantism vs Acromegaly vs Dwarfism

Think of growth hormone as a water tap. TOO LITTLE before growth = DWARFISM (the whole body is small — tap was closed early). TOO MUCH before adulthood = GIGANTISM (the whole body is huge — tap was open too long). TOO MUCH after adulthood = ACROMEGALY (only hands, feet, jaw grow — the tap opens late, only parts that can still grow, respond). Remember: Khali (the wrestler) has acromegaly.

Hook 7: Ethylene — The Ripening Gas That Welds

Ethylene (also called acetylene) is used in WELDING torches to melt metal. The same gas ripens fruits. Imagine a welder holding a banana — he accidentally ripens all the fruit in the shop. This double role (fruit ripening + welding) is a classic exam link.

Hook 8: Mimosa & Venus Flytrap — Touch Moves

MIMOSA (touch-me-not): Imagine someone super shy — the moment you touch them, they collapse and close up. VENUS FLYTRAP: Imagine a Venus goddess trap — she lures insects with beauty, then SNAP! Eats them. Both move on touch (thigmonastic) but NEVER grow from it.

Hook 9: Glycogenesis vs Glycogenolysis

Genesis = creation/beginning (Bible: Genesis = book of creation). GLYCO-GENESIS = creating glycogen from glucose (building up, storing). Lysis = breaking/dissolving (like electrolysis = breaking by electricity). GLYCOGENO-LYSIS = breaking glycogen down to glucose.

⚠ TRAP 4: Auxin Direction (MOST COMMON TRICK)

Wrong thinking: "Auxin moves toward light, so the side with more auxin grows more, so plant bends toward light."

Correct: Auxin moves AWAY from light. It concentrates on the SHADED side. That side grows taller. Plant bends TOWARD light. Auxin itself flees from light, but its effect bends the plant toward light.

⚠ TRAP 7: Largest Gland Confusion

Largest gland overall = LIVER (exocrine)
Largest ENDOCRINE gland = THYROID
Examiner will say "largest endocrine" → answer is thyroid, NOT liver. Or say "largest gland" → answer is liver.

⚠ TRAP 8: Insulin released by which cell?

Alpha cells → Glucagon (A for Alpha, A for anti-insulin, raises sugar)
Beta cells → Insulin (B for Beta, B for blood sugar lowering)
Students often flip these. Alpha = Glucagon. Beta = Insulin.

Tricky Statements Examiners Love

  • ❌ "Auxin moves toward sunlight" → FALSE (moves away from light)
  • ❌ "Thymus is located in the neck" → FALSE (it's behind the sternum)
  • ❌ "Liver is an endocrine gland" → FALSE (it's exocrine)
  • ❌ "Glucagon converts glucose to glycogen" → FALSE (that's insulin)
  • ✅ "Pituitary controls other endocrine glands" → TRUE (master gland)
  • ✅ "Ethylene is a gaseous plant hormone" → TRUE
  • ✅ "Pancreas is a mixed gland" → TRUE

  • Pineal → Melatonin
  • Pituitary (anterior) → GH, Prolactin
  • Pituitary (posterior) → ADH, Oxytocin

Disease → Cause

  • Cretinism → Thyroxine deficiency

  • Addison's Disease → Cortisol deficiency

❓ MCQ Active Recall Quiz

Test yourself. Select an answer — get instant feedback.

1. Which is the largest endocrine gland in the human body?

A. Liver
B. Thyroid
C. Pituitary
D. Pancreas
✅ Thyroid is the largest ENDOCRINE gland (butterfly-shaped, neck). Liver is the largest gland overall but it's EXOCRINE.

2. Auxin hormone in plants moves _______ when sunlight falls on one side.

A. Toward the light
B. Upward through the xylem
C. Away from light (to shaded side)
D. Toward the roots
✅ Auxin moves AWAY from light. The shaded side gets more auxin → grows faster → plant bends toward light. This is the classic trap!

3. Deficiency of which hormone causes Diabetes Insipidus?

A. Insulin
B. ADH (Vasopressin)
C. Glucagon
D. Thyroxine
✅ ADH = Anti-Diuretic Hormone = Vasopressin. It helps reabsorb water. Deficiency = can't retain water = Diabetes Insipidus. NOT insulin (that causes Diabetes Mellitus).

4. Which cells in the pancreas secrete Glucagon?

A. Alpha cells
B. Beta cells
C. Delta cells
D. Gamma cells
✅ Alpha → Glucagon (glycogen to glucose). Beta → Insulin (glucose to glycogen). Remember: Alpha comes BEFORE Beta alphabetically, just as Glucagon is the first responder to LOW sugar.

5. Melatonin hormone is secreted by which gland?

A. Pituitary
B. Thyroid
C. Adrenal
D. Pineal
✅ Pineal gland → Melatonin → regulates Circadian Rhythm (sleep-wake cycle). Pineal is pea-sized in the brain.

6. Venus Flytrap's leaf-closing action is an example of:

A. Photonastic movement
B. Geotropism
C. Thigmonastic movement
D. Chemotropism
✅ Thigmonastic = movement due to touch. "Thigmo" = touch in Greek. Venus flytrap and Mimosa pudica both show thigmonastic movement. No growth involved.

7. Which plant hormone is also known as the "stress hormone" and inhibits growth?

A. Auxin
B. Abscisic Acid (ABA)
C. Cytokinin
D. Gibberellin
✅ Abscisic Acid (ABA) is the stress/growth inhibitor hormone. It closes stomata during drought and causes leaf fall. All others are GROWTH hormones.

8. Ghrelin hormone is released by which organ and is famous for:

A. Stomach — Hunger hormone
B. Liver — Digestion hormone
C. Kidney — Water hormone
D. Pancreas — Sugar hormone
✅ Ghrelin is released by the STOMACH and is the HUNGER hormone. People with higher ghrelin levels feel hungrier. Classic MCQ trap: "which hormone makes you feel hungry?"

9. Pollen tube growing toward an ovule in a flower is an example of:

A. Positive geotropism
B. Negative phototropism
C. Positive chemotropism
D. Thigmonastic movement
✅ The ovule releases chemicals. The pollen tube grows TOWARD those chemicals = Positive Chemotropism. This is a favourite unique question that few students know.

10. Ethylene hormone in plants is unique because:

A. It is secreted only in roots
B. It is a gaseous hormone and ripens fruits
C. It promotes cell division
D. It is found only in aquatic plants
✅ Ethylene is the only GASEOUS plant hormone. It ripens fruits. Also called acetylene — used in welding! This dual chemistry-biology link is a high-value exam fact.
