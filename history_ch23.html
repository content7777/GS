Mughal Empire

SSC CGL · High-Retention Revision Notes · FRB 2.0

1526 – 1857 6 Great Mughals Panipat × 2 Navratnas Mansabdari System Architecture Giants
🔭

Big Picture

The Mughal Empire (1526–1857) was the last great medieval empire of India. It began when Babur defeated Ibrahim Lodi at the First Battle of Panipat (1526) and ended with Bahadur Shah Zafar being exiled to Rangoon after 1857. Six rulers — Babur, Humayun, Akbar, Jahangir, Shah Jahan, Aurangzeb — are called the "Great Mughals." The empire's rise was built on military innovation (gunpowder, Tughlama), administrative genius (Mansabdari, Land Revenue), and cultural brilliance (architecture, art, literature). Its fall began the moment Aurangzeb went south in 1681 and never returned.

Chapter Memory Palace

Walk Through the Mughal Durbar
🏯 Imagine a grand Durbar hall with 7 rooms, one for each ruler. Walk in…
🚪 Room 1 · Babur The entrance gate has a gun (first gunpowder user) and a pincer claw on both sides (Tughlama formation). A book on the desk reads "Turkish." A skull with a Uzbek cap — he was chased from Fergana. He drank water → ate food → collected charity → went home: 4 battles, 1526-29.
🚪 Room 2 · Humayun In this room, the floor is cracked — he fell from library stairs. A Persian carpet (he fled to Iran). His sister's diary on the shelf (Gulbadan Begum wrote Humayunama). A charbagh garden outside the window (his tomb).
🚪 Room 2½ · Sher Shah A temporary room built by squatter Farid Khan. He holds a Rupee coin and a map with GT Road running across it. A clock shows 10 years (Dasal system). A bomb explosion picture — died at Kalinjar.
🚪 Room 3 · Akbar The biggest, most decorated room. 9 gems glitter on a table (Navratna). A wedding album with a Rajput princess. A translation desk (Maktabkhana). A small flame labeled "Din-e-Ilahi" — only Birbal touched it. Fatehpur Sikri model in the corner.
🚪 Room 4 · Jahangir A golden chain hangs from the ceiling (Chain of Justice). Two portraits: a Rathore girl and a widow named Meher Nisa (Noor Jahan) who actually runs the room. A letter from Thomas Roe on the table. A diary written in Persian.
🚪 Room 5 · Shah Jahan White marble everything — Red Fort, Taj Mahal, Jama Masjid models everywhere. A Peacock Throne with a "STOLEN – Nadir Shah 1739" sticker. A portrait of Dara Shikoh crossed out by Aurangzeb. Shah Jahan sits locked in a corner looking at Taj Mahal through the window from his Agra Fort prison.
🚪 Room 6 · Aurangzeb A crumbling room. A map showing him stuck in the South for 25 years. A Fatwa Alamgiri book. A "Jaziya Reinstated" sign. A broken bridge labeled "The Mughals." A simple grave in Daulatabad — the humblest of all Mughal tombs.

Babur (1526–1530)

Founder of Mughal Empire
🌙
Zahiruddin Muhammad Babur
Born 1483 · Ruled 1526–1530 · Died Agra, Buried Kabul
Original name: Zahiruddin
Descended from: Timur + Genghis Khan
Throne of FerganaSat at age 12 → chased by Uzbeks → came to Kabul 1504 (age 22)
Innovation 🔥First use of gunpowder on mass scale in Indian subcontinent (1st Battle of Panipat)
TughlamaFlanking formation — enemy is encircled from both sides, cannot escape. Right flank led by Humayun.

DeathDied 1530 in Agra, permanently buried in Kabul (Afghanistan)
🧠 Memory Hook — Babur's 4 Battles (1526→27→28→29)

Imagine Babur as a thirsty traveller: First he DRANK water (Battle of Panipat 1526 — defeated Ibrahim Lodi), then ATE food (Battle of Khanwa 1527 — defeated Rana Sanga of Mewar), then COLLECTED donations (Battle of Chanderi 1528 — defeated Medini Rai), then WENT HOME (Battle of Ghaghra 1529 — defeated Afghans).
Year sequence: 26 → 27 → 28 → 29 — like counting up.

Sher Shah Suri (1540–1545)

The Afghan Interlude · Administrative Genius
🦁
Farid Khan → Sher Shah Suri
Son of Hasan Khan (Jagirdar of Sasaram, Bihar) · Ruled 1540–1545
Original name: Farid Khan
ConquestsMalwa 1542 · Raisen 1543 · Ranthambore 1542 · Chittor 1544 · Kalinjar 1545 (died here)
DeathDied at Kalinjar Fort — bomb/gunpowder accidentally exploded near him → injured → died
Currency 🔥Introduced Rupee coin with fixed standard weights and measures
GT Road 🔥Grand Trunk Road — Chittagong (Bangladesh) to Peshawar. Built Sarais (rest shelters) along the way.
Land RevenueLand measured · Tax fixed at 1/3 of average yield · Patta (title deed) + Kabuliyat (deed of agreement) given to farmers
Direct Tax 🔥Removed landlords as middlemen → collected tax directly from farmers
LegacyAkbar adopted his entire administrative system — this is a frequently asked exam question!
🧠 Memory Hook — Sher Shah's Admin

Sher Shah was basically the startup founder Akbar acquired. The Rupee, GT Road, Patta-Kabuliyat, direct tax — ALL of it was taken and scaled by Akbar. If exam asks "who originated Akbar's system?" → Sher Shah Suri.

🏅 Mansabdari System (1571)

Zat (Jet)
  • Number of soldiers maintained
  • Determines your rank (Mansab)
  • Range: 10 to 10,000
  • Akbar's max: 7,000 (Mirza Aziz Koka + Man Singh)
Sawar
  • Number of horsemen maintained
  • Secondary rank indicator
Payment
  • Cash (for smaller mansabs)
  • Jagir (land grant) for larger mansabs
  • From Jagir, maintain army + keep revenue
Key Points
  • Mansabs were NOT hereditary
  • Jagirdari system = land rights within Mansabdari
  • Inspired by Sher Shah's system
  • Max rank under Jahangir: 10,000

Aurangzeb (1658–1707)

The Beginning of the End
⚖️
Muhi-ud-Din Muhammad → Aurangzeb "Alamgir"
Ruled 1658–1707 · Died Ahmednagar → Buried Daulatabad (Sambhaji Nagar)
Title: Alamgir · Also called: Zinda Peer
Rise to PowerKilled Dara Shikoh (Shah Jahan's favourite son). Imprisoned Shah Jahan in Agra Fort till death.
Guru Tegh Bahadur 🔥9th Sikh Guru · Captured + killed 1675 · Refused to convert to Islam · Was opposing forced conversion of Kashmiri Pandits
JaziyaReintroduced Jaziya (Akbar had abolished it)
South India 🔥Left Delhi 1681 → Captured Bijapur + Golconda (they had earlier accepted suzerainty). BUT could NOT capture Maratha Empire (Shivaji → Sambhaji). Stayed in South 25 years. Never returned. Mughal Empire began collapsing.
Fatwa AlamgiriDigest of Muslim Laws — appointed religious officers to enforce Islamic practices
Death + TombDied 1707 · Buried in Khuldabad, Daulatabad (now Sambhaji Nagar, Maharashtra) — simplest Mughal tomb
Bibi Ka Maqbara 🔥Built by Aurangzeb's son Prince Azam Shah in 1660 for Aurangzeb's wife Dilras Banu Begum. Called "Mini Taj Mahal." In Maharashtra.
🧠 Memory Hook — Why Mughal Empire Collapsed

Imagine you're the CEO of a company and you leave your headquarters to open a new branch in another city. You stay there for 25 years. What happens to headquarters? It collapses. That's Aurangzeb. He left Delhi in 1681, got stuck fighting Marathas in the South, never came back, and died there in 1707. The empire had no central control for 25 years → total disintegration.

Exam Traps

What Students Always Get Wrong
⚠ High-Frequency Confusion Zones
⚡
Chanderi (1528) ≠ Chandawar (1194) — Chandawar is where Muhammad Ghori defeated Raja Jaichand (Gahadvala Rajput). Chanderi is where Babur defeated Medini Rai.
⚡
Humayunama: Sister vs Wife — Biography (Humayunama) = written by sister Gulbadan Begum. Tomb = built by wife Haji Begum / Bega Begum.
⚡
Khusro ≠ Khurram — Khusro = son of Jahangir who revolted (given shelter by Guru Arjun Dev). Khurram = Shah Jahan (different son).
⚡
Badshahnama ≠ Shahnama — Badshahnama (Shah Jahan's chronicle) = Abdul Hamid Lahori. Shahnama = Firdausi (for Mahmud Ghaznavi, ~1000 AD).
⚡
Ain-i-Akbari ≠ Akbarnama — Ain-i-Akbari is Vol III of Akbarnama. Exams may ask separately. Both written by Abul Fazal.
⚡
Bibi Ka Maqbara: who built, for whom — Built by Prince Azam Shah (Aurangzeb's son) for Dilras Banu Begum (Aurangzeb's wife). NOT built by Aurangzeb.
⚡
Thomas Roe ≠ William Hawkins — Hawkins came first, got mansab 400, got NO trading rights. Roe came 1615, was James I's ambassador, GOT trading rights at Surat.
⚡
Tavernier visited India HOW MANY times? — SIX times. Wrote "Travels in India."
⚡
Jahangir killed which Sikh Guru? — 5th Guru Arjun Dev (not Tegh Bahadur — that was Aurangzeb's 9th Guru kill).
⚡
Birbal and Din-e-Ilahi — First AND ONLY Hindu to accept it. These are two different facts but mean the same thing — don't get confused if question asks "first" vs "only."
⚡
Babur buried where? — Initially in Agra, then permanently in Kabul. If exam says "buried in Agra" — that's a trap!
⚡
Noor Jahan ≠ Shah Jahan's mother — Shah Jahan's mother was Jagat Gosai (Rathore princess). Noor Jahan was his step-mother (Jahangir's 2nd wife).
⚡
Moti Masjid — which city, which ruler — Lahore=Jahangir, Agra=Shah Jahan, Delhi=Aurangzeb.
