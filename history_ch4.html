Big Picture

This chapter is the story of how Indian political consciousness evolved from small regional clubs (1814–1884) into a full-scale national party (INC, 1885) — and how British administrators shaped India's political landscape through acts, wars, and policies (1772–1947). Think of it as two parallel streams: Indians organising themselves, and British governors reshaping India. The exam loves firsts, years, presidents, and the exact session where each milestone happened.

Chapter Memory Palace — The Grand Delhi Station

Imagine a grand old railway station called "India Station."

You enter the waiting room (1814–1866): Raja Ram Mohan Roy sits reading a newspaper — he started Atmiya Sabha, Brahmo Samaj, Hindu College. Beside him, Dadabhai Naoroji (old man with white beard = Grand Old Man) holds a book: "Poverty and Un-British Rule".

You walk to Platform 1 (1867–1884): A Marathi platform. Ranade stands at Prarthana Samaj sign (1867). Shishir Kumar Ghosh sells Amrit Bazar Patrika (India League 1875). Surendranath Banerjee and Anand Mohan Bose share a bench — Indian National Association 1876. Feroze Shah Mehta announces Bombay Presidency Association 1885. A signboard reads MGP → Madras Mahajan Sabha 1884.

The station master's office (1885) has AO Hume inside — a birdwatcher (Father of Indian Ornithology) who founded INC. The station was supposed to be in Pune but PLAGUE struck, so it moved to Bombay. 72 delegates board the train. WC Banerjee is the driver.

Each train compartment is a Congress session. Compartment 1906 = Naoroji's third presidency + Kolkata; 1907 = Surat crack in the compartment wall (SPLIT); 1916 = Lucknow — crack REPAIRED (Merger); 1917 = Annie Besant — first woman pilot; 1929 = Lahore — Nehru declares PURNA SWARAJ at the final destination.

On the other side of the station sit the Governor Generals: Warren Hastings at the first window (Regulating Act 1773), Cornwallis polishing his police badge (Father of ICS), Dalhousie with tiny train toys (first railway 1853), Canning watching the 1857 revolt fire and becoming first Viceroy, Ripon building a mini city (Father of Local Self-Govt), and Mountbatten locking the station door — last British Governor General.

Indian National Congress — Formation (1885)

WHO FORMED IT?

Allan Octavian Hume (AO Hume)
Called: Father of Indian Ornithology
(Ornithology = Study of Birds)

WHERE?

Gokuldas Tejpal Sanskrit College, Bombay
Originally planned: Pune (shifted due to plague outbreak)
72 delegates attended
President: WC Banerjee (Umesh Chandra Banerjee)

🔥 Immediate Cause — Ilbert Bill Controversy

Ripon was Viceroy · Ilbert composed the bill · Bill said Indian Judicial Magistrates can try Britishers · Britishers PROTESTED · Ripon had to withdraw it · This exposed British double standards → fuelled need for INC

📚 Three Theories About INC Formation
  • 🔴 Safety Valve Theory — Lala Lajpat Rai: British created INC as a pressure valve to release anti-British steam safely
  • ⚡ Lightning Conductor Theory — Gopal Krishna Gokhale: INC acts like a lightning rod — channels dangerous energy safely
  • 😡 Factory of Sedition — Dufferin (Viceroy when INC was formed) called it this. (He ironically named it!)

Smart Memory Hooks

Hook 1 — The LOCAL PERSON rule (Congress Sessions)

Wherever a session was held, the local/majority person did NOT preside. Madras = mostly Hindu → Muslim presided (Tyabji). Allahabad = Hindu + Muslim → Christian/Britisher presided (George Yule). Use this logic to deduce the president!

Hook 2 — Naoroji's THREE presidencies

Dadabhai Naoroji presided 1886, 1893, 1906. Think: "Naoroji scored THREE centuries: 86, 93, 06." Like a cricket batsman with three tons!

Hook 3 — Women Presidents of INC

Annie Besant (1917) = First WOMAN president but she was British → "Annie came FIRST but was a foreigner."
Sarojini Naidu (1925) = First INDIAN woman → "Sarojini sang India's song, so she's the Indian one."

Hook 4 — Cornwallis has TWO graves

Only Governor General to die in India. "Cornwallis loved India so much, he stayed TWICE — one grave in Ghazipur, one in history books."

Hook 5 — Dalhousie's FIRST Railway (1853)

Dalhousie = DAL-housie = DAL (lentils) train. Imagine a train carrying dal from Bombay to Thane, 34 km. First railway. Dal was the first thing India exported by train!

Hook 6 — Subsidiary Alliance trick

Wellesley BROUGHT it. Dupleix (French) CONCEIVED it. "Dupleix was the BRAIN, Wellesley was the BRAWN." First to sign = Hyderabad 1798, Mysore 1799.

Hook 7 — Surat Split vs Lucknow Merger

Think of it as a CRACK (1907 Surat) and a WELD (1916 Lucknow). Surat cracked the glass. Lucknow welded it back. Time gap = 9 years.

Hook 8 — Gandhi's ONLY presidency — Belgaum 1924

Gandhi presided ONCE only. He was released from jail in 1923 (post Chauri Chaura). Imagine him walking out of jail straight to Belgaum to take the chair — one entry, one session, one exit.

Exam Traps — Do NOT Get These Wrong

TRAP 1 — Annie Besant vs Sarojini Naidu

Annie Besant (1917) = First WOMAN president (but British)
Sarojini Naidu (1925) = First INDIAN woman president
👉 If exam asks "first woman" → Annie Besant. If "first Indian woman" → Sarojini Naidu.

TRAP 2 — Warren Hastings vs Lord Hastings

These are TWO different people!
Warren Hastings = First Governor General of Bengal (1772)
Lord Hastings = Governor General 1813–1823 (3rd Maratha War, Treaty of Sagauli, Ryotwari system)
👉 Don't confuse them on match-the-following!

TRAP 3 — Swaraj (1906) vs Purna Swaraj (1929)

Swaraj (1906 Kolkata) = Self-rule. NOT full independence.
Purna Swaraj (1929 Lahore) = Complete independence.
👉 Exam will try to make you say 1906 = complete independence. WRONG!

TRAP 4 — Treaty of Amritsar vs Treaty of Lahore

Treaty of Amritsar 1809 = With Maharaja Ranjit Singh (Minto I). Sutlej river boundary.
Treaty of Lahore = After 1st Anglo-Sikh War (Hardinge I). Completely different!
👉 Both involve Sikhs but completely different context & Viceroy.

TRAP 5 — Census: 1872 vs 1881

1872 (Mayo) = First census, but INCOMPLETE and unsynchronised
1881 (Ripon) = First COMPLETE census
👉 "First census" = 1872. "First complete/synchronized census" = 1881.

TRAP 6 — Delhi Durbar dates

1st Delhi Durbar = 1877 (Lytton) — Queen Victoria
2nd Delhi Durbar = 1903 (Curzon)
3rd Delhi Durbar = 1911 (Hardinge II) — George V + Capital shift announced
👉 Exams often mix these up!

TRAP 7 — INC First Session Location

Session was planned in Pune but held in Bombay due to plague outbreak.
👉 Exam may ask "originally planned location" — answer = Pune.

TRAP 8 — Subsidiary Alliance Origin

Wellesley IMPLEMENTED it. Dupleix (French Governor General) was its ORIGINATOR/BRAINCHILD.
👉 If asked "who introduced Subsidiary Alliance" → Wellesley. "Whose idea was it originally?" → Dupleix.

TRAP 9 — Cornwallis — 1st GG of Bengal? No!

Warren Hastings = 1st GG of Bengal (Regulating Act 1773)
William Bentinck = 1st GG of India (Govt of India Act 1833)
Cornwallis = Father of ICS + Father of modern police. But NOT the first GG of anything!

TRAP 10 — Capital Shift: Announced vs Shifted

Capital shift announced in 1911 (at 3rd Delhi Durbar under Hardinge II)
Capital actually shifted in 1912
👉 Both years can be asked!
