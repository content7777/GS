🗂 Big Picture

Part V of the Constitution deals with The Union. It has 5 chapters: Executive → Parliament → President's Legislative Power → Supreme Court → CAG. The Union Executive (Art. 52–78) includes: President, Vice President, Prime Minister + Council of Ministers, and Attorney General.

Today's focus: President (Art. 52–62, 72) and Vice President (Art. 63–71). The President is the constitutional head of India — nominal/titular, while real power rests with the PM. The VP is ex-officio Chairman of Rajya Sabha.

Every fact from the lecture — article by article

THE PRESIDENT

Art. 52

🔥 There shall be a President of India. Establishes the office. Nothing more to it.

Art. 53

🔥 Executive power of the Union is vested in the President.

  • President is Supreme Commander of all Defence Forces (Army, Navy, Air Force)
  • All war declarations and treaties are made in the name of the President
Art. 54

🔥 Electoral College for President's Election

  • Elected MPs + Elected MLAs participate
  • Nominated MPs and Nominated MLAs do NOT participate
  • No direct public vote — representatives vote
Art. 55

⭐ Manner of Election

  • Indirect election
  • Proportional Representation + Single Transferable Vote
  • Secret Ballot (no EVM — ballot paper; showing your vote = cancellation; Mulayam Singh case with Pranab Mukherjee)
  • 50 proposers + 50 seconders required to even nominate
Quota = Total Votes ÷ (Number of Vacancies + 1) + 1
Since only 1 President vacancy: Quota = Total Votes ÷ 2 + 1
Value of MLA's Vote = State Population ÷ (Total MLAs × 1000)
Value of MP's Vote = Total MLA Votes of All States ÷ (Total MPs × 1000)
Art. 56

🔥 Term of Office

  • 5 years from date of entering office
  • Resignation goes to Vice President
  • Can hold office beyond 5 years until successor enters
Art. 57

⭐ Re-election Eligibility

  • A person can be elected President any number of times — NO limit
  • Unlike USA — max 2 terms for US President
Art. 58

🔥 Qualifications to Become President

  • Must be a citizen of India
  • Must have completed 35 years of age
  • Must be qualified to be elected as a member of Lok Sabha (House of People)
  • Must NOT hold any office of profit
  • Exceptions: Minister, Chairman of National Commissions (SC/ST/Women) are NOT office of profit
  • Office of profit is NOWHERE defined in the Constitution
Art. 59

⭐ Conditions of Office

  • President shall NOT be a member of Lok Sabha, Rajya Sabha, State Legislative Assembly, or Legislative Council
  • If already a member → must resign upon election as President
  • Allowances CANNOT be diminished (reduced) during tenure — can only be increased
  • Salary charged from Consolidated Fund of India (non-votable part of budget)
Art. 60

⭐ Oath / Affirmation

  • Administered by Chief Justice of India (CJI)
  • If CJI absent → by the senior-most judge of Supreme Court
  • Oath = for theists (believers in God)
  • Affirmation = for atheists (non-believers)
Art. 61

🔥 Impeachment of the President

  • Ground: Violation of the Constitution (violation is NOT defined anywhere in Constitution)
  • Resolution can be started in EITHER house (Lok Sabha or Rajya Sabha)
  • NOT in State Legislature (Vidhan Sabha/Parishad)
  • 14-day prior notice to the Presiding Officer (Speaker if LS; Chairman if RS)
  • Minimum ¼ of total house members must sign
  • Must be passed by 2/3 special majority in BOTH houses
  • Second house investigates the charges — that's why it's called Quasi-Judicial
  • Borrowed from Constitution of USA
  • No President has EVER been impeached in India. No resolution even initiated.
Special Majority = 2/3 of TOTAL membership of the House
Art. 62

⭐ Filling Vacancy in President's Office

  • Vacancy created by: Death / Resignation / Removal / End of Term
  • If term ending normally → election before term expires
  • Premature vacancy → VP acts as President → election within 6 months
  • Historical: Zakir Hussain died (1969) → VV Giri (VP) refused acting role → wanted permanent presidency → CJI M. Hidayatullah became acting President
  • After VV Giri became permanent President → Hidayatullah returned to CJI role
  • Parliament passed a law: VP absent → CJI → Senior-most SC Judge
Art. 72

🔥 Pardoning Powers of the President

PowerWhat it means
PardonFull acquittal — go free, all charges gone
CommutationForm of punishment changes (death → life imprisonment)
RemissionDuration is reduced (20 years → 10 years)
RespiteDuration reduced on health grounds (pregnancy, old age, illness)
ReprieveTemporary stay/hold on execution — not permanent

Nirbhaya case: 3 convicts went to President seeking pardon → President refused → hanging carried out.

GOVERNOR vs PRESIDENT — Pardoning Power Difference

President (Art. 72)
Can pardon death sentences
Can pardon Court Martial sentences
All 5 powers: Pardon, Commute, Remit, Respite, Reprieve
Governor (Art. 161)
CANNOT pardon death sentences
CANNOT pardon Court Martial sentences
Same 5 powers otherwise

THE VICE PRESIDENT

Art. 63

There shall be a Vice President of India.

Art. 64

🔥 VP = Ex-Officio Chairman of Rajya Sabha

  • Automatically becomes Chairman upon becoming VP
  • No separate election for Chairman of Rajya Sabha
  • Shall not hold any office of profit
Art. 65

VP Acting as President

  • Casual Vacancy = President's seat permanently vacant (death, removal, resignation)
  • Absence = President temporarily unavailable (illness etc.)
  • In BOTH cases, VP discharges presidential functions
Art. 66

🔥 VP Election

  • Electoral College: ALL MPs (elected + nominated BOTH)
  • No MLAs participate (unlike President's election)
  • Same people who impeach President elect the VP
  • Proportional Representation + Single Transferable Vote
Art. 67

🔥 VP Term + Removal

  • Term: 5 years
  • Resignation: given to President
  • Removal resolution can START ONLY in Rajya Sabha
  • 14-day notice — to President or Deputy Chairman (NOT VP himself)
  • Rajya Sabha: Effective majority needed
  • Then goes to Lok Sabha → Simple majority to agree
  • Ground for removal: NO specific ground mentioned — any ground works
  • Re-election: no limit, any number of times
Effective Majority = Total Seats ÷ 2 + 1 (excluding vacant seats)
Simple Majority = Members Present & Voting ÷ 2 + 1
Art. 68

VP vacancy due to death/resignation/removal → election within 60 days.

Art. 69

VP's oath administered by President (reverse: President's oath by CJI)

Art. 70

If VP also can't discharge presidential duties in other contingencies → Parliament may make provisions.

Art. 71

🔥 Any dispute relating to election of President or VP → Supreme Court has exclusive jurisdiction. No other court can hear it.

⚠️ Exam Traps

What most students get wrong — every single exam

🪤 TRAP 1: Nominated vs Elected in Electoral College

President's ELECTION (Art. 54): Only ELECTED MPs + MLAs. Nominated OUT.

President's IMPEACHMENT (Art. 61): ALL MPs = Elected + Nominated BOTH. MLAs OUT.

VP's ELECTION (Art. 66): ALL MPs = Elected + Nominated BOTH. MLAs OUT.

👉 Memory: "To elect the President, you need people reps (MLAs). To remove/replace him at Parliament level — only Parliament members matter."

🪤 TRAP 2: Resignation — To WHOM?

President resigns → to Vice President (Art. 56)

Vice President resigns → to President (Art. 67)

They resign to EACH OTHER. Don't mix them up!

🪤 TRAP 3: VP Removal — Where Does Resolution START?

President's removal → EITHER house (Lok Sabha OR Rajya Sabha)

VP's removal → resolution can START ONLY in Rajya Sabha

Logic: VP is Chairman of RS. That's where charges against him must originate. But LS still needs to agree (simple majority).

🪤 TRAP 4: Majority Type Confusion

President impeachment → Special majority (2/3 total membership) in BOTH houses

VP removal from RS → Effective majority

VP removal from LS → Simple majority

Simple ≠ Effective ≠ Special ≠ Absolute — Learn all four!

🪤 TRAP 5: Qualification — Lok Sabha vs Rajya Sabha

Qualify for President → Must be Lok Sabha eligible (House of People) [Art. 58]

Qualify for VP → Must be Rajya Sabha eligible (Council of States) [Art. 66]

Age for Lok Sabha = 25; Age for Rajya Sabha = 30. But both President AND VP must be 35 years old to hold office!

🪤 TRAP 6: Vacancy Timelines

President vacancy → fill within 6 months

VP vacancy → fill within 60 days

Don't swap these! VP is filled FASTER (60 days) than President (6 months).

🪤 TRAP 7: Pardoning — Commutation vs Remission vs Respite

Commutation = FORM of punishment changes (death → life imprisonment)

Remission = DURATION reduces (20 yrs → 10 yrs), no form change

Respite = DURATION reduces BUT specifically for HEALTH reasons

Reprieve = Only TEMPORARY hold — punishment not removed permanently

🪤 TRAP 8: Oath Administrator

President's oath → by CJI

VP's oath → by President

They are NOT the same! Most students assume CJI for both.

🪤 TRAP 9: "Defined Nowhere in Constitution"

"Violation of Constitution" (ground for Prez impeachment) → NOT defined

"Office of Profit" → NOT defined in Constitution

Both are deliberately left undefined. If asked — answer: NOT defined.

🪤 TRAP 10: Re-election Limit

India President → NO LIMIT on re-elections

USA President → MAX 2 terms

If asked "can Indian President be elected for third term?" → YES (there is no limit)

🪤 TRAP 11: Notice for Removal

President's impeachment → 14-day notice to Presiding Officer (Speaker/Chairman)

VP's removal → 14-day notice to President OR Deputy Chairman (NOT to VP)

Recent real example: Resolution to remove Jagdeep Dhankhar was rejected because 14-day notice wasn't properly given.

❓ Click-to-Reveal Questions

Which article says the President is Supreme Commander of Defence Forces?
Hint: Same article that vests executive power in President
Art. 53 — Executive power of Union vested in President + Supreme Command of Defence Forces
What is the difference between Reprieve and Respite?
Reprieve = Temporary STAY of execution (not permanent). Respite = Reduced punishment specifically on HEALTH grounds (pregnancy, old age, illness).
Why is Presidential impeachment called a "Quasi-Judicial" process?
Because the second house INVESTIGATES the charges put by the first house — this is judicial work. But it is NOT done by the judiciary — Parliament does it. Hence half-judicial = Quasi-Judicial.
Fill in the blank: The nomination for Presidential candidate requires ___ proposers and ___ seconders.
50 proposers + 50 seconders = 100 people total needed just to file nomination!
Which country's constitution did India borrow the Impeachment process from?
United States of America (USA)
If President's salary is ₹5,00,000 per month and Parliament reduces it to ₹4,50,000 — is this constitutional?
NO — Art. 59 says allowances of President CANNOT be diminished (reduced) during tenure. The salary is charged from Consolidated Fund of India and is non-votable.
Reverse Question: "Proportional Representation + Single Transferable Vote + Secret Ballot" describes which article?
Art. 55 — Manner of election of President. Also applies to VP election (Art. 66).
Who administers oath to the President if CJI is absent?
The senior-most judge of the Supreme Court (Art. 60)
Can a sitting Rajya Sabha member become President without first resigning?
NO. Art. 59 says President shall not be a member of any house. If elected as President while being RS member → must resign from RS immediately.
