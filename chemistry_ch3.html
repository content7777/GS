Big Picture
What is this chapter really about — and why does it matter for SSC CGL?
The Core Idea

The periodic table is humanity's master organizer of all matter. Scientists kept finding elements that behaved similarly, and they slowly realized: order by atomic mass → pattern; order by atomic number → perfection. Every exam question on this chapter is really testing whether you know who said what, when, with what limitation.

What SSC Asks
  • → Who gave which law and in which year
  • → Limitations of each early table
  • → Names of element groups (halogens, etc.)
  • → Direction of periodic trends
  • → Highest/lowest values of each property
  • → Important element atomic numbers
Chapter at a Glance
  • 1 History: Döbereiner → Newlands → Mendeleev → Moseley
  • 2 Modern table: 118 elements, 18 groups, 7 periods
  • 3 Blocks: S, P, D, F
  • 4 Trends: size, metallic character, IE, EN
  • 5 Valency, atomicity, isoelectronic
🧠 Golden Rule for This Chapter
"Top → Bottom = Size ↑ IE ↓ EN ↓ | Left → Right = Opposite of Top→Bottom"

If you remember ONLY one thing from this chapter, let it be this. All trend questions collapse into this single rule.

Story Setup

Imagine you enter a grand Science Museum. There are 5 rooms inside. Each room has scientists, displays, and charts. As you walk room to room, you recall the entire chapter.

🚪
Room 1 — Hall of Three Triads (1817)
You see Döbereiner standing with three identical triplets. He's weighing them — the middle one always weighs exactly the average of the outer two. Three groups of triplets play around: Li-Na-K, Ca-Sr-Ba, Cl-Br-I. But the hall is small — only 9 elements fit. He shrugs: "That's all I can accommodate."
🎵
Room 2 — Musical Octave Room (1865)
Newlands plays piano. Sa Re Ga Ma Pa Dha Ni Sa — 7 notes, then the 8th repeats. He lines elements by atomic mass; every 8th element resembles the 1st. But iron sits next to oxygen (a non-metal!) and cobalt+nickel share one seat. Piano breaks after calcium — only 56 elements, from H to Th.
📋
Room 3 — Mendeleev's Workshop (1869)
Mendeleev (from Russia 🇷🇺) has 63 cards on a giant board — 8 columns, 7 rows. He leaves gaps deliberately and writes predictions: "Eka-Boron → Scandium (44), Eka-Silicon → Germanium (72), Eka-Aluminium → Gallium." His predictions come TRUE years later.
☢️
Room 4 — Moseley's X-Ray Lab (1913)
Henry Moseley fires X-rays at metals and reads their atomic numbers like a barcode scanner. He announces: "It's atomic NUMBER, not atomic mass that determines chemical properties!" 18 groups, 7 periods, 118 elements (94 natural). Modern periodic table born.
📐
Room 5 — Trend Observatory
A giant see-saw: as you go DOWN the group, size and metallic character go UP; IE, EN, EGA go DOWN. From LEFT to RIGHT it's the exact opposite. Fluorine flag at top-right: highest EN. Chlorine badge: highest EGA. Francium at bottom-left: biggest, most metallic.

Historical Development of the Periodic Table
From Döbereiner's triads to the modern table — every scientist, every year, every limitation.
1817 / 1829
Döbereiner — Law of Triads

Arranged elements by increasing atomic mass. Found that in groups of three (triads), the atomic mass of the middle element ≈ average of 1st and 3rd.

Triads to Remember
Li – Na – K (alkali metals)
Ca – Sr – Ba (alkaline earth)
Cl – Br – I (halogens)
⚠ Limitation
Worked only for a few elements — not universal. Could not accommodate all known elements.
1865
John Alexander Reina Newlands — Law of Octaves

Arranged elements by increasing atomic mass. Discovered: the 1st and 8th element show similar properties — just like musical octaves (Do Re Mi Fa Sol La Ti Do).

Musical Notes → Element Pairs
NotePositionExample
Do (Sa)1H, F, Cl...
Re2Li, Na...
Mi3Be, Mg...
Fa4B, Al...
Sol (So)5C, Si...
La6N, P...
Ti7O, S...
⚠ Limitations of Newlands
  • 1 Fe was placed with O & S (a metal with non-metals — totally wrong)
  • 2 Co and Ni shared one slot — impossible for two elements
  • 3 Law worked only till Calcium — failed beyond that
  • 4 Total elements covered: 56 (H to Th)
1864
Lothar Meyer — Atomic Volume vs Mass Graph

Published 1864. Plotted Atomic Volume vs Atomic Mass. First to show periodic trends — how properties change with mass. Precursor to Mendeleev's table.

1869
Dmitri Mendeleev — Father of Periodic Table 🇷🇺

Arranged 63 elements by increasing atomic mass → 8 groups, 7 periods. Left gaps and predicted properties of undiscovered elements.

🏆 Mendeleev's Genius
  • ★ Left gaps intentionally for undiscovered elements
  • ★ Predicted mass, density, oxide formula
  • ★ Predictions came TRUE:
PredictionActual ElementMass
Eka-BoronScandium (Sc)44
Eka-SiliconGermanium (Ge)72
Eka-AluminiumGallium (Ga)~68
❌ Mendeleev's Defects
  • ✗ Couldn't fix position of Hydrogen
  • ✗ Used atomic mass (wrong basis) — should be atomic number
  • ✗ Lanthanides & Actinides had no place
  • ✗ Isotopes couldn't be accommodated
1913
Henry Moseley — Modern Periodic Law

Used X-rays (discovered by Wilhelm Röntgen) to find atomic numbers. Declared: "Chemical properties depend on atomic number, NOT atomic mass."

🧠 Timeline Mnemonic

Periodic Trends
The most tested section. Master the direction of every trend.
The Master Rule (Memorize This First!)
DOWN THE GROUP ↓
Size ↑ Metallic ↑
IE ↓ EN ↓ EGA ↓
ACROSS PERIOD →
Size ↓ Metallic ↓
IE ↑ EN ↑ EGA ↑
Atomic Size
  • → Each new period = new shell added → size increases DOWN the group
  • → Li: 2 shells (K,L); Na: 3 (K,L,M); K: 4 (K,L,M,N)
  • → Going right in a period: same shells, more protons pulling electrons inward → size DECREASES
Ionization Energy (IE)

Definition: Energy required to REMOVE the outermost electron from a gaseous atom.

  • → Bigger atom = electron farther from nucleus = less pull = LESS energy needed to remove
  • → IE decreases DOWN the group (bigger atoms = easier to ionize)
  • → IE increases LEFT to RIGHT (smaller atoms = tighter hold)
  • ★ Highest IE: He (noble gas, fully stable)
  • ★ Lowest IE: Francium (largest atom)
Electronegativity (EN)

Definition: Tendency of an atom to attract the SHARED pair of electrons in a bond.

  • → Metals give electrons → low EN; Non-metals grab electrons → high EN
  • → EN decreases DOWN the group (metals become more metallic = less greedy)
  • → EN increases LEFT to RIGHT
  • ★ HIGHEST EN: Fluorine (F) — most electronegative element
Electron Gain Affinity/Enthalpy (EGA)

Definition: Energy RELEASED when an electron is ADDED to a neutral atom.

  • → Similar trend to EN: decreases down group, increases left to right
  • ★ HIGHEST EGA: Chlorine (Cl) — NOT Fluorine!
⚠ CRITICAL DISTINCTION
Highest EN = Fluorine ≠ Highest EGA = Chlorine
Why? Fluorine is so small its electron cloud is already crowded — adding another electron causes more repulsion. Cl has more room.
Diagonal Relationship

Going DOWN increases a property; going RIGHT decreases it. The diagonal movement cancels these effects → diagonal elements show SIMILAR properties.

Diagonal Pairs
Li ↔ Mg  |  Be ↔ Al  |  B ↔ Si

Valency, Octet Rule & Atomicity
Combining capacity, stability rules, and how many atoms make a molecule.
Octet Rule vs Duet Rule

Octet Rule

All atoms want 8 electrons in their valence shell for stability. This applies to all elements EXCEPT H and He.

Duet Rule

Only for H and He: they achieve stability with just 2 electrons (K shell only).

How to Find Valency
Valence ElectronsActionValencyExample
1Lose 1+1Na, K (Group 1)
2Lose 2+2Mg, Ca (Group 2)
3Lose 3+3Al (Group 13)
4Share (exception)4C, Si (Group 14)
5Gain 3 (8−5)−3N, P (Group 15)
6Gain 2 (8−6)−2O, S (Group 16)
7Gain 1 (8−7)−1F, Cl (Group 17)
8Already complete0He, Ne (Group 18)
Formula
If valence e⁻ ≥ 5: Valency = 8 − (valence electrons)

⚠️ Exam Traps
Common confusions, similar facts, and what students always get wrong.
Trap 1 — Highest EN vs Highest EGA

❌ Students write: "Fluorine has highest electron gain affinity"
✅ Correct: Fluorine = Highest Electronegativity | Chlorine = Highest EGA

Trap 2 — Döbereiner's Year

Both 1817 and 1829 appear in sources. Remember BOTH.

Trap 3 — Mendeleev vs Lothar Meyer

Lothar Meyer (1864) plotted Atomic Volume vs Mass — periodic trend graph.
Mendeleev (1869) made the actual table with 63 elements, 8 groups, 7 periods.
⚠ Don't confuse their years or contributions.

Trap 4 — Atomic Mass vs Atomic Number

Early tables used Atomic Mass (Döbereiner, Newlands, Mendeleev).
Modern law uses Atomic Number (Moseley, 1913).
⚠ If a question says "Mendeleev used atomic number" — it's FALSE.

Trap 5 — Newland's Numbers

Total elements in Newland's table: 56, from H to Th (Thorium).
Law valid only till: Calcium.
⚠ Don't mix these two numbers.

Trap 6 — Xenon's Nickname

Xenon is called "Stranger Gas" — not "Strange element" or anything else.

Trap 7 — Technetium

First artificially produced element = Technetium (Tc), atomic number 43.

Trap 8 — Noble Gas Group

Noble gases are Group 18 but also called Group 0. Both names are valid.

Trap 9 — Francium's Half-Life

Half-life of Francium = 22 minutes. Not 22 hours. Not 22 days.

Trap 10 — Sulphur vs Phosphorus Atomicity

Phosphorus = P₄ (atomicity 4) | Sulphur = S₈ (atomicity 8)
⚠ Students often swap these.

Trap 11 — Eka Elements

"Eka" is a Sanskrit word meaning "one beyond". Mendeleev's predictions:
Eka-Boron → Scandium | Eka-Silicon → Germanium | Eka-Aluminium → Gallium

Trap 12 — Highest Density Metal

Osmium (Os), atomic number 76, Group 8 → Highest density among all elements.

THE STORY IN 5 LINES

Döbereiner grouped 3 elements in triads (1817). Newlands matched notes to elements — 1st and 8th alike (1865, only till Ca). Mendeleev predicted 63 elements in 8×7 table, left gaps, and was proven right by Sc/Ge/Ga (1869). Moseley used X-rays to prove it's atomic number not mass (1913). Today we have 18 groups, 7 periods, 118 elements (94 natural).

THE 6 FACTS EXAMINERS LOVE

1. Highest EN = F | Highest EGA = Cl
2. Down the group: Size↑ Metallic↑ IE↓ EN↓
3. Technetium (Z=43) = 1st artificial element
4. Xenon = Stranger Gas
5. Francium = most metallic; half-life = 22 min
6. Sulphur = S₈ (8 atoms) | Phosphorus = P₄ (4 atoms)

WHAT NOT TO CONFUSE TOMORROW

❌ "Mendeleev used atomic number" → He used atomic MASS
❌ "Fluorine has highest EGA" → It's CHLORINE
❌ "Eka is Latin" → It's SANSKRIT
❌ "Noble gases are diatomic" → They're MONOATOMIC
