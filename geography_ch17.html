💎 Non-Metallic Minerals — Mica, Limestone, Diamond
Mica 🔥

Used in electrical industry — excellent dielectric strength, low power loss. Found at Northern edge of Chota Nagpur Plateau.

Ranking: Andhra Pradesh (1) → Rajasthan (2) → Bihar (3) → Jharkhand (4)

🛢 Petroleum & Natural Gas
Key Concept
Petroleum found in fault traps between porous and non-porous rocks. Gas (lighter) always floats above the oil. So wherever oil = gas on top.
Petroleum Locations
  • Mumbai High, Gujarat, Assam — main production
  • Digboi, Assam (1901) — Asia's oldest oil refinery still in operation
  • Also in Assam: Canakti, Moran-Hugrijan
  • Andhra Pradesh: Tatipaka
  • Gujarat: Ankleshwar, Khambat, Kalol
Natural Gas
  • Main component: Methane (70–90%)
  • Methane also called Marsh Gas
  • Released from: paddy fields, wells (anaerobic decomposition)
  • Found: Mumbai High, Cambay Basin, Krishna-Godavari Belt
  • HVJ Pipeline (1986) — 1700 km — Hazira (Gujarat) → Vijaypur (MP) → Jagdishpur (UP)
  • Built under GAIL — Gas Authority of India Limited
⚛ Nuclear Energy & Reactors
Nuclear Reactor Components 🔥
ComponentFunctionMaterial Used

⚠ Chernobyl disaster (1986) — Ukraine (then USSR) — control rod insertion worsened fission reaction instead of stopping it.

🌱 Non-Conventional Energy Sources
Tidal Energy

Best where coast is funnel-shaped. In India:

  • Gulf of Khambhat (Cambay)
  • Gulf of Kutch — both in Gujarat, funnel-shaped
Geothermal Energy

Heat from earth's interior heats groundwater (aquifer) → hot springs → energy.

  • India: Parvati Valley, Manikaran — Himachal Pradesh
  • India: Puga Valley — Ladakh
  • World's most famous: Yellowstone National Park, USA (geyser)
Wind Energy

India's largest wind farm cluster: Tamil Nadu — between Nagarkoil and Madurai.

Biogas

Gas from farm waste, animal dung, crop stubble = Biogas / Cow-dung gas. Mainly used at local/rural level.

⚡ Energy Classification — Conventional vs Non-Conventional
Conventional ≈ Non-Renewable
  • Coal, Oil, Natural Gas (fossil fuels)
  • Commercial + Non-commercial sources
  • Used for a very long time
  • Mostly polluting
Non-Conventional ≈ Mostly Renewable
  • Solar, Wind, Tidal, Geothermal, Biogas
  • Nuclear — Non-conventional but NON-RENEWABLE ⚠
  • Mostly non-polluting
  • Emerged prominently in recent times
⚠ COMMON EXAM CONFUSION: Nuclear energy is classified as NON-CONVENTIONAL but it is also NON-RENEWABLE. Most non-conventional sources ARE renewable — but nuclear is the exception.

Concept Connections

Mental Network Building
The Ore → Metal Chain
Ore extracted → Metal + Gang (impurity) → Metallurgical process (refining) → Pure Metal

Gang = impurity in ore. Anthracite = Coking Coal = used in metallurgy. This is why it's called "metallic coal".

Limestone → Karst Connection
CaCO₃ Limestone → Porous + Soft → Water percolates → Karst Topography

Erosional: Sinkhole, Doline, Boyle, Lapies | Depositional: Stalactite (top), Stalagmite (bottom), Pillar

Age → Coal Quality

Gondwana (200M yr) = BETTER than Tertiary (55M yr). Older = Higher carbon = Better quality. Like aged wine → older is richer.

Nuclear Fission Chain
⁹²U²³⁵ + Neutron → Barium + Krypton → 3 Neutrons released → Hit more U²³⁵ → Chain Reaction

Moderator slows neutrons → Control rods absorb excess → Coolant removes heat → Steam → Electricity

Petroleum + Gas Geometry

Petroleum found in fault traps between porous + non-porous rock layers. Gas (lighter) always sits above oil. So drilling for oil = also finding gas.

Copper Age → Chalcolithic

Copper = first metal discovered by humans. This gives us the Chalcolithic Age in history. Connect minerals chapter to history chapter here.

Exam Traps

What Students Usually Get Wrong
Trap 1 — Reserves ≠ Production
Iron Ore: Max Reserves = Odisha | Max Production = Odisha (both same here ✓)
Coal: Max Reserves = Odisha | Max Production = Chhattisgarh (DIFFERENT ⚠)
Gold: Max Reserves = Bihar (shocking!) | Main mines = Karnataka
Trap 2 — Nuclear = Non-Conventional BUT Non-Renewable
Most students think Non-Conventional = Renewable. Nuclear energy is NON-CONVENTIONAL but also NON-RENEWABLE. The question will say "which non-conventional source is non-renewable?" → Answer: Nuclear.
Trap 3 — Bauxite ≠ Aluminium
Bauxite is the ore. Aluminium is the metal. Q: "Primary ore of aluminium?" → Bauxite. Q: "What is bauxite used for?" → Making aluminium.
Trap 4 — Diamond only MP (but not the "only" state anymore)
SSC asked "which state is the only producer of diamond?" with answer Madhya Pradesh. But technically, diamond traces exist elsewhere. Follow what SSC's source says — MP is the answer for exam purposes.
Trap 5 — Manganese is Ferrous (has iron content)
Manganese sounds like it should be non-ferrous. But ferromanganese alloy has iron content → so Manganese goes under Ferrous category. Don't put it in Non-Ferrous.
Trap 6 — Stalactite vs Stalagmite
Stalactite hangs from Ceiling → C-tite. Stalagmite rises from Ground → G-mite. "Stalactite holds tight to the ceiling."
Trap 7 — Digboi: Asia's oldest, not India's
Digboi (1901, Assam) is described as Asia's oldest oil refinery still in operation — not just India's. The question will say "oldest oil refinery in Asia" → Digboi.
Trap 8 — Lignite = Brown Coal (high moisture)
Lignite has HIGH moisture content and LOW calorific value. Main Indian source: Neyveli, Tamil Nadu. Brown coal = Lignite. Don't confuse with Bituminous.
Trap 9 — Gondwana Coal Age
Gondwana = 200 million years. Tertiary = 55 million years. Not 100 million or 250 million. Exact numbers can appear in questions.
Trap 10 — CaCO₃ appears as Limestone AND Marble AND Chalk
All three are CaCO₃ = calcium carbonate. SSC may ask which ones are CaCO₃ in MCQ options — select all three: Limestone ✓ Marble ✓ Chalk ✓
