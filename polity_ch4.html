🌐 Big Picture

Fundamental Rights are India's constitutional guarantee that the state cannot trample on its citizens. They are the shield citizens carry against government overreach. Without them, democracy is just a word. Without Article 32, they are just words on paper. The entire chapter is a tension between freedom and reasonable restriction — a balance every question exploits.

SOURCE
Borrowed From USA
Fundamental Rights taken from US Constitution. Magna Carta (UK) inspired the concept but FR are NOT borrowed from UK.
NATURE
Qualified, Not Absolute
FR have reasonable restrictions. They are not sacrosanct — can be amended/deleted. Originally 7, now 6.
JUSTICIABLE
Enforceable in Court
FR are justiciable (vad-yoga) — you can go to court if violated. DPSP are non-justiciable.
PURPOSE
Political Democracy
FR seek to establish political democracy. DPSP seek to establish economic democracy.

📖 Complete Revision Notes

— ART. 12 & 13: GATEWAY ARTICLES —

ART. 12 🔥 Must Know
Definition of STATE
STATE = Central Govt + State Govts + Parliament + State Legislatures + Local Bodies (Panchayat, Municipality) + All bodies working under Govt (LIC, ONGC etc.).

NOT STATE: BCCI (SC ruling), Zee Media (SC ruling).

Exam tip: If "All of the above" is an option in a State-definition question — pick it. 99% correct.
ART. 13 🔥 Must Know
Judicial Review + Void Laws
Any law inconsistent with Fundamental Rights → NULL & VOID.
This is Judicial Review — borrowed from USA.

Parliament/State Legislature makes laws → If violates FR → Judiciary can strike it down.

Also borrowed from USA: Judicial Independence.

— RIGHT 1: RIGHT TO EQUALITY (ART. 14–18) —

ART. 14 🔥
Equality Before Law + Equal Protection
Equality before law (from UK): Rich or poor — same law applies to all.

Equal protection of law (from USA): Like should be treated alike. Ambani ≠ handcart vendor; can't demand same treatment. But all Ambanis treated equally, all vendors treated equally.

Exceptions: President & Governor — no criminal proceedings while in office. MLAs/MPs — no civil proceedings without 2-month notice.
ART. 15 🔥
No Discrimination (5 grounds)
State shall NOT discriminate on: Religion, Race, Caste, Sex, Place of birth.

Clause 2: Public places (shops, restaurants, cinema halls) — no discrimination, even by private persons.
Clause 3: Special provisions for Women & Children ✅ (not violation).
Clause 4: Special provisions for Backward classes ✅.

Champakam Durai Rajan Case 1950 → SC said caste-based reservation violated Art.15 → Led to Clause 4 being added.
ART. 16 ⭐
Equality in Public Employment
No discrimination in govt jobs on: Religion, Race, Caste, Sex, Place of birth, Descent (note: Descent added here, not in Art. 15).

Clause 3: Parliament can set residence as eligibility.
Clause 4: Reservation for backward classes.
Clause 5: Reservation for SC/ST including in promotions.

1st Backward Class Commission: Kaka Kalelkar | 2nd = Mandal Commission: B.P. Mandal — 27% OBC reservation.
Indira Sawhney Case (Mandal Case): SC said reservation cannot exceed 50%. EWS 10% struck down (later restored by 103rd Amendment).
ART. 17 🔥
Abolition of Untouchability
Untouchability is abolished. Practice of untouchability is a punishable offence.
ART. 18 ⭐
Abolition of Titles
State CANNOT confer any title.
Exceptions: Military distinctions, Academic honours (meritocracy).

Indian accepting foreign title → needs President's permission.
Foreigner living in India + foreign title → also needs President's permission.

— RIGHT 2: RIGHT TO FREEDOM (ART. 19–22) —

ART. 19 CLAUSE 1 — THE SAMRO TRICK (Sub-clauses A,B,C,D,E,G — no F!)

S
Sub-clause A — Speech & Expression (includes media, RTI, memes, cartoons)
A
Sub-clause B — Assemble Peacefully Without Arms
M
Sub-clause C — form associations and unions (Movement — Association)
R
Sub-clause D — Move freely throughout India
O
Sub-clause E — Reside & settle anywhere in India
🚫F
Sub-clause F DELETED by 44th Amendment 1978 (Right to Property removed)
G
Sub-clause G — Occupation/Profession/Trade/Business
Art. 19 Clause 2 — Reasonable Restrictions 🔥

On Speech: no defamation, no threats to sovereignty, no breach of public order/morality. Example: India's Got Latent controversy = breach of morality. Rahul Gandhi "all Modis are thieves" = defamation case. Gaurav Taneja gathering outside metro = disturbed public order.

ART. 20 🔥
Protection Against Conviction
3 Protections:
1. No ex-post facto law — Punished only under law existing at time of crime. (Nirbhaya minor: 2012 law = juvenile home, can't apply 2016 law retroactively).
2. No double jeopardy — Can't be punished twice for same crime. (Doesn't mean you can murder again!)
3. No self-incrimination — Can't be forced to testify against yourself. But: blood sample, thumbprint, handwriting CAN be taken (these don't change under pressure).
ART. 21 🔥🔥
Right to Life & Personal Liberty
"No person shall be deprived of his life or personal liberty except according to procedure established by law" — from Japan's Constitution.

Most implicit right — hides dozens of rights:
Right to health, right to sleep, right against delayed justice, right against public hanging, right to travel abroad, right to pollution-free environment, right to privacy (KS Puttaswamy/Aadhaar case 2016).

AK Gopalan Case: Narrow view — just follow procedure.
ADM Jabalpur vs Shivkant Shukla: Same narrow view.
Maneka Gandhi Case 1978: SC said we'll also check due process of law (USA concept) — is the law fair/just?
ART. 21A ⭐
Right to Education
Free & compulsory education for children 6–14 years.
Added by 86th Constitutional Amendment 2002. Not originally in constitution.
ART. 22 ⭐
Protection Against Arrest
Two types: Punitive (after crime) & Preventive (before crime, suspicion).

Common rights in both:
① Must be told reason for arrest.
② Right to legal practitioner of choice.

Punitive only: Presented before magistrate within 24 hours.

Preventive only: Detention NOT more than 3 months unless High Court advisory board approves extension.

— RIGHT 3: RIGHT AGAINST EXPLOITATION (ART. 23–24) —

ART. 23 ⭐
Prohibition of Human Trafficking & Forced Labour
Forced labour (work without pay) = prohibited.

Exception: Compulsory military service during attack/emergency — state can call people; cannot refuse.
ART. 24 ⭐
Prohibition of Child Labour
No child in hazardous work (mines, factories, etc.).

Children 16–18 years CAN work in non-hazardous work (grocery shop, sewing buttons, etc.).

— RIGHT 4: RIGHT TO RELIGION (ART. 25–28) —

ART. 25 ⭐
Freedom of Conscience & Religion (Individual)
You can profess, practice, propagate any religion. Light incense, ring bells, organize kirtans — all fine.

NO right to FORCEFUL conversion.
ART. 26 ➕
Manage Religious Affairs (Group)
Religious groups can manage their own affairs. E.g., Akshardham temple = no jeans rule. Their space, their rules.
ART. 27 ⭐
No Tax for One Religion
State CANNOT levy tax to promote any ONE religion. Tax money must promote ALL religions equally. → India follows positive secularism (state can interfere in religion, unlike USA's strict separation).
ART. 28 ➕
Religious Instruction in Schools
Fully govt-funded: NO religious instruction at all.
State-administered (partial aid): Religious instruction on voluntary basis.
Private trust-built (own funds): Can set their own religious rules.

— RIGHT 5: CULTURAL & EDUCATIONAL RIGHTS (ART. 29–30) —

ART. 29 ⭐
Protection of Interests of Any Section
Any section of citizens (not just minorities!) with distinct language/script/culture → right to protect it.

Clause 2: No one can be denied admission in state-funded educational institution on grounds of religion, race, caste, sex or language.
ART. 30 ⭐
Minorities' Right to Establish Schools
Only for minorities (religious or linguistic).
They can establish AND administer their own educational institutions.

Note: The word "minority" is NOT defined anywhere in the Constitution!

— RIGHT 6: RIGHT TO CONSTITUTIONAL REMEDIES (ART. 32) —

Art. 32 — The Heart & Soul of the Constitution 🔥🔥🔥

Called the "Heart and Soul" by Dr. Ambedkar. You can DIRECTLY approach the Supreme Court for FR violation. SC has original jurisdiction — cannot REFUSE to issue a writ when FR is violated. This is itself a Fundamental Right.

Art. 226: High Court writs — NOT a fundamental right, so HC can refuse. BUT HC has WIDER jurisdiction: can issue writs for both FR AND legal right violations (e.g., right to property). SC only does FR.

Habeas Corpus
"To have the body"
Used when someone is unlawfully detained/arrested. Bring the person before the court.
Mandamus
"We command"
When a govt body is NOT doing its compulsory duty. Can't be issued against President/Governor or private individuals.
Prohibition
"To prohibit"
Superior court → Inferior court: "Stop going beyond your limits." Issued when case is ONGOING.
Certiorari
"To be certified"
Superior court → Inferior court: quash the order & transfer the case. Issued AFTER a decision is made.
Quo Warranto
"By what authority?"
Person holding public office WITHOUT eligibility. Court asks: by what right are you here?

— ART. 33–35: SPECIAL PROVISIONS —

ART. 33
Rights of Armed Forces
Only Parliament can restrict FR of armed forces/CAPF. Example: BSF jawan complained about food → dismissed (can't use Art. 19 freedom).
ART. 34
Martial Law
When martial law imposed (e.g., AFSPA in Northeast), FR can be suspended. Armed forces get protection for acts done during martial law.
ART. 35
Parliament's Exclusive Power
Only Parliament (not state legislatures) can make laws for certain FR. State legislatures have NO power here.
🌍 Rights Only for Citizens (NOT Foreigners) — 15, 16, 19, 29, 30 🔥

Remember: 15-16-19-29-30 = ONLY Indians. All others (14, 17, 18, 20, 21, 22, 23, 24, 25...) = available to foreigners too.

Quick check: "Is right to public employment available to foreigners?" → Art. 16 → In the list 15,16,19,29,30 → NO, only Indians.

🔗 Smart Memory Hooks
HOOK 1 — WHY 6 NOT 7?
Picture 7 kids in a classroom. One day, Right to Property (the richest kid) gets expelled by the 44th Amendment 1978 (Morarji Desai as PM). He moves to Article 300A's house — now just a "legal right," not a fundamental right. 6 kids remain.
HOOK 2 — SAMRO TRICK FOR ART. 19
Imagine shouting "SAMRO KARKE!" while exercising your rights: Speech, Assemble, Move (Association), Reside, Occupation. Sub-clause F is missing from your SAMRO sequence — it was kicked out in 1978!
HOOK 3 — AMBANI vs. VENDOR (Art. 14)
Ambani demands same subsidy as handcart vendor = ABSURD. That's why Equal protection ≠ same treatment. Likes must be treated alike. Ambani can't be compared with vendor.
HOOK 4 — MANEKA GANDHI CASE (Art. 21)
Maneka Gandhi = Gandhi family + passport confiscated. She fought for her right to travel abroad. SC in 1978 said: we won't just check IF a procedure exists — we'll check if it's fair, just, reasonable (due process). That's how Art. 21 expanded from "one right" to "100 rights."
HOOK 5 — AMBEDKAR'S "HEART & SOUL"
Without Art. 32, your rights are like ₹1 lakh you can never spend. Art. 32 is the ATM card. Ambedkar = the bank manager who made sure the ATM works. That's why he called it the "heart and soul."
HOOK 6 — PROHIBITION vs. CERTIORARI
Think of a match being played: Prohibition = referee blows whistle DURING the game (case ongoing). Certiorari = referee REVIEWS the recorded footage AFTER the game and overturns the decision.
HOOK 7 — NIRBHAYA CASE for Art. 20
The juvenile in Nirbhaya case (2012) escaped death penalty because the law at that time (2012) didn't allow it for minors. Even though 2016 amendment changed this, you CAN'T apply it retroactively. That's no ex-post facto law.

⚠️ Exam Traps
⚠️ EXAM TRAPS — What Students Get Wrong
TRAP 1: FR borrowed from USA, NOT UK. (Magna Carta is UK but FR source = USA Constitution.)
TRAP 2: Originally 7 FRs, now 6. Right to Property removed by 44th Amendment 1978, PM was Morarji Desai, now in Art. 300A as legal right.
TRAP 3: Art. 12 State includes LIC, ONGC, Panchayats. But BCCI and Zee Media are NOT state.
TRAP 4: Art. 14 — Equality before law from UK, Equal protection of law from USA. These are two different things!
TRAP 5: Art. 15 has 5 grounds. Art. 16 has 6 grounds (adds Descent). Don't confuse!
TRAP 6: 1st Backward Class Commission = Kaka Kalelkar. Mandal Commission = B.P. Mandal. Don't mix the chairmen!
TRAP 7: Art. 19 Sub-clause F is deleted. The sequence goes A,B,C,D,E,G. No F!
TRAP 8: Art. 21's "procedure established by law" taken from Japan (NOT USA). Due process of law concept = USA, but NOT written in Indian constitution.
TRAP 9: Prohibition writ = case still ONGOING. Certiorari = after DECISION is given. Students mix these two up constantly!
TRAP 10: Art. 32 (SC writs) = Fundamental Right → SC CANNOT refuse. Art. 226 (HC writs) = NOT fundamental right → HC CAN refuse. But HC has WIDER jurisdiction.
TRAP 11: Art. 29 = for ANY section of citizens (not just minorities). Art. 30 = exclusively for minorities (religious/linguistic).
TRAP 12: The word "minority" is NOT defined anywhere in the Constitution. If question asks "is it clearly defined?" → Answer: NO.
TRAP 13: President & Governor → no CRIMINAL proceedings while in office. MLAs/MPs → no CIVIL proceedings without 2-month notice. Don't swap criminal/civil!
TRAP 14: 15, 16, 19, 29, 30 = only for CITIZENS. All others (14, 17, 18, 20, 21, 22, 23, 24, 25...) = foreigners too.
TRAP 15: Art. 21A (Right to Education) = age 6 to 14, added by 86th Amendment 2002. Don't confuse with 6-18 or other age groups.
TRAP 16: EWS 10% reservation was struck down in Indira Sawhney case. Later restored by 103rd Constitutional Amendment. Many students don't know it was struck down first.
TRAP 17: Mandamus cannot be issued against President or Governor, and not against private individuals. Only govt bodies with public duties.
