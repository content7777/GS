Enamel = hardest substance in the HUMAN BODY (not just the tooth). Diamond is harder, but that's external. In body = enamel wins.
🐘Special Tooth Facts — Exam Traps!
🔥 Elephant's Tusk = INCISOR (not canine!)
They stick out from the front, look sharp like canines, but are actually modified incisors. Examiners LOVE this.
⭐ Bird's Beak = Modified Teeth
A bird's beak is a modified form of teeth. Birds don't have actual teeth — the beak evolved from tooth tissue.
➕ Wisdom Teeth
3rd molars (last molars). Erupt between age 16–20. Not everyone gets all 4. Sometimes require removal.
Topic 3 — Genetics & Heredity
🧬 Basics & Mendel
The foundation of how traits pass from parents to children
👨‍🔬Gregor Mendel — Father of Genetics
🔥 Core Fact
Gregor Mendel = Father of Genetics / Father of Heredity. Conducted experiments on Pea plant (Pisum sativum). Austrian monk.
Memory Hook — "R.I.P. Mendel"
Mendel = "Rest In Peas" — he worked on Pea plants his whole life. Whenever you see Mendel, think Pea plant.
🧩Key Vocabulary
TermDefinitionExample
HeredityPassing traits from parents to offspring via DNAYour eye colour from parents
Acquired TraitGained from environment, NOT passed to childrenLearning to speak, abusing, accent
Inherited TraitPassed via DNA from parentsHeight, nose shape, natural hair colour
GeneUnit of heredity on a chromosomeEye colour gene
AlleleAlternate forms of the same geneBrown eyes / Blue eyes = two alleles of eye colour gene
DominantExpressed when present — masks recessiveTall (T) dominates Short (t)
RecessiveOnly expressed when both copies are recessiveShort height (tt)
🎲Gametes & Reproduction
Gametes
Sex cells that carry genetic information. In humans: Sperm (male) and Ovum/Egg (female). Each carries one allele per gene.
F1 and F2 Generations
F = Filial (from Latin, meaning "of son/daughter"). F1 = First generation offspring. F2 = Second generation (offspring of F1 × F1).
Genetics · Laws
⚖️ Mendel's 3 Laws of Inheritance
The pillars of classical genetics — all three are exam-ready.
1️⃣Law of Dominance
🔥 The Law
When parents with contrasting (opposite) traits are crossed, only the dominant trait appears in F1 offspring. The recessive trait is hidden.
Pure Tall (TT) × Pure Short (tt)
↓
F1: All Tall (Tt) — 100% tall, short is hidden
Real Life Example
If your father is "pure tall" (all ancestors tall) and mother is "pure short" — you will be tall. All your siblings will also be tall. Short is dominated.
2️⃣Law of Segregation
🔥 The Law
During formation of gametes (sex cells), the pair of alleles separate from each other. Each gamete gets only one allele.
Tall parent has alleles: T and t
↓ during gamete formation
Some gametes carry T | Other gametes carry t
↓ these combine randomly
F2 offspring: TT, Tt, Tt, tt → Ratio 3:1
3️⃣Law of Independent Assortment
🔥 The Law
Alleles of two different genes separate and combine independently during gamete formation. One gene does NOT influence another.
Example
Gene 1 = Height (Tall/Short). Gene 2 = Colour (Yellow/Green). These mix freely: a plant can be Tall-Yellow or Short-Green or Tall-Green or Short-Yellow. They are independent units.

Genetics · Crosses
🔀 Crosses & Ratios
The numbers examiners love to test
1️⃣Monohybrid Cross (1 trait)
Setup
Cross two parents differing in one trait. e.g. Pure Tall × Pure Short.
GenerationPhenotype RatioGenotype RatioWhat you see
F1All TallAll Tt100% dominant trait
F23 : 1 (Tall : Short)1 : 2 : 1 (TT : Tt : tt)3 tall, 1 short
🔥 Key Numbers: 3:1 and 1:2:1
F2 phenotype = 3:1 | F2 genotype = 1:2:1. The 3:1 is the more commonly asked one.
2️⃣Dihybrid Cross (2 traits)
Setup
Cross parents differing in two traits. e.g. Round Yellow × Wrinkled Green.
GenerationKey RatioWhat it means
F1All Round YellowBoth dominant traits expressed
F29 : 3 : 3 : 1See below
Breaking down 9:3:3:1
9 = Round + Yellow (both dominant)  |  3 = Round + Green (1 dom + 1 rec)  |  3 = Wrinkled + Yellow (1 rec + 1 dom)  |  1 = Wrinkled + Green (both recessive)
Memory: 9=Both dom, 1=Both rec, 3+3=mixed
Think of it as a 4×4 box: 9 in the biggest corner (dominant×dominant), 1 in the opposite corner (recessive×recessive), and 3+3 on the sides (mixed).

Genetics · Evolution
🦅 Homologous & Analogous Organs
Structure vs Function — a classic exam confusion pair
🔬Homologous Organs
Same Structure, Different Function
Organs that have similar anatomy (origin/structure) but perform different functions.
  • Human arm (for holding/manipulation) vs Bird wing (for flying) — same bone structure, different use
  • Shows divergent evolution — one ancestor, different paths
🦇Analogous Organs
Same Function, Different Structure
Organs that perform the same function but have different anatomy/origin.
  • Bat wing vs Bird wing — both used for flying, but structurally different internally
  • Shows convergent evolution — different ancestors, same solution
⚡Quick Memory Trick
HoMologous = saME structure (M in both!)
HoMologous → saMe structure. Analogous → sAme function. The capital letter M in "same" links to hoMologous.
HomologousAnalogous
Structure✅ Same❌ Different
Function❌ Different✅ Same
Evolution typeDivergentConvergent
ExampleHuman arm + Bird wingBat wing + Bird wing

⚠️ Exam Traps & Common Confusions
Everything SSC students get wrong — study these more than the actual content.
⚠️ Stapes vs Femur — Smallest vs Longest
Stapes (ear)
Smallest bone in body
Also called Stirrup
vs
Femur (thigh)
Longest + Strongest bone
Also called thigh bone
If "strongest" and Femur is NOT in options → answer is Jaw/Mandible
⚠️ Elephant's Tusk — Looks like canine, IS an incisor
Tusks are front teeth, stick out like sharp canines → students say "canine". But tusks = modified incisors. The examiner is counting on your confusion here.
⚠️ Homologous vs Analogous
Homologous
Same structure
Different function
vs
Analogous
Different structure
Same function
⚠️ Bat wing vs Bird wing — Same or Different?
Both fly → Same function → ANALOGOUS. Students confuse this with homologous because both are wings. Bat and bird are very different animals — different structure internally.
⚠️ Dental Formula — Adult vs Child
Adult
2-1-2-3 / 2-1-2-3
32 teeth total
vs
Child
2-1-0-2 / 2-1-0-2
20 milk teeth total
Key difference: Children have NO premolars. The "0" in the child formula is frequently asked.
⚠️ 300 vs 206 Bones
Children have 300 bones. Adults have 206. The reduction happens because many bones fuse together as we grow. If asked "how many bones in adult" → 206. Never say 300 for adults.
⚠️ Coccyx (Tailbone) = Vestigial
Coccyx is a vestigial organ — removing it has NO effect on body function. Do not confuse with sacrum (also one bone, but not vestigial).
⚠️ Floating Ribs — Which ones?
Only ribs 11 and 12 are floating (not connected to sternum at all). Ribs 8-10 are "false ribs" but they are still connected to the 7th rib. Floating = completely free in front.
⚠️ Monohybrid F2 Ratios
Phenotype ratio
3 : 1
(visible appearance)
vs
Genotype ratio
1 : 2 : 1
(actual genes)
In 1:2:1 → 1 pure dominant, 2 hybrid dominant, 1 pure recessive.
⚠️ Hyoid Bone — Unique bone
Hyoid is the only bone in the human body not connected to any other bone. It's located below the chin. Frequently asked as a tricky identification question.

🔥 Q1. How many bones does an adult human body have? What about a child?
👆 Click to reveal answer
Adult = 206 | Child = 300 (bones fuse as we grow)
🔥 Q2. What is the smallest bone in the entire human body? What are its other names?
👆 Click to reveal answer
Stapes (ear ossicle) = smallest bone. Also called Stirrup. Part of the 3 ear ossicles: Malleus (Hammer) → Incus (Anvil) → Stapes (Stirrup).
🔥 Q3. Which ribs are called floating ribs and why?
👆 Click to reveal answer
Ribs 11 and 12 = floating ribs. They are NOT connected to the sternum in front — they are only attached to the spine at the back. They literally "float."
🔥 Q4. What connects the skull to the spine?
👆 Click to reveal answer
Atlas bone (1st cervical vertebra). Like the god Atlas holding the world.
🔥 Q5. Give the spinal vertebrae count in order from top to bottom.
👆 Click to reveal answer
Cervical 7 → Thoracic 12 → Lumbar 5 → Sacrum 1 → Coccyx 1 = 26 total. Memory: 7 days, 12 months, 5 fingers.
🔥 Q6. Elephant's tusk — is it an incisor or a canine?
👆 Click to reveal answer
Incisor! Despite looking sharp like a canine, elephant tusks are modified incisors. Classic exam trap.
🔥 Q7. What is the dental formula of an adult? Of a child?
👆 Click to reveal answer
Adult: 2-1-2-3 / 2-1-2-3 (total 32) | Child: 2-1-0-2 / 2-1-0-2 (total 20). Difference: Children have NO premolars (0 in formula).
🔥 Q8. Who is the Father of Genetics? What plant did he work on?
👆 Click to reveal answer
Gregor Mendel = Father of Genetics. Worked on Pea plant (Pisum sativum). "Rest In Peas."
🔥 Q9. What is the F2 phenotype ratio in a monohybrid cross? The genotype ratio?
👆 Click to reveal answer
Phenotype: 3:1 (3 dominant : 1 recessive) | Genotype: 1:2:1 (pure dominant : hybrid : pure recessive)
🔥 Q10. What is the F2 phenotype ratio in a dihybrid cross? Break down each category.
👆 Click to reveal answer
9:3:3:1 — 9 (both dominant) : 3 (dom+rec) : 3 (rec+dom) : 1 (both recessive). e.g. 9 Round+Yellow, 3 Round+Green, 3 Wrinkled+Yellow, 1 Wrinkled+Green.
⭐ Q11. What is the difference between Homologous and Analogous organs? Give one example each.
👆 Click to reveal answer
Homologous = Same structure, different function. e.g. Human arm + Bird wing. | Analogous = Different structure, same function. e.g. Bat wing + Bird wing (both fly).
⭐ Q12. What is the longest bone? The strongest? What if the longest is not in the options?
👆 Click to reveal answer
Longest + Strongest = Femur (thigh bone). If Femur not in options for "strongest" → answer is Jaw (Mandible).
⭐ Q13. Name the 3 types of synovial joints. Which has 360° rotation?
👆 Click to reveal answer
Ball & Socket (360°), Hinge (180°), Pivot (rotation but limited), Saddle (multi-directional), Gliding (sliding). Ball & Socket = 360° — shoulder and hip.
🔥 Q14. What is the outer covering of a tooth crown? What is it made of?
👆 Click to reveal answer
Enamel = outer covering of crown. Made of Calcium Phosphate. It is the hardest substance in the human body.
➕ Q15. What is the coccyx? What makes it vestigial?
👆 Click to reveal answer
Coccyx = tailbone. Last bone of the spine. It's vestigial because removing it has NO effect on body function — it's a remnant of an evolutionary tail.
🔥 Q16. Name the axial skeleton total bones and its three components.
👆 Click to reveal answer
Axial = 80 bones. Components: Skull (22) + Spine (26) + Rib cage + Sternum (~25) + Ear ossicles (6) = 80.
⭐ Q17. What is the 'beauty bone'? Where is it?
👆 Click to reveal answer
Clavicle = collar bone = beauty bone. The visible bone across the upper chest/shoulder. Part of the shoulder girdle.
🔥 Q18. What is the extra bone in the leg that doesn't exist in the arm?
👆 Click to reveal answer
Patella = knee cap. It compensates for the 1 fewer tarsal (7 vs 8 carpals in arms), keeping the total at 60 bones per pair of limbs.
