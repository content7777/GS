Squares

Most important base values for Compound Interest, Geometry, and Number Series.

🧠 Memory Trick (Base 50): To find the square of a number near 50 (e.g., $50 - x$), the first two digits are $25 - x$ and the last two digits are $x^2$.
Example: $48^2 \rightarrow x=2 \rightarrow (25-2) = 23$ and $2^2 = 04 \rightarrow 2304$.

Pythagorean Triplets

Do NOT memorize all. Only memorize these SSC-specific triplets. Used heavily in Trigonometry, Mensuration, and Geometry.

💡 Pro Tip: Multiplying any triplet by a constant gives another triplet.
Example: $(3,4,5) \times 2 = (6,8,10)$. SSC loves using decimals like $(0.3, 0.4, 0.5)$.
