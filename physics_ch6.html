Big Picture
// what this chapter is really about

The Core Thread

This chapter is about forces that act at a distance (gravity) and what those forces do to matter in motion (work, energy). Everything here is connected: gravity pulls → displacement happens → work is done → energy converts. It is one long chain from Newton's Law to Pascal's Law.

The 4 Acts of This Chapter

ACT 1 — GRAVITY LAWS

Newton's Universal Gravitation + Kepler's 3 Planetary Laws. The universe-level rules. 🔥 Must Know

ACT 2 — GRAVITY VARIATIONS

Why g changes at poles vs equator, with height, on Moon. Weight vs Mass. 🔥 Must Know

ACT 3 — FLUID LAWS

Archimedes, Bernoulli, Pascal, Density, Relative Density. Gravity applied to fluids. ⭐ Important

ACT 4 — WORK, ENERGY, POWER

What work is, kinetic & potential energy, conservation law, energy converters (dynamo, motor etc.), Archimedes Principle, noble gases in bulbs. 🔥 Must Know

Why Examiners Love This Chapter

This chapter touches Physics + Chemistry + Geography all at once (gravity varies by shape of Earth; noble gases in bulbs; energy units). SSC CGL 2023 Mains already asked the PE→KE conversion question. Expect 2–4 questions from here every attempt.

Complete Revision Notes
// every fact from the lecture, nothing dropped

1. Newton's Universal Law of Gravitation

🔥 Every object in the universe attracts every other object with a force that is:

  • Directly proportional to the product of their masses
  • Inversely proportional to the square of the distance between their centres
F = G · m₁ · m₂ / r²

G = Gravitational Constant = 6.67 × 10⁻¹¹ N·m²/kg²

Discovered by Henry Cavendish in 1798

Analogy: Same structure as Coulomb's Electrostatic Force (F = k·q₁q₂/r²), where k = 1/4πε₀

2. Mass vs Weight

Mass
• Amount of matter in an object
• SI unit: kg
• Never changes (same on Moon, in space)
• Scalar quantity
• Directly proportional to inertia (Newton's 1st Law)
Weight
• Force by which Earth attracts a mass
• SI unit: Newton (N)
• Changes with location
• Vector quantity
• W = mg (varies with g)

1 Newton = 10⁵ Dynes (Newton = SI unit, Dyne = CGS unit)

CGS = Centimetre Gram Second system

3. Density & Relative Density

Density = Mass / Volume → SI unit: kg/m³
Relative Density = Density of substance / Density of water

Density of water = 1 kg/m³ (reference)

Relative Density has NO UNIT (units cancel out)

4. Kepler's Planetary Laws (1609)

LawNameStatement
1stLaw of OrbitPlanets move in elliptical orbit with Sun at the focus
2ndLaw of AreasPlanet sweeps equal areas in equal time (speed ↑ near Sun)
3rdLaw of Time PeriodT² ∝ a³ (square of time period ∝ cube of semi-major axis)

All three laws given in 1609 by Johannes Kepler

5. Variation of Gravity (g)

g = GM / r²

g is inversely proportional to r²

  • Poles: r is smaller (Earth is flat at poles) → g is MORE
  • Equator: r is larger (Earth bulges) → g is LESS
  • With altitude: g decreases as you go up
  • On Moon: g = g(Earth)/6 = 9.8/6 ≈ 1.63 m/s²
  • Earth's shape = Geoid (oblate spheroid — flat at poles, bulged at equator)

g on Earth = 9.8 m/s² | Acceleration due to gravity is a vector quantity

On Moon: mass unchanged (mass ∝ inertia) | weight becomes 1/6 (W = mg, g → g/6)

6. Archimedes' Principle

🔥 Buoyant force = weight of fluid displaced

Any object submerged in fluid experiences an upward force (buoyancy) equal to the weight of fluid it displaces.

  • Ship floats → large volume displaced → large buoyant force
  • Nail sinks → tiny volume displaced → negligible buoyancy vs. weight
  • Ice cube melting → water level does NOT change (melted water exactly fills displaced volume)
  • Ship: Sea → River → river water is less dense (less salt) → less buoyancy → ship sinks lower

7. Work

Work is done when a force causes a displacement in the direction of force.

W = F · d · cosθ

Work is a scalar quantity | SI unit = Joule (J) = N·m | Named after James Prescott Joule

ConditionWork Done
Force parallel to displacement (θ=0°)Positive
Force anti-parallel to displacement (θ=180°)Negative
Displacement = 0 (box doesn't move)Zero
θ = 90° (coolie carrying load sideways)Zero

8. Thrust & Pressure

Thrust = Force applied perpendicularly (at 90°) to a surface. Vector quantity. Examples: hammer hitting nail, airplane engines, rockets.

Pressure = Thrust / Area → SI unit: Pascal (Pa) = N/m² = kg/m·s²

Pressure is a scalar quantity. Area ↑ → Pressure ↓ (inversely proportional)

Examples: lying in sand spreads area → less pressure → less sinking.

Parsec ≠ Pascal: Parsec measures celestial distances (like light year). Pascal measures pressure.

9. Power

Power = Work / Time = Force × Velocity

Rate of doing work. SI unit = Watt (W) = J/s

1 Horsepower = 746 Watts = 0.746 kW (Horsepower is NOT SI unit)

10. Energy

Capacity to do work. SI unit = Joule (J) (same as work). Biggest source = Sun.

Kinetic Energy (KE)

KE = ½mv² = p²/2m (where p = linear momentum = mv)
  • If momentum doubles → KE becomes 4 times
  • If momentum halves → KE becomes 1/4

Potential Energy (PE)

PE = mgh (gravitational potential energy)

Energy stored due to position or state. Example: stretched bow, raised object.

11. Law of Conservation of Energy

🔥 "Energy can neither be created nor destroyed. It can only be converted from one form to another."

Ball in free fall (exam favourite — CGL Mains 2023):

PositionPEKETotal
Top (just released)mgh (100%)0mgh
Midpointmgh/2 (50%)½mv² (50%)mgh
Bottom (just touching)0½mv² (100%)mgh

14. Bernoulli's Principle

P + ρgh + ½ρv² = constant

Sum of pressure energy + potential energy + kinetic energy remains constant at every point in a flowing fluid. Based on conservation of energy.

15. Pascal's Law

🔥 Pressure applied to an incompressible fluid in a closed container is transmitted equally in all directions.

  • Car hydraulic lift: one foot press → whole car lifts
  • Hydraulic brakes: small foot force → large braking force
🪝 Smart Memory Hooks
// vivid images that stick for months

Hook 1 — G = 6.67 × 10⁻¹¹ (Cavendish 1798)

Imagine a very OLD man (1798 = old) named Cavendish opening a lock. The lock number is 667. He has a huge negative power (-11) tattooed on his forehead. Every time you see "G", picture this old man with the 667 lock. Done — you'll never forget G's value.

Hook 2 — Kepler's 3 Laws (1609)

It's 1609 — a famous painter is painting 3 pictures of his planet girlfriend: (1) She's running in an OVAL track (ellipse) — Orbit; (2) She sweeps the same floor area every hour — Areas; (3) Her watch (Time²) is tied to her long arm (a³) — Time Period. The year 1609 was when he painted all three.

Hook 3 — Poles MORE gravity, Equator LESS

Earth is like a fat man sleeping. His belly (equator) sticks OUT — farther from centre → less gravity. His head and feet (poles) are squished FLAT — closer to centre → more gravity. Whenever you see "equator" think "belly OUT" = gravity down.

Hook 4 — 1 Newton = 10⁵ Dynes

One NEWTON is a big powerful man. One DYNE is a tiny ant. It takes 100,000 ants (10⁵) to equal one Newton. Picture Newton stepping on 100,000 ants.

Hook 5 — Ship Sea→River sinks LOWER

Imagine a ship carrying salt. In the salty sea, the salt in the water holds the ship up like a pillow. When it enters the fresh river, the pillow disappears — the ship drops. Salt = support. No salt = no support = sink lower.

Hook 6 — Ice Cube in Water — Level Unchanged

Imagine you paid for exactly 1 glass of water. The ice cube was already "charging" you for the space it was taking. When it melts, it just "pays back" the exact space it borrowed. Net change = zero. The ice was honest!

Hook 7 — Coolie's Work = Zero

The coolie is walking sideways (horizontal) while gravity pulls down (vertical). Gravity and displacement are strangers — they're at 90° to each other. Strangers don't do business together. Work done by gravity = ZERO.

Hook 8 — Energy Converters (Microphone vs Speaker)

Microphone: you SPEAK into it → Sound IN, Electrical OUT. Loudspeaker: sound comes OUT → Electrical IN, Sound OUT. Micro = small input (your voice). Loud = big output (speaker sound). They are mirror opposites. Same logic for Dynamo (mechanical→electrical) ↔ Motor (electrical→mechanical).

Hook 9 — Noble Gas Mnemonic

"HiNa, AarKar Xena Raat mein" = He, Ne, Ar, Kr, Xe, Rn. Imagine Hina from a Bollywood film telling Xena the warrior princess to "do something (Aar-Kar) at night (Raat mein)." All discovered by William Ramsay except Helium (Janssen + Lockyer found it in the Sun).

Hook 10 — KE = p²/2m (Momentum doubles → KE × 4)

KE depends on p². If you double p, you square it: (2p)² = 4p². So KE becomes 4× bigger. Think: doubling your speed on a scooter doesn't just double the crash damage — it quadruples it. That's why speeding kills.

Hook 11 — Tungsten in Bulb (symbol W)

Tungsten's symbol is W because in German it's called Wolfram. The bulb has a Wolf inside it (tungsten) that glows with anger (heat + light) when electricity flows. The wolf is surrounded by Argon gas — the bodyguard that prevents the wolf from reacting.

Hook 12 — Pascal's Law: Car Lift

Imagine a mechanic lifting a 2-tonne car with just ONE FOOT. He's not Hercules — he's using Pascal. In his hydraulic lift, the pressure from his little foot spreads equally through the fluid and comes out with massive force at the other piston. Pascal = pressure equaliser.

Newton ↔ Coulomb Analogy

Newton's Gravity Law and Coulomb's Electrostatic Law have identical mathematical structure:

F_gravity = G·m₁m₂/r² ↔ F_electric = k·q₁q₂/r²

Both are inverse-square laws. Both have a constant of proportionality. This is not a coincidence — nature loves this pattern.

⚠ Exam Traps
// what students get wrong every single time

⚠ Trap 1 — Weight ≠ Mass

Mass is in kg and never changes. Weight is in Newton and changes with gravity. Exam question: "On Moon, a person's _______ stays same." Answer: MASS. "Weight on Moon becomes _______ of Earth weight." Answer: 1/6.

⚠ Trap 2 — g is MORE at Poles (not Equator)

Common confusion: students think equator is "closer to Earth's centre" because Earth is round. Actually the equator BULGES OUT — it is farther from the centre. Poles are closer to the centre → g is MORE at poles. Remember the "fat man sleeping" hook.

⚠ Trap 3 — Relative Density has NO UNIT

Students often write kg/m³ as the unit of relative density. Wrong. Relative density = density of substance / density of water. Both units cancel = dimensionless quantity. No unit.

⚠ Trap 4 — Kepler's 3rd Law: T² ∝ a³ (not T³ ∝ a²)

Easy to mix up: it is the SQUARE of T that is proportional to the CUBE of a (semi-major axis). Not the other way. T² ∝ a³. Exam writers will swap the exponents to trap you.

⚠ Trap 5 — Parsec ≠ Pascal

Pascal (Pa) = unit of pressure (N/m²). Parsec = unit of astronomical distance (like light-year). Two completely different things. Exam loves to put them in a match-the-column.

⚠ Trap 6 — Horsepower ≠ SI Unit

Horsepower is NOT the SI unit of power. The SI unit is Watt. 1 HP = 746 W = 0.746 kW. Exam will ask "SI unit of power" — answer is Watt, not Horsepower.

⚠ Trap 7 — Ice Melting in Water → Level UNCHANGED

Intuition says water level should rise when ice melts. It does NOT. The ice was already displacing water equal to its mass. When it melts, it occupies that exact space. Level = constant. This is a classic MCQ trap.

⚠ Trap 8 — Dynamo vs Motor Direction

Dynamo: Mechanical → Electrical. Motor: Electrical → Mechanical. Students often swap them. Trick: a MOTOR moves things (mechanical output). A DYNAMO/GENERATOR generates electricity (electrical output).

⚠ Trap 9 — Microphone vs Loudspeaker

Microphone: you speak INTO it (sound input → electrical output). Loudspeaker: sound comes OUT of it (electrical input → sound output). They are exact reverses. A common trap is asking "which converts electrical to sound?" → Loudspeaker (not microphone).

⚠ Trap 10 — Work Done by Coolie = Zero (not positive)

Coolie carries load horizontally. Gravity acts downwards (vertical). Angle between force (gravity) and displacement (horizontal) = 90°. cos90° = 0. Work done by gravity = ZERO. But physically he IS doing work against his muscles — only gravitational work is zero.

⚠ Trap 11 — Helium Discoverer is NOT Ramsay

William Ramsay discovered Ne, Ar, Kr, Xe, Rn. But Helium was discovered by Janssen and Norman Lockyer (in the Sun's spectrum). Exam WILL ask who discovered Helium — don't write Ramsay.

⚠ Trap 12 — Xenon = "Stranger Gas"

Xenon is nicknamed "Stranger Gas." Don't confuse with any other noble gas. "Xenos" in Greek = stranger. Xenon = the strange noble gas with many unusual properties.

⚠ Trap 13 — G vs g

G (capital) = Universal Gravitational Constant = 6.67×10⁻¹¹ N·m²/kg² — NEVER changes anywhere in the universe. g (small) = acceleration due to gravity = 9.8 m/s² on Earth — CHANGES with location. Classic trap: "Which of G/g is constant everywhere in the universe?" → G.

Universal Gravitation
G = 6.67×10⁻¹¹ N·m²/kg² (Cavendish, 1798). F∝m₁m₂, F∝1/r². Same form as Coulomb's Law.
Mass vs Weight
Mass (kg) = constant. Weight (N) = mg = varies. On Moon: mass same, weight = 1/6. 1N = 10⁵ Dyne.
Kepler's Laws — 1609
1st = ellipse (Sun at focus). 2nd = equal areas. 3rd = T² ∝ a³. All three in 1609.
Gravity Variation
g = GM/r². Poles: more g (r small). Equator: less g (r big). Altitude: g decreases. Earth shape = Geoid.
Density / Archimedes
Density = m/V. Relative density = no unit. Buoyancy = displaced weight. Ice melts = no level change. Sea→River = ship sinks lower.
Work, Pressure, Power
W = Fd cosθ. Joule (scalar). θ=90° → W=0. Pressure = F/A = Pascal. Power = W/t = Watt. 1 HP = 746 W.
Energy & Conservation
KE = ½mv² = p²/2m. PE = mgh. p×2 → KE×4. Free fall: Top=all PE, Mid=half/half, Bottom=all KE. Energy neither created nor destroyed.
Energy Converters
Dynamo/Generator: Mech→Elec. Motor: Elec→Mech. Mic: Sound→Elec. Speaker: Elec→Sound. Bulb: Elec→Light+Heat. Filament = Tungsten (W). Gas = Argon.
Noble Gases (Critical)

Pascal & Bernoulli
Pascal: incompressible fluid → pressure equally distributed (hydraulic lift, brakes). Bernoulli: P + ρgh + ½ρv² = constant.

G ≠ g | Pascal ≠ Parsec | Ramsay ≠ Helium | Dynamo ≠ Motor | Mic ≠ Speaker | Ice melts → NO change. Horsepower ≠ SI unit. T² ∝ a³ (not T³ ∝ a²).
