Big Picture

Why this chapter exists, in one breath

Everything here is one idea wearing different costumes: things pull on each other, and pulling can do work. Gravitation is the universe's favourite kind of pulling. Work & Energy is the accounting system for what pulling (or pushing) accomplishes.

The chapter in 4 dominoes

1

Gravitation

Every mass pulls every other mass. This pull is weight. Weight changes with location; mass never does.

2

Fluids feel gravity too

Because gravity pulls fluids down, fluids push back up on anything inside them — that's Archimedes' buoyancy, and it's why ships float and nails sink.

3

Pulling = potential to do Work

If gravity pulls something through a distance, work is done. Work is just "force successfully causing displacement."

4

Energy is the ledger

Work done = energy transferred. Energy never disappears — it only changes costume (potential ↔ kinetic ↔ electrical ↔ sound ↔ heat). This is the Law of Conservation of Energy.

Chapter Memory Palace

The Space Station → Ocean → Workshop Journey

Walk this journey in your head before every revision. Each location is a fact-cluster. If you forget a fact, ask "where in the journey was I standing?" — the location will hand the fact back to you.

Picture yourself as an astronaut-cum-physics-inspector doing one tour: you start in orbit, fall down to Earth, walk into the ocean, dry off in a workshop, and end the day in a glowing room full of gas bulbs. Six stops. Six fact-clusters.

🪐

Stop 1 — Orbit Control Room (Gravitation + Kepler)

You float in space holding two objects of different mass. They drift toward each other — Newton's Universal Law: F ∝ m₁m₂/r². The constant G = 6.67×10⁻¹¹ was nailed down by Cavendish in 1798 (note: "100 cubic meters" in the transcript is a mis-transcription of "Cavendish" — lock in the correct name). Through the window you watch a planet trace an ellipse around the Sun sitting at one focus — that's Kepler's Law of Orbits (1609). A radar sweep shows the planet covering equal areas in equal times — Law of Areas. A flight computer beeps: T² ∝ a³ — Law of Periods.

🔑 Recall trigger: "floating + ellipse + radar sweep + beeping computer" = Newton's law + Kepler's 3 laws.

⚖️

Stop 2 — The Re-Entry Capsule (Mass vs Weight, g-variation)

You re-enter Earth's atmosphere. Your mass hasn't changed one gram since launch — but your body suddenly feels heavier because weight = mg and g just jumped up. You land near a pole (not the equator) — poles are closer to Earth's center because Earth bulges at the equator (geoid shape), so g is higher at poles, lower at equator, and lower at altitude. A note on your capsule wall reminds you: on the Moon, g is 1/6th, so your weight is 1/6th too, but your mass is identical.

🔑 Recall trigger: "landing capsule, feeling heavier, pole vs equator sign" = mass-weight + g variation cluster.

🌊

Stop 3 — The Harbour (Archimedes, Floatation, Fluids)

You walk to a harbour. A spring balance shows a stone weighing 7 N in air, then only 3 N underwater — the missing 4 N is the buoyant force, equal to the weight of water displaced. Nearby, a ship floats easily (large displaced volume → large buoyancy spread over a big hull) while a dropped nail sinks instantly (tiny displaced volume, can't support its own weight). A worker tells you a ship sailing from sea → river sinks slightly lower (river water is less salty/dense, less buoyant force). An ice cube melts in a glass and the water level doesn't change — what it displaced as ice equals the space it fills as water.

🔑 Recall trigger: "harbour, spring balance, ship vs nail, ice cube" = the entire Archimedes cluster.

📦

Stop 4 — The Loading Dock (Work, Thrust, Pressure)

At the dock, you push a heavy crate that doesn't move — physics says zero work despite your sweat. A coolie walks past carrying a load on his head — force is vertical (mg), motion is horizontal, angle = 90°, so gravity does zero work on the load too. A worker hammers a nail perpendicular to a block — that perpendicular push is thrust. You notice footprints: standing (small area, you sink deep — high pressure) vs lying down (large area, shallow sink — low pressure), because pressure = thrust/area.

🔑 Recall trigger: "dock, immovable crate, coolie, hammer, footprints in sand" = work/thrust/pressure cluster.

⚡

Stop 5 — The Engine Room (Power, Energy, Conservation)

The dock's crane engine display reads power in watts (J/s) and also in horsepower (746 W). You drop a 10 kg wrench from 10 m — it carries 1000 J of potential energy that converts smoothly into kinetic energy as it falls, total energy constant throughout — the Law of Conservation of Energy. A stretched bow nearby stores potential energy that becomes the arrow's kinetic energy on release.

🔑 Recall trigger: "engine room display, falling wrench, stretched bow" = power + energy + conservation cluster.

💡

Stop 6 — The Bulb Storage Room (Energy Conversions + Noble Gases)

The final room is lined with devices: a dynamo/generator (mechanical→electrical), a motor (electrical→mechanical, the reverse), a microphone (sound→electrical) and a loudspeaker right next to it doing the reverse (electrical→sound). In the centre, a bulb glows — tungsten (W) filament inside argon gas, converting electrical energy into light + heat. A poster on the wall lists the noble gas family with William Ramsay's name attached to neon, argon, krypton, xenon, radon (only helium breaks the pattern — discovered by Janssen & Lockyer).

🔑 Recall trigger: "bulb room with paired machines on the walls" = all energy conversion pairs + noble gas discoverers.
