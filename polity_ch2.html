Overview
Big Picture
What this chapter is really about — and why it matters for the exam

The Indian Constitution is the longest written constitution in the world. This chapter teaches you WHERE our constitution borrowed its features from and HOW those features are structured inside the constitution (schedules).

Think of it this way: India's constitution makers were like master chefs — they tasted the best dishes from 10+ countries and created a unique recipe. Your job is to remember which ingredient came from which country.

🇬🇧
UK — Our Ruler
Parliamentary system, Rule of Law, Single Citizenship, Writs, Bicameralism
🇺🇸

📌 Key Insight for Exam

The exam loves asking "Which feature was borrowed from which country?" and "Which Schedule contains what?" These two are the backbone of this chapter. Master them first.

Sources of the Constitution
Which features were borrowed from which country — the most asked topic
🔥 #1 Priority: GOI Act 1935

The single biggest source — ~40% of our constitution comes from here. Emergency provisions, Supreme Court, Public Service Commission, Federal Scheme, Governor's Office are all from here.

⚠️ Critical Distinction — Japan vs USA
Japan → Art 21 IndiaProcedure Established by Law
USA concept (not in our constitution)Due Process of Law

The Supreme Court discussed "Due Process of Law" in the Maneka Gandhi case but it is NOT part of our constitution. Only Procedure Established by Law (Japan) is our Art 21.

🪝 The A.V. Dicey Hook

Whenever you see Rule of Law → think A.V. Dicey (UK legal scholar). Whenever Rule of Law comes in an MCQ, the concept-giver is always A.V. Dicey. Don't mix with Montesquieu (Separation of Powers).

The 12 Schedules
Original: 8 schedules. Current: 12 schedules. Master the trick: "Tears of Old PM"
🪝 The Master Trick — "TEARS OF OLD PM"
Territories (Sch 1) · Emoluments (Sch 2) · Oaths (Sch 3) · Allocation Rajya Sabha (Sch 4) · Scheduled Areas (Sch 5) · Tribal Areas (Sch 6) · Federal Provisions/Lists (Sch 7) · Languages (Sch 8) · Land Reforms (Sch 9) · Defections (Sch 10) · Panchayat (Sch 11) · Municipality (Sch 12)
SCHEDULE 1
🗺️ Territories & States
Names and territorial jurisdictions of all States and Union Territories. 🔥
SCHEDULE 2
💰 Emoluments (Salaries)
Salaries of: President, Vice President, CAG, Judges of SC & HC. Note: No President/VP oath here — that's Schedule 3! 🔥
SCHEDULE 3
🤝 Oaths & Affirmations
Oaths of: CAG, SC & HC Judges, MPs, MLAs, Ministers of Centre & State. NOT President & VP — their oath is in Art 60/69! 🔥
SCHEDULE 4
🏛️ Rajya Sabha Seats
Allocation of seats to states and UTs in Rajya Sabha. ⭐
SCHEDULE 5
🏔️ Scheduled Areas
Administration of Scheduled (tribal) areas — General. ⭐
SCHEDULE 6
🌿 Tribal Areas — NE States
Tribal areas of Assam, Tripura, Mizoram, Meghalaya. Hook: ATM Money (A=Assam, T=Tripura, M=Mizoram, M=Meghalaya). ⚠️ Manipur NOT included! 🔥
SCHEDULE 7
📋 Three Lists
Union List (100 subjects), State List (61 subjects), Concurrent List (52 subjects). Originally: 97, 66, 47. 🔥
SCHEDULE 8
🗣️ Official Languages
Originally 14 → now 22. Added: Sindhi (21st Amend), KMN—Konkani, Manipuri, Nepali (71st Amend), BDSM—Bodo, Dogri, Santhali, Maithili (92nd Amend). No language added since 1996; only Oriya→Odia renamed. 🔥
SCHEDULE 9
🌾 Land Reforms
Added by 1st Constitutional Amendment 1951. Key case: IR Coelho case — SC can review laws in Schedule 9 if inserted after 24 April 1973. 🔥
SCHEDULE 10
🏃 Anti-Defection Law
Added by 52nd Amendment 1985. An MP/MLA leaving their party = defection. Exceptions: independent joining after win, nominated member after 6 months. 🔥
SCHEDULE 11
🏘️ Panchayats (29 subjects)
Added by 73rd Amendment 1992. ⭐
SCHEDULE 12
🏙️ Municipalities (18 subjects)
Added by 74th Amendment 1993. ⭐

Federal vs Unitary Features
India is neither fully federal nor fully unitary — remember the scholars' views
🎓 Famous Descriptions of India's Federation
K.C. WheareQuasi-Federal (half-incomplete federal)
Granville AustinCooperative Federalism
Ivor JenningsFederation with a Unitary Bias
Morris JonesBargaining Federalism
✅ Federal Features
✓Written Constitution → supreme, hard to amend → protects states
✓Division of powers (Union, State, Concurrent Lists)
✓Independent Judiciary (no political control)
✓Bicameral Parliament (Rajya Sabha = states' voice)
✓Dual Government (Centre + State both exist)
✓Rigid Constitution → federal feature
⚡ Unitary Features
→Single citizenship (not dual like USA)
→Single constitution (states have no separate constitution)
→Governor appointed by Centre (not elected by state)
→Residual powers with Centre (not states)
→During President's Rule: Centre makes laws on State List subjects
→Flexibility of constitution → unitary feature

Separation of Powers — USA vs India

USA — STRICT Separation

Legislature makes laws. Executive implements them. Judiciary checks them. No overlap at all. Each organ stays in its lane strictly.

India — NOT Strict

Separation exists (Art 50 — state shall separate Executive & Judiciary) but Judicial Activism means judiciary sometimes enters executive/legislative territory. Not strict separation.

LegislatureMakes the law (Parliament)
ExecutiveImplements the law (Government)
JudiciaryChecks the law (Courts)

SOVEREIGN — India is fully independent; no external authority controls it. Was in original Preamble. NOT added by 42nd Amendment. Examiners love putting Sovereign in options — it was already there!
SOCIALIST — Added by 42nd Amendment 1976. India follows a mixed economy — neither pure capitalism nor pure socialism.
SECULAR — Added by 42nd Amendment 1976. No official state religion. Equal respect for all religions.
DEMOCRATIC — Power vested in the people. Representatives elected. Was in original Preamble.
REPUBLIC — Head of state (President) is elected, not hereditary. Borrowed concept from France. Was in original Preamble.
JUSTICE — Three types: Social, Economic, Political. Borrowed from USSR/Russia. Social = no discrimination; Economic = equal pay for equal work; Political = universal suffrage.
LIBERTY — Five types: Thought, Expression, Belief, Faith, Worship. Borrowed from French Revolution.
EQUALITY — Two types: of Status and of Opportunity. Borrowed from French Revolution.
FRATERNITY — Brotherhood among citizens. Assuring: Dignity of Individual AND Unity & Integrity of Nation. The word "Integrity" was added by 42nd Amendment.

Amendment Cases — Preamble

1960 — Berubari Union Case
SC says: Preamble is NOT a part of the Constitution → cannot be amended.
1973 — Keshavananda Bharati Case
SC reverses: Preamble IS a part of the Constitution → CAN be amended. But Basic Structure cannot be destroyed.
LIC vs Union of India Case
SC reaffirms Keshavananda Bharati ruling — Preamble is part of Constitution.
1976 — 42nd Amendment
Only amendment to the Preamble (till date). Added: Socialist, Secular, Integrity.
Also called "Mini Constitution" — amended many parts of the Constitution.

Memory System
Smart Memory Hooks
Tricks that work months after you learn them
🪝 Hook 1 — "TEARS OF OLD PM"
Imagine an old Prime Minister crying (tears). Remember the 12 schedules: Territory · Emolument · Oath · Allocation (RS) · Scheduled Areas · Tribal Areas · Federal Lists · Languages · Land Reforms · Defection · Panchayat · Municipality
🪝 Hook 2 — ATM Money for Schedule 6
ATM — but there's NO Money (Manipur). Assam, Tripura, Mizoram, Meghalaya are in Schedule 6. Manipur is NOT, even though it's a Northeast state like the others. When you go to this ATM, it denies your card → No Manipur!
🪝 Hook 3 — STL for Australia
Australia = STL: Sitting (Joint Sitting of Parliament) · Trade (Freedom of Trade & Commerce) · List (Concurrent List). Picture a kangaroo (Australia) sitting at a trade fair making a list.
🪝 Hook 4 — Canada = "Centre Wins" country
Everything from Canada gives power to the Centre: Residual Powers, Strong Centre Federation, Advisory Jurisdiction (Centre can ask SC), Governor appointment. Canada = Centre Always Nabs Domain Advantage.
🪝 Hook 5 — KMN & BDSM for Schedule 8 Languages
KMN (74th Amendment): Konkani · Manipuri · Nepali. BDSM (92nd Amendment): Bodo · Dogri · Santhali · Maithili. Sindhi was alone — added by 21st Amendment.
🪝 Hook 6 — 42nd Amendment "ISS" words in Preamble
42nd Amendment added ISS: Integrity · Socialist · Secular. Remember: ISS (International Space Station) was launched in the same spirit as 1976 — adding new things to an existing structure. Sovereign was ALREADY there — examiners try to trick you with it!
🪝 Hook 7 — Objective Resolution vs Preamble (The Book Analogy)
You can't write the preface/summary of a book BEFORE writing the book. Same way, the Preamble (summary of constitution) was written AFTER the whole constitution was ready. The Objective Resolution (1946-47) was the VISION → not the same as Preamble.
🪝 Hook 8 — Three Organs of Government (Traffic Metaphor)
Legislature = the city planner who makes traffic rules. Executive = the traffic police who implements them. Judiciary = the court that checks if the police is doing it right or if the rules are fair. When police starts making rules = executive overreach. When court starts policing = judicial activism.

⚠️ Exam Traps
What Examiners Use to Trick You
Study these harder than the main content — these are where marks are lost
⚠️ TRAP 1 — Sovereign was ALREADY in Preamble
The 42nd Amendment added Socialist, Secular, and Integrity. NOT Sovereign. Examiners almost always put "Sovereign" as one of the options to confuse you. When the question asks "which words were added by 42nd Amendment" → answer is Socialist, Secular, Integrity → NOT Sovereign.
⚠️ TRAP 2 — Manipur in Schedule 6
Schedule 6 covers tribal areas of Assam, Tripura, Mizoram, Meghalaya. Manipur is NOT in Schedule 6. Examiners put Manipur as an option specifically to trap students. The ATM no money trick: A=Assam, T=Tripura, M=Mizoram, M=Meghalaya. Manipur = No Money = Not in it.
⚠️ TRAP 3 — Objective Resolution vs Preamble dates
Objective Resolution presented: 13 Dec 1946. Adopted: 22 Jan 1947. The Preamble was NOT adopted on these dates. Preamble was part of the final constitution on 26 Nov 1949. Do NOT use 1946/1947 for the Preamble's dates.
⚠️ TRAP 4 — Japan's "Procedure Established by Law" vs USA's "Due Process"
India uses Procedure Established by Law (from Japan) in Article 21. "Due Process of Law" is the USA concept — it was discussed in Maneka Gandhi case but is NOT written in our constitution. Don't mix these.
⚠️ TRAP 5 — President/VP Oath NOT in Schedule 3
Schedule 3 has oaths of: MPs, MLAs, Ministers, SC/HC Judges, CAG. President's oath is in Article 60. VP's oath is in Article 69. They are NOT in Schedule 3. Schedule 2 has their salaries (emoluments), not oaths.
⚠️ TRAP 6 — Office of Governor vs Appointment of Governor
From GOI Act 1935, we borrowed the Office of Governor (the position itself). The concept that the Centre appoints the Governor is a Unitary Feature taken from Canada. Don't say appointment of Governor comes from GOI Act 1935.
⚠️ TRAP 7 — "Strict Separation of Powers" — India has it? NO.
India has separation of powers (borrowed from USA) but NOT strict separation. Article 50 directs the state to separate Executive and Judiciary, but judicial activism means the judiciary often enters others' territory. Only USA has strict separation.
⚠️ TRAP 8 — Schedule 8 Languages count
Original: 14 languages. Current: 22 languages. Sindhi added by 21st Amendment. Not by 22nd or any other. KMN added by 71st Amendment. BDSM added by 92nd Amendment. Know which amendment added which group.
⚠️ TRAP 9 — Union List, State List, Concurrent List numbers
Original: Union 97, State 66, Concurrent 47. Current: Union 100 (↑3), State 61 (↓5), Concurrent 52 (↑5). The State List DECREASED. Examiners may say it increased — wrong. The 4 subjects from State to Concurrent (42nd Amendment) is a common question.

🔥 High Probability Questions

Q. The concept of Rule of Law was given by whom? ▼
A.V. Dicey (British legal scholar). Rule of Law borrowed from UK.
Q. Which words were added to the Preamble by the 42nd Amendment? ▼
Socialist, Secular, and Integrity. NOT Sovereign (it was already there). Year: 1976. Also called Mini Constitution.
Q. How many times has the Preamble been amended? ▼
Only once — by the 42nd Amendment in 1976.
Q. Which Schedule has tribal areas of NE India? Which states are included? ▼
Schedule 6. States: Assam, Tripura, Mizoram, Meghalaya. Manipur is NOT included (ATM — no Money for Manipur).
Q. The single biggest influence on the Indian Constitution is? ▼
Government of India Act 1935 — approximately 40% of our constitution is borrowed from it.
Q. In which case did the SC say the Preamble IS part of the Constitution? ▼
Keshavananda Bharati case (1973). Earlier in Berubari Union case (1960), SC said it is NOT. Keshavananda reversed that.
Q. Which amendment added Education and Forest to the Concurrent List? ▼
42nd Amendment 1976. Transferred Education, Forest & Wildlife, Weights & Measures, Administration of Justice from State to Concurrent List.
Q. Fundamental Duties and Five Year Plan concept — from which country? ▼
USSR (Russia). Also, ideals of Justice (Social, Economic, Political) from USSR/Russia.
Q. Ivor Jennings described India's constitution as? ▼
"Federation with a Unitary Bias". K.C. Wheare said "Quasi-Federal", Granville Austin said "Cooperative Federalism", Morris Jones said "Bargaining Federalism".
Q. What is residual power and where have we taken it from? ▼
Residual power = power to make laws on subjects NOT in Union, State, or Concurrent Lists. This power is with the Centre. Taken from Canada.
