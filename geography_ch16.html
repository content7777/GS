🔥 Soil Horizons (O → A → B → C → Parent Rock) 🔥 MUST KNOW
O
Organic / Humus Layer
Dark coloured, amorphous, colloidal compound. NOT found everywhere — only where humus is deposited. Also called Elution Zone — has important minerals.
TOPMOST
A
Topsoil (Eluviation Zone)
Important mineral content. Transition zone between O and B. Most fertile layer.
FERTILE
B
Weathered Rock Layer
Weathering + upliftment has occurred. Alluvion layer.
WEATHERED
C
Partially Weathered Rock
Transition between weathered and unweathered rock.
TRANSITION
R
Parent Rock (Unweathered)
Base rock. Not broken, no erosion. Determines soil colour and texture.
BASE ROCK
Factors of Soil Formation
  • Parent Rock → determines colour, texture, mineral content
  • Climate → decides type of weathering, moisture content
  • Vegetation → roots hold soil; humus content depends on vegetation
  • Time → determines thickness of soil layer
  • Relief Feature / Topography → slope = no soil accumulation; flat = soil accumulates

# Soil Type % Area Key Features Found In Crops
1 Alluvial 40% 🥇 Formed by river sediments; Khadar (new) & Bhangar (old); Rich in Potash; Poor in Phosphorus Northern Plains, UP, Bihar, WB, Assam, deltas of E-flowing rivers Rice, Wheat, Sugarcane

Nitrogen (N) Phosphorus (P) Potassium (K)

MICRO Nutrients (needed in small quantities)
Everything else (Iron, Zinc, Manganese, Boron, Copper, etc.) — just remember, if not in macro list → it's micro

🔥 Soil Erosion & Conservation 🔥 MUST KNOW
TYPES OF EROSION
  • Sheet Erosion — Entire layer washes away (rain + slope + no vegetation)
  • Gully / Rill Erosion — River cuts through → rill → gully → ravine → badland
  • Chambal River → famous for ravines/badlands (bad land topography)
Agents: Wind Glaciers River Water
CONSERVATION METHODS
  • Contour Bunding — Stone barriers on slopes to reduce water velocity
  • Shelter Belt — Row of trees to stop wind erosion in open fields
  • Terrace Farming — Stair-cut hillsides (Sikkim, Darjeeling, Kerala, TN, W.Ghats)
  • Mulching — Crop residue/leaves placed on topsoil to retain moisture
  • Afforestation — Plant trees (roots bind soil)
  • Contour Ploughing — Same logic as contour bunding

⭐ Types of Agriculture / Farming
TYPE 1 Primitive Subsistence Agriculture
Very old/primitive tools (hoe, shovel) | Slash & burn = Shifting cultivation | Not environment friendly
TYPE 2 Intensive Subsistence Agriculture
High population + low land | Maximize every inch of land | High dose of biochemicals, pesticides, fertilizers | India — land fragmentation due to right of inheritance
TYPE 3 Commercial Agriculture
Grow to SELL | Started by Europeans in Indian Subcontinent | Large land, single crop, workers employed | Uses HYV seeds, fertilizers, insecticides | Crops: Tea, Coffee, Rubber, Sugarcane, Banana
TYPE 4 Plantation Agriculture
Subset of commercial | Single crop grown in very large area | Tea, Coffee, Jute, Coconut, Rubber brought by Europeans | Truck farming = vegetable farming
TYPE 5 Mixed Farming
Agriculture + Livestock together | Cows, buffaloes, goats alongside crops | Side income from livestock when crops fail
TYPE 6 Mixed Cropping
Seeds of 2+ crops mixed before sowing, grown together in same field simultaneously
TYPE 7 Intercropping
2 crops grown in alternating rows in same field at same time | Pattern: Crop A row → Crop B row → Crop A row
TYPE 8 Crop Rotation
Different crops grown in different cropping seasons on same land | Rabi → Kharif → Zaid alternation

⚠️ Exam Traps
WHAT TOPPERS KNOW THAT OTHERS GET WRONG
⚠️ TRAP #1 — Khadar vs Bhangar

Khadar = NEW alluvium (near river, floods annually, more fertile) | Bhangar = OLD alluvium (away from river, elevated, less fertile). Exam may swap them. Remember: Khadar = Kooool new stuff, Bhangar = Big old stuff.

⚠️ TRAP #2 — Mixed Farming ≠ Mixed Cropping ≠ Intercropping

Mixed Farming = Agriculture + Livestock (animals involved!) | Mixed Cropping = seeds of 2 crops mixed and sown together | Intercropping = 2 crops in alternating ROWS. Three totally different things — SSC loves asking this!

⚠️ TRAP #3 — Red Soil found where there's LESS rain (NOT more)

Students confuse red soil location. Red soil is found on the leeward side (shadow area) of Western Ghats = less rain. Laterite soil is found where there is MORE rain. Opposite! Rain = Laterite. No rain = Red.

⚠️ TRAP #4 — Black Soil is NOT the most abundant

Many students think black soil (cotton soil, regur) is most common because it's famous. Wrong! Alluvial soil = 40% (most abundant). Black = 15% (3rd). Red = 18% (2nd).

⚠️ TRAP #5 — Father of Green Revolution (India vs World)

MS Swaminathan = India | Norman Borlaug = World. Nobel Prize went to Borlaug (1970). Bharat Ratna went to Swaminathan (2024). Swaminathan died in 2024. Don't confuse the two.

⚠️ TRAP #6 — Laterite = High Rain, Arid Soil = Low Rain

Both sound similar but are opposites. Laterite: formed by leaching (heavy rain washes away silica). Arid/Desert soil: very little rain, salty. Remember: Leaching needs LOTS of water. Desert needs NO water.

⚠️ TRAP #7 — Alkaline vs Acidic Treatment

Alkaline soil → Gypsum (G = Gold = Alkaline correction) | Acidic soil → Lime. Students often swap these. Note: Arid soil is saline/alkaline → treated with gypsum.

⚠️ TRAP #8 — Chambal = Ravines (NOT Alluvial plain)

Chambal river creates ravines/badland topography through gully erosion. Don't confuse it with alluvial plains. Chambal = Bad land. Northern Plains = Good alluvial land.

⚠️ TRAP #9 — Zaid (ZD) Season — Very short, often forgotten

Zaid crops: Watermelon, Muskmelon, Cucumber, Fodder. Many students only remember Kharif and Rabi. Zaid = summer short season (April sowing → July harvesting). SSC can ask: "Cucumber belongs to which season?"

⚠️ TRAP #10 — Alluvial Soil: Phosphorus Deficient (NOT Potash)

Alluvial soil is RICH in Potash but DEFICIENT in Phosphorus. Many students think it's rich in both or poor in both. The distinction matters for exam questions on soil nutrients.

⚠️ TRAP #11 — White Revolution ≠ Green Revolution

White = Milk → Kurien → Operation Flood → AMUL | Green = Food grains → Swaminathan (India). Yellow = Oilseeds → Sam Pitroda. Blue = Fish → Hiralal Chaudhary. Don't mix fathers and revolutions.

Q08 · IMPORTANT
How is Laterite soil formed? Why is it called "brick soil"?
tap to reveal →
🧱 Formed by leaching process — in areas with high temperature and heavy rainfall, silica and important minerals get washed away, leaving behind iron-rich, hardened soil. "Laterite" comes from Latin "later" = brick. The soil becomes so hard it's used to make bricks.

Q11 · IMPORTANT
Why does alluvial soil's sand content decrease from West to East?
tap to reveal →
Rivers carry sediments from mountains. Heavy, coarse grains (sand) deposit first (in the west/upstream). Fine grains travel further and deposit in the east. So west = more sand (coarse), east = less sand (fine/silt).

  • Commercial ≠ Plantation (Plantation = single crop in very large area)
  • Black soil = NOT most abundant (Alluvial is most, Black is 3rd)
