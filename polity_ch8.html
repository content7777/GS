Chapter Overview

What is this chapter really about?

Articles 79–88 define the architecture of India's Parliament — who sits in it, how they get there, how long they stay, and what they can do.

The One-Line Summary

Parliament = President + Lok Sabha + Rajya Sabha. Every article from 79–88 zooms into one part of this triangle.

79Constitution of Parliament (who's in it)
80Composition of Rajya Sabha (250 max)
81Composition of Lok Sabha (550 max)
82Delimitation after each Census
83Duration — RS permanent, LS 5 yrs
84Qualifications — age, citizenship
85Sessions, Prorogation, Dissolution
86President's Right to Address
88Ministers + AG rights in Parliament
The Parliament Building Mental Image

Picture a grand triangle-shaped Parliament House. The top floor is Rajya Sabha (upper house — wise elders, 30+). The ground floor is Lok Sabha (lower house — the people, 25+). And standing at the main gate is the President — nothing enters or leaves (becomes law) without passing through the gate.

Where did we borrow this?
🇬🇧
Parliamentary System → borrowed from UK
🇺🇸
USA has Presidential System (not Parliamentary)

The Museum Story

You are a tourist visiting the Parliament Museum. The guard at the entrance hands you a ticket numbered 79. You walk through 10 rooms. Each room has a sign and an exhibit.

🚪 Entrance Gate — "Who's inside?"
Three statues at the entrance: Lok Sabha, Rajya Sabha, President. This is Parliament. You must pass all three.
80

250 blue chairs (238 elected by state assemblies + 12 nominated by President for Literature/Science/Art/Social Service). Only 245 are filled today — 5 UTs without assemblies left empty.

550 green chairs (530 from States + up to 20 from UTs). Used to be 552 — two Anglo-Indian chairs removed after 104th Amendment.
82
📐 Survey Room — "Redrawing the map"
After every Census, a Delimitation Commission redraws constituencies. Formed 4 times: 1952, 1962, 1972, 2002. Frozen till 2026 (42nd Amendment→2000, 84th→2026).
83
⏱️ Clock Room — "Who runs out of time?"
Rajya Sabha clock = ∞ (permanent). Lok Sabha clock = 5 years max. Rajya Sabha members: 6-year term, 1/3 retire every 2 years — like an evergreen forest.
84
🪪 ID Booth — "Are you eligible?"
Citizen of India ✓. Rajya Sabha: age ≥ 30 (elders!). Lok Sabha: age ≥ 25 (youth!). That's why RS = House of Elders.
85
📅 Calendar Room — "When do they meet?"
3 sessions/year. Budget = longest. Winter = shortest. Gap between sessions ≤ 6 months. President summons & prorogues. President can dissolve Lok Sabha only.
86

President gives Special Address at start of first session each year + first session of new Lok Sabha. Called Motion of Thanks. If it fails → government lost majority.

Every Minister + Attorney General can speak in ANY house. But can vote only in their own house. AG is not a member of either house → can never vote.
Article 79 · 🔥 Must Know

Constitution of Parliament

Defines who makes up the Parliament of India.

The Triangle Formula

Parliament = President + Lok Sabha + Rajya Sabha

The President is part of Parliament because no bill can become an Act without Presidential assent.

House Lok Sabha Rajya Sabha
Official Name House of the People Council of States
Also called Lower House / First Chamber Upper House / Second Chamber / House of Elders
Election type Direct election (First Past The Post) Indirect election (Proportional Representation + Single Transferable Vote)
Elected by Citizens directly State Legislative Assemblies
Hindi name adopted 1954
First sitting 17 April 1952
First meeting 13 May 1952
Date Trap — 17 April vs 13 May

First SITTING = 17 April 1952 (like a new student showing up on Day 1 — just sitting there).

First MEETING = 13 May 1952 (actual business starts after some settling in — May is later).

⚡ Rule: If the question says "first meeting" → 13 May. If it says just "first", still tick 13 May — it's the safer answer.

Union Territories in Rajya Sabha

Only UTs with Legislative Assemblies can send representatives to RS.

🏙️
Delhi → 3 seats in Rajya Sabha
🌊
Puducherry → 1 seat in Rajya Sabha
🏔️
Jammu & Kashmir → 4 seats in Rajya Sabha
❌
Other 5 UTs → No legislative assembly → No Rajya Sabha seats
Rajya Sabha Seats — Key States
🏆
Maximum: Uttar Pradesh — 31 seats
📍
Lok Sabha Maximum: Uttar Pradesh — 80 seats
Article 80 · 🔥 Must Know

Composition of Rajya Sabha

The Council of States — how many, from where, and who nominates.

Maximum Strength

250

238 elected + 12 nominated

Current Strength

245

5 seats vacant (5 UTs without assembly)

The 12 Nominated Members

Nominated by the President — for persons with special knowledge or practical experience in:

📚 Literature 🔬 Science 🎨 Art 🤝 Social Service

⚠ These exact 4 fields are mentioned in the Constitution. No others.

Hook: Remember "LASS"

The President nominates people from Literature, Art, Science, Social Service = LASS. Think of a talented lass (young woman) who is too talented to contest elections but deserves to be in Parliament.

Why 245 and not 250?

India has 8 UTs total. Only 3 have Legislative Assemblies (Delhi, Puducherry, J&K) → only they get RS seats. The remaining 5 UTs have no assembly → 5 potential seats are vacant. Hence 250 − 5 = 245 currently.

Article 81 · 🔥 Must Know

Composition of Lok Sabha

The House of the People — maximum seats, reservation, and the Anglo-Indian clause.

Seat Breakdown
🏛️
530 members — chosen by direct election from States
🏙️
Up to 20 members — from Union Territories
🔢
Maximum = 550 (current maximum after 104th Amendment)
104th Constitutional Amendment — Key Change
❌
Stopped: Nomination of 2 Anglo-Indians by President
Before 104th Amend., max was 552 (550 + 2 Anglo-Indian)
✅
Continued: SC/ST reservation in Lok Sabha — extended for another 10 years
ℹ️
Rajya Sabha has NO SC/ST reservation
552 → 550 After 104th Amendment

Before: 550 + 2 Anglo-Indians = 552.
After 104th Amendment (2020): Anglo-Indian nomination removed. Now max = 550.

Think of it as: "One oh four = 2 go out the door."

The Equality Principle (Delimitation)

The Constitution mandates that the ratio of seats : population must be the same across all states. So if UP grows faster than Tamil Nadu, UP gets more seats after the next Census. This is why states like Tamil Nadu oppose delimitation — they followed family planning, reduced population, and fear losing parliamentary seats.

Article 82 · ⭐ Important

Delimitation Commission

Redrawing constituency boundaries after every Census.

What is Delimitation?

After each Census, population changes. Delimitation is the process of redrawing the boundaries and increasing/decreasing seats of constituencies to ensure equal representation (population : seats ratio stays constant).

Delimitation Commission — Key Facts
📌
Mentioned in Constitution but NOT a Constitutional body
4️⃣
Formed 4 times till date: 1952 · 1962 · 1972 · 2002
Remember 52-62-72-2002

The four years follow a pattern: 52, 62, 72 (every 10 years for the first 3), then a big jump to 2002 (skipping 1982 and 1992 due to freeze). Say it fast: "Fifty-two, sixty-two, seventy-two, two thousand and two."

42nd
42nd Amendment — Indira Gandhi
Froze delimitation till 2000. Representation fixed based on 1971 Census.
84th
84th Amendment
Extended freeze till 2026. Still based on 1971 Census.
2027
Census 2027 (upcoming)
After this census, Delimitation Commission will sit again. States like Tamil Nadu are already objecting — they fear losing seats for following family planning.
⚠️ Common Trap

Delimitation Commission is NOT a Constitutional body — it is a statutory body created under a Parliament Act. Students often confuse this. The Constitution mentions it, but it's not created BY the Constitution.

Article 83 · 🔥 Must Know

Duration of Houses

How long do the houses last? When can they be dissolved?

Rajya Sabha

PERMANENT HOUSE ♾️

✅
Never dissolved
📅
Members serve 6 years
🔄
1/3 members retire every 2 years
Lok Sabha

5-YEAR TERM ⏱️

✅
Max term = 5 years
❌
No minimum term (can end in 13 days!)
🔐
Only extended during Emergency, 1 year at a time
The Evergreen Forest Analogy

Rajya Sabha = An evergreen forest. It always looks full and green NOT because leaves never fall, but because they fall one by one (1/3 every 2 years), never all at once. The forest is never completely bare. That's why Rajya Sabha is called a permanent/continuing house.

Emergency Extension of Lok Sabha
📋
Can be extended 1 year at a time during National Emergency
♾️
No cap on total extension — as long as Emergency continues
🏛️
Parliament must approve each extension — it's not automatic
🗓️
After Emergency ends → elections within 6 months
When does President Dissolve Lok Sabha?
1️⃣
No-confidence motion passed against government
2️⃣
PM resigns and no alternative majority is possible
3️⃣
PM dies and no party can prove majority
❌
President CANNOT dissolve Rajya Sabha — it's permanent
Article 84 · ⭐ Important

Qualifications for Parliament Membership

Who can become an MP?

Qualification Lok Sabha Rajya Sabha
Citizenship Must be a citizen of India
Minimum Age 25 years 30 years
Other Any other qualifications as prescribed by Parliament by law
Age Hook: Lower House → Lower Age

Lok Sabha = Lower House = Lower age (25).
Rajya Sabha = Upper House = Upper age (30).

It's a pyramid: the higher you go, the older you need to be. That's literally why Rajya Sabha is called the "House of Elders."

Articles 85–86 · 🔥 Must Know

Sessions, Prorogation & Presidential Address

When does Parliament meet, and what does the President say?

3 Sessions of Parliament
📊
Budget Session — Longest session (Feb–May)
🌧️
Monsoon Session — July–August
❄️
Winter Session — Shortest session (Nov–Dec)
❌
No "Summer Session" — a common trap question!
The 6-Month Rule

The gap between any two sessions (recess period) must NOT exceed 6 months. This ensures at least 2 sessions per year.

Action Who Does It Meaning
Summon President "Come in, let's meet"
Prorogue President "Session is over, go home — come back next session"
Dissolve President "You're done. New elections needed." (Lok Sabha only)
Adjourn Speaker / Chairman "Break within a session"
Prorogue vs Dissolve — The Hotel Analogy

Prorogue = "Check out for now, your reservation is still valid. Come back for the next session." (The same Lok Sabha continues.)

Dissolve = "Hotel demolished. Everyone out. A brand-new hotel will be built after elections."

Article 86 — Presidential Special Address
📅
Happens at: (1) First session of each year AND (2) First session of a new Lok Sabha after general elections
📜
Called Special Address in Article 86; in practice called Motion of Thanks
⚡
If Motion of Thanks is defeated → signals the government has lost majority in Lok Sabha
Article 88 · 🔥 Must Know

Rights of Ministers & Attorney General

Who can speak in which house? Who can vote?

The Rule

Every Minister and the Attorney General can speak in either House of Parliament, but can vote only in the House they are a member of.

Person Member of Can Speak in Can Vote in
PM (Modi) Lok Sabha Both houses Lok Sabha only
Nirmala Sitharaman Rajya Sabha Both houses Rajya Sabha only
S. Jaishankar Rajya Sabha Both houses Rajya Sabha only
Attorney General Neither house Both houses Cannot vote anywhere
The "Spectator with a Mic" Rule

The Attorney General is like a guest anchor on a debate show. He can speak on any channel (Lok Sabha or Rajya Sabha), but since he owns no share in the company (not a member), he has no voting rights anywhere.

Exam Special: No-Confidence Motion Trap

A no-confidence motion can only be introduced in Lok Sabha. If a minister is a Rajya Sabha member (like Manmohan Singh was PM via RS), then during a no-confidence motion:

✅
They can go to Lok Sabha and speak/debate
❌
They CANNOT vote to save their own government

This is a frequently asked question: "In which case can the PM not vote in a no-confidence motion?" → When the PM is a member of Rajya Sabha.

PM from Rajya Sabha — Historical Examples
🏅
Manmohan Singh — PM and Rajya Sabha member (could never have won a direct Lok Sabha election)
🏅
Indira Gandhi — was also a Rajya Sabha member at one point
⚠️ Exam Traps

What Examiners Use to Trip You Up

Every trap here has cost students marks. Read each one carefully.

🪤 Trap 1 — First Sitting vs First Meeting

First Sitting = 17 April 1952 (both houses sat separately for first time)
First Meeting = 13 May 1952 (joint business began)

If no qualifier → go with 13 May 1952.

🪤 Trap 2 — Rajya Sabha Max vs Current

Maximum = 250 | Current = 245
Students write 250 when asked "current strength." Always check if Q says "maximum" or "present/current."

🪤 Trap 3 — Lok Sabha Maximum

Before 104th Amendment: 552 (550 + 2 Anglo-Indian).
After 104th Amendment: 550.
Current actual Lok Sabha = 543 seats elected.
Don't confuse 550 (constitutional max) with 543 (actual).

🪤 Trap 4 — Delimitation Commission is NOT Constitutional

It is mentioned IN the Constitution but it is a statutory body (created by Parliament's law), NOT a Constitutional body (created directly by the Constitution like UPSC, CAG, EC).

🪤 Trap 5 — No Summer Session

Parliament has 3 sessions: Budget, Monsoon, Winter. There is NO summer session. A question may give "summer session" as an option — it's always wrong.

🪤 Trap 6 — Rajya Sabha Dissolution

President can dissolve Lok Sabha ONLY. Rajya Sabha is a permanent house and can never be dissolved by anyone.

🪤 Trap 7 — 12 Nominated Members' Fields

The Constitution says exactly: Literature, Science, Art, Social Service. "Sports" or "Technology" or "Law" are NOT in the list. Watch out for these fake options in exams.

🪤 Trap 8 — Delimitation Dates

Commission formed: 1952, 1962, 1972, 2002 (note: NOT 1982 or 1992 — those were frozen periods).
Frozen by 42nd Amendment till 2000.
Extended by 84th Amendment till 2026.

🪤 Trap 9 — AG Voting Rights

Attorney General can speak in both houses but CANNOT vote in either because he is not a member of any house.

🪤 Trap 10 — Emergency Extension

Lok Sabha can be extended "one year at a time" during Emergency. This does NOT mean just once — it can be extended repeatedly for as long as Emergency continues. Parliament must approve each extension.
