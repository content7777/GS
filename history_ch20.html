Big Picture

Chapter Overview

Delhi Sultanate is the story of Islamic rule in India (1206–1526). After Ghori's invasion, his slave Qutubuddin Aibak founded it. Five dynasties ruled in sequence: Slave → Khilji → Tughlaq → Syed → Lodhi. It ends when Ibrahim Lodi loses the First Battle of Panipat to Babur in 1526, beginning Mughal rule.


For SSC CGL, the most important rulers are: Alauddin Khilji, then Balban, then Iltutmish, then Mohammad Bin Tughlaq. Everything else is supporting cast.

Dynasty Timeline

Chronology
1206–1290

Slave Dynasty

Founded by Qutubuddin Aibak (slave of Ghori). Key rulers: Iltutmish, Balban. Ends with Kaiqubad (Balban's grandson).

1290–1320 🔥

Khilji Dynasty

Jalaluddin Khilji → Alauddin Khilji (the most important ruler). Ended when Ghiyasuddin Tughlaq kills Khusro Khan.

1320–1414 🔥

Tughlaq Dynasty

Ghiyasuddin → Mohammad Bin Tughlaq (wisest fool) → Firoz Shah Tughlaq → Mahmud Shah Tughlaq (Timur attacks). Largest kingdom.

1414–1451

Syed Dynasty

Founded by Khizr Khan (put by Timur's commander). Only ~37 years. Rulers: Khizr → Mubarak → Mohammad → Alam Shah. Known as Kule Daran (cone hat wearers).

1451–1526

Lodhi Dynasty

Afghan. Bahlul Lodhi founder. Sikandar Lodi built Agra (1504). Ibrahim Lodi: last ruler of Delhi Sultanate. Died at First Battle of Panipat (1526) to Babur.

Smart Memory Hooks

Retention Tricks
🦜 Amir Khusro Double Title Hook

Think of a parrot (Tute-Hind) in a court. Alauddin gave the parrot its name (Tute-Hind). The parrot praised back its owner (Khusro called Alauddin "Sultan Jahan"). Two-way exchange. The parrot also sang qawwali. Done. Never forget.

💰 Hazar Dinari Hook

Picture a market stall in Gujarat. A man is sold for exactly 1000 gold coins (Hazar Dinari). The buyer is Alauddin. The slave becomes his greatest general who conquers ALL of South India. The price tag of 1000 = the name forever.

🤡 Wisest Fool Hook

Imagine a genius professor in class: "I will solve poverty, shift the capital, conquer Khurasan, and create a new currency!" Then one by one, each plan flops. Students laugh. He's not dumb — he's the Wisest Fool. Kaifi Khan gave him this tag. And his book? Ibn Batuta = Rihla (not Rihal).

🎩 Kule Daran Hook

Syed Dynasty rulers wore cone-shaped birthday hats. So they were called Kule Daran. Short dynasty, short hats. Founder = Khizr Khan. Last = Alam Shah. Think: birthday party of 37 years that ended when Lodhi arrived.

🏟️ 7 Cities of Delhi Hook

Delhi has 7 historical cities. First = Tomar dynasty. Second = Siri by Alauddin Khilji. That's all you need to know for the exam: Alauddin built SIRI, the 2nd city.

📊 Alauddin's 3 Markets → Sarai Adil

Market 1 = Food. Market 2 = Cloth = Sarai Adil (remember: "Adil" sounds like "textile deal"). Market 3 = Horses & Cattle. The cloth market has the special name. Examiners love this.

⚔️ Power Succession Hook (End of Khilji)

Think of it like a relay race of betrayals:
Alauddin dies → Kafur makes Umar king → Mubarak Khan kills Kafur & Umar → Khusro Khan kills Mubarak → Ghiyasuddin kills Khusro → Tughlaq dynasty begins.
Each person in the relay kills the one before them.

Concept Connections

Mental Network
→

Alauddin's low soldier pay + strict market control = soldiers could still live well because prices were kept artificially low. That's why 3 markets + Shahna + Daag/Chehra were all one system.

→

Token currency (Mohammad Tughlaq) failed because bronze itself was cheap → people minted fake coins at home → economy collapsed. Compare: Alauddin succeeded because he controlled prices, not money supply.

→

Timur's attack → Tughlaq dynasty weakened → Timur's commander placed Khizr Khan → Syed Dynasty began. The Syed dynasty was essentially a puppet of Timur's legacy.

→

Firoz Shah Tughlaq's empty treasury (inherited from Mohammad's failed schemes) forced him to use Wajeha (land payment instead of cash) and impose 4 Quranic taxes to maintain Muslim court support.

→

Daulat Khan Lodi invited Babur → First Battle of Panipat (1526) → Ibrahim Lodi died → Delhi Sultanate ended → Mughal Empire began.

→

Vijayanagar & Bahmani kingdoms emerged during Mohammad Bin Tughlaq's reign — because his failed policies weakened central control, allowing regional kingdoms to rise.

Exam Traps

Common Mistakes
⚠ Trap 1 — Rihla vs Rihal

Ibn Batuta's book is Rihla — NOT "Rihal". Common misspelling. The teacher explicitly warned about this.

⚠ Trap 2 — 3 taxes vs 4 taxes

Alauddin imposed 3 taxes: Ghar, Charai, Kharaj. Firoz Shah imposed 4 taxes: Kharaj, Zakat, Jaziya, Khamz. Don't mix them. BCC already asked about Alauddin's = 3.

⚠ Trap 3 — Diwan-e-Riyasat vs Diwan-e-Risalat

Riyasat = Market control (Alauddin). Risalat = Appeals Department. They sound almost identical. Riyasat = market (think "retail"). Risalat = appeal (think "resolution").

⚠ Trap 4 — Uluq Khan (Multiple Rulers)

Uluq Khan is the original name of BOTH Balban AND Mohammad Bin Tughlaq. Don't let the exam confuse you with this.

⚠ Trap 5 — Tute-Hind direction

Alauddin gave Khusro the title Tute-Hind. Khusro gave Alauddin the title Sultan Jahan. Students often reverse this. Remember: the KING gave the cool parrot name. The POET praised the king.

⚠ Trap 6 — Who built Jaunpur?

Jaunpur was built by Firoz Shah Tughlaq in memory of Jauna Khan (= Mohammad Bin Tughlaq). Not by Mohammad himself.

⚠ Trap 7 — Capital Shift to Agra

Agra was built (1504) and capital was shifted there by Sikandar Lodi, NOT Bahlul Lodi. Don't mix the two Lodhis.

⚠ Trap 8 — Daulatabad = Devagiri

When Mohammad Tughlaq shifted capital to Daulatabad, it was the SAME PLACE as Devagiri (previously the Yadava capital). Daulatabad was its renamed version. Examiners use both names.

⚠ Trap 9 — Padmavat Author

Padmavat was written by Malik Mohammad Jayasi — NOT Amir Khusro. Amir Khusro was Alauddin's court poet, but Jayasi wrote the Padmavat poem much later.

⚠ Trap 10 — Sarai Adil

Sarai Adil was the CLOTH market (not food, not horses). The cloth market had a special name. The food grain market's controller was called Shahna-e-Mandi.
