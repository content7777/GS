01 · Big Picture

What This Chapter Is Really About

This chapter is a fact-dense, date-heavy, name-heavy chapter where SSC loves to test you on who gave which theory, which planet has which unique property, and which came first. Almost every single planet has at least one "only planet that…" fact — memorise those. Theories of the universe and solar system formation are a goldmine for 1-mark questions. The chapter tests breadth, not depth.

🔑 Key insight: If you know every planet's 2–3 unique facts and every theory + its year + its giver, you've covered ~80% of what can be asked.

🧠 The Space Museum Story

Imagine you are walking through a grand Space Museum. Each room is a topic. You walk room to room, and everything attaches itself to your mental map.

1
🚪 Museum Entrance — "BOOM Room"
A massive explosion painting on the wall. Label: "George Lemaitre, 1927 (published 1931)". Next to it, a steady dial with "Fred Hoyle" written — the museum guard who says "everything is steady here."
2
🌀 Room 2 — The Spinning Cloud Room
Kant (1755) first spun the cloud. Laplace (1796) modified it. Then Chamberlain + Moulton (1905) — the year Bengal was partitioned — threw in the Planetesimal theory.
3
☀️ Room 3 — The Sun Throne Room
A massive glowing throne (Sun) at the center. 99% of mass here. Corona visible only during eclipse. Photosphere glows. Sunspots are dark cold patches. Solar flares shoot out.
4
🪨 Room 4 — The Rocky Inner 4
Four rocky chairs: Mercury (fastest lap, no air), Venus (hottest, backward spinner, beautiful goddess name), Earth (blue water world), Mars (red, twin volcanos, Phobos & Deimos).
5
💨 Room 5 — The Gas Giants Hall
Jupiter: largest, fastest spin, Great Red Spot, Galileo's moons (1610). Saturn: rings (Huygens 1655), lowest density, floats on water. Uranus: rolls sideways, green (methane), William Herschel 1781. Neptune: farthest, slowest revolution, Le Verrier's math prediction.

Pluto (demoted 2006, Clyde Tombaugh), Eris (2nd largest), Haumea (fastest spin), Makemake (310 yrs), Ceres (asteroid belt — all others in Kuiper belt).

Origin of the Universe

Scale: Universe → Galaxy → Solar System → Planet

🔥 Universe = Cosmos. Study of universe = Cosmology. Universe contains many galaxies; galaxies contain thousands of solar systems.

Memory trick for 1905: Bengal partition happened in 1905. The Chamberlain-Moulton theory also split from the nebular theory in that same year. Two splits in 1905!

☀️ The Sun

🔥 Only star of our solar system. Powerhouse of the solar system.

Contains 99% of total mass of solar system.

Composition: 73% Hydrogen · 25% Helium · ~2% others

Size: 109 times the diameter of Earth.

Distance from Earth: 150 million km (average); perihelion = 147M km, aphelion = 152M km.

Light travel time: ~8 min 20 sec to reach Earth.

Layers of the Sun

LayerPositionSpecial Feature
CoreInnermostHighest temperature
PhotosphereMiddle / visibleGlowing visible surface; source of solar flares
CoronaOutermostVisible ONLY during total solar eclipse
⚠ Sunspot Trap

Sunspots = darker, cooler areas on the Sun's surface. NOT hotter. Examiner loves reversing this!

🌕 The Moon

  • • Natural satellite of Earth; non-luminous (reflects sunlight)
  • • Distance from Earth: 3,84,000 km
  • • Moon's gravity = 1/6 of Earth's gravity
  • • Rotation = Revolution = 27.3 days → We always see only one side of Moon
  • • Highest point: Mons Huygens (also called Leibnitz Mountains)

04 · Smart Memory Hooks

🧠 Memory Hooks

💣

Big Bang = A bomb that created everything. The bomb-maker? Lemaitre. His bomb exploded in 1927, and the explosion report was published in 1931. The universe is still expanding from that blast. Fred Hoyle (the opposing guard) says "relax, it's steady!"

🏃

Revolution Speed = Distance from Sun. Close to Sun → short orbit → fast revolution. Far from Sun → long orbit → slow revolution. Mercury (closest) = fastest. Neptune (farthest) = slowest. No exception needed, just remember the logic!

♀️

Venus = The Rebel Planet. Hottest (not Mercury!), Brightest, Morning Star, Evening Star, No satellite, Goddess name, Rotates backwards, Earth's twin, Slowest rotation. If a question says "the most ___ planet," always consider Venus first.

🔭

Galileo's discovery: 1610. Galileo Galilei saw Jupiter's 4 big moons through his telescope. Those 4 moons are called Galilean moons in his honour. The largest is Ganymede — remember: "G for Galileo, G for Ganymede = Grandest moon."

🪐

Saturn floats! Density = 0.69 g/cm³ (water = 1.0). So if you had a giant bathtub, Saturn would float in it. Every other planet sinks. Great exam fodder: "Which planet has the lowest density?" → Saturn.

🔬

William Herschel discovered TWO things in 1781: Uranus (planet) AND infrared rays. One man, one year, two discoveries. Remember: "Herschel went outside in 1781, felt heat (infrared) and looked up to see Uranus."

📐

Neptune found by maths, not eyes. Le Verrier calculated where an unknown planet must be based on gravitational anomalies. John Galle pointed the telescope exactly there in 1846 and found Neptune. "Le Verrier did the math, Galle did the looking."

06 · Exam Traps

⚠️ Exam Traps & Common Confusions

⚠ Mercury vs Venus — Hottest Planet

Mercury is CLOSEST to Sun but Venus is HOTTER. Mercury has no atmosphere, so heat escapes. Venus has thick CO₂ + H₂SO₄ atmosphere — traps heat = extreme greenhouse effect.

⚠ Meteor vs Meteorite vs Meteoroid

Meteoroid = in space. Meteor = burning in atmosphere (shooting star). Meteorite = survived, landed on Earth. Exam often swaps meteor and meteorite!

⚠ Big Bang: 1927 or 1931?

Lemaître proposed it in 1927, but it was published/widely recognised in 1931. Some books say 1931. Know BOTH.

⚠ Largest Constellation = Hydra (NOT Ursa Major)

Ursa Major is famous (Saptarishi) but Hydra is the largest constellation. Students confuse fame with size.

⚠ Sunspots are COOLER (not hotter)

Sunspots appear dark because they are cooler than surrounding surface. Examiner will say "hottest part" — wrong!

⚠ Ceres is in Asteroid Belt, NOT Kuiper Belt

All other dwarf planets (Pluto, Eris, Haumea, Makemake) are in Kuiper Belt. Ceres alone is in Asteroid Belt.

⚠ Venus has no satellite (nor does Mercury)

Only Mercury & Venus have NO natural satellites. Mars has 2 (Phobos, Deimos). Don't confuse.

⚠ Earth is densest planet (NOT largest)

Earth's density = 5.5 g/cm³ = highest. Jupiter is biggest but much lower density.

⚠ Deimos = smallest satellite (not Phobos)

Mars has 2 moons — Phobos and Deimos. Deimos is the smallest satellite in the solar system. Examiners like to reverse this.

⚠ Mons Huygens ≠ Huygens who discovered Saturn's rings

Mons Huygens = highest point on Moon. Huygens (Christian Huygens) = discovered Saturn's rings in 1655. Same name, different contexts!

08 · Active Recall

❓ Self-Test Questions

Try to answer each question in your head, then reveal the answer.

Q1. Which planet is hottest and why — even though it's not closest to the Sun?
Venus. Thick atmosphere of CO₂ + H₂SO₄ creates extreme greenhouse effect, trapping heat. Mercury has no atmosphere so heat escapes.
Q2. Fill in the blank: Moon's rotation period = _____ days = Revolution period. This causes us to always see _____ side of the Moon.
27.3 days. One (same) side.
Q3. Name the two planets that rotate clockwise. What direction do others rotate?
Venus and Uranus rotate clockwise (east→west). All other planets rotate anti-clockwise (west→east).
Q4. The Big Bang theory was given by _______ in _______ (two years — explain the difference).
George Lemaître. Proposed in 1927, published in 1931. Both years may appear in different books/exams.
Q5. What is the Galilean Moons? Who discovered them and when? Which is the largest?
4 largest moons of Jupiter: Io, Europa, Callisto, Ganymede. Discovered by Galileo Galilei in 1610. Ganymede is the largest (also largest moon in solar system).
Q6. Arrange in increasing order of density: Saturn, Jupiter, Earth, Mars.
Saturn (0.69) < Jupiter < Mars < Earth (5.5 g/cm³ — densest planet).
Q7. A meteoroid enters Earth's atmosphere and partially burns but some lands on Earth. What is it called when burning? What when it lands?
While burning in atmosphere = Meteor (shooting star). After landing on Earth = Meteorite.
Q8. Which dwarf planet is in the asteroid belt? Where are the rest?
Ceres is in the asteroid belt. All others (Pluto, Eris, Haumea, Makemake) are in the Kuiper Belt.
Q9. Name the planet: largest volcano, smallest satellite in solar system, red colour, 2 moons.
Mars. Olympus Mons (largest volcano), Deimos (smallest satellite), Iron oxide = red, Moons = Phobos & Deimos.
Q10. What is William Herschel famous for discovering in 1781? (Hint: two things)
William Herschel discovered: (1) Uranus — first planet discovered by humans. (2) Infrared rays.
