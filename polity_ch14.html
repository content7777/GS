Big Picture

What This Chapter Is Really About

India's governance runs on three kinds of bodies. The Constitution either created them directly, Parliament gave them birth through Acts, or the government just issued an order. That simple 3-way split decides: how they're created, how hard they are to kill, and how independent they are.

Constitutional Bodies
Article in Constitution (hardest to remove)
Statutory Bodies
Created by Parliament's Act (moderately protected)
Executive Bodies
Govt. Order / Resolution (easiest to kill)
💡 Master Key Insight: NITI Aayog replaced Planning Commission. Both were Executive bodies — that's WHY Modi could dissolve Planning Commission in a single day, no Parliament needed.
Chapter Memory Palace

🧠 The Supreme Court Courthouse Journey

🏛️ Walk through the Courthouse of India's Governance
Gate
The Attorney General (76) stands at the gate in a lawyer's robe — he's the gatekeeper lawyer of the entire government. No fixed tenure, serves at President's pleasure.
Lobby
CAG (148–151) sits in the lobby counting money from a huge purse labelled "Public Purse." He's the guardian of the public purse. Alone — no deputy — single-member body. Term: 6 yrs or 65 yrs.
Courtroom
Election Commission (324–329) is in Courtroom 15 (Part 15!). 1 Chief + 2 Commissioners sit on the bench. They control ALL elections except Panchayat/Municipality.
Treasury
Finance Commission (280) is in the Treasury room: 1 Chairman + 4 Members deciding how to split the nation's tax money between Centre and States.
HR Dept
UPSC (315–323) is in the HR Department — they hire people for India Inc. Part 14 of the building. Term: 6 yrs or 65 yrs, resign to President.
SC/ST Wing
National Commissions (338, 338A, 338B) — three offices in one wing. SC→338, ST→338A, OBC→338B. Each separated by constitutional amendments.
Outside
NHRC, CVC, CBI, NITI Aayog are outside the building. No constitutional article, created by Act (NHRC, CVC) or executive order (CBI, NITI Aayog).
Complete Revision Notes

📋 All Constitutional Bodies

1. Attorney General of India (AG)

⚖️ Attorney General · Mahanyayvadi
Article 76 · Part V · Chapter I (Union Executive)

🔥 Highest Law Officer of India — the Central Government's lawyer

🔥 Appointed by President · Serves at President's pleasure · NO fixed tenure

⭐ Qualification: Must be eligible to be a Supreme Court judge (HC advocate 5 yrs OR HC judge 5 yrs OR HC advocate 10 yrs OR distinguished jurist)

🔥 Rights: Audience in ALL courts in India · Can participate in Parliament (both houses) — can speak, CANNOT vote

⭐ Gets all Parliamentary Privileges & Immunities of an MP

➕ Salary determined by President

🔥 First AG: M.C. Setalvad (also chairman of first post-independence Law Commission 1955)

🔥 Current AG: R. Venkataramani

⭐ Assisted by Solicitor General (NOT a constitutional body) · Current SG: Tushar Mehta (appointed June 2023)

🔥 State equivalent: Advocate General → Article 165 (76 + 89 = 165 trick!)

2. Comptroller & Auditor General (CAG)

🔍 CAG · Niyantrak Evam Mahalekhapareekshak
Articles 148–151 · Part V · Chapter V (last chapter)

🔥 Single-member body (no deputy CAG, no assistant commissioners — unlike ECI/UPSC)

🔥 Guardian of the Public Purse (= Consolidated Fund of India, Art. 266)

⭐ Guide & Philosopher of the Public Accounts Committee (PAC)

🔥 Appointed by President

🔥 Term: 6 years OR 65 years of age, whichever is earlier

🔥 Removal: Same as Supreme Court judge (Judges Inquiry Act 1968) — Lok Sabha: 100 members, Rajya Sabha: 50 members → 3-member committee → Special majority (2/3 total members) → President removes

🔥 NOT eligible for reappointment

⭐ Salary determined by Parliament

➕ Head of Indian Audit and Accounts Department

🔥 First CAG: V. Narhari Rao

⭐ Current CAG: appointed to audit WHO data; from same state as President of India

Article duties: 149 = duties by Parliament · 150 = accounts kept as prescribed by President on CAG's advice · 151 = Audit report → President (central) / Governor (state) → tabled in Parliament/Legislature

Compiles & maintains State Government accounts (Central accounts removed by 42nd Amendment 1976)

CAG's advice to President is NOT binding on President

3. Election Commission of India (ECI)

🗳️ Election Commission of India
Articles 324–329 · Part XV (15)

🔥 Permanent & Independent body (independence reduced by 2023 amendment — see below)

🔥 Composition: 1 + 2 (Chief Election Commissioner + 2 Election Commissioners)

🔥 Was single-member at formation → became multi-member first time in 1989 (after 61st Amendment reduced voting age 21→18 in 1988, enforced 1989)

⭐ Appointment: President (now via Selection Committee: PM + Cabinet Minister + Leader of Opposition)

🔥 Term: 6 years OR 65 years

⭐ Salary earlier = SC judge; now = Cabinet Minister (Parliament can change)

🔥 NOT eligible for reappointment

🔥 Conducts: Lok Sabha · Rajya Sabha · State Assemblies · Legislative Councils · President & Vice President elections

➕ State Election Commission → Panchayats & Municipalities (separate)

🔥 Powers: Administrative + Advisory + Quasi-judicial

🔥 First Chief Election Commissioner: Sukumar Sen

⭐ Current CEC: Rajiv Kumar · Commissioners: Gyanesh Kumar + Sukhbir Singh Sandhu (SSS hint)

Key Articles: 324 = Superintendence, direction & control · 325 = No exclusion from electoral roll (religion/race/sex/birth) · 326 = LS + Legislative Assembly elections · 329 = Bar to court interference in electoral matters

4. Finance Commission of India

💰 Finance Commission
Article 280 · Quasi-judicial powers

🔥 Composition: 1 + 4 (1 Chairman + 4 Members)

🔥 Constituted by President

⭐ Eligible for reappointment (unlike CAG, ECI, UPSC)

⭐ Qualification of Chairman = knowledge of public affairs (determined by Parliament)

🔥 Recommendations to President → NOT binding

🔥 Functions: Distribution of net tax proceeds between Centre & States · Grants-in-aid to states · How consolidated fund of states should ensure adequate funds for Panchayats & Municipalities

⭐ First Chairman: K.C. Niyogi

⭐ 15th Finance Commission: Headed by N.K. Singh

🔥 16th Finance Commission: Headed by Arvind Panagariya (from 2026)

5. National Commissions for SC / ST / OBC

🏛️ National Commissions (SC/ST/OBC)
Articles 338, 338A, 338B

🔥 Originally = Special Officer → 65th Amendment converted it to a Commission (SC+ST together)

🔥 89th Amendment (2002? No — check: Article 21A, Art 45 subject changed, Art 51A sub-k added... 86th was education, 89th separated SC & ST) → National Commission for SC went to Art 338, National Commission for ST went to Art 338A

🔥 338B = National Commission for Backward Classes → created by 102nd Constitutional Amendment (previously statutory)

⭐ Composition: Chairman + Vice Chairman + 3 other members · Appointed by President

➕ 106th Amendment = 1/3 seats reserved in Parliament & State Legislature for Women

6. Public Service Commission (UPSC / State PSC)

📝 UPSC & State PSC
Articles 315–323 · Part XIV

🔥 JPSC (Joint PSC) = NOT constitutional → created by Parliament through an Act (Statutory body)

🔥 Term: 6 years OR 65 years for UPSC/JPSC · 6 years OR 62 years for State PSC

⭐ Resignation: UPSC/JPSC → to President · State PSC → to Governor

🔥 Removal: Only President can remove (even State PSC members!)

⭐ Grounds for removal: Bankrupt · Paid employment · Misbehaviour · Incapacity · Insanity

🔥 After retirement: CANNOT hold any office of PSC or any lower post (anti-favouritism rule)

➕ Can become Chairman of UPSC from Member. Cannot go to same or lower post.

⭐ Expenses from CFI (UPSC/JPSC) or CFS (State PSC) — Article 322

➕ Reports: UPSC → President · State PSC / JPSC → Governor (Article 323)

Article 315 = Appointment · 317 = Removal · 318 = Regulations · 319 = Prohibition after retirement · 320 = Functions · 321 = Extension of functions · 322 = Expenses · 323 = Reports

7. Inter-State Council & Zonal Councils

🤝 Inter-State Council
Article 263 · Constitutional (President establishes)

🔥 Chairman: Prime Minister

⭐ Members: CMs of all states + CMs of UTs with Assemblies (Delhi, Puducherry) + LGs where no CM + 6 Cabinet-rank Union Ministers (nominated by PM)

⭐ Sarkaria Commission (1988) recommended making it a permanent body

➕ Punchhi Commission (2007) also gave recommendations on interstate relations & Governor's role

🗺️ Zonal Councils
States Reorganisation Act, 1956 · NOT in Constitution

🔥 Total = 5 Zonal Councils (North, South, East, West, Central)

🔥 Chairman: Home Minister (same for all 5)

⭐ Vice-Chairman: CM on rotational basis

🔥 North-East Council: Separate — created by North Eastern Council Act, 1972

➕ States Reorganisation Act 1956 → established 14 states & 6 Union Territories (after Fazal Ali Commission / States Reorganisation Commission)

📋 Non-Constitutional Bodies

1. NHRC — National Human Rights Commission

⚖️ NHRC · Statutory Body
Protection of Human Rights Act, 1993

🔥 Composition: 1 + 5 (Chairperson + 5 members, who are ex-officio chairs of NCW, NCSC, NCST, Minorities Commission)

⭐ Appointment by President

➕ National Commission for Women: established 1992, Act passed 1990 (gap of 2 years!)

2. Central Information Commission (CIC)

📂 CIC · Statutory Body
Right to Information Act, 2005

🔥 RTI is a Fundamental Right under Article 19

⭐ Term: 5 years OR 65 years · NOT eligible for reappointment

⭐ Appointment: President

➕ State equivalent: State Information Commission → appointment by Governor

3. Central Vigilance Commission (CVC)

👁️ CVC · Statutory Body (since 2003)
CVC Act, 2003 (established 1963 as executive body)

🔥 Was Executive body (1963) → became Statutory body (CVC Act 2003)

⭐ Composition: 1 + 2 (same as ECI!)

🔥 Term: 4 years OR 65 years (note: unlike most others which are 6 years!)

⭐ Anti-corruption agency — only about corruption (unlike CBI)

4. Lokpal & Lokayukta

🏛️ Lokpal · Statutory Body
Lokpal & Lokayukta Act, 2013

🔥 Anna Hazare movement: 2011–12 → Act passed: 2013

🔥 Composition: 1 + 8 (Chairperson + 8 members)

⭐ Chairperson = retired Chief Justice of India or judge

⭐ Of 8 members: half from SC/ST/OBC + half judicial members

🔥 Can investigate up to Prime Minister's office level

🔥 Lokayukta: at state level, can go up to Chief Minister

➕ First Lokpal: Piraki Chandra Ghosh

➕ Current Lokpal: Ajay Manik Rao Khanvilkar

➕ Maharashtra = first state to establish Lokayukta (1973) — not mandatory then

➕ First Lokayukta in India: Nagaur, Rajasthan (2 Oct 1959)

5. CBI — Central Bureau of Investigation

🔎 CBI · Executive Body
Established 1964 · Derives power from Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, 1946

🔥 Executive body — no act of its own, derives power from DSPE Act 1946

⭐ Earlier under Ministry of Home Affairs → now under Ministry of Personnel

➕ Handles not just corruption — also other crimes (rape cases, etc.)

6. Law Commission of India

📜 Law Commission · Executive Body
No constitutional article, no act

⭐ Pre-independence (1833): First Law Commission → Chairman: Thomas Babington Macaulay

🔥 Post-independence (1955): Established → First Chairman: M.C. Setalvad (also first AG!)

⭐ Current (23rd Law Commission): Headed by Dinesh Maheshwari

7. NITI Aayog

🚀 NITI Aayog · Executive Body
National Institution for Transforming India · Est. 1 Jan 2015

🔥 Replaced Planning Commission (also executive — that's why it was easy to abolish)

🔥 Chairman: Prime Minister (ex-officio)

⭐ Vice Chairperson: Appointed by PM · Current: Suman K. Beri

⭐ Vice Chairperson enjoys rank of Cabinet Minister

⭐ CEO: BVR Subramaniam

➕ Governing Council: All CMs + CMs of UTs with assemblies + LGs of other UTs

➕ Planning Commission brought 5-Year Plans; NITI Aayog does NOT bring 5-Year Plans

Smart Memory Hooks

🪝 Memory Hooks That Fight Forgetting

Hook 1 · Article Number Trick

Part V (Central) + 89 = Part VI (State equivalent)
Attorney General Art 76 → Advocate General Art 76+89=165 ✓
Money Bill Art 110 → State Money Bill Art 110+89=199 ✓
Memorize Part V articles, get Part VI FREE.

Hook 2 · The "6 and 65" Pattern

CAG, ECI, UPSC (Central), Finance Commission → all follow 6 years OR 65 years
State PSC → 6 years OR 62 years (state = 62, centre = 65)
CVC is the rebel → 4 years OR 65 years
CIC → 5 years OR 65 years

Hook 3 · Who Removes State PSC Members?

Appointment of State PSC = Governor · But Removal = President always
Think: "The Governor can hire but can't fire — only the President can fire."

Hook 4 · Home Minister vs Prime Minister

Zonal Council Chairman = HOME MINISTER (5 councils, 1 HM chairs all)
Inter-State Council Chairman = PRIME MINISTER
Hook: Zones are internal HOME affairs → Home Minister. Interstate = national → PM.

Hook 5 · Who is Reappointable?

CANNOT be reappointed: CAG, ECI Commissioners, CIC Members, UPSC/State PSC members (same or lower post)
CAN be reappointed: Finance Commission members
Hook: Finance Commission is the exception — money people can come back!

Hook 6 · The 89th Amendment Split

Think "89 = split" → 89th Amendment split SC+ST Commission into two:
338 = SC (Scheduled Caste) · 338A = ST (Scheduled Tribe) · 338B = OBC (Backward = Beyond, 102nd amendment)

Hook 7 · NCW Timeline Confusion Buster

NCW Act = 1990 · NCW Established = 1992 · Remember: "Act before establishment" by 2 years.

Hook 8 · Single vs Multi Member Bodies

Single member: CAG (alone with the public purse)
Multi-member: ECI (1+2), Finance Commission (1+4), NHRC (1+5), Lokpal (1+8), UPSC (Chair + members)
Remember CAG is the only loner among constitutional bodies.

Exam Traps

⚠️ Exam Traps

⚠️ AG vs Advocate General
  • AG = Central Govt lawyer → Article 76
  • Advocate General = State Govt lawyer → Article 165
  • Trap: Examiners ask "highest law officer of state" → Advocate General NOT AG!
⚠️ Solicitor General — Not Constitutional!
  • SG assists AG but is NOT a constitutional body
  • SSC JE actually asked about new SG appointment — always current affairs trap
  • Current SG: Tushar Mehta
⚠️ CAG vs ECI — Removal Confusion
  • Both removed like Supreme Court judge (Special majority, etc.)
  • But CAG salary → Parliament · AG salary → President
  • CAG = single member · ECI = 1+2 multi-member
⚠️ JPSC — Most Students Think It's Constitutional
  • UPSC → Constitutional · State PSC → Constitutional · JPSC → NOT Constitutional (Statutory!)
  • JPSC is created by Parliament through an Act as per states' needs
⚠️ Age Confusion: 62 vs 65
  • UPSC/JPSC = 65 years · State PSC = 62 years
  • CVC = 4 yrs OR 65 (NOT 6 yrs!) · CIC = 5 yrs OR 65
⚠️ NCW Act Year vs Establishment Year
  • NCW Act = 1990 · NCW Established = 1992
  • Students always mix these up!
⚠️ ECI Conducting Elections — What It Does NOT Do
  • ECI conducts: Lok Sabha, Rajya Sabha, State Assembly, Council, President, Vice President
  • ECI does NOT conduct: Panchayat & Municipality elections → State Election Commission does!
⚠️ Finance Commission — Eligible for Reappointment!
  • CAG → NOT eligible · ECI → NOT eligible · UPSC → NOT eligible
  • Finance Commission → IS eligible for reappointment (the only major exception!)
⚠️ 86th vs 89th Amendment
  • 86th Amendment (2002): Right to Education (Art 21A), changed Art 45 subject, added Art 51A(k)
  • 89th Amendment: Split SC+ST Commission
  • Examiners love to swap these!
⚠️ Zonal Council vs Inter-State Council Chairman
  • Inter-State Council → PM is Chairman
  • Zonal Council → HOME MINISTER is Chairman
  • North-East Council → separate act (1972), NOT part of States Reorganisation Act
⚠️ CVI vs CBI
  • CVC → Statutory (2003) · Anti-corruption only
  • CBI → Executive · Derives from DSPE Act 1946, not its own act · Handles multiple crime types
⚠️ Art 329 — Courts Cannot Interfere in Elections
  • Article 329 bars court interference in electoral matters
  • This is why SC/HC appear "silent" on election commission decisions during elections
