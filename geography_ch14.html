Big Picture

What this chapter is really about

India has 3 seasons (Winter · Summer · Monsoon). The chapter answers ONE master question: WHY does rain fall where it falls in India and when? Everything — Western Disturbances, Arabian Sea branch, Bay of Bengal branch, rain shadows, retreating monsoon, El Niño — is an answer to that one question. Keep this thread and you won't get lost.

Chapter Memory Palace

STORY FRAMEWORK
The Scene

You are standing on a giant weather control station shaped like India. Walk through it room by room — each room = one major concept.

1
ENTRANCE GATE — Factors Affecting Climate

Six bouncers guard the gate: Latitude Altitude Pressure/Wind Relief Ocean Current Distance from Sea. The bouncer LONGITUDE was fired — he doesn't affect climate at all.

2
ROOM 1 — Winter Season

Cold Arctic winds rush in from Central Asia. The Himalaya bouncer BLOCKS them. But up in the sky, a Mediterranean cyclone arrives by jet-stream taxi → drops rain on Rabi crops. Rajasthan calls this rain "Mawat".

3
ROOM 2 — Pre-Monsoon Showers

Before the main event, three warm-up acts appear on stage: Nor'westers / Kal Baisakhi (West Bengal+NE), Bardoi Chira / Bordoi Shila (Assam), Blossom & Mango Showers (Kerala / coastal Karnataka).

4
ROOM 3 — The Monsoon Engine Room

Huge machine in the centre: Sun heats Tibetan Plateau like a frying pan → intense LOW pressure over India. Madagascar sits in a cool pool → HIGH pressure. Winds rush from HIGH to LOW = Monsoon Born. Easterly Jet Stream at 14-15°N provides final ignition.

5
ROOM 4 — Two Branches Hall

Monsoon splits into two river channels. Arabian Sea Branch: hits Western Ghats → orographic rain → rain shadow (Vidarbha dry). Bay of Bengal Branch: drenches Andamans → Khasi Hills (Mawsynram/Cherrapunji) → deflects west. Both branches meet like old friends in Punjab Plains by 15 July.

6
ROOM 5 — The Exit & Return

Monsoon exits NW India first. Coriolis Force spins it → becomes North East Monsoon (Retreating Monsoon). Now it rains on Coromandel Coast (Tamil Nadu) in Oct–Nov. Cyclones form on exit. Sticky October Heat fills the hall.

7
BASEMENT — El Niño / La Niña Control Panel

Two buttons: El Niño (Boy) weakens trade winds → hot water piles in E Pacific → destroys Peru fishing → weak/delayed Indian monsoon. La Niña (Girl) = opposite = strong Indian monsoon. Pressure difference measured between Darwin & Tahiti. Negative = El Niño.

02 — Factors Affecting Climate
LATITUDECloser to equator = hotter. Closer to poles = colder. Latitudes affect climate; longitudes do NOT.
ALTITUDEHigher altitude = colder. Lapse Rate = 6.5°C per 1 km (= 1°C per 165 m). This is in the Troposphere.
PRESSURE & WINDTrade winds, Westerlies, local winds like Loo (advection — horizontal heat transfer).
RELIEFWestern Ghats → windward gets rain, leeward = rain shadow (Vidarbha dry).
OCEAN CURRENTWarm currents = more rain. Cold currents near coasts = deserts (Atacama, Mojave). Most deserts on western sides of continents in tropics.
DISTANCE FROM SEANear sea = moderating influence (Mumbai, Chennai — not extreme). Far from sea = continentality effect (Delhi, Kanpur — very hot summers, very cold winters).
03 — Winter Season
  • 🔥 MUST KNOW Cold winds from Central Asia / Arctic → blocked by the Himalayas
  • Sun below equator → slanting rays → cold in India
  • Western Disturbances = frontal cyclones from Mediterranean Sea → travel via Jet Streams (9–13 km altitude, upper troposphere) → cause winter rainfall in North India
  • Called Mawat in Rajasthan
  • 🔥 MUST KNOW Western Disturbances are beneficial for the Rabi crop (wheat etc. sown in winter)
  • Night clouds during winter = morning rain coming (clouds trap outgoing radiation)
  • Jet streams causing winter rain = Westerly Jet Streams

06 — Two Branches of the Monsoon
Arabian Sea Branch
  • Hits Western Ghats → orographic/relief rainfall
  • Windward = wet (Mumbai, Mangalore)
  • Leeward = rain shadow (Vidarbha, Marathwada → dry)
  • Splits into 3 sub-branches
  • Sub-branch 1: flows parallel to Narmada river (between Vindhya & Satpura)
  • Sub-branch 2: flows parallel to Aravalli
  • Sub-branch 3: meets Bay of Bengal branch
Bay of Bengal Branch
  • First hits Andaman & Nicobar → evergreen forests
  • Hits Karbi Anglong Plateau, Meghalaya Hills
  • Khasi Hills (Mawsynram & Cherrapunji) = highest rainfall in world
  • Deflects west → meets Arabian Sea branch in Punjab Plains
⚠ Why Coromandel Coast gets NO rain in summer

Arabian Sea branch doesn't reach it. Bay of Bengal branch runs parallel to the Coromandel coast → passes by without raining. Result: Tamil Nadu coast stays dry in SW Monsoon season!

⚠ Why Thar Desert is dry

Aravalli runs parallel to the Arabian Sea branch → doesn't block wind → no orographic rain. Both branches pass by. Result: Great Indian Desert (Thar) in NW of Aravalli = permanent rain shadow / desert.

07 — Retreating Monsoon (Oct–Nov)
  • Monsoon withdraws from NW India first
  • Coriolis Force deflects retreating winds to the right (Northern Hemisphere) → wind now comes from NE direction
  • Called North East Monsoon or Retreating Monsoon
  • 🔥 Now rains on Coromandel Coast (Tamil Nadu) — which got no rain in SW Monsoon!
  • Cyclones form during retreating monsoon → hit Odisha, Andhra Pradesh coast (Oct–Nov)
  • October Heat = sticky, humid heat as monsoon exits — sun comes out suddenly, humidity stays high

09 — Continentality vs Moderating Influence of Sea
Coastal Cities (Moderate)

Mumbai, Kochi, Chennai — near sea → summers NOT very hot, winters NOT very cold. Diurnal & annual range of temperature = LOW.

Inland Cities (Extreme)

Delhi, Kanpur, Jalandhar — far from sea → very hot summers, very cold winters. Diurnal & annual range of temperature = HIGH.

Annual Rainfall Range Rule (SSC Exam)

Where rainfall is HIGH → annual range of rainfall is LOW (consistent). Where rainfall is LOW → annual range is HIGH (erratic). Inland areas = erratic. Coastal = consistent.

Smart Memory Hooks

Hook 1 — Pre-Monsoon Showers

Think of a Bengali wedding (Kal Baisakhi = calamity at the wedding in the month of Baisakh). The wedding is in an Assamese tea garden (Bardoi Chira). The wedding cake smells like coffee blossom from Kerala (Blossom Shower). And the centerpiece is a mango from Karnataka (Mango Shower). One wedding = all four pre-monsoon showers remembered.

Hook 2 — El Niño vs La Niña

El Niño is a naughty boy who reverses everything — winds reverse, water reverses, Peru's fish die, India's rain weakens. La Niña is a good girl who restores normal — good rains for India, good fishing for Peru. Boy = bad news for India. Girl = good news.

Hook 3 — Koeppen small w vs capital W

Imagine a small child (small w) saying "I'm dry in winters" — she's in the Ganga plains, perfectly normal. Now imagine a CAPITAL wrestler (capital W) who lives in the DESERT — only the Thar can handle him. Small = winter dry. BIG = DESERT.

Hook 4 — Why Thar is a Desert

The Aravalli mountains are like a bad host — they run parallel to the monsoon winds instead of blocking them. The monsoon just walks past without stopping. No blocking = no orographic rain = no rain = desert. Aravalli "forgot" to stand perpendicular to the wind.

Hook 5 — Retreating Monsoon gives Tamil Nadu its rain

Tamil Nadu missed the SW Monsoon party. But when the monsoon is leaving (Oct–Nov), Coriolis spins it around — now it's a North East wind with water picked up from the Bay. Tamil Nadu finally gets its invitation. What one season denies, the next season delivers.

Hook 6 — Lapse Rate Numbers

Remember: "6.5 at 1 km" — Six and a half degrees for every kilometre you climb. Or: every 165 metres = 1°C drop (165 = 1°C, 1 km = 6.5°C). The mountain is a natural refrigerator that gets colder by 6.5°C every floor.

⚠ Exam Traps

LONGITUDE vs LATITUDE

Longitude (N-S lines) does NOT affect climate. Latitude (E-W lines) DOES. Common trick question: "which of these does NOT affect climate?" → Answer = Longitude.

SMALL w (winter dry) vs CAPITAL W (desert)

small w = winter dry (e.g., Cwg for Ganga plains). Capital W = desert (only with B, e.g., BWh for Thar). Appeared in CGL Mains! One letter, one capital = completely different meaning.

WESTERLY JET STREAM vs EASTERLY JET STREAM

Westerly Jet Stream → brings Western Disturbances → WINTER rain in N India. Easterly Jet Stream (at 14-15°N, June-July) → facilitates MONSOON arrival. Wrong jet stream = wrong season!

MAWSYNRAM vs CHERRAPUNJI

Both in Khasi Hills, Meghalaya. Mawsynram = highest AVERAGE annual rainfall. Cherrapunji = historically famous for highest rainfall records. Both can appear in questions — note the difference.

SW MONSOON vs NE MONSOON

SW Monsoon (June–Sept) = main monsoon, gives rain to MOST of India EXCEPT Tamil Nadu coast. NE Monsoon (Oct–Nov) = retreating monsoon, gives rain to Tamil Nadu coast. Many students get these backwards!

EL NIÑO = BAD / LA NIÑA = GOOD (for India)

El Niño weakens/delays Indian monsoon → drought risk. La Niña strengthens Indian monsoon → flood risk. Don't flip these. El Niño = bad boy = bad news for India.

DARWIN vs TAHITI (El Niño Measurement)

Pressure difference measured between Darwin (Australia) and Tahiti (E Pacific). If this difference is NEGATIVE → El Niño conditions. Students often confuse which city is which.

BLOSSOM SHOWERS vs MANGO SHOWERS

Both occur in Kerala and Karnataka. Blossom Showers help coffee FLOWERS bloom. Mango Showers help mangoes RIPEN early. Different effect, same region. Don't swap them in MCQs.

WINDWARD vs LEEWARD

Windward side = facing the wind = gets rain. Leeward side = sheltered from wind = rain shadow, dry. Mumbai is windward (wet). Vidarbha is leeward (dry). Know which side is which for both Western Ghats AND Aravalli.

KAL BAISAKHI vs BARDOI CHIRA

Kal Baisakhi = West Bengal (also NE India and Bangladesh). Bardoi Chira / Bordoi Shila = Assam only. Both are pre-monsoon Nor'westers but named differently region-wise.

Active Recall Section

Click each question to reveal the answer. Test yourself before looking!

What is the difference between weather and climate?
CLICK TO REVEAL
Weather = short-term atmospheric changes. Climate = long-term pattern (~30 years of data).
Which factor does NOT affect climate — latitude or longitude?
CLICK TO REVEAL
LONGITUDE (north-south lines). Only LATITUDE (east-west lines) affects climate.
What is the lapse rate? Give the exact figures.
CLICK TO REVEAL
Temperature decreases by 6.5°C per 1 km altitude rise (= 1°C per 165 metres). This occurs in the Troposphere.
What are Western Disturbances? Which crop do they benefit?
CLICK TO REVEAL
Frontal cyclones originating in the Mediterranean Sea. Travel to India via Westerly Jet Streams. Cause winter rainfall. Benefit RABI crops (wheat etc.).
What is Mawat? Where is this term used?
CLICK TO REVEAL
Mawat = winter rainfall in Rajasthan (caused by Western Disturbances).
Name all pre-monsoon showers with their regions and benefits.
CLICK TO REVEAL
1) Kal Baisakhi / Nor'westers → West Bengal + NE → jute & tea. 2) Bardoi Chira / Bordoi Shila → Assam → tea. 3) Blossom Showers → Kerala & coastal Karnataka → coffee flowers. 4) Mango Showers → Kerala & Karnataka → early ripening of mangoes.
What is the Mascarene High and why does it matter for India?
CLICK TO REVEAL
Mascarene High = intense high pressure zone east of Madagascar. This high pressure pushes winds toward India's low pressure zone → drives the South West Monsoon.
What is the ITCZ? Where does it shift during July?
CLICK TO REVEAL
ITCZ = Intertropical Convergence Zone — where NE and SE trade winds meet. In July, it shifts to 20°–25° North latitude → creates severe low pressure → triggers monsoon.
Which Jet Stream helps monsoon arrive? At what latitude does it blow?
CLICK TO REVEAL
Easterly Jet Stream at 14–15° North latitude (in June–July). Different from Westerly Jet Stream (which brings winter rain).
Where do both branches of the monsoon meet?
CLICK TO REVEAL
Punjab Plains. By 15th July, monsoon covers all of India.
Why does Vidarbha receive very little rainfall?
CLICK TO REVEAL
Vidarbha lies in the RAIN SHADOW (leeward side) of the Western Ghats. The Arabian Sea branch drops all its rain on the windward side (Mumbai/Mangalore), leaving nothing for Vidarbha.
Why does the Thar Desert exist?
CLICK TO REVEAL
The Aravalli range runs PARALLEL to the Arabian Sea branch. It doesn't block the wind → no orographic lifting → no rain. Both monsoon branches pass without raining on NW India.
What is the Retreating Monsoon? Why does Tamil Nadu get rain from it?
CLICK TO REVEAL
Also called NE Monsoon (Oct–Nov). As SW Monsoon retreats from NW India, Coriolis Force deflects it → becomes NE wind. Picks up moisture from Bay of Bengal → rains on Coromandel Coast (Tamil Nadu).
What is El Niño? Spanish meaning? Effect on Indian monsoon? Frequency?
CLICK TO REVEAL
El Niño = "Child Christ / Small Boy" (Spanish). Trade winds weaken → warm water reverses to E Pacific → Madagascar High weakens → Indian monsoon weakens/delays. Occurs every 3–7 years.
Where is pressure difference measured to track El Niño?
CLICK TO REVEAL
Between Darwin (Australia) and Tahiti (E Pacific). Negative pressure difference = El Niño conditions.
In Koeppen's classification, what does B-type represent? Why is it unique?
CLICK TO REVEAL
B = Dry climate. It is the ONLY Koeppen type based on PRECIPITATION (not temperature). Evaporation > Precipitation. Example: Thar Desert (BWh).
Fill in the blank: Koeppen gave his classification in _____ and it is called _____ classification.
CLICK TO REVEAL
1884. Empirical classification (based on observed temperature and precipitation data).
What is October Heat? When and why does it occur?
CLICK TO REVEAL
Occurs in October when monsoon is retreating. Sun comes out suddenly after monsoon rains, combined with high humidity → creates sticky, suffocating heat. Called "October Heat."
