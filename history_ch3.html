Section 01
Big Picture
This chapter is the story of why Indians — soldiers, tribals, kings, peasants — all said "enough" to the British between 1764 and 1857. It starts with small, scattered fires (regional revolts) and culminates in the Great Fire of 1857. The British doused it — but it permanently changed how India was governed.
Root Cause

British interference with land, religion, culture & identity of Indians

Spark

Greased cartridges — cow + pig fat combined — attacked Hindu + Muslim sentiments together

Result

Company rule ended. Crown rule began. Canning became first Viceroy.

Imagine you are walking through a burning palace with 7 rooms. Each room holds one chapter cluster. Walk through it every time you revise.

You enter through a gate with three locks: Cornwallis 1793 Bengal (Zamindari), Munro 1820 Madras (Ryotwari), Bentinck 1822 Bengal-Punjab (Mahalwari). Each lock has a name plate. Memorise the plate = memorise the system.

🕌 The Prayer Hall (Regional Revolts)

Hindu priests (Sanyasi 1764) and Muslim mullahs pray together. A warrior from Orissa (Paika 1817) storms in. Ahom king watches from Assam corner (1828). Coal miners bang drums (Coal Mutiny 1831). Birsa Munda stands at the altar (Munda 1899).

3
🍒 The Kitchen (Doctrine of Lapse)

Dalhousie sits at a table eating kingdoms like cherries: Satara 1848 → Jaipur 1849 → Jhansi 1853 → Nagpur 1854 → Awadh 1856. Each cherry swallowed = one kingdom gone. Awadh is "the cherry about to fall in his mouth."

4
🔫 The Armoury (Immediate Cause)

Mangal Pandey throws down a greased cartridge. It smells of beef AND pork. Every soldier — Hindu and Muslim — recoils. The armoury explodes on 10 May 1857.

5
🏰 The Throne Room (Leaders)

Old Bahadur Shah Zafar sits on the throne — more symbol than soldier. General Bakht Khan commands the troops. Five doors lead to five cities: Delhi, Lucknow, Kanpur, Jhansi, Bihar — each with their leader.

6
🌹 The Garden (British Suppressors)

Rose gave a rose to Rani (Jhansi). Campbell rang the Kanpur bell (Ghantghar). Lawrence in Lucknow. Nicholson in Delhi. Remember the garden's four corners.

7
👑 The New Throne Room (Aftermath)

The Company's logo is torn off the throne. Queen Victoria hangs her portrait. Canning gets a new title: Viceroy. Peel Commission is formed to fix the army ratio. Secretary of State + 15-member council is created.

Section 04
Land Revenue Systems

Root cause of tribal and peasant unrest. 3 systems — remember Who, When, Where for each.

System Introduced By Year Region Key Feature
Permanent Settlement
(Zamindari System)		 Lord Cornwallis 1793 Bengal, Bihar, Odisha Zamindar pays fixed tax to British. Sunset Clause: miss sunset = lose Zamindari. Based on Ricardian Theory of Rent.
Ryotwari System Munro & Reed 1820 Madras (South India) Direct collection from Rayat (farmer). No middleman.
Mahalwari System William Bentinck (+ Holt Mackenzie) 1822 Bengal, Punjab, NWFP Mahal = group of villages. Headman collects, pays British.

Section 05
Causes of the 1857 Revolt
Cause 1 — General Service Enlistment Act 🔥
  • Introduced by Canning (then Governor General, not yet Viceroy)
  • Indian soldiers could be posted overseas in any British colony
  • In Hinduism, crossing the sea (kala pani) = loss of caste purity
  • Soldiers = deeply resistant
Cause 2 — Doctrine of Lapse (Dalhousie) 🔥

No natural male heir (even adopted son excluded) → Kingdom goes to British.

KingdomYear AnnexedMethod
Satara1848Doctrine of Lapse
Jaipur / Sambhalpur1849Doctrine of Lapse
Jhansi1853Doctrine of Lapse
Nagpur1854Doctrine of Lapse
Awadh1856Misgovernance (NOT Doctrine of Lapse)
Dalhousie on Awadh: "Awadh is like a cherry that is about to drop into the mouths of the British." — asked in multiple CGL + CDS exams.
Cause 3 — Greased Cartridges (Immediate / Trigger Cause) 🔥
  • Cartridges were greased with beef fat (forbidden in Hinduism) + pork fat (forbidden in Islam)
  • To load the rifle, soldiers had to bite the cartridge
  • Mangal Pandey (34th Bengal Native Infantry) refused → shot an officer → hanged 8 April 1857
  • 24 April 1857: 3rd Native Cavalry refused en masse
  • 10 May 1857: Revolt officially begins → Delhi Chalo campaign

Section 07
Aftermath & Consequences
What Changed After 1857 🔥
  • East India Company abolished — British Crown (Queen Victoria) took direct control
  • Governor General → Viceroy: Canning became the first Viceroy of India
  • Secretary of State post created — British Parliament member; assisted by 15-member council
  • Peel Commission formed → recommended reducing the ratio of Indian soldiers in the army
  • Commercial functions of EIC were removed in 1833; administrative functions now removed too
Why the Revolt Failed
  • Limited territorial extent — confined to North India; South India largely unaffected
  • No unified leadership — everyone fighting for their own kingdom, not for India
  • No political vision — no concept of "free India"; no national ideology
  • No coordination among leaders across centres
  • Some Indian princes and zamindars actively supported the British
Historians' Interpretations 🔥
  • VD Savarkar: Called it the "First War of Independence"
  • RC Majumdar: "Neither first, nor national, nor a war of independence" — (exact line, frequently asked)

Section 10
⚠ Exam Traps
⚠ Trap 1 — Awadh was NOT taken by Doctrine of Lapse

Awadh was taken under the rule of misgovernance (Nawab Wajid Ali Shah = too busy with Kathak and music). Doctrine of Lapse requires no natural heir. Awadh had an heir. Don't confuse.

⚠ Trap 2 — Moplah 1836 vs Moplah 1920

Both are in Malabar. Both are called Moplah Uprising. The 1920 version is during the Non-Cooperation Movement. The 1836 version is a standalone colonial-era revolt. If a question gives a year, use the year. They rarely appear in the same options.

⚠ Trap 3 — Rani Lakshmibai's Tomb is in GWALIOR, not Varanasi

Manikarnika Ghat is in Varanasi (where she was born / named). But she died fighting in Gwalior — her tomb is there. Her birth name was Manu (short for Manikarnika).

⚠ Trap 4 — Sanyasi Revolt Year is debated

Some books say 1763, some say 1764. Teacher's note: it happened in the 1760s. Don't panic if you see both — focus on leaders and the Anand Math connection.

⚠ Trap 5 — Indigo Revolt is AFTER 1857

Indigo Revolt = 1859. It is 2 years after the 1857 Revolt. Students mistakenly place it before. Remember: indigo farmers were fighting a new battle after the big revolt.

⚠ Trap 6 — Nana Saheb vs Tatia Tope

Nana Saheb = adopted son of Baji Rao II (last Peshwa), led from Kanpur initially. Tatia Tope = Nana Saheb's military commander who continued the fight and also aided Rani Lakshmibai in Gwalior. They are two different people.

⚠ Trap 7 — General Service Enlistment Act introduced by CANNING (not Dalhousie)

Dalhousie did Doctrine of Lapse. Canning brought the General Service Enlistment Act. Two different GGs. Don't swap them.
