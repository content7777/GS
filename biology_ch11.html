🍞 Carbohydrates

Primary energy source · 4 cal/gram · Simple & Complex

🔥 Core Facts Must Know
  • Function: Energy supply
  • Energy yield: 4 cal per gram
  • Simplest form: Glucose / Sugar
  • Test for sugar: Benedict's solution
  • Plants store carbs as starch
  • Salivary amylase breaks starch → simple sugars
📐 Classification
SIMPLE CARBS
🔹 Monosaccharides — 1 sugar molecule
🔹 Disaccharides — 2 sugar molecules
COMPLEX CARBS
🔹 Polysaccharides — many sugar molecules
(dissolves into many monomers)

🧠 Memory Hook — The Mono/Di/Poly Building Block Story
Imagine sugar as LEGO bricks. One brick = Monosaccharide. Snap two together = Disaccharide. Build a massive castle = Polysaccharide.

For disaccharides remember: SM-L rule → Sucrose, Maltose, Lactose.
Lactose = Milk (Lac = Latin for milk). Maltose = Grains (think malted barley). Sucrose = Sweet table sugar.
⚠ Lactose Intolerance: Lactose passes through the small intestine undigested — the body lacks the enzyme lactase.

🥑 Fats (Lipids)

High-energy storage · 9 cal/gram · Adipose tissue

🔥 Core Facts
  • Energy yield: 9 cal per gram (highest!)
  • Storage location: Adipose tissue (below skin)
  • Bile juice → emulsifies fat (from liver, stored in gall bladder)
  • Lipase → breaks fat into fatty acids (from pancreatic juice)
  • Nuts (almonds, walnuts) → contain Omega-3 (good fat)
🔢 Fat Types Ranking (Best → Worst)
✅ Unsaturated — Easy to digest · Vegetable oils, plant fats, nuts, omega-3
⚠ Saturated — Hard to digest · Animal fat, meat, pork · increases triglycerides
💀 Trans Fat — WORST · Reused cooking oil, chips · Blocks arteries · causes triglycerides
🧠 Memory Hook — The Oil Pan Story
Imagine a chef re-frying chips for the 5th time in the same oil. That oil is turning black and toxic = TRANS FAT. A doctor walks in and says "Your arteries look like clogged drains!" → Trans fat is the villain of the fat world.

BLAG = Bile (emulsify) | Lipase (break) | Adipose (store) | Gall bladder (bile stored here)
🔗 Concept Connection
Bile juice
Produced by LIVER, STORED in Gall Bladder → emulsifies fats
Lipase
From pancreatic juice → breaks fat into fatty acids
Adipose tissue
Fat-storing tissue located below the skin
Omega-3
Unsaturated fat found in walnuts, almonds → good for heart
Cholesterol in egg yolk
Good cholesterol + Vitamin D + fat + vitamins (not harmful if moderate)
⚠ Exam Trap
Students confuse: Where is bile produced vs. stored?
✅ Bile is produced by LIVER but stored in GALL BLADDER. Don't swap them!

💪 Proteins

Body building nutrients · Growth & Repair · 20 amino acids

🔥 Core Facts
  • Called "body building nutrients"
  • Made of amino acids
  • Total amino acids: 20
  • Essential: 9 (body can't make → must eat)
  • Non-essential: 11 (body makes on its own)
  • Egg white = protein | Egg yolk = fat + vitamins
  • Best vegetarian source = Soybean

🔬 Collagen — Special Star Protein 🔥 High Yield
  • Most abundant protein in human body
  • Found in skin, bones, joints
  • Structure: Triple Helical
  • Discoverer: G.N. Ramachandran (Indian scientist)
  • For absorption of collagen → Vitamin C is essential
Compare Structures:
DNA → Double Helix → Watson & Crick (1953)
Collagen → Triple Helix → G.N. Ramachandran

Hook: DNA is a couple (double), Collagen is a trio (triple)!
🧠 Memory Hook — 9 vs 11 Rule
ESSENTIAL = 9 → Think: "I need 9 of these or I'll DINE OUT" (you go outside = must consume from food)
NON-ESSENTIAL = 11 → Body makes 11 at home. Total = 20.

Hook for collagen: "Ramachandran has THREE strands of hair — just like his triple helix collagen!"
⚠ Exam Trap
DNA double helix → Watson & Crick (1953)
Collagen triple helix → G.N. Ramachandran
These are DIFFERENT structures discovered by DIFFERENT scientists. Very common confusion!

B3 deficiency makes you a 3D monster: your mind goes dark (Dementia), your skin explodes (Dermatitis), and your gut revolts (Diarrhea). All three start with D = 3D disease.
🧠 Hook — Vitamin K for K(oagulation)

  • Deficiency → Rickets (kids) → Osteomalacia (soft bones) → Osteoporosis (porous bones)
⚠ Exam Traps — Vitamin Edition

  • B12 deficiency → Pernicious Anemia (not iron-deficiency anemia)
  • Vitamin D = sunshine vitamin = ALSO a hormone. Students forget the hormone part!
  • Vitamin E → infertility. Vitamin K → bleeding. Easy to swap!

🌿 Photosynthesis & Plastids

Plant nutrition · Chloroplast · Light & Dark Reactions

📐 Photosynthesis Equation 🔥
CO₂ + H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + O₂
  • Takes place in Chloroplast
  • Plants store food as Starch (complex carbohydrate)
  • Salivary amylase digests starch → simple sugars
⚡ Light Reaction (Light Dependent)
  • Occurs in: Grana (thylakoids)
  • What happens: Light splits water → H + O
  • Needs: Light
  • Produces: Energy (ATP, NADPH)
🌑 Dark Reaction (Light Independent)
  • Occurs in: Stroma
  • What happens: CO₂ → Glucose
  • Also called: Calvin Cycle
  • Does NOT need direct light
🧠 Hook — Grana vs Stroma
GRANA = LIGHT → Grana sounds like "Grandma lit the stove" (stove = thylakoid disc = light reaction)
STROMA = DARK → Stroma = Shadows. Dark reaction in the shadowy fluid.
🌈 Plastids — The Plant Cell's Organelle Factory 🔥

Note: Plastids have their own DNA and ribosomes (like mitochondria)

PlastidColorFunctionSub-types
Chloroplast🟢 Green (chlorophyll)Photosynthesis — makes food—
Chromoplast🟡🟠 VariousProvides color to flowers/fruits—
Leucoplast⚪ ColorlessFood storageAmyloplast (carbs), Elaioplast (fat), Aleuroplast (protein)
🧠 Why is Chlorophyll Green?
Chlorophyll contains Magnesium (Mg) metal.
It ABSORBS blue and red light → REFLECTS green light → therefore looks green!
Hook: "Mag the magician reflects green and absorbs everything else."
🔬 Chloroplast Structure
Thylakoid
Disc-shaped sac-like structure inside chloroplast
Grana
Bundle/stack of thylakoids → Light reaction here
Stroma
Fluid filling chloroplast → Dark/Calvin cycle here
⚠ Exam Trap
  • Light reaction = Grana (NOT stroma). Dark reaction = Stroma (NOT grana). Swap = Wrong!
  • Calvin cycle = Dark reaction = Light INDEPENDENT reaction (not the same as "happens at night")
  • Leucoplast has 3 sub-types: remember AEA = Amyloplast, Elaioplast, Aleuroplast
  • Chloroplast has its own DNA — similar to mitochondria (endosymbiotic theory)

🔄 Modes of Nutrition

Autotrophic vs Heterotrophic · All organisms

📊 Nutrition Mode Tree ⭐
ModeWhoWhat they doExamples
Autotrophic Plants, Phytoplankton Make their own food (photosynthesis) All green plants
Holozoic Humans, Amoeba Ingest food → digest → excrete waste Humans, Amoeba (food vacuole)
Saprophytic Decomposers Feed on dead/decaying matter Fungi, Yeast, some bacteria
Parasitic Parasites Derive nutrition from living host (harming host) Cuscuta (plant), Plasmodium, Nematodes/Roundworms
🧠 Hook — Saprophyte vs Parasite
Saprophyte = Scavenger. Eats what's already dead. (Like a vulture)
Parasite = Pickpocket. Steals from the living. Harms the host. (Like a blood-sucking leech)

Cuscuta = the vine that climbs up a healthy plant and drains it. Classic exam parasite!
🔬 Amoeba — Holozoic in Action
  • Extends pseudopods (false feet) around food particle
  • Engulfs food → forms food vacuole inside
  • Digests inside → excretes waste out
  • This entire process = Holozoic nutrition
⚠ Exam Trap
  • Cuscuta = plant parasite (NOT saprophyte). It's alive, climbs living plants → parasitic.
  • Nematodes (roundworms, Ascaris) = parasites living in small intestine → pseudocoelomates with cylindrical body
  • Plasmodium = parasite causing malaria (NOT saprophyte)

⚠️ Exam Traps

What students get wrong · Study this before every exam

🔴 TRAP 1 — Bile: Production vs Storage
❌ Students say: "Liver stores bile"
✅ Correct: Liver PRODUCES bile. Gall Bladder STORES bile.
🔴 TRAP 2 — DNA vs Collagen Helices
❌ Students confuse discoverers
✅ DNA Double Helix → Watson & Crick (1953)
✅ Collagen Triple Helix → G.N. Ramachandran (Indian scientist)
🔴 TRAP 3 — Types of Anemia
❌ Students write "iron deficiency" for B12 deficiency
✅ Iron deficiency → Anaemia (regular)
✅ B12 (Cyanocobalamin) deficiency → Pernicious Anaemia
🔴 TRAP 4 — Vitamin D is a Hormone!
❌ Students only call it a vitamin
✅ Vitamin D is also considered a HORMONE. This fact is frequently tested.
🔴 TRAP 5 — Light vs Dark Reaction Locations
❌ Students swap Grana and Stroma
✅ Light reaction → GRANA (thylakoids)
✅ Dark reaction (Calvin cycle) → STROMA
🔴 TRAP 6 — Calorie Values
❌ Students say fat = 4 cal/g (confusing with carbs/protein)
✅ Fat = 9 cal/g (highest) | Carbs = Protein = 4 cal/g
🔴 TRAP 7 — Vitamin B7 = Vitamin H
❌ Students don't know Biotin has an alternate name
✅ Vitamin B7 = Biotin = Vitamin H — all three names for same vitamin
🔴 TRAP 8 — Cuscuta is a Parasite NOT Saprophyte
❌ Students call Cuscuta a saprophyte
✅ Cuscuta climbs LIVING plants and drains them → Parasitic (not feeding on dead matter)
🔴 TRAP 9 — Vitamin A and Night Blindness
❌ Students also add xerophthalmia but forget the name
✅ Vitamin A (Retinol) → Night Blindness + Xerophthalmia (dryness of eyes)
🔴 TRAP 10 — Mono vs Di vs Poly Classification
❌ Students put Disaccharides in Complex
✅ Simple = Mono + Di. Complex = Poly only.
