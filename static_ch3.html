🗺️ Big Picture

What is Folk Dance?

Folk dances are community celebrations — performed by ordinary people during harvest, sowing, rain, birth, weddings, festivals. No expert training needed. Anyone can perform. They belong to the land, not the stage.

Classical dance (8 total) requires years of formal training → expert performers. Folk dance is for the people → infinite variety, no fixed count.

Key Distinction — Remember This Forever
  • Classical = Requires expertise, formal gurukul/training, stage performance
  • Folk = Community celebration, anyone can participate, performed at life events
  • Chhau is a folk-style martial dance — Ministry of Culture considers it Classical
  • Bihu = Dance + Festival both (previously asked in SSC!)

🧠 Chapter Memory Palace
A journey through an imaginary "Dance Museum of India" — walk through it mentally to recall every state.
The Story: India's Dance Museum

You enter a grand circular museum. The entrance arch says "FOLK DANCES OF INDIA." You walk clockwise through halls arranged alphabetically. Each hall has a unique scene that burns the dances into memory.

🚪 Hall A1 — Andhra Pradesh

A copper plate on the floor. Banjara tribals making "burr" from Bamlu (bamboo) branches. The Lambadi madam in tall dress walks by. HOOK: Bamlu + Burr = Butta Buma Lu; Banjara tribe.

🚪 Hall A2 — Arunachal Pradesh

Two sisters — Aruna and Rekha — are arguing. Aruna tells Rekha's husband: "Go to your daughter-in-law wearing a MASK and watch Lion + Peacock dance!" You see "CHAM" written on the mask. HOOK: Aji-Vancho, Buiya, Paasi-Kanki, Chalo, Popin, Bardo Chham → wherever you see CHAM = Arunachal OR Sikkim.

🚪 Hall A3 — Assam

A Bodo tribal woman dances Baguramba. A daughter-in-law beats her husband to death → BIHU (festival + dance). A Jhumur jhumjhum sound plays. HOOK: Bodo→Baguramba. Bihu=dance+festival. Jhumur(Assam) ≠ Jhumar(Haryana).

🚪 Hall B — Bihar

A DJ plays bohemia rap. Jat-Jatan people rap "Foreigners rap Dhajia-Dhiya in Bihar because they're fans of Bohemia." Paika and Jharni posters on the wall. HOOK: Jat-Jatan, Videshiya, Jhijhiya, Jharni, Paika, Kajri, Dibiya.

🚪 Hall C — Chhattisgarh

A parrot (SUA) watches lazy cultists kill Gediya with Pandavas, dancing all the while. "Pandavani Teejan Bai" poster on wall — 2009 Padma Vibhushan. HOOK: Sua=parrot dance. Pandavani=Padma Vibhushan 2009. Karma, Saila also here.

🚪 Hall G1 — Goa

Mandvi (a girl, like river Mandvi of Goa) went to Goa to see a Jaguar car and fly kites. "Don't cry, sleep" the elder says → FUGDI. Horse cart racing nearby → DHALO. HOOK: Mando, Fugdi, Dhalo, Dekhni, Corridor.

🚪 Hall G2 — Gujarat

Durga fights Mahishasur with DANDIYA sticks during Navratri. Krishna plays GARBA with Gopis. Matki filled with water (water shortage area). HOOK: Garba+Dandiya=must know. Matukdi=Matki of water. Bhavai, Huḍo, Siddhi Dhamal, Tippani, Padhar.

🚪 Hall H — Haryana

GUGGA (a don in goggles) walks in. Panchkula → CHARKULA. Holi fog → Phag. Manjira jingles everywhere. Loor dance by women. HOOK: Gugga, Charkula(Panchkula→Charkula), Loor, Manjeera, Dhamal, Khoriya, Dauf, Phag, Jhumar.

🚪 Hall H2 — Himachal Pradesh

Short boy Aman (=peace, ironic — no peace here) wears a demon's robe, puts a garland on a bull, beats a Dang drum, leaves a Chhamm print. HOOK: Jhora, Jhali, Chham, Danda Naach, Chhapa, Dangi Naach.

🚪 Hall J&K — Jammu & Kashmir

Pakistani bowler Harris Rauf is jumping and dancing — he won from Canada but will be left in the dust (Dhumal). Bhand Pathar puppets mock him. HOOK: Hikat, Kud, Rauf, Dhumal, Damal, Bhand Pathar.

🚪 Hall Jh — Jharkhand

Birsa MUNDA walks in → MUNDARI dance. Eastern India corner — Santhali, Fagua, Karma, Paika, Jhumar, Damkach, Chhau. HOOK: Munda→Mundari. Chhau = Eastern India (Jharkhand+Orissa+Bihar). Jhumar≠Jhoomar.

🚪 Hall K — Karnataka

Your friend speaks frankly: "Say less, yaar!" A ghost in doll costume (Dollu Kunitha) destroys his bravery (Veeragase). Yakshagana story plays out. HOOK: Karga, Veeragase, Dollu Kunitha, Yakshagana, Suggi Kunitha.

🚪 Hall Ke — Kerala

Every dance here ends in "-KALI" or "-ATTAM." You see TT (double-T) everywhere — Ottam Thulla, Thitambu Nritham. Kolkali, Curvakali, Thiruvathirakali. HOOK: Kali=Kerala. Double-TT=Kerala or Tamil Nadu. Nee Attam→Kerala, Li Attam→Tamil Nadu.

🚪 Hall M — Manipur

People get MONEY (Manipur). They buy KHAMBA and LAI (=chakna). They drink ethanol all night and perform RAAS LEELA. Thangtaviya (martial) poster. Pung drum (dholak-type). HOOK: Raas Leela, Khamba-Thoibi, Lai Haraoba, Dhol Cholom, Thang-ta (martial).

🚪 Hall Me — Meghalaya

Three hills: GARO (Bengali dance=Festival of 100 Drums), KHASI (naked child running in rain gets cough=Nongkrem), JAINTIA. HOOK: Garo→Wangala (100 Drums Festival). Khasi→Nongkrem. Jaintia→Laho.

🚪 Hall Mi — Mizoram

"Khullam Khulla Chillam Chal Rahi" — Cheraw (bamboo dance) and Khuallam filling the stage. Chheihlam and Zangtalam. HOOK: Cheraw (bamboo dance)=most famous. Khuallam, Chheihlam, Zangtalam.

🚪 Hall N — Nagaland

Multicoloured snakes crawl. People eat dogs cooked in bamboo with potatoes and tea → Chang Lo / Chang Cow eats Chang → Changs. HOOK: Chang Lo, Zeliang, Rengma, Naga tribal dances.

🚪 Hall O — Odisha (Orissa)

Pua ate dal (Pua=one who ate dal). He roams with a stick (Rappa). Karma, Jhoomar, Mahari, Chhau all in the same room. Mayurbhanj district sign. HOOK: Mahari, Karma, Jhoomar, Chhau, Paika, Rappa, Pua. Teejan Bai connects Chhattisgarh+Odisha.

🚪 Hall R — Rajasthan

GHOOMAR (Bhil tribe). GANGAUR (Shiv-Parvati worship). KALBELIYA (snake tribe, Gulabo Sapera). Raja + Susani (Hrithik's ex-style) clapping by Ganga. HOOK: Ghoomar=Bhil tribe. Kalbeliya=snake tribe=Gulabo Sapera. Gangaur dedicated to Parvati. Chari, Terahtali.

🚪 Hall Si — Sikkim

CHAM again (remember: Arunachal or Sikkim). Chu Faat festival dedicated to Mt. Kanchenjunga (worshipped as God). Singhi Chham = Snow Lion dance. HOOK: Singi Chham, Yak Chham, Chhutkey Naach, Chu Faat (festival+dance).

🚪 Hall T — Tamil Nadu

Attam here is different — "Li Attam" (Kolattam) and "Di Attam" (Kummi). Kavadi festival → Kavadi dance. Double-TT rule still applies. HOOK: Kolattam, Kummi, Kavadi, Oyilattam, Karagam, Karakam.

🚪 Hall Te — Telangana

Lambadi madam from Andhra is still here (Telangana split June 2, 2014 — same Banjara beans). Madhuri ji comes singing with oil in hair. HOOK: Perini, Lambadi, Dapppu, Gusadi. Perini=ancient warrior dance revived.

🚪 Hall Tr — Tripura

Biju Patnaik lost in Orissa → becomes GARIYA (VK Pandya's mother type situation). Hojagiri=graceful headbalancing dance. HOOK: Gariya, Hojagiri, Lebang, Hai-Hak.

🚪 Hall UP — Uttar Pradesh

UP afternoon: 90 people playing cards. FM 93.2 plays Kajri. Someone waiting for Ram Leela. Going home for Nautanki. Raas Leela in Mathura. CHARKULA dance (circular pot on head). HOOK: Ram Leela, Raas Leela, Nautanki, Kajri, Khayal, Charkula.

🚪 Hall UK — Uttarakhand

Bordering Himachal Pradesh (short Aman's neighbour). Similar printed patterns. Choliya, Jhora, Tand, Naata, Langvir Nritya. HOOK: Same as Himachal border vibes. Choliya=war dance of Kumaon weddings.

🚪 Hall WB — West Bengal

KKR wins. Gambhir is coach. "Bolo bolo" → if name ends in "-a" sound like "Purulia" → West Bengal. Gambhir's runs = Chhau of Purulia. HOOK: Chhau (Purulia), Gambhira, Brita, Jhumur (eastern type).

🏝️ Union Territories
  • Ladakh: Bucket Dance, Shondol Dance, Khato Janmo (Khatak Chanmo)
  • High Dance / Fusion Dance: Not state-specific — promoted by Uday Shankar (Ravi Shankar's brother, sitar maestro)

🌴 South India — Folk Dances
Andhra Pradesh · Telangana · Kerala · Tamil Nadu · Karnataka

Andhra Pradesh 🔥

Key Dances

Butta Buma Lu, Kolu Attam, Lambadi, Dappu, Veeranatyam, Bhoomi Kalas (earth element), Dhimsa

🧠 Memory Hook

BAMLU → BURR → BUTTA BUMA LU: Brave Banjara tribals make "burr" using Bamlu bamboo branches. Tall Lambadi madam walks by.

  • Bhoomi Kalas = Earth element, stand on copper plate, dance
  • Lambadi → Banjara tribe (also in Telangana — same people, split state)
  • Dhimsa → tribal folk dance

Telangana 🔥

Key Dances

Perini Sivatandavam, Lambadi, Dappu, Gusadi, Ojha Nritya

🧠 Memory Hook

Telangana split from AP on June 2, 2014. Lambadi/Banjara people didn't move — they're still there! Madhuri ji comes out singing after applying oil in hair → Perini (ancient warrior dance revived for Kuchipudi-style masculine energy).

Kerala 🔥

Key Dances

Kolkali, Curvakali, Thiruvathirakali, Thitambu Nritham, Ottam Thulla, Nee Attam, Kaikottikali, Thappu Kali

🧠 Memory Hook — THE KALI RULE

Wherever you see "KALI" or "ATTAM" → think Kerala first.

Double-T rule: Any dance with "TT" (Ottam, Thitambu) → Kerala OR Tamil Nadu (95% cases).

NEE Attam → Kerala. Li Attam / Di Attam → Tamil Nadu. Easy separation!

Tamil Nadu 🔥

Key Dances

Kolattam, Kummi, Karagam, Kavadi, Oyilattam, Karakam, Puli Attam

🧠 Memory Hook

Kavadi = festival + dance (like Kavad Yatra). Kolattam = stick dance (like Kolkata? No! TN). Kummi = clapping dance. Remember: "Li-Attam and Di-Attam are Tamil Nadu's Attams."

Karnataka ⭐

Key Dances

Yakshagana, Dollu Kunitha, Veeragase, Karga, Suggi Kunitha, Siddhi Dhamal

🧠 Memory Hook

"Say less (KAR-na-taka = KAR = less)" — Karga, Veeragase (bravery). Ghost in doll costume = Dollu Kunitha. Yaksha + Gana = Yakshagana (dramatic folk theatre-dance).

🏔️ North India — Folk Dances
Haryana · Himachal · Uttarakhand · Uttar Pradesh · J&K · Punjab

Haryana 🔥

Key Dances

Gugga, Charkula, Loor, Manjeera, Dhamal, Khoriya, Dauf, Phag, Jhoomar

🧠 Memory Hook

GUGGA = Don in goggles. PANCHKULA (city in Haryana) → CHARKULA (similar sound). Holi fog → PHAG. Manjira jingling at fairs → MANJEERA. JHOOMAR = Haryana (≠ Jhumur of Assam ≠ Jhumar of Eastern India).

⚠️ TRAP

Jhoomar (Haryana) ≠ Jhumur (Assam) ≠ Jhumar (Eastern India = Jharkhand/Odisha/Chhattisgarh). Three different spellings, three different states!

Himachal Pradesh ⭐

Key Dances

Jhora, Jhali, Chham, Danda Naach, Chhapa, Dangi Naach, Nati

🧠 Memory Hook

Short boy AMAN (short → Jhora) puts on demon's robe (Dangi/Danda), puts garland on bull, hits Dang drum, leaves Chham print. CHAM here → also in Arunachal & Sikkim. Remember HP+Arunachal+Sikkim all have Cham-type dances.

Uttarakhand ➕

Key Dances

Choliya, Jhora, Tand, Naata, Langvir Nritya, Chhopati

🧠 Memory Hook

Uttarakhand borders Himachal → same printed patterns, similar dances. Choliya = warrior dance performed at Kumaoni weddings (sword + shield). Langvir Nritya = acrobatic folk dance unique to UK.

Uttar Pradesh 🔥

Key Dances

Ram Leela, Raas Leela, Nautanki, Kajri, Khayal, Charkula, Dadadari

🧠 Memory Hook

UP afternoon: Cards, FM Radio 93.2 (Kajri song), waiting for Ram Leela, doing Nautanki at home, Raas Leela in Mathura. Charkula = circular pot balanced on head (also in Haryana but mainly UP).

Jammu & Kashmir ⭐

Key Dances

Hikat, Kud, Rauf, Dhumal, Damal, Bhand Pathar, Dumhal

🧠 Memory Hook

Harris Rauf (Pakistani bowler) jumping with colours after winning, left in Dhumal dust. Bhand Pathar = satirical folk theatre (jesters/bhand). Dumhal = famous serpentine dance of Wattal tribe.

🌿 East & Northeast India
Assam · West Bengal · Jharkhand · Odisha · Bihar · Manipur · Meghalaya · Nagaland · Mizoram · Tripura · Arunachal · Sikkim

Assam 🔥

Key Dances

Bihu, Baguramba, Jhumur, Bhor Tal, Devdani, Ali Ai Gang

🧠 Memory Hook

BIHU = dance AND festival (asked in SSC!). Baguramba = Bodo tribe (B→B). Classical dance of Assam = SATRIYA (Guru Shankar Dev). Added to classical list in 2000 (same year as Jharkhand+Chhattisgarh+Uttarakhand formation).

⚠️ TRAP

JHUMUR (Assam) ≠ JHUMAR (Jharkhand/Odisha) ≠ JHOOMAR (Haryana). Assam's Jhumur goes East, Haryana's Jhoomar goes West!

West Bengal ⭐

Key Dances

Chhau (Purulia), Gambhira, Brita, Jhumur, Kathi

🧠 Memory Hook

KKR wins → Gambhir becomes coach → GAMBHIRA dance. Purulia district → PURULIA CHHAU. "If name ends in -a, BOLO" → Gambhira, Brita, Jhumura = WB.

Jharkhand 🔥

Key Dances

Chhau, Mundari, Santhali, Fagua, Karma, Paika, Jhumar, Damkach

🧠 Memory Hook

Birsa MUNDA → MUNDARI dance (M→M). Chhau = Eastern India (Jharkhand + Odisha + WB Purulia). CHHAU: Ministry of Culture considers it Classical despite being folk!

Odisha 🔥

Key Dances

Mahari, Karma, Jhoomar, Chhau, Paika, Rappa, Pua, Mayurbhanj Chhau

🧠 Memory Hook

PUA ate DAL, roams with stick (Rappa), not doing Karma, dancing instead. Mayurbhanj = district in Odisha = unmask Chhau (Mayurbhanj Chhau is performed WITHOUT mask, unlike Seraikela Chhau!).

⚠️ CHHAU TRAP

Chhau has 3 styles: Purulia (WB) = with mask, Seraikela (Jharkhand) = with mask, Mayurbhanj (Odisha) = WITHOUT mask.

Bihar ⭐

Key Dances

Jat-Jatan, Videshiya, Jhijhiya, Jharni, Paika, Kajri, Dibiya

🧠 Memory Hook

Jat-Jatan people rap Bohemia songs in Bihar. Foreigners (Videshiya) join. Jhijhiya-Jharni as beats. Dibiya = lamp dance. Paika seen in Jharkhand and Odisha too (border states share!)

Manipur 🔥

Key Dances

Raas Leela, Lai Haraoba, Khamba-Thoibi, Dhol Cholom, Pung Cholom

🧠 Memory Hook

Get MONEY (Manipur) → buy KHAMBA and LAI (ethanol + chakna) → drink all night → perform RAAS LEELA. PUNG = drum of Manipur (like dholak, played in martial manner). Thang-ta = martial dance of Manipur.

Meghalaya ⭐

Key Dances

Wangala (Garo), Nongkrem (Khasi), Laho (Jaintia)

🧠 Memory Hook

Three hills = three dances: GARO hills → WANGALA ("Festival of 100 Drums"). KHASI hills → NONGKREM (naked child running in rain gets COUGH = Nongkrem). JAINTIA → LAHO. Mawsynram (highest rainfall) is in Khasi Hills.

Nagaland ➕

Key Dances

Chang Lo, Zeliang, Rengma, Naga War Dance

🧠 Memory Hook

Nagaland = snakes crawling in colors. People cook dog meat in bamboo with potatoes, eat with tea. CHANG LO = harvest dance. CHANG eats cow = Chang eats = Chang dance!

Mizoram ⭐

Key Dances

Cheraw (Bamboo Dance), Khuallam, Chheihlam, Zangtalam, Sarlamkai

🧠 Memory Hook

"Khullam Khulla Chillam Chal Rahi" → Khuallam + Chillam = Chheihlam! CHERAW = most famous = Bamboo Dance. Dancers step in/out of bamboo poles rhythmically.

Tripura ➕

Key Dances

Gariya, Hojagiri, Lebang, Hai-Hak

🧠 Memory Hook

Biju Patnaik lost in Odisha → became GARIYA. VK Pandya type mother situation → GARIYA. HOJAGIRI = graceful dance where women balance objects on head + light lamps in both hands.

Arunachal Pradesh ⭐

Key Dances

Aji-Vancho, Buiya, Chalo, Paassi Kanki, Popir, Bardo Chham

🧠 Memory Hook

Sisters ARUNA + REKHA. Aruna tells husband: "Wear MASK, go to daughter-in-law, watch Lion+Peacock dance." Aji=Aji, Vancho=Vancho, Bahu(daughter-in-law)=Buiya, Paas=Paassi, Kanki=Kanki, Chalo=Chalo, Laan(Lion)+Peacock dance. CHAM in name = Arunachal or Sikkim.

Sikkim ⭐

Key Dances

Singhi Chham, Yak Chham, Chhutkey Naach, Chu Faat, Ghantu, Tamang Selo

🧠 Memory Hook

SIKKIM + CHAM → Singhi CHHAM (Snow Lion). CHU FAAT = festival dedicated to Mt. Kanchenjunga (worshipped as God in Sikkim). It's both festival AND dance. Yak Chham = Yak dance.

☀️ West & Central India

Gujarat 🔥

Key Dances

Garba, Dandiya Raas, Bhavai, Huḍo, Tippani, Matukdi, Padhhar, Siddhi Dhamal, Gop Raas

🧠 Memory Hook

DANDIYA RAAS = fight between Durga + Mahishasur shown through sticks, performed at NAVRATRI. GARBA = Krishna's Raas Leela with Gopis = GOp RAAS. MATUKDI = Matki (pot) dance — water scarce in Gujarat, fill matki while dancing. Siddhi Dhamal = African-origin tribe in Gujarat!

Goa ⭐

Key Dances

Mando, Dhalo, Fugdi, Dekhni, Corridor, Jagar

MANDVI girl (like River Mandvi of Goa) goes to Goa: sees JAGUAR car (=Jagar), watches horse cart race (=Dhalo), flies kites (=Fugdi). Elder: "Don't cry, sleep" (=Mando, which means slow+romantic). River Mandvi → Mando dance!

Maharashtra ⭐

Key Dances

Lavni, Tamasha, Koli, Lezim, Gondhal, Nakata, Powada

🧠 Memory Hook

Money Moni Roy does drama (Tamasha). KOLI = Kohli (cricketer) → fishermen dance. VADA PAV → Pav Daw → Powada. LAVNI = famous Lavani folk dance. LEZIM = martial dance of Maharashtra.

Rajasthan 🔥

Key Dances

Ghoomar, Gangaur, Kalbeliya, Chari, Terahtali, Kathputli, Bhawai

GHOOMAR = Bhil tribe (NOT Kalbeliya!). KALBELIYA = snake tribe = Gulabo Sapera (famous exponent). GANGAUR = dedicated to Parvati ji (Shiv-Parvati worship). Raja + Susani (Hrithik's ex-style name) clap by Ganga banks = TERAHTALI (13 cymbal clapping dance).

⚠️ TRAP

Ghoomar ≠ Kalbeliya. Ghoomar = Bhil tribe. Kalbeliya = snake tribe. Gulabo Sapera = Kalbeliya, NOT Ghoomar!

Madhya Pradesh ⭐

Key Dances

Tertali, Maanch, Jawara, Grida, Lehangi, Karma, Bhagoria Haat

🧠 Memory Hook

Fair LAILA dances on stage: pot on head with JAWARA (wheat) + flowers + leaves, claps (TERAHTALI). BHAGORIA HAAT = tribal fair where Gond/Bhil youth choose life partners (different from Rajasthan's Gangaur). Malla Khamba (martial) = from M to M!

Chhattisgarh ⭐

Key Dances

Sua (Parrot Dance), Karma, Saila, Pandavani, Raut Nacha, Soowa

🧠 Memory Hook

SUA = PARROT doesn't do its work. Instead, mad cultists with Pandavas kill Gediyas and dance. PANDAVANI = Teejan Bai = Padma Vibhushan 2009 (second highest civilian award). KARMA = shared with Jharkhand + Odisha (same belt).

⚔️ Martial Dances of India
High-yield category — frequently asked separately in SSC CGL
What is Martial Dance?

A form of folk dance that depicts fighting techniques, warfare, and combat skills. Often performed with actual weapons or weapon-shaped props.

Complete List — State-wise
🟠 Punjab Gatka Sikh martial art + stick/sword dance. Lassi + Gatka = Punjab.
🔵 West Bengal Lathi Khela Lathi (stick) fighting dance. Election season vibes in WB!
🟤 Tamil Nadu Silambam Bhuvan Bam + Sheela → Silambam Bam Sheela. Staff/stick fighting.
🟣 Manipur Thang-Ta Thang = sword, Ta = spear. Thangtaviya sisters. Elite martial form.
🟢 Mizoram Cheraw (Bamboo) Drive aunts away! Bamboo-based martial-folk dance form.
🔴 Maharashtra Lezim Manly game. Percussion instrument + acrobatic martial dance.
🟡 Bihar Pari Khanda Fairy (Pari) + Khanda (sword). Papa's fairy with sword = Bihar!
🟠 Madhya Pradesh Malla Khamba M→M. Malla (wrestler) + Khamba (pole). Gymnastics + wrestling on pole.
🔵 Kerala Kalaripayattu World's oldest martial art. Mother of all martial arts!
🧠 Master Memory Hook — Martial Dances

Gatka (Punjab) | Lathi (WB) | Silambam (TN) | Thang-ta (Manipur) | Cheraw (Mizoram) | Lezim (Maharashtra) | Pari Khanda (Bihar) | Malla Khamba (MP)

Story: "Gatka-Lathi-Silambam boys met Thang-ta master. Cheraw bamboo bridge crossed. Lezim music played. Pari arrived with sword. Malla climbed pole."

🌟 Special: High Dance / Fusion Dance

Uday Shankar promoted FUSION/HIGH DANCE in India. He is the brother of Ravi Shankar (sitar maestro). Remember: Ravi Shankar = Sitar → his BROTHER Uday Shankar = Fusion Dance. It is NOT associated with any single state.

⚠️ Exam Traps — What Students Get Wrong
These are the exact confusions that appear in SSC CGL. Study each one carefully.
TRAP 1 — The Jh/Jhu/Jhoo Confusion 🔥 CRITICAL

JHUMUR = Assam (also found in eastern India belt)
JHUMAR = Jharkhand, Odisha, Chhattisgarh (Eastern India belt)
JHOOMAR = Haryana (North India)

Remember: East = JHUMAR (like eastern = Jhumar). Haryana is north = JHOOMAR (H for Haryana, H sounds like "Jhoo"). Assam = JHUMUR (shortest word, smallest state size context).

TRAP 2 — Ghoomar vs Kalbeliya

GHOOMAR = Bhil tribe of Rajasthan. Circular spinning dance.
KALBELIYA = Snake tribe (Kalbeliya tribe) of Rajasthan. Famous exponent = Gulabo Sapera.
Students often mix these! Remember: Kal-BELIYA → BELIA means snake people → snake tribe.

TRAP 3 — Chhau Styles (3 types, 3 states)

Purulia Chhau (West Bengal) → WITH elaborate mask
Seraikela Chhau (Jharkhand) → WITH mask
Mayurbhanj Chhau (Odisha) → WITHOUT mask (the odd one out!)
Ministry of Culture considers Chhau as Classical despite being folk origin.

TRAP 4 — Bihu is BOTH Dance and Festival

Bihu = folk DANCE of Assam AND a FESTIVAL of Assam. This has been asked directly in SSC. Don't just remember it as a dance. Also: Bihu has three types — Bohag Bihu (spring), Kongali Bihu (autumn), Bhogali Bihu (harvest).

TRAP 5 — Attam Confusion (Kerala vs Tamil Nadu)

NEE Attam → Kerala
LI Attam (Kolattam), DI Attam (Kummi) → Tamil Nadu
OTTAM Thulla, THITAMBU Nritham → Kerala (Double-T rule → 95% Kerala/TN)
Never say "Ottam = Tamil Nadu" — it's Kerala!

TRAP 6 — CHAM Dance (3 states)

CHAM (or CHHAM) appears in: Arunachal Pradesh, Sikkim, Himachal Pradesh.
Don't confuse: Bardo CHHAM (Arunachal), Singhi CHHAM (Sikkim), CHHAM (HP).
If you see "CHAM" in options — look for these three states first!

TRAP 7 — Charkula is in BOTH Haryana and UP

Charkula = circular pot/earthen lamps balanced on head while dancing.
Panchkula (city in Haryana) → Charkula (sounds similar). But Charkula is mainly associated with Uttar Pradesh (Braj region). If asked primary state → UP.

TRAP 8 — Paika appears in Multiple States

Paika = martial-style folk dance seen in Bihar, Jharkhand, Odisha. Don't say it belongs to only one! It's a shared martial folk tradition of the eastern belt.

TRAP 9 — Uday Shankar vs Ravi Shankar

Ravi Shankar = Sitar maestro (Bharat Ratna)
Uday Shankar = Promoted FUSION DANCE / HIGH DANCE in India (his brother!)
Exam often twists these. Uday = DANCE, Ravi = SITAR.

TRAP 10 — Gangaur is NOT only Rajasthan

Gangaur is primarily associated with Rajasthan (dance dedicated to Parvati), but it's also celebrated as a festival in parts of Madhya Pradesh and Uttar Pradesh. The DANCE is Rajasthan's specialty.

TRAP 11 — Lambaḍi/Banjara (Two States)

Lambadi dance is associated with both Andhra Pradesh AND Telangana because the Banjara tribe lives in both (Telangana was carved from AP in 2014). Don't say "Lambadi = only Andhra"!

TRAP 12 — Malla Khamba is MP's Martial Dance

Malla Khamba = pole gymnastics/wrestling. It's the state sport of Madhya Pradesh (not just a dance). MP also declared it their traditional sport. M→Malla, M→Madhya Pradesh.
