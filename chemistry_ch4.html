⭐ Special Case Burning of Candle = BOTH physical + chemical

⭐ Important

2. Exothermic vs Endothermic

Exothermic

Heat is released to surroundings.
Examples: Combustion, most combination reactions, lime + water

Endothermic

Heat is absorbed from surroundings.
Examples: Most decomposition reactions, electrolysis

🔥 Must Know

3. Combination Reaction

Multiple reactants → single product. Mostly exothermic.

CaO + H₂O → Ca(OH)₂ + Heat // Quick lime + water → Slaked lime [EXOTHERMIC]
Ca(OH)₂ + CO₂ → CaCO₃ + H₂O // Lime water + CO₂ → Calcium Carbonate [milky white]
CaCO₃ = Limestone
= Marble
= Chalk
= Calcite

🎯 CO₂ turns lime water milky → forms CaCO₃

CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O + Heat // Combustion = type of Combination [Exothermic]

Combustion = burning of anything in presence of O₂. It is a combination reaction and exothermic.

2Mg + O₂ → 2MgO + Blinding White Light // Burning Mg ribbon → intense white light
🔥 Must Know

4. Decomposition Reaction

Single reactant → multiple products. Mostly endothermic. Opposite of combination.

2Pb(NO₃)₂ → 2PbO + 4NO₂ + O₂ // Lead Nitrate → BROWN FUMES (NO₂)
CaCO₃ → CaO + CO₂ // Limestone → Quick Lime [used in cement industry]
2AgCl → 2Ag + Cl₂ [sunlight] // Silver Chloride → used in B&W Photography
2H₂O → 2H₂ + O₂ [electricity] // Electrolysis of water
Electrolysis of Water
Cathode (−) → H₂ (Hydrogen) | Anode (+) → O₂ (Oxygen)
🔥 Must Know

5. Displacement Reaction

More reactive metal displaces less reactive metal from its salt solution.

Fe + CuSO₄ → FeSO₄ + Cu // Iron displaces Copper (Fe more reactive)
// Cu + FeSO₄ → NO REACTION (Cu less reactive than Fe)

🎯 CuSO₄ = BLUE → FeSO₄ = GREEN (colour change is asked in exams!)

6. Double Displacement Reaction

Two compounds swap ions with each other. Also called Ion Exchange Reaction. Produces a precipitate.

Na₂SO₄ + BaCl₂ → BaSO₄↓ + 2NaCl // White ppt of Barium Sulphate
Pb(NO₃)₂ + 2KI → PbI₂↓ + 2KNO₃ // Yellow ppt of Lead Iodide
BaSO₄ → White ppt
PbI₂ → Yellow ppt
🔥 Must Know

7. Oxidation & Reduction (REDOX)

Oxidation

Addition of O₂ or Loss of Electrons
OIL → Oxidation Is Loss

Reduction

Removal of O₂ or Gain of Electrons
RIG → Reduction Is Gain

CuO + H₂ → Cu + H₂O
// CuO loses O → CuO is REDUCED → CuO is OXIDISING AGENT
// H₂ gains O → H₂ is OXIDISED → H₂ is REDUCING AGENT
Key Rule: The substance that gets oxidised = Reducing Agent (Reductant) | The substance that gets reduced = Oxidising Agent (Oxidant)

🧠 Smart Memory Hooks
Sticky images, stories & mnemonics built for long-term recall
HOOK 1 — PHYSICAL vs CHEMICAL (The Door Story)
Imagine two revolving doors. The LEFT door spins back (reversible = physical). The RIGHT door has a ONE-WAY lock (irreversible = chemical). A candle on the reception desk uses BOTH doors at once — wax melts through the left (physical), flame chars through the right (chemical).
HOOK 2 — EXO vs ENDO (The Oven Rule)
EXO = heat EXITs = like an oven releasing heat to kitchen. ENDO = heat ENTERs = like a fridge absorbing heat from inside. Combination = oven. Decomposition = fridge.
HOOK 3 — COMBINATION (The Painter)
Picture a painter with a white bucket. He dumps quicklime (CaO) into water — the bucket STEAMS and SIZZLES (heat released = exothermic!). Then he slaps it on the wall. CO₂ from air creeps in and the wall turns solid white limestone. Two reactants always merge into ONE product.
HOOK 4 — DECOMPOSITION (The Brown Cloud)
A scientist heats lead nitrate — suddenly a cloud of BROWN SMOKE fills the lab (NO₂). Remember: any time you see "brown fumes" in a question → NO₂ → Lead Nitrate decomposition.
Electrolysis: imagine two electrodes — the anode is POSITIVE (+) so Oxygen (negative atoms) goes there, and cathode is NEGATIVE (−) so Hydrogen (positive ions) goes there.
HOOK 5 — REACTIVITY SERIES (Katrina's Car Story)
K Na Ca Mg Al Zn Fe [H] Ag Au

"Katrina Na(asked) Car (for) Car Alto Zen Ferrari, But Got Silver Audi"

K & Na react with cold water (so reactive they explode!)
Ca, Mg → warm water
Al, Zn, Fe → steam/hot water
Ag, Au → almost nothing. Au needs Aqua Regia (3HCl + 1HNO₃)
HOOK 6 — OIL RIG for Oxidation/Reduction
Oxidation Is Loss (of electrons)
Reduction Is Gain (of electrons)

Visual: An OIL RIG at sea. The rig is on FIRE (oxidation). The sea is calm (reduction = gaining stability). Fire = loss of electrons. Sea = gain.
HOOK 7 — RANCIDITY (The Chips Packet)
Why does your chips bag feel puffy? It's full of NITROGEN gas. If oxygen were inside, it would attack the fat/oil → oxidation → rancidity (stale smell). Nitrogen is inert = bodyguard. Every time you open a chips packet, remember: Rancidity = Oxidation of fats.
HOOK 8 — CORROSION Colours
🔴 IRON rusts → Reddish-brown (iron oxide)
🟢 COPPER corrodes → Green (copper carbonate)
Memory image: A reddish-brown old Indian taxi (iron/rust) and a green Statue of Liberty (copper/patina).
HOOK 9 — DOUBLE DISPLACEMENT (The Badge Swap)
Two people walk into a room. They rip off each other's name badges and wear the other's. That's ion exchange! The precipitate that falls = the badge that hit the floor. Remember: BaSO₄ (white), PbI₂ (yellow).

Reaction Type Hierarchy

ALL CHEMICAL REACTIONS ├── By Energy │ ├── Exothermic (heat out) → Combination, Combustion, most displacement │ └── Endothermic (heat in) → Decomposition, electrolysis └── By Type ├── Combination → A + B → AB [Exo mostly] │ └── Combustion is a SUBTYPE of combination ├── Decomposition → AB → A + B [Endo mostly] ├── Displacement → A + BC → AC + B [depends on reactivity] ├── Double Displacement → AB + CD → AD + CB [ion exchange, ppt formed] └── Redox → Oxidation + Reduction happen SIMULTANEOUSLY ├── Rancidity → oxidation of fats (subtype) └── Corrosion → oxidation of metal (subtype) └── Rusting → corrosion of IRON specifically

Cause → Effect Chains

CaO + H₂O→Ca(OH)₂ → reacts with CO₂ in air → CaCO₃ (limestone wall)
More reactive metal→Can displace less reactive metal from salt solution
Oxygen reaches fats→Oxidation → Rancidity → N₂ gas filled to prevent it
Oxygen + Iron + Moisture→Rusting (a type of corrosion, a type of oxidation)
AgCl + Sunlight→Ag + Cl₂ → Black & White Photography
Electrolysis of H₂O→H₂ at cathode (−) | O₂ at anode (+) → Decomposition reaction
Aqua Regia→3HCl + 1HNO₃ → Only dissolves Gold

Feature → Exception Map

Combination = Exo→Mostly, not always. Combustion always is.
Decomposition = Endo→Mostly, not always.
Rusting = Corrosion→But corrosion ≠ rusting (corrosion is broader — all metals)
Rancidity→It IS an oxidation reaction, NOT a separate type
Combustion→It IS a combination reaction — not a separate category

Reducing Agent vs Oxidising Agent Logic

Substance that GETS OXIDISED → called REDUCING AGENT (it helps the other substance get reduced)

Substance that GETS REDUCED → called OXIDISING AGENT (it helps the other substance get oxidised)

In CuO + H₂ → Cu + H₂O:
CuO gets reduced → CuO is OXIDISING AGENT
H₂ gets oxidised → H₂ is REDUCING AGENT

⚠️ Exam Traps
What toppers know that average students get wrong
TRAP 1 — Rusting vs Corrosion
Students write "rusting = corrosion." WRONG. Rusting is a TYPE of corrosion that occurs only in IRON. Corrosion is the broader term (all metals). Copper corrodes but does NOT rust.
TRAP 2 — Reducing Agent GETS Oxidised (counter-intuitive!)
Students think "reducing agent = gets reduced." WRONG. The reducing agent is the substance that causes reduction in something else — and it itself gets OXIDISED. H₂ is the reducing agent in the CuO + H₂ reaction but H₂ itself is oxidised.
TRAP 3 — Cathode vs Anode in Electrolysis
Cathode = NEGATIVE terminal → Hydrogen collects here. Anode = POSITIVE terminal → Oxygen collects here. Students often reverse this. Memory: "A for Anode, A for Air (O₂)."
TRAP 4 — Combination ≠ Always Exothermic
The exam may say "all combination reactions are exothermic — True/False." Answer: FALSE. Most are exothermic, NOT all. Same applies to decomposition being endothermic.
TRAP 5 — What Turns Lime Water Milky?
Answer is CO₂. Lime water = Ca(OH)₂ solution. CO₂ + Ca(OH)₂ → CaCO₃ (white/milky precipitate). Students confuse lime water with lime (CaO) or slaked lime Ca(OH)₂ in solid form.
TRAP 6 — Candle Burning is BOTH Physical and Chemical
Wax melting = physical (reversible). Wax vapour burning + wick burning = chemical (irreversible). If asked "what type of change is candle burning?" — answer is BOTH.
TRAP 7 — Names of CaCO₃
CaCO₃ is called Limestone AND Marble AND Chalk AND Calcite — all the same compound! Exam may try to confuse by listing these as separate substances.
TRAP 8 — Brown Fumes = NO₂ specifically
"Which gas produces brown fumes?" → Always NO₂ (Nitrogen Dioxide). Formed in decomposition of Lead Nitrate. Do not confuse with Cl₂ (yellow-green) or SO₂ (colourless).
TRAP 9 — Aqua Regia Composition
Aqua Regia = 3 parts HCl + 1 part HNO₃ (NOT the other way around). Students often flip the ratio. 3:1 = Concentrated HCl : Concentrated HNO₃.
TRAP 10 — Rancidity is Oxidation (not a separate type)
Rancidity is NOT listed as a separate reaction type. It is classified as an OXIDATION REACTION. Same for corrosion. If asked "what type of reaction is rancidity?" → Oxidation.
TRAP 11 — Photography uses Silver CHLORIDE or BROMIDE
Black & White photography uses BOTH AgCl and AgBr (silver chloride and silver bromide) — both decompose in sunlight. The exam may try one or the other to confuse.

Quick MCQ Quiz

Q1. Which of the following is an endothermic reaction?
A) Burning of magnesium ribbon
B) Lime + Water reaction
C) Electrolysis of water
D) Combustion of methane
Electrolysis of water requires electrical energy (heat absorbed = endothermic). All others release heat (exothermic).
Q2. Iron reacts with copper sulphate solution. What is the role of iron?
A) Oxidising agent
B) More reactive metal displacing copper
C) Iron undergoes reduction
D) Iron is a catalyst here
Fe is more reactive than Cu. It displaces Cu from CuSO₄ solution. Fe + CuSO₄ → FeSO₄ + Cu. Blue → Green colour change.
Q3. Brown fumes produced during decomposition of lead nitrate are due to:
A) SO₂
B) Cl₂
C) NO₂
D) CO₂
2Pb(NO₃)₂ → 2PbO + 4NO₂ + O₂. Nitrogen Dioxide (NO₂) = brown fumes. Classic exam question!
Q4. In the reaction CuO + H₂ → Cu + H₂O, which substance is the reducing agent?
A) CuO
B) H₂
C) Cu
D) H₂O
H₂ gets oxidised (gains oxygen) → it is the reducing agent. CuO gets reduced (loses oxygen) → it is the oxidising agent. OIL RIG!
Q5. Rusting of iron is a type of:
A) Combination reaction only
B) Decomposition reaction
C) Oxidation → Corrosion → Rusting (all three apply)
D) Displacement reaction
Rusting IS a type of corrosion. Corrosion IS a type of oxidation. So rusting falls under all three. Rusting specifically happens only in IRON.
