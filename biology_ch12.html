Chapter 01 · Biology

Diseases & Pathogens

Everything you need to know about pathogens, disease transmission, genetic disorders, and plant diseases — packaged for maximum retention inside the exam hall.

🏛️ Big Picture

This chapter is about WHO causes disease and HOW it spreads. Four main agents — Viruses, Bacteria, Protozoa, Fungi — each attack the body differently, spread via different routes, and require different treatments.

On top of that, some diseases come from within — our own faulty genes and chromosomes (genetic disorders). And a small bonus: diseases in plants.

Section 01

Disease Classification

Before diving into diseases, know how to classify them. Examiners love definition-based and compare-contrast questions here.

⏱️ By Duration

ACUTE 🔥 MUST KNOW

Short duration — days to ~2 weeks

  • Cold, Flu, Cough, Fever
  • Passes quickly, symptoms intense but brief
CHRONIC 🔥 MUST KNOW

Long duration — months to years

  • Diabetes, Arthritis, TB, Elephantiasis
  • Persists, manages rather than cures easily

🤧 By Transmission

COMMUNICABLE

Spreads person to person

  • AIDS, Cold, Chicken Pox, Covid
  • Via contact, air, blood, body fluids
NON-COMMUNICABLE

Does NOT spread by contact

  • Diabetes, Arthritis, Glaucoma
  • Genetic, lifestyle, or environmental causes

🌍 By Geographic Spread

TypeDefinitionExample
ENDEMIC Constantly present in a region; relatively low/stable spread Ebola in parts of Africa
EPIDEMIC Sudden large increase across a large population / region Early stage of Covid in China
PANDEMIC Sudden increase across multiple countries, continents, or the whole world Covid-19 globally (2020–22)
Memory Hook — Scope of Spread

Think of a forest fire: Endemic = a small campfire in one village (always there, small). Epidemic = fire spreads to the whole forest (big jump). Pandemic = fire jumps oceans and burns all forests on Earth (everywhere).

🐾 Zoonotic Diseases

Zoonotic diseases = transferred from animals to humans.

  • Rabies — from dog bite
  • Anthrax — from animals
  • Bird Flu — from birds
  • Swine Flu — from pigs
Section 02

🦠 Viral Diseases

Viruses have NO cell wall → antibiotics are useless. Treated by antivirals and prevented by vaccines.

VIRUS — Key Properties
  • Genetic material: DNA or RNA
  • RNA virus = Retrovirus (e.g., HIV)
  • Protein envelope = Capsid
  • Non-living outside host; living inside host
  • Antibiotics DO NOT work (no cell wall to attack)
  • First antibiotic: Penicillin from Penicillium fungus, discovered by Alexander Fleming

Section 03

🧫 Bacterial Diseases

Bacteria are prokaryotes with a cell wall (peptidoglycan). Antibiotics work by blocking cell wall formation.

BACTERIA — Key Properties
  • Prokaryotes, unicellular, living entities
  • Genetic material called Genophore
  • Can be parasitic or saprophytic
  • Cell wall made of Peptidoglycan
  • Treated with Antibiotics

🔬 Bacterial Shapes

Rod-like shape

Spiral shape
e.g. Helicobacter

🔴🟣 Gram Staining

GRAM POSITIVE (Purple)
  • Thick peptidoglycan cell wall
  • More susceptible to antibiotics
  • Show purple colour in staining
  • Examples: Streptococcus, Salmonella, Staphylococcus
GRAM NEGATIVE (Pink/Red)
  • Thin peptidoglycan cell wall
  • More resistant to antibiotics
  • Show pink/red colour in staining
  • Examples: E. coli (Escherichia coli)

  • Leishmania: binary fission along only ONE fixed plane
  • Plasmodium: multiple fission (not binary fission!)

Section 05

🧬 Genetic Disorders

Disorders arising from faulty genes or chromosomes — not from external pathogens. Key for SSC CGL!

Foundation Knowledge
  • Total chromosomes: 23 pairs (46 total)
  • Autosomes: 22 pairs (not related to sex)
  • Sex chromosomes: 1 pair — XX (female) or XY (male)
  • Gene: Functional segment of DNA (double helix)
  • Alleles: Different variants of same gene
  • Mendelian Disorders: Problem in genes (not chromosome number)
  • Mitosis → uncontrolled = Cancer
  • Meiosis → uncontrolled = Genetic Disorders
  • Gregor Mendel = Father of Genetics (pea plant experiments)

🧬 Chromosomal Disorders

DisorderChromosome InvolvedConditionFeatures
Down Syndrome Autosome #21 Trisomy 21 (extra copy) Small head, intellectual disability
Turner Syndrome Sex chromosome (X) 45 chromosomes — XO (missing X) Only in females; reduced female traits
Klinefelter Syndrome Sex chromosome 47 chromosomes — XXY (extra X in male) Male with some female characteristics

🧬 Mendelian / Gene Disorders

Memory Hook — Sex-Linked Disorders

The three that link to X chromosome: H-T-C = Hemophilia, Turner syndrome (missing X), Color blindness. All three affect males more (because males only have ONE X — if it's faulty, no backup).

🧬 Mendel's 3 Laws

LAW 1
Dominance
One trait masks the other
LAW 2
Segregation
Alleles separate during gamete formation
LAW 3
Independent Assortment
Different traits inherited independently

Retention System

💡 Smart Memory Hooks

These are the core images and stories that make this chapter impossible to forget.

Hook 1 — The Death Colour Rainbow

Black Death = Plague (Yersinia pestis) — imagine a black rat running
Blue Death = Cholera (Vibrio) — blue tap water = contaminated
White Plague = TB (Mycobacterium TB) — white clouds in lungs
Yellow Fever = Jaundice — yellow eyes, yellow skin

Hook 2 — Mycobacterium Twins

Two diseases, one family: Mycobacterium tuberculosis → TB (lungs) and Mycobacterium leprae → Leprosy/Hansen's disease (skin). Remember: Myco brothers — one attacks lungs, one attacks skin.

Hook 3 — The Vaccine Hall of Fame

• Edward Jenner → Smallpox (first ever vaccine, 1796)
• Jonas Salk → Polio IPV (inject = Salk)
• Albert Sabin → Polio OPV (oral = Sabin)
• Alexander Fleming → Penicillin (antibiotic from fungus, 1928)

Hook 4 — The Mosquito Sisters

Two mosquito sisters cause disease:
Sister Anopheles (female, wears a malaria badge) → carries Plasmodium → Malaria
Sister Aedes (female, wears a tiger stripe) → carries Flavivirus → Dengue, also Chikungunya
Memory: Anopheles = Ancient disease (Malaria). Aedes = A modern striker (Dengue).

Hook 5 — ELISA and AIDS Day

AIDS → HIV → Retrovirus (RNA). Test = ELISA (Enzyme Linked ImmunoSorbent Assay). World AIDS Day = December 1. Memory: "December 1 — Don't be Number 1 to spread it."

Hook 6 — Hepatitis ABC Rule

A = food (A for Aloo — you eat it) → Hepatitis A
B = body contact/sex (B for Bodily) → Hepatitis B
C = contaminated blood (C for Cut/Blood) → Hepatitis C
India's own vaccine for Hep A = Hevesh

Hook 7 — Chromosome Numbers

Normal human = 46 chromosomes (23 pairs)
Turner syndrome = 45 (45, X — missing one X)
Klinefelter = 47 (XXY — one extra X in male)
Down syndrome = still 47 (trisomy 21 — extra autosome #21)
Memory: Turner = fewer. Klinefelter = more. Down = more on 21.

Hook 8 — STD Summary

STDs from bacteria: Gonorrhea + Syphilis (both reproductive organs)
STDs from virus: AIDS/HIV + Cervical Cancer/HPV + Hepatitis B
Memory: "Bacteria gave 2 STDs. Virus gave 3."

Danger Zone

⚠️ Exam Traps & Common Confusions

These are exactly the places where students lose marks. Memorise each trap individually.

⚠️ Trap 1 — Varicella vs Variola
  • Varicella Zoster = Chicken Pox
  • Variola = Smallpox
  • Edward Jenner discovered SMALLPOX vaccine (not chicken pox)
  • Eradicated in India: Smallpox in 1977 (not chicken pox)
⚠️ Trap 2 — Virus Cannot Be Treated With Antibiotics
  • Antibiotics only work on BACTERIA (they block cell wall formation)
  • Viruses have NO cell wall → antibiotics are useless against them
  • Exam may say "antibiotic treats common cold" → FALSE
⚠️ Trap 3 — Malaria vs Dengue Carrier
  • Malaria → Female Anopheles mosquito
  • Dengue + Chikungunya → Female Aedes aegypti
  • Both are female mosquitoes, different species!
  • Malaria = Protozoa (Plasmodium). Dengue = Virus (Flavivirus)
⚠️ Trap 4 — Salk vs Sabin (Polio)
  • Jonas Salk → IPV (Inactivated Polio Vaccine — INJECTED)
  • Albert Sabin → OPV (Oral Polio Vaccine — DROPS in mouth)
  • India eradicated polio — announced 2023
  • Exam usually asks for Salk (IPV)
⚠️ Trap 5 — Gram Positive vs Negative Colour
  • Gram Positive → PURPLE/Violet colour (thick wall)
  • Gram Negative → PINK/RED colour (thin wall)
  • Students reverse these in exam stress!
  • Memory: Positive = Pretty Purple. Negative = Not purple = Pink.
⚠️ Trap 6 — Gonorrhea is Bacterial, NOT Viral
  • Gonorrhea = Bacterial STD (Neisseria gonorrhoeae)
  • Syphilis = Bacterial STD (Treponema pallidum)
  • AIDS = Viral STD (HIV)
  • Don't confuse: all three are STDs but different agents!
⚠️ Trap 7 — Turner vs Klinefelter
  • Turner syndrome: Only in FEMALES (XO, 45 chromosomes)
  • Klinefelter syndrome: Only in MALES (XXY, 47 chromosomes)
  • Turner = LESS (missing X). Klinefelter = MORE (extra X)
  • Down syndrome (Trisomy 21) ≠ sex chromosome disorder
⚠️ Trap 8 — Leprosy's Other Name
  • Leprosy = Hansen's Disease = Kushth Rog
  • Caused by Mycobacterium leprae
  • Don't confuse Mycobacterium leprae (skin/leprosy) with Mycobacterium tuberculosis (lungs/TB)
⚠️ Trap 9 — Common Cold Virus
  • Common Cold = Rhinovirus (not Influenza virus!)
  • Influenza virus causes FLU (which is more severe)
  • Cold ≠ Flu in biology (different viruses!)
⚠️ Trap 10 — Thalassemia vs Sickle Cell
  • Both are blood disorders with abnormal hemoglobin
  • Thalassemia = body produces LESS hemoglobin → anemia
  • Sickle Cell = ABNORMAL hemoglobin → sickle-shaped RBCs
  • Both are Mendelian (genetic) disorders
