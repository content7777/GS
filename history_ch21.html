Big Picture — What This Chapter Is Really About

Two great medieval powers co-existed in the Deccan region for nearly 200 years. Vijayanagara (Hindu, to the south of Krishna River) and Bahmani (Muslim, to the north) were rivals, neighbours, and sometimes trading partners. The chapter is about understanding who ruled when, what they built, who visited them, and how the empires ended — all classic exam attack points.

⚡ Vijayanagara in One Line

South of Krishna River · Hindu empire · 4 dynasties (Sangam → Saluva → Tuluva → Aravidu) · Capital = Hampi · Greatest ruler = Krishnadeva Raya · Ended Battle of Talikota 1565

⚡ Bahmani in One Line

North of Krishna River · Muslim empire · Founded 1347 · Capital shifted Gulbarga→Bidar · Broke into 5 Deccan Sultanates · Eventually absorbed by Mughals

Chapter Memory Palace — The Victory City Story

Imagine you are walking through a magnificent ancient city on the banks of a river. This is your memory palace. Each room = one key cluster of facts.

🌊 Room 1 · The River Gate (Basics)

You enter through two rivers — Krishna and its tributary Tungabhadra. The city name on the gate reads "VIJAY-NAGAR" = City of Victory. The gatekeeper is named Pampa Devi — she tells you this city is also called Hampi after her. The flag on the gate shows Lord Virupaksha (Shiva), because kings rule in His name.

🏛 Room 2 · The Hall of Four Dynasties

Inside, the hall has 4 thrones in a row — Sangam (1336–1485) · Saluva (1486–1505) · Tuluva (1505–1570) · Aravidu. On the first throne sit two brothers Harihar & Bukka. On the third throne, a giant golden crown belongs to Krishnadeva Raya (KDR) — the most magnificent.

🧳 Room 3 · The Travellers' Lounge

Four travellers are seated. Man from Morocco — Ibn Battuta (wrote Rehala). Man from Italy with a painting brush — Nicolo di Conti (Deva Raya I). Man from Persia — Abdul Razzaq (Deva Raya II). Two Portuguese sailors — Barbosa & Domingo Paes (KDR). Another Portuguese — Fernao Nunes (Achyuta Deva Raya).

🏰 Room 4 · The North Wing (Bahmani)

Cross the Krishna River — you are now in the Bahmani Palace. The founder Alauddin Hasan Bahman Shah (Hasan Gangu) sits at a desk shifting his capital from Gulbarga → Bidar. His clever prime minister Mahmud Gawan is writing trade agreements. The palace then cracks into 5 pieces — Berar, Bijapur, Ahmednagar, Golconda, Bidar.

💣 Room 5 · The Battle Room (End)

Year 1565. Three armies (Bijapur + Ahmednagar + Golconda) break through the door and destroy the city. The last great battle — Talikota / Rakshasi Tangadi. The king inside was Sadashiva Raya (nominal) but the one fighting was Aliya Rama Raya (de facto). The city of Victory falls.

Complete Revision Notes — Dynasties of Vijayanagara

📍 Foundation Facts
  • Vijayanagara = "City of Victory" (Vijay + Nagar)
  • Capital = Hampi (also spelled Hampi, NOT Humpi). Named after Pampa Devi (goddess, consort of Virupaksha/Shiva)
  • Location: On banks of Tungabhadra River (tributary of Krishna)
  • Located south of Krishna River (Bahmani was north)
  • Contemporary rivals: Bahmani Sultans (Ashwapatis) + Gajapatis of Orissa
  • Local merchants dealing in horses = Kudirai Chetti merchants
  • Early rulers known to contemporaries as: Karnataka Samrajyam (to Bahmani), Hindu Suratans (early usage)
1336 – 1485 AD
🔸 Sangam Dynasty 🔥 Must Know
  • Founders: Harihar & Bukka — were feudatories of Kakatiyas of Warangal
  • Harihar's title: Karnataka Vidya Vilas
  • Bukka renamed Vijayanagara → Vidyanagara
  • Traveller during Harihar & Bukka: Ibn Battuta (from Morocco, wrote Rehala)
  • Traveller during Deva Raya I: Nicolo di Conti (from Italy) 🔥
  • Deva Raya I: built dams, tanks, canals (infra work)
  • Traveller during Deva Raya II: Abdul Razzaq (from Persia) 🔥
  • Abdul Razzaq noted: seven levels of fortification in Vijayanagara
  • Tajuddin Firuz Shah (Bahmani) defeated Deva Raya II and married his daughter
1486 – 1505 AD
🔹 Saluva Dynasty ⭐ Important
  • Founder: Saluva Narasimha
  • Short-lived, not many exam facts — mainly serves as transition
1505 – 1570 AD
🔶 Tuluva Dynasty 🔥 Must Know
  • Founder: Veer Narasimha (1505–1509). Also called Naras Nayak at some places
  • His minister Saluvathimma made his brother KDR sit on throne
  • Greatest ruler: Krishnadeva Raya (KDR) — 1509 to 1529 AD
  • Traveller during Achyuta Deva Raya: Fernao Nunes
  • Battle of Talikota / Rakshasi Tangadi: 1565 AD — effectively ends Vijayanagara
  • De jure ruler at time of Talikota: Sadashiva Raya; de facto: Aliya Rama Raya
  • Attackers: combined forces of Bijapur + Ahmednagar + Golconda
Post 1565
🔷 Aravidu Dynasty ➕ Good to Know
  • Continued in diminished form after Talikota
  • Capital shifted: Hampi → Penukonda → Chandragiri
  • Last ruler: Sriranga III
🧠 Memory Hook — 4 Dynasties

"Some Saints Truly Arrive" — Sangam → Saluva → Tuluva → Aravidu. Picture 4 saints walking into a temple in this order.

KDR Deep Dive — Krishnadeva Raya (1509–1529)

KDR is the single most important ruler of this chapter. If you know nothing else, know KDR. Every sub-topic has exam potential.

🏗 What KDR Built
  • Vijay Mahal — House of Victory
  • Hazara Rama Temple
  • Vitthal Swami Temple
  • Gopuram of Virupaksha Temple at Hampi
  • Founded city Nagalapuram — named after his mother
  • Kamalapuram tank (step tank)
🎖 KDR's Titles
  • Yavana Raja Sthapanacharya — restorer of Yavana (Greek = Bahmani) Kingdom. Helped resolve Bahmani internal disputes.
  • Abhinava Bhoja
  • Andhra Bhoja — named after Bhoja of North India, KDR = Bhoja of Andhra
  • Andhra Pitamaha
📚 KDR's Literary Works
  • Amukta Malyada — Telugu; on statecraft and polity 🔥
  • Jambavati Kalyanam — Sanskrit drama
  • Was a scholar of both Telugu and Sanskrit
🧳 Travellers During KDR
  • Duarte Barbosa — Portuguese 🔥
  • Domingo Paes — Portuguese 🔥
  • Both came from Portugal
⚙ Amarnayak System
  • Military commanders called Amarnayak
  • Similar to Iqta system of Delhi Sultanate (land given → extract revenue → maintain army)
  • Had to present themselves at Mahanavami festival at Mahanavami Dibba
  • ⚠ Not exclusive to KDR — existed throughout Vijayanagara Empire
  • Village committee for administration = 12 members (Anga system)
🤝 KDR & Albuquerque (Portuguese)

Albuquerque was the most powerful Portuguese governor (Blue Water Policy was Almeida). KDR gifted the Fort at Bhatkal (a port city) to Albuquerque.

🎭 Eight Great Poets (Ashtadiggajas) in KDR's Court

KDR's court had 8 great scholars. Key name: Tenali Rama (Ramakrishna). Others: Pedana, Thimana, Mallana, Asurana. (You don't need to memorize all 8 — just Tenali Rama)

Vijayanagara Architecture

🏙 Two Zones of the Capital

ROYAL CENTRE

  • Lotus Palace (Kamal Mahal)
  • Elephant Stables (11 stables found)
  • Hazara Rama Temple

SACRED CENTRE (where public lived)

  • Vitthal Swami Temple (Vitthal = Lord Vishnu)
  • Virupaksha Temple
🔴 Mahanavami Dibba 🔥 NDA-level Fact
  • A raised red platform with intricate relief carvings (trees, animals, processions)
  • Used during Navaratri / Mahanavami festival — king would watch performances from top
  • Amarnayaks would present themselves before the king here
  • ⚠ NOT a sweet box! It is a ceremonial stepped platform.
💧 Water Infrastructure
  • Dams, tanks, canals — Deva Raya I
  • Kamalapuram Tank (step tank) — KDR
  • Anant Raji Sagar Tank
  • Hiriya Canal
  • Abdul Razzaq described 7 levels of fortification walls
🧠 Virupaksha Temple — Don't Confuse!

Two Virupaksha Temples:
1️⃣ Virupaksha in Pattadakal → built by Queen Lok Mahadevi (Chalukya era) by Lakkan Dandesh
2️⃣ Virupaksha in Hampi → the famous one in Vijayanagara; its Gopuram was built by KDR
Both are dedicated to Virupaksha = Lord Shiva

Bahmani Kingdom — Complete Notes

🏗 Foundation
  • Founded: 1347 AD; dissolved: mid-1500s
  • Founder: Alauddin Hasan Bahman Shah (also known as Hasan Gangu) 🔥
  • Original capital: Gulbarga (not Galbarga!)
  • Also defeated the Kakatiyas of Warangal
  • North of Krishna River (opposite to Vijayanagara)
📅 Key Rulers Timeline
#RulerKey Act
1Alauddin Hasan Bahman Shah (Hasan Gangu)Founded Bahmani; capital = Gulbarga
2Tajuddin Firuz Shah (1397–1422)Defeated Deva Raya II; married his daughter; then lost to him
3Ahmad Shah Wali (1422–1435)Shifted capital Gulbarga → Bidar 🔥
4Humayun ShahGave title Malik Tajar (Chief of Merchants) to Mahmud Gawan
🤵 Mahmud Gawan — The Super-Minister
  • NOT a ruler — was the Peshwa (Prime Minister) of Bahmani Kingdom
  • Title: Malik Tajar (Chief of Merchants) — given by Humayun Shah
  • Also received title: Khwaja Jahan
  • One of the most able administrators in Bahmani history
⚙ Bahmani Administration
  • Taraf = Province. Four provinces: Gulbarga, Bidar, Berar, Daulatabad
  • Tarafdar = Governor of the province
  • Two groups in court: Afaqi (foreign Sunnis from Iran/Iraq) vs Dakhni (local Shia Muslims)
  • Ibrahim Adil Shah introduced Dakhni as court language (in Bijapur)

🧠 5 Sultanates Hook — "Big Angry Gorillas Beat Bandits"

Berar · Ahmednagar · Golconda · Bijapur · Bidar

Or picture: 5 angry uncles fighting over their father's (Bahmani's) property, each running to their corner — Berar/NW, Bidar/centre, Bijapur/SW, Ahmednagar/NW, Golconda/SE

🏙 Mohammed Quli Qutb Shah (Golconda/Qutb Shahi)
  • Greatest ruler of Qutb Shahi dynasty
  • Built Charminar of Hyderabad 🔥
  • Founded Hyderabad city (earlier called Bhagya Nagar)
  • ⚠ He did NOT build Golconda Fort — it was built by Kakatiyas, later renovated
🕌 Gol Gumbaz (Bijapur)
  • Built during Ibrahim Adil Shah II of Adil Shahi dynasty
  • Second largest dome in the world
  • Famous for Whispering Gallery
  • Architect: Yaqut of Dabul ⭐

Smart Memory Hooks — All Key Facts

🧠 Hook 1 — KDR's Titles

"A Yavan Abhinav Andhra Pitamaha"
Picture KDR standing wearing a Greek toga (Yavan), calling himself a new Bhoja (Abhinav), while elders call him Andhra's grandfather (Pitamaha). He got the Greek title because he helped Bahmani (called Yavana/Greek) sort their internal fights.

🧠 Hook 2 — Hasan Gangu / Alauddin Hasan

He has TWO names. Think: Alauddin Hasan is his official name, Hasan Gangu is his street name. Like how your friend has a nickname. The Bahmani dynasty is literally named after him — Bahman Shah → Bahmani.

🧠 Hook 3 — Ibn Battuta's Book

Rehala = Reh + Ala = "Path of Ala (God)" — think of Ibn Battuta walking the path of God across continents from Morocco. He always ends up wherever there's chaos — Tughlaq's capital shift AND Harihar-Bukka's new empire.

🧠 Hook 4 — Deva Raya I vs II Travellers

I → Italy (Nicolo) · II → Iran/Persia (Abdul Razzaq)
"One = Italy, Two = Persia" — just pair the Roman numerals with the first letter of the country.

🧠 Hook 5 — Battle of Talikota 1565

Three Ss destroyed Vijay: Sixty Five (1565) + Three Sultanates (Bijapur, Ahmednagar, Golconda). Sadashiva = nominal king, Aliya Rama Raya = real boss but got killed. Like a CEO getting attacked while the Chairman hides.

🧠 Hook 6 — Gol Gumbaz

Gol = Round, Gumbaz = Dome. Round dome of Bijapur. Architect = Yaqut of Dabul. Famous for Whispering Gallery (whisper at one end = heard at the other). 2nd largest dome = think of it as the Silver medal dome of the world.

🧠 Hook 7 — Amarnayak vs Iqtadar

Same concept, different dynasty:
Delhi Sultanate → Iqta system → officer called Ikhtadar / Mukti / Wali
Vijayanagara → same land-for-army system → officer called Amarnayak
Think: they borrowed the idea but gave it a new name.

Concept Connections

Mohammad Bin Tughlaq ↔ Vijayanagara
  • Shifted capital Delhi → Daulatabad partly to control emerging South Indian powers
  • Powers did NOT weaken → he shifted capital back to Delhi
  • Ibn Battuta came during BOTH Tughlaq's time AND Vijayanagara's early period
Iqta System ↔ Amarnayak
  • Iqta: Introduced by Iltutmish, ended by Alauddin Khilji
  • Both systems: land given → revenue extracted → army maintained
  • Amarnayak had to appear at Mahanavami Dibba during Navaratri
Virupaksha Temple — Two Versions
  • Pattadakal: Built by Lok Mahadevi (Chalukya queen), builder = Lakkan Dandesh
  • Hampi: Gopuram built by KDR
  • Both = Virupaksha = Lord Shiva
Bahmani End ↔ Mughal Rise
  • Bahmani broke → 5 sultanates
  • Mughals absorbed them one by one: Ahmednagar first, then Bijapur, then Golconda
  • Vijayanagara ended at Talikota (1565) — Mughals not directly responsible

⚠ Exam Traps — What Students Get Wrong

⚠ TRAP 1 — Hampi Spelling

It is H-A-M-P-I, not HUMPI. Exam may write HUMPI as a wrong option — don't fall for it.

⚠ TRAP 2 — Nicolo di Conti vs Abdul Razzaq

Nicolo di Conti = Italy = Deva Raya I.
Abdul Razzaq = Persia = Deva Raya II.
Examiners LOVE swapping the country or the ruler. The "I = Italy" trick saves you.

⚠ TRAP 3 — Who Built Golconda Fort?

NOT Mohammed Quli Qutb Shah. It was built by Kakatiyas. Qutb Shah only renovated it. He built Charminar.

⚠ TRAP 4 — Mahmud Gawan was NOT a Sultan

He was the Peshwa (PM), not a ruler. His titles were Malik Tajar and Khwaja Jahan. Given by Humayun Shah.

⚠ TRAP 5 — Sadashiva Raya vs Aliya Rama Raya at Talikota

Sadashiva Raya = de jure (nominal) ruler. Aliya Rama Raya = de facto ruler (real power). The Deccan Sultans attacked because of Aliya Rama Raya's interference. Don't mix them up.

⚠ TRAP 6 — Yavana = Bahmani, NOT literal Greeks

KDR's title Yavana Raja Sthapanacharya → "Yavana" here refers to Bahmani Sultans, not ancient Greeks. He helped them resolve their internal disputes.

⚠ TRAP 7 — Two Virupaksha Temples

One in Pattadakal (Chalukya, Lok Mahadevi). One in Hampi (Vijayanagara, Gopuram by KDR). Exam may ask builder of a specific one.

⚠ TRAP 8 — Harihar vs Bukka — What Did Each Do?

Both are co-founders. Harihar's title = Karnataka Vidya Vilas. Bukka renamed city to Vidyanagara. Examiners swap their actions.

⚠ TRAP 9 — Mahanavami Dibba

Students confuse it with a sweet or random term. It is a red ceremonial platform in the Royal Centre. Relief carvings. Used for Navaratri performance viewing.

⚠ TRAP 10 — Amarnayak ≠ Only KDR

Amarnayak system existed throughout Vijayanagara, not just under KDR. If asked "who introduced it" — answer is unclear/not associated with one ruler.
