🗺 Big Picture

The Constitution's first two parts answer two fundamental questions every nation must answer: "What land is ours?" (Part I, Articles 1–4) and "Who are our people?" (Part II, Articles 5–11). Think of Part I as defining India's body and Part II as defining who belongs to that body.

ARTICLE 1 🔥 Must Know
Name and Territory of the Union
India, that is Bharat, shall be a union of states.

Territory includes:
  ① States (listed in 1st Schedule)
  ② Union Territories (listed in 1st Schedule)
  ③ Any territory India may acquire in future
India is an indestructible union of destructible states.
— Dr. B. R. Ambedkar  |  USA = indestructible union of indestructible states

Why "Union" NOT "Federation"? (Ambedkar's Logic)

🇮🇳 India (Union) 🇺🇸 USA (Federation)
Origin States exist BECAUSE of India 13 colonies AGREED to form USA
Agreement No agreement between states Formal agreement between states
Right to Leave ❌ States CANNOT secede Theoretically debated historically
Nature Indestructible Union, Destructible states Indestructible Union, Indestructible states
ARTICLE 2 ⭐ Important
Admission / Establishment of NEW States
Deals with territory that is CURRENTLY NOT PART OF INDIA.
Parliament can admit or establish new states on such terms as it thinks fit.

Real Example: Sikkim → admitted via 36th Amendment, 1975
Hypothetical: If Bangladesh/Pakistan were to join India → Art. 2
ARTICLE 3 🔥 Must Know
Formation / Alteration of EXISTING States
Deals with territory ALREADY PART OF INDIA. Parliament can:
① Form a new state by separating territory from any state
② Increase/Diminish area of any state
③ Alter boundaries
④ Change the name of any state

Real Examples:
• Uttarakhand carved from UP
• Chhattisgarh carved from MP
• Jharkhand carved from Bihar
• J&K made a UT in 2019

Procedure:
→ Bill introduced ONLY in Parliament (not state legislature)
→ President's recommendation required
→ President refers bill to affected state → State gives opinion
→ Opinion is ADVISORY, NOT BINDING on President or Parliament
→ 5th Amendment gave President power to set TIME LIMIT for state's opinion
ARTICLE 4 ⭐ Important
Not an Amendment under Article 368
Changes made under Articles 2 & 3 do NOT need constitutional amendment process.
Can be done by SIMPLE MAJORITY (1/2 + 1 of present and voting).

Such changes also require updating:
• 1st Schedule → Names & territories of states/UTs
• 4th Schedule → Allocation of Rajya Sabha seats

📌 Berubari Union Case, 1959 ⭐

Issue: Can India give territory to another country by simple majority?

SC Ruling: NO. Giving Indian territory to a foreign country requires Constitutional Amendment under Article 368 — simple majority is NOT enough.

Real Application: India-Bangladesh Land Boundary Agreement (2014) required 100th Constitutional Amendment — enclaves were exchanged between India and Bangladesh.

🪪 Part II: Citizenship (Arts. 5–11)

⚠ CRITICAL POINT
Articles 5–11 deal ONLY with citizenship provisions at the commencement of Constitution (26 Jan 1950). They have NO validity after that date. Current citizenship is governed by Citizenship Act, 1955.
ARTICLE 5
Citizenship at Commencement
Must have domicile in India + any ONE of:
① Born in India, OR
② Either parent born in India, OR
③ Resident in India for at least 5 years before 26 Jan 1950
ARTICLE 6
Migration FROM Pakistan (Partition)
• Came before 19 July 1948 → Automatic citizen
• Came after 19 July 1948 → Must register
ARTICLE 7
Migration TO Pakistan then returned
• Went to Pakistan BEFORE 1 March 1947 → Can return + get citizenship
• Went after → Must get permit; still can acquire citizenship
ARTICLE 8
Indians Residing Outside India (Abroad)
Persons of Indian origin living outside India (USA, Australia etc.) — their citizenship provisions
ARTICLE 9
Voluntary Foreign Citizenship = Loss of Indian Citizenship
If someone voluntarily acquires foreign citizenship → loses Indian citizenship
ARTICLE 10
Continuance of Rights
Once a citizen, your citizenship continues — Parliament cannot take it away arbitrarily
ARTICLE 11 🔥
Parliament's Power Over Citizenship
Only Parliament can make laws on citizenship — State Legislature CANNOT
Citizenship is in the Union List (7th Schedule)

🏛 Citizenship Act, 1955 — HOW TO ACQUIRE & LOSE

5 Ways to ACQUIRE Citizenship 🔥 Must Know

1. By Birth — Born in India. BUT: Earlier was Jus Soli (just being born on soil). NOW: at least ONE parent must be Indian. (USA still follows Jus Soli — a tourist's baby born in USA gets US citizenship)

2. By Descent — Born OUTSIDE India but parent is Indian. Must register with Indian Embassy/Consulate within 1 year

3. By Registration — Indian origin (parents were Indian), living outside. Stay in India for 7 years → can apply

4. By Naturalization — No connection to India. Live in India for 14 years, of which last 11 years continuous

5. By Incorporation of Territory — When a new territory joins India, its people automatically become Indian citizens (with conditions)

3 Ways to LOSE Citizenship ⭐

1. Renunciation — You voluntarily give up Indian citizenship
2. Termination — Government terminates because you acquired foreign citizenship without informing (since we don't allow dual citizenship)
3. Deprivation — Government strips citizenship if obtained through fraud / wrong documents

Taking territory in = small key (simple majority). Giving territory OUT = big key (constitutional amendment 368). Berubari case established: you need Article 368 to give away Indian land.

🔗 Concept Connections

Art. 2 → 1st Schedule: New state added → 1st Schedule updated
Art. 3 → 1st & 4th Schedule: Existing state changed → 1st Schedule (territory) + 4th Schedule (RS seats)
Art. 4 → Not Art. 368: These changes need only simple majority, NOT full constitutional amendment
Art. 11 → 7th Schedule (Union List): Citizenship is Union List subject → only Parliament legislates
Citizenship Act 1955 → Art. 11: The Act is Parliament exercising its power under Art. 11
Jus Soli abandoned → Single citizenship: India moved away from soil-based citizenship to maintain tighter control

⚠ Exam Traps

🔴 TRAP 1: Article 2 vs Article 3
  • Art. 2 = FOREIGN territory joining India (Sikkim, hypothetical Pakistan)
  • Art. 3 = EXISTING Indian territory being reorganized (Uttarakhand from UP)
  • ❌ Students often reverse this
🔴 TRAP 2: Union vs Federation
  • India is NOT a federation — it's a UNION of states
  • Ambedkar deliberately avoided "federation" word
  • States have NO right to secede from Indian Union
🔴 TRAP 3: State's Opinion on Art. 3 Bill
  • State's opinion is ADVISORY, NOT BINDING
  • State CANNOT block Parliament from changing its territory/name
  • Parliament is supreme here
🔴 TRAP 4: Citizenship Articles 5–11
  • These articles are NOT applicable today
  • They were only for 26 Jan 1950 commencement period
  • Current citizenship → Citizenship Act, 1955
🔴 TRAP 5: Dates in Art. 6 & 7
  • Art. 6: Magic date = 19 July 1948 (Pakistan migration)
  • Art. 7: Magic date = 1 March 1947 (went to Pakistan before this)
  • ❌ Students often mix these two dates up
🔴 TRAP 6: Naturalization Timing
  • Naturalization = 14 years total, 11 years CONTINUOUS (recent)
  • NOT 11 total and 14 continuous — it's the opposite!
  • Registration (Indian-origin, living abroad) = 7 years stay in India
🔴 TRAP 7: JVP Committee Full Form
  • J = Jawaharlal Nehru
  • V = Vallabhbhai Patel
  • P = Patta Sitaramayya (NOT Potti Sriramalu — Potti was the fasting man!)
