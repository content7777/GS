India 1924–1930
Rise, Revolt & Civil Disobedience
Socialism · HRA · Simon Commission · Dandi March · Round Table Conferences

This chapter covers the most turbulent decade before Gandhi's great Salt revolt. It's the era when Indian nationalism split into two streams: the constitutional path (Nehru Report, Round Tables) and the revolutionary path (HRA, bomb attacks, Chittagong raid). Socialism arrived from Russia, caste movements challenged social order, and the British fumbled with the Simon Commission — an all-white committee that accidentally ignited the very freedom they hoped to manage. The chapter ends with Dandi March 1930 — Gandhi's master stroke that made salt a symbol of sovereignty.

🧠 Chapter Memory Palace — "The Revolutionary Hotel"

Imagine a grand old hotel called "India 1924" with different rooms, each holding major events. Walk through it before every exam.

HOTEL LOBBY — Socialism Arrives

As you enter, a Russian soldier stands at the door — he represents the Russian Revolution bringing Socialism. He hands you a card: "MN Roy, Tashkent, 1920" — CPI founded. A board inside reads: Kanpur 1925 — CPI formalised. Near the reception is a big union sign: AITUC 1920 — three men run it: NM Joshi (the Joshi from Junction), Lala Lajpat Rai (Lion of Punjab), and Joseph Baptista (the Baptism priest). The Father of Unions poster shows NM Lokhande.

ROOM 1 — Caste Movements

Inside Room 1, a giant poster of Periyar in Tamil Nadu: Self Respect Movement 1924 — led by EV Ramaswamy Nayakar (Periyar), started by the Ezhava community in Kerala. On the wall: Ambedkar breaking a pot of water at Mahad (1927) — Mahad Satyagraha. And a temple gate: Kalaram Temple 1930 — Temple Entry Movement by Ambedkar.

ROOM 2 — The Revolutionaries' Den

Three beds: Bismil, Sachin Sanyal, Jogesh Chatterjee — they formed HRA in 1924. On the table: a train model labelled Kakori 1926 — Ashfaqullah Khan, Roshan Singh, Ram Prasad Bismil, Rajendra Lahiri raided it. After the British crackdown, they rebranded — a new flag on the wall reads HSRA, formed at Feroz Shah Kotla, Delhi with Chandrashekhar Azad, Bhagat Singh, Sukhdev.

ROOM 3 — The Simon Fiasco & Saunders

A British officer sits alone at a round table — 7 white men, zero Indians — that's the Simon Commission 1927. Banners: "Simon Go Back" — coined by Yusuf Meher Ali. Lala Lajpat Rai is hit by James Scott with a baton — he dies of injuries. Bhagat Singh, Sukhdev, and Rajguru rush in and shoot — but they get the wrong man: Saunders instead of Scott. Hanging date: 23 March 1931.

HOTEL CONFERENCE HALL — Reports & Sessions

A report on the table: Nehru Report 1928 — chairman Motilal Nehru (NOT Jawaharlal). Muslim League counter: Delhi Proposals. Jinnah's page: 14 Points. The hall hosts the Lahore Session 1929 — Jawaharlal Nehru presides, Poorna Swaraj declared, 26 Jan 1930 set as First Independence Day. That's why Constitution was enforced on 26 Jan 1950.

ROOFTOP — Dandi March

Gandhi stands at the top with salt in hand. He left Sabarmati on 12 March 1930 with 78 followers, walked 240 miles, reached Dandi on 6 April 1930, made salt — breaking the Salt Law. He's arrested on 4 May. Sarojini Naidu and Manilal carry on. Around the rooftop: Rajagopalachari (Trichinopoly → Vedraniyam), Khan Abdul Ghaffar Khan (Frontier Gandhi), Surya Sen (Chittagong), Rani Gaidinliu (Manipur).

HOTEL EXIT — Pacts & Conferences

At the exit: Gandhi and Irwin shake hands — Gandhi-Irwin Pact 1931. Gandhi gets: release of non-violent prisoners, return of unsold property, right to make salt. Gandhi gives: suspend CDM, attend 2nd Round Table Conference. Three RTC signs on the door: 1930 London (Congress absent), 1931 London (Gandhi attends), 1932 London (Congress absent again). Ambedkar attends all three.

1. Socialism & Its Spread in India
What is Socialism?
🔥
Philosophy: abolish the ruling class; power to the people. Opposite of capitalism (few own everything). Sibling of communism.
🔥
Socialism in India arrived via the Russian Revolution.
Communist Party of India (CPI)
🔥
Founded 1920 by Manindra Nath Roy (MN Roy) in Tashkent, Uzbekistan.
🔥
Formalised in Kanpur in 1925.
⭐
Kanpur Bolshevik Conspiracy Case 1924 — involved Muzaffar Ahmed, Shaukat Usmani (same person).
All India Trade Union Congress (AITUC)
🔥
Founded 1920 by Narayan Malhar Joshi, Lala Lajpat Rai, Joseph Baptista.
🔥
Father of Trade Unions in India = NM Lokhande (not the founders of AITUC!).
2. Caste Movements
🔥

🔥
Mahad Satyagraha 1927 — led by Dr. BR Ambedkar. Right to drink water from public tank.
🔥
Temple Entry Movement — Ambedkar from Kalaram Temple, 1930.

🔥

6. Simon Commission
🔥
Background: GOI Act 1919 (Montagu-Chelmsford Reforms) had provision for review after 10 years. But commission came in 1927 — 8 years later (not 10!).
🔥
Chairman: John Simon. 7 members — ALL British, 0 Indians. Called "White Commission."
🔥
Report published in 1928 as a White Paper.
🔥
Slogan "Simon Go Back" coined by Yusuf Meher Ali.
⭐
Almost all parties boycotted it. Hindu Mahasabha did NOT oppose it — exam trap!
⭐
Madras Session 1928 — Congress gave official approval to boycott Simon Commission (special session, not a regular annual session).
🔥
Lala Lajpat Rai beaten by James Scott during protest. Died of injuries → avenged by killing of Saunders.

🎯 Smart Memory Hooks
🧠 Hook 1 — CPI Founders

Imagine MN Roy as a Russian spy (Mister Night Roy) whispering secrets in Tashkent (a Uzbek tea shop). He's sipping chai and founding a party. Then in 1925, he opens a proper office in Kanpur — "Kanpur ka daftar."

🧠 Hook 2 — AITUC vs Father of Trade Unions

AITUC founded by NM Joshi + Lala + Baptista → think "Three Men in a Union": a Joshi (the organiser), a Lion (Lala Lajpat Rai), and a priest doing a Baptism (Baptista). But the Father is NM Lokhande — he came earlier, he's the grandfather of them all.

🧠 Hook 3 — HRA Founders

Three names to remember: Bismil + Sachin Sanyal + Jogesh Chatterjee. Imagine them as BSJ = Bharat Swatantra Jodis — best freedom trio. Bismil writes poetry (Sarfaroshi ki Tamanna), Sachin runs a Samiti, Jogesh plays chess. Together they start HRA 1924 in Bihar/UP/Punjab.

🧠 Hook 4 — Kakori 4 Names

The Kakori 4: A-R-A-R = Ashfaqullah, Roshan Singh, Ashfaqullah wait… → Ashfaqullah Khan, Ram Prasad Bismil, Roshan Singh, Rajendra Lahiri = "AR3". Picture 4 rebels stopping a train near Lucknow and stealing British guns. The British hang them — which creates the martyr fuel for Bhagat Singh.

🧠 Hook 5 — Simon Commission (White & Wrong)

7 white men → 0 Indians → "All White, All Wrong." Slogan: "Simon Go Back" = coined by Yusuf Meher Ali (remember: the Ali who sent Simon back). Came in 1927 — remember: "8 not 10" — Simon came early to insult Indians early.

🧠 Hook 6 — Nehru Report Confusion Buster

Students always confuse which Nehru. "The REPORT was from the FATHER, not the SON." Motilal Nehru = father = chairman of Nehru Committee 1928. Jawaharlal = son = presided Lahore Session 1929. Father made the report, Son declared independence.

🧠 Hook 7 — Dandi March Numbers

12-3-1930 to 6-4-1930 → March 12 to April 6. Distance: 240 miles. Followers: 78. Memory: "2-4-0 and 7-8" → "two for zero Indians had to walk 78 miles each... no wait, 240 miles with 78 people." Just lock: 240 miles, 78 followers.

🧠 Hook 8 — Frontier Gandhi

Khan Abdul Ghaffar Khan = red-shirted Pashtun warrior who was actually a Gandhian pacifist. Picture a fierce-looking Pashtun chieftain wearing a Gandhi cap and a red shirt — that's the Khudai Khidmatgar. His nickname "Frontier Gandhi" = he was the Gandhi of the frontier/border region. One of 2 non-Indians with Bharat Ratna. (Who is the other? Research it!)

🧠 Hook 9 — Bomb in Assembly (Context)

Bhagat Singh didn't throw to kill. He threw to make the deaf hear. Target: Trade Disputes Bill which hurt workers. The bomb made noise, Bhagat Singh and BK Dutt waited to be arrested — they wanted a platform to speak. Remember: "Deaf Parliament + Worker's Bill = Bomb for attention."

🧠 Hook 10 — RTC Attendance Formula

Ambedkar = Perfect Attendance Award (all 3 RTCs). Gandhi = "Came once, left unsatisfied" (only 2nd). Congress boycotted 1st and 3rd. Think: Gandhi came to the middle conference — the 2nd one in 1931 — and left empty-handed. That's why CDM resumed.

⚠️ EXAM TRAPS — Where Students Lose Marks
⚠️ TRAP 1 — Which Nehru?

Nehru Report 1928 = Chairman was MOTILAL Nehru (father). NOT Jawaharlal. But Lahore Session 1929 was presided by JAWAHARLAL Nehru (son). Confusing them is the #1 mistake.

⚠️ TRAP 2 — James Scott vs Saunders

James Scott BEAT Lala Lajpat Rai (not killed directly). Bhagat Singh's group INTENDED to kill James Scott but ACCIDENTALLY killed Saunders instead. Examiners love asking "who was killed by Bhagat Singh?"

⚠️ TRAP 3 — Simon Commission: 8 years not 10

GOI Act 1919 said review after 10 years (= 1929). But Simon Commission was formed in 1927 — 8 years early. Students write 1929. Answer: 1927 (formed), 1928 (report).

⚠️ TRAP 4 — Who boycotted Simon Commission?

Almost ALL parties boycotted, EXCEPT: Hindu Mahasabha did NOT boycott / oppose it. This is frequently asked in statement-based questions.

⚠️ TRAP 5 — CPI Founded vs Formalised

CPI founded 1920 by MN Roy in Tashkent. CPI formalised 1925 in Kanpur. Don't mix up year+place.

⚠️ TRAP 6 — Father of Trade Unions vs AITUC Founders

AITUC founders = NM Joshi, Lala Lajpat Rai, Joseph Baptista (1920). Father of Trade Unions = NM Lokhande (different person, earlier). Do NOT say NM Joshi = Father of Trade Unions.

⚠️ TRAP 7 — Bomb in Assembly Purpose

Bhagat Singh and BK Dutt threw bomb in Central Legislative Assembly NOT to kill anyone but to protest the Trade Disputes Bill. Examiners ask: purpose = "to make the deaf hear" / protest against Trade Disputes Bill.

⚠️ TRAP 8 — Round Table Conference Attendance

Ambedkar attended ALL 3. Gandhi/Congress attended only 2nd (1931). Students often mark Gandhi at 1st or 3rd.

⚠️ TRAP 9 — Dandi March Dates
  • Start: 12 March 1930
  • End/Salt making: 6 April 1930
  • Gandhi arrested: 4 May 1930
  • Distance: 240 miles (not km, not 340, not 250)
  • Followers: 78
⚠️ TRAP 10 — Martyrs' Day Dates

TWO Martyrs' Days: 23 March (Bhagat Singh, Sukhdev, Rajguru, 1931) and 30 January (Gandhi assassination, 1948). Both are official. Don't get confused which is which.

⚠️ TRAP 11 — HSRA Formation Location

HSRA formed at Feroz Shah Kotla, Delhi (not Lahore, not Lucknow). Main founder = Chandrashekhar Azad.

⚠️ TRAP 12 — Self Respect Movement Geography

Movement was started by Periyar (EV Ramaswamy Nayakar). Led by Ezhava community. Location is Kerala/South India region. Started in 1924.
