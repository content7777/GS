Jainism & Buddhism

SSC CGL high-retention revision system  ·  built to survive 100 revisions  ·  FRB 2.0

Big Picture: Two reformist movements rose together in 6th century BC Eastern India, both reacting against Vedic ritualism and Brahmin dominance, both patronized by the same kings (Bimbisara, Ajatashatru), both founded by Kshatriya princes who renounced royal life. Jainism stayed small and intensely ascetic; Buddhism scaled globally and split into schools. Every fact below hangs on this one skeleton: two parallel lives, two parallel doctrines, two parallel councils, two parallel architectures.

2. Mahavira — Complete Life Profile

Birth & Death

  • Born: 540 BC, Kundagram, Vaishali (Bihar)
  • Died: 468 BC, Pavapuri, near Bihar Sharif
  • Lived to age 72

Family

  • Father: Siddhartha (Jnatrika Kshatriya clan)
  • Mother: Trishala
  • Wife: Yashoda
  • Daughter: Anuja Priyadarshini
  • Son-in-law: Jamali — first disciple

Renunciation → Enlightenment

  • Left home at age 30, with Makhali Gosala
  • Gosala later broke away → founded Ajivika Sect (fatalism + extreme asceticism)
  • Attained Kevalya Jnana at age 42
  • Place: under a Sal tree, village Jrimbhikagrama, on bank of river Rijupalika

Titles & First Acts

  • First sermon: at Pava
  • Died at: Pavapuri (different from Pava — don't confuse!)
  • Title Jitendra — conqueror of senses
  • Title Kevalin — the omniscient one
Memory hook — Pava vs Pavapuri: Picture Mahavira giving his first speech at "Pava" (short word, short moment — a quick sermon) and, decades later, walking a few extra steps to "Pavapuri" (longer word, longer journey — his final, longest rest, i.e. death). Short name = beginning. Long name = the end of the road.

3. Key Jain Vocabulary

TermMeaning
BasadiJain monasteries / temples
Triratna of JainismSamyak Jnana (right knowledge) + Samyak Darshan (right faith) + Samyak Charitra (right conduct)

Memory hook — Aparigraha's payoff: Imagine a man who owns nothing, so nothing can be taken from him — he can't be made happy by gain or sad by loss. He just floats in one calm, level mood. That flat, unshaken line is what "Samyak Charitra"-style stability feels like — useful for remembering why Aparigraha is on the list, not just that it is.

4. Classical Languages of India — Side Fact

Originally 6 classical languages: Tamil, Sanskrit, Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam, Odia. Later 5 more added: Pali, Prakrit, Assamese, Bengali, Marathi.

Hook: "TaSTe Kalo MaaN" → Tamil, Sanskrit, Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam, Odia(N) — the original six tasted (TaSTe) first. The five latecomers Pali, Prakrit, Assamese, Bengali, Marathi joined later — "PPABM" walked in after the original party (PPABM ≈ think of 5 latecomers knocking — Please Please Allow Bro, Man!).

6. The Great Schism — Shwetambara vs Digambara

Famine in Magadha→Chandragupta Maurya + commander Bhadrabahu migrate→Shravanabelgola, Karnataka→Bhadrabahu performs Sallekhana (ritual death)

His followers had stopped wearing clothes due to the famine and stayed unclothed in the South → became Digambaras ("sky-clad"). Those who stayed back (under Sthulabhadra) resumed wearing white clothes → became Shwetambaras ("white-clad").

DigambaraShwetambara
Founder figureBhadrabahuSthulabhadra
ClothingNone ("sky-clad")White clothes
RegionMigrated South (Karnataka)Stayed North
Stance on 1st CouncilBoycotted itAccepted (presided by Sthulabhadra)
Memory hook: "Digambara" — sounds bare, naked, gone-South. "Shwet" literally means white — easy: Shwetambara = white clothes. Once you lock "Shwet=white," Digambara is whatever Shwet is NOT — bare.

10. The Four Noble Truths (Arya Satya)

  1. Dukkha — The world is full of suffering
  2. Samudaya — The cause of suffering is desire (Tanha)
  3. Nirodha — Suffering can be ended
  4. Marga — The path to end suffering is the Eightfold Path (Ashtangika Marga)
Memory hook — why he discovered this: A prince who was shielded from every sorrow still saw, on one ride outside the palace, a sick man, a dead body, an old man, and a wandering ascetic — the Four Sights. That single ride compresses into the Four Truths: sickness/age/death = "world is full of suffering"; his own restless wanting = "desire is the cause"; the ascetic = proof suffering can be conquered; the path he eventually found = the Eightfold Path.

14. The Great Split: Hinayana vs Mahayana

Hinayana ("Lesser Vehicle")Mahayana ("Greater Vehicle")
Idol worshipNo — Buddha seen as ordinary teacherYes — Buddha seen as divine being
Belief in rebirth of BuddhaNoYes
FocusIndividual enlightenment via personal effortHelping others toward enlightenment (Bodhisattva ideal)
LanguagePaliSanskrit
Region todaySri Lanka, Thailand, Cambodia, Vietnam (South/SE Asia)China, Korea, Japan

Bodhisattva = an enlightened being who postpones their own nirvana to help others attain enlightenment. Examples: Padmapani, Amitabha (not Amitabh Bachchan!).

Tantric Buddhism — developed mainly in North Bengal and Sikkim; tantra-mantra elements merged into Buddhist practice.

Hook: "Hina" sounds like "minus/lesser" — lesser vehicle, no idols, just a wise teacher. "Maha" sounds like "mega/grand" — grand vehicle, grand divine Buddha, statues everywhere. The country list follows the same logic: SE Asia kept it simple (Hinayana); East Asia (China/Korea/Japan) built it grand (Mahayana).

1Name & Spelling Traps

Bindusara ≠ Bimbisara. Bindusara = Mauryan king, son of Chandragupta Maurya, father of Ashoka, patron of 1st Jain Council (298 BC). Bimbisara = Magadhan king, much earlier era, contemporary of both Mahavira and Buddha.
Pava ≠ Pavapuri. Mahavira gave his first sermon at Pava. He died at Pavapuri. Easy to swap under exam pressure.
Mahakassapa / Sabakami / Moggaliputta Tissa / Vasumitra presided over the four Buddhist councils respectively — easily jumbled with the four patron kings (Ajatashatru, Kalashoka, Ashoka, Kanishka). Question may ask "who presided" vs "who patronized" — read carefully.
Milinda ≠ a random king. Milinda is the same person as Menander, an Indo-Greek ruler. Exams may use either name.

2Similar Concepts, Different Religions

"Triratna" exists in both religions but means different things. Jain Triratna = right knowledge/faith/conduct. Buddhist Triratna = Buddha, Dhamma, Sangha. A question simply saying "Triratna" without naming the religion is testing whether you know both.
Both Mahavira and Buddha have a daughter "Anuja Priyadarshini" and son-in-law/first disciple "Jamali" in this lecture's framing. This is the single most dangerous trap in the whole chapter — always verify via clan name (Jnatrika = Mahavira; Shakya = Buddha) before answering.
Both founders left home accompanied/guided by a figure who later broke away to form a rival sect with extreme/fatalistic views — for Mahavira this is Makhali Gosala → Ajivika sect. Don't confuse Ajivika (fatalism) with either Digambara or Hinayana.

3Sect/School Confusions

Digambara vs Shwetambara — remember by clothing literally: Shwet = white (clothes), Digambara = sky-clad (no clothes). Founders: Digambara→Bhadrabahu, Shwetambara→Sthulabhadra.
Hinayana vs Mahayana — remember by scale of imagination: Hinayana keeps Buddha human-sized (teacher, no idols, Pali); Mahayana makes Buddha cosmic (divine, idols, Sanskrit, Bodhisattvas). Geography: Hinayana → South/SE Asia; Mahayana → East Asia (China, Korea, Japan).

4Number & Date Traps

Council date jumps look patterned (~100 years apart): 480→383→250 BC, then a long jump to 72 AD (Kanishka revives Buddhism centuries later under Kushans). Don't assume a fixed 100-year rule applies to the 4th council.
Mahamastakabhisheka festival at Shravanabelgola is every 12 years — easily confused with other "12-year cycle" facts from other chapters (e.g., Kumbh Mela). Keep them in separate mental boxes.
1st Jain Council = 298 BC; 1st Buddhist Council ≈ 480/483 BC. Don't accidentally merge these two "first councils" into one date.

5Geography Traps

Largest stupa in India (Kesaria, Bihar) vs largest stupa in the world (Borobudur, Indonesia) — a classic "in India" vs "in the world" qualifier trap.
Dhamekh Stupa's distinguishing feature is its cylindrical shape — most other stupas are described as hemispherical domes; this is the outlier shape to remember.
Two Pala-dynasty universities (Vikramshila by Dharmapala, Odantapuri by Gopala) vs Nalanda, which is Gupta-dynasty (Kumaragupta I) — don't lump all three under one dynasty.
