🗺️ Big Picture

This chapter has two unrelated empires fighting for two different things — keep them in separate mental boxes:

North India — Tripartite Struggle

Three powers, one prize: Kannauj. Gurjara-Pratiharas, Palas, and Rashtrakutas spent the 8th–9th century AD fighting over Kannauj because whoever held it controlled the Gangetic heartland — the ancient "seat of power" since Harshavardhana's Vardhana dynasty.

South India — Chola Empire

Two Chola lives. Sangam-age Cholas (3rd c. BC–3rd c. AD, minor power, squeezed out by Chera & Pandya) die out — then re-emerge in the 9th century AD as Imperial/Later Cholas, who go on to build a genuine empire stretching to Sri Lanka, Maldives, and even raid Southeast Asia.

🧠 Chapter Memory Palace

🏯 THE BATTLE FOR KANNAUJ — "The City With Three Doors"

Picture Kannauj as a fortress city with three gates — North Gate, West Gate, East Gate. Three armies are marching toward it from three directions. Walk through the gates in order:

🗡️

West Gate — Gurjara-Pratiharas (from Bhinmal)

Founder Nagabhatta I marches in first and briefly grabs Kannauj. His descendant Nagabhatta II holds it longer — until the Rashtrakuta king Govinda III rides up from the south and humiliates him.

📿

East Gate — Palas (from Bengal)

Gopala founds the dynasty and builds Odantapuri University. His son Dharmapala builds the more famous Vikramashila University and briefly plants the Pala flag on Kannauj. Generations later, weak king Mahipala is beaten down by Rashtrakuta Indra III.

⛰️

South Gate — Rashtrakutas (the "referee" from the Deccan)

They never permanently hold Kannauj themselves — they just keep beating up whoever does. Think of them as the strongest wrestler in the ring who keeps throwing both other fighters out, but never sits on the throne himself. (Founder Dantidurga, builder of Kailasa Temple = Krishna I.)

🎬 Mental Movie: Two men (Pratihara & Pala) are fighting over a crown at the gate of Kannauj — and a giant strongman (Rashtrakuta) keeps walking in, knocking BOTH of them down, taking nothing, and walking back to the Deccan. That's the whole Tripartite Struggle in one image.

🐅 THE TIGER'S TWO LIVES — Chola Memory Palace

Picture a tiger flag (Chola emblem) being lowered in 300 AD... and raised again 600 years later, bigger and fiercer. Walk the tiger's journey:

🐯

Life 1: The Sangam Cub (3rd c. BC – 3rd c. AD)

Capital Uraiyur/Tanjore, port Puhar (Kaveripattinam). Squeezed between Chera (bow & arrow, capital Vanji) and Pandya (fish emblem, capital Madurai) until it fades out.

🦁

Life 2 begins: Vijayalaya steals back Tanjore (9th c. AD)

Vijayalaya, a Pallava feudatory, founds the Imperial Cholas, snatching Tanjore from the Muttaraiyars and building a temple to Nishumbasudani there.

👑

Raja Raja I — the Builder-Conqueror

Defeats Cheras (Trivandrum) and Pandyas (takes Madurai), invades (does not fully conquer) Sri Lanka's capital Anuradhapura, touches the Maldives, and builds the Brihadeshwara Temple at Thanjavur — hence also called Rajarajeshwara Temple.

🌊

Rajendra I — the "Napoleon of South India"

Son of Raja Raja I. Fully conquers Anuradhapura (Sri Lanka). Crosses the Ganga, defeats Pala kings there, takes the title Gangaikonda Chola ("conqueror of the Ganga") and builds new capital Gangaikonda Cholapuram with its own Brihadeshwara temple. Also raids the Buddhist Srivijaya Empire (Shailendra dynasty, modern Indonesia) — once a friend, now a target.

📜

Administration Room — Uttaramerur Inscription

A village in Chingleput district, Tamil Nadu, that records Chola local self-government: Ur (commoners' assembly) and Sabha (learned Brahmins' assembly), 3-year terms, property-owners only could contest. Taxes: Vetti (forced/bonded labour) and Kadamai (land revenue).

🌅

Sunset — 13th century decline

Cholas fade; Pandyas and Hoysalas take their place in the south, while Yadavas (Devagiri) and Kakatiyas (Warangal) replace the Later Chalukyas in the Deccan — both later raided by Alauddin Khilji's general Malik Kafur.

📖 Complete Revision Notes

🗡️ A. Tripartite Struggle (Kannauj Triangle) — Must Know

What: A 3-way fight for control of Kannauj between the Gurjara-Pratiharas, Palas, and Rashtrakutas, c. 8th–9th century AD (commonly dated from ~790 AD).

Why Kannauj mattered: Earlier called Kanyakubja. Originally the capital of the Maukharis, then became the capital of Harshavardhana's Vardhana dynasty — making it the symbolic seat of North Indian imperial power.

Rashtrakutas (Deccan) — the "spoilers"

  • Founder: Dantidurga — defeated Kirtivarman II (last Chalukya of Badami).
  • Krishna I built the monolithic rock-cut Kailasa Temple, Ellora (Maharashtra) — not the Kailasanatha Temple of Tamil Nadu, which was built by Narasimhavarman II (Pallava).
  • Govinda III defeated Gurjara-Pratihara king Nagabhatta II.
  • Indra III defeated Pala king Mahipala.
  • Rashtrakutas never permanently kept Kannauj — they intervened, won battles, and withdrew to the Deccan.

Gurjara-Pratiharas (West/Rajasthan)

  • Founder: Nagabhatta I, capital Bhinmal; first captured Kannauj briefly.
  • Nagabhatta II held Kannauj later but was defeated by Rashtrakuta Govinda III.
  • Later disintegration gave rise to Rajput clans: Pratiharas → Parmars, Chandelas, Chauhans emerge from this collapse.
  • The Tomars were the first Rajput rulers of Delhi, succeeded by the Chauhans (covered later in Medieval History).

Palas (Bengal)

  • Founder: Gopala — established Odantapuri University.
  • Dharmapala (Gopala's son) founded the more famous Vikramashila University; briefly held Kannauj.
  • Order of rulers: Gopala → Dharmapala → Devapala → Mahipala → Rampala...
  • Religion: Followers of Mahayana Buddhism; ruled the Bengal region.
  • Mahipala defeated by Rashtrakuta Indra III.
  • Succeeded eventually by the Sena dynasty.

🦁 C. Chola Empire — Phase 2: Imperial / Later Cholas (from 9th c. AD)

Self-styled descendants of the old Cholas. Founder: Vijayalaya — originally a feudatory (subordinate) of the Pallavas.

Background: the Pallava connection

  • Pallava founder: Simhavishnu; capital Kanchipuram.
  • Pulakeshin II (Chalukya) defeated Pallava king Mahendravarman I.
  • Mahendravarman I's son Narasimhavarman I avenged this — defeated Pulakeshin II and took the title "Vatapikonda."
  • Narasimhavarman I captured Tanjore (Thanjavur) from the Muttaraiyars (who ruled it as Pallava subordinates) and built a temple to Nishumbasudani Devi there.

Rise of the Imperial Cholas (9th century AD)

  • Defeated the Pandyas at Vellore, but lost to Rashtrakuta king Krishna III.

🔥 Raja Raja I

  • Defeated Cheras at Trivandrum.
  • Defeated Pandyas; conquered their capital Madurai.
  • Invaded (not fully conquered) Sri Lanka, then called Ceylon — attacked capital Anuradhapura.
  • Sent his son and daughter to Sri Lanka to spread Buddhism.
  • Conquered the Maldives.
  • Built the Brihadeshwara Temple at Thanjavur, also called Rajarajeshwara Temple after him.

🔥 Rajendra I (son of Raja Raja I)

  • Approx. period: 1014 AD onward (reign roughly 1014–1044 AD-ish window mentioned in lecture).
  • Contemporary of Mahmud Ghazni.
  • Known as the "Napoleon of South India."
  • Fully and completely conquered Sri Lanka's capital Anuradhapura (Raja Raja I had only invaded it).
  • Overran/defeated the Cheras and Pandyas.
  • Crossed the Ganga river, defeated local kings including those of the Pala dynasty.
  • Took the title "Gangaikonda Chola" ("conqueror of the Ganga") and built the new capital city Gangaikonda Cholapuram, with its own Brihadeshwara Temple.
  • Attacked the Srivijaya Empire (Shailendra dynasty, Buddhist, in present-day Indonesia) — relations had soured despite earlier cordial ties.

Conflict with the Deccan & Decline

  • Constant wars with the Chalukyas of Kalyani (Later/Western Chalukyas).
  • Chola Chalukya lineage: Badami Chalukyas (capital Vatapi) → Eastern Chalukyas (capital Vengi, founder Vishnuvardhan) → Later Chalukyas of Kalyani (capital Kalyani).
  • Chola Empire began declining in the early 13th century.
  • Replaced in the south by Pandyas and Hoysalas (capital Dwarasamudra/Halebidu; famous for star-shaped temples — Chennakeshava Temple for Vishnu, Hoysaleswara Temple for Shiva).
  • Later Chalukyas replaced by Yadavas (capital Devagiri) and Kakatiyas (capital Warangal) — later raided by Alauddin Khilji's commander Malik Kafur.

⭐ Reminder: the Satavahanas (dynasty name starts with "S", capital starts with "P" — Pratishthana) were the first to introduce land grants to Brahmins, and the first to issue lead coins — a separate fact, don't confuse with Chola land grants.

Local Self-Government

  • Ur — assembly of common people.
  • Sabha — assembly of learned Brahmins.
  • Village affairs managed by executive committees; elections held; members served 3-year terms; only property owners could contest elections.
  • Source: Uttaramerur Inscription, Chingleput district, Tamil Nadu.

Taxation

  • Vetti — tax paid as forced/bonded labour.
  • Kadamai — tax in the form of land revenue.

🪝 Smart Memory Hooks

Hook 1 — The Two Universities of Bengal

Gopala = Grandfather, builds the older house (Odantapuri). Dharmapala = son, builds the bigger, more famous house (Vikramashila). Alphabetically: Gopala → Odantapuri (both come "first"); Dharmapala → Vikramashila is the "famous one," like a son outshining his father.

Hook 2 — Kailasa Temple: Don't Mix Your Krishnas

Picture two temples both called "Kailash/Kailasanatha" — one in Maharashtra carved by Krishna I (Rashtrakuta) at Ellora (a rock-cut monolith, carved top-down like a sculpture, not built brick by brick), and one in Tamil Nadu built by Narasimhavarman II (Pallava). Mental tag: "Ellora's Krishna carves rock; Tamil Nadu's Narasimha builds stone."

Hook 3 — "Konda" = Conquered

Two Chola kings, two "Konda" titles: Narasimhavarman I → "Vatapikonda" (conqueror of Vatapi, the Chalukya capital). Rajendra I → "Gangaikonda Chola" (conqueror of the Ganga). Whenever you see "-konda" in a Tamil-dynasty title, it always means "who conquered ___."

Hook 4 — Invaded vs Conquered (Sri Lanka)

Raja Raja I = "knocked on the door" (invaded Anuradhapura). Rajendra I = "walked in and took the house" (fully conquered Anuradhapura). Father started it, son finished it — same pattern as the Ganga campaign being completed by Rajendra.

Hook 5 — Vetti vs Kadamai

Vetti sounds like "sweat" — forced labour tax (you sweat with your body). Kadamai sounds like "cut from my land" — land revenue tax (you pay from your land's produce).

Hook 6 — Ur vs Sabha (link to Vedic Age)

Just like Early Vedic Sabha (elite) and Samiti (commoners), Chola villages had Sabha (learned Brahmins) and Ur (common people). Same two-tier idea, 1,500+ years apart — humans keep reinventing the same village democracy.

⚠️ Exam Traps

Kailasa Temple — TWO different temples, TWO different builders

Kailasa Temple, Ellora (Maharashtra) → built by Krishna I, Rashtrakuta. Kailasanatha Temple, Tamil Nadu → built by Narasimhavarman II, Pallava. SSC loves swapping these two.

Two Brihadeshwara Temples

One at Thanjavur by Raja Raja I (also called Rajarajeshwara). One at Gangaikonda Cholapuram by Rajendra I. Don't assign both to the same king.

Kandariya Mahadev ≠ Chola Temple

It's part of the Khajuraho Group, built by the Chandela dynasty in Madhya Pradesh — Nagara style. A favourite "odd one out" trap question among Chola/Dravida temples.

Pandyas vs Pandavas

Easy misread under exam pressure — Cholas fought the Pandyas (dynasty), not the Pandavas (Mahabharata).

Invaded vs Conquered — Sri Lanka

Raja Raja I invaded Anuradhapura (did not fully hold it). Rajendra I fully conquered it. A classic statement-based trap: "Raja Raja I conquered Sri Lanka" = FALSE.

Gopala vs Dharmapala — who built which university

Gopala → Odantapuri. Dharmapala (his son) → Vikramashila. Frequently swapped in MCQs.

Who defeated whom — Rashtrakuta wins

Govinda III defeated Nagabhatta II (Pratihara). Indra III defeated Mahipala (Pala). Don't cross these wires — match the right Rashtrakuta king to the right victim.

Three Chalukya branches — similar names, different capitals

Badami Chalukyas (capital Vatapi) → Eastern Chalukyas (capital Vengi) → Later/Western Chalukyas of Kalyani (capital Kalyani). The Cholas fought the Kalyani branch specifically.

Vatapikonda vs Gangaikonda

Narasimhavarman I (Pallava) → "Vatapikonda." Rajendra I (Chola) → "Gangaikonda Chola." Different dynasties, similar-sounding "-konda" titles.

Hoysaleswara vs Chennakeshava

Chennakeshava Temple = Vishnu. Hoysaleswara Temple = Shiva. Both at Halebidu/Dwarasamudra (Hoysala capital) — easy to flip the deity.

Land grant types — similar-sounding Sanskrit terms

Brahmadeya (Brahmins) vs Devadana (temples) vs Palli-chchandam (Jains) vs Shalabhoga (schools) — all start differently but sound similar in a hurried read. Also don't confuse this Chola practice with the Satavahanas, who were the first to grant land to Brahmins historically.

🎯 Examiner's Favourite Questions

Match the Following (high probability)

  • Kingdom ↔ Emblem (Chera–Bow&Arrow, Pandya–Fish, Chola–Tiger)
  • King ↔ Title ("Vatapikonda" – Narasimhavarman I; "Gangaikonda Chola" – Rajendra I; "Napoleon of South India" – Rajendra I)
  • Temple ↔ Builder (Brihadeshwara Thanjavur–Raja Raja I; Brihadeshwara Gangaikonda Cholapuram–Rajendra I; Kandariya Mahadev–Chandelas; Kailasa Ellora–Krishna I)
  • Dynasty ↔ Founder (Pala–Gopala; Rashtrakuta–Dantidurga; Gurjara-Pratihara–Nagabhatta I; Imperial Chola–Vijayalaya)
  • Land grant type ↔ Recipient (Brahmadeya–Brahmins; Devadana–temples; Palli-chchandam–Jains; Shalabhoga–schools)

Statement-Based / Assertion-Reason

  • "Raja Raja I completely conquered Sri Lanka" — FALSE (he invaded; Rajendra I conquered fully).
  • "The Kailasa Temple at Ellora was built by a Pallava king" — FALSE (Rashtrakuta Krishna I; Pallava Narasimhavarman II built the Tamil Nadu one).
  • "Uttaramerur inscription tells us about Pallava local government" — FALSE (it's Chola).
  • "Vetti was a land revenue tax" — FALSE (Vetti = forced labour; Kadamai = land revenue).

Chronology Questions

  • Order of Pala rulers: Gopala → Dharmapala → Devapala → Mahipala → Rampala.
  • Order of events: Sangam Cholas decline (by 3rd c. AD) → Imperial Cholas rise (9th c. AD, Vijayalaya) → Raja Raja I → Rajendra I → decline (13th c.) → Pandya/Hoysala/Yadava/Kakatiya rise.
  • Chalukya branches in order: Badami → Eastern (Vengi) → Later/Kalyani.

Confusing / Frequently Misread Facts

  • Two kings named "Krishna" across dynasties — Krishna I (Rashtrakuta, Kailasa Temple) vs Krishna III (Rashtrakuta, defeated Imperial Cholas at one point) — same dynasty, different kings, don't merge their achievements.
  • Pandyas (dynasty) vs Pandavas (epic) — spelling trap under time pressure.
  • "Konda" suffix appears in titles of both Pallava and Chola kings — read carefully which dynasty the question is about.
