Section 01
🌐 Big Picture

This chapter covers India's final push to freedom — from 1932 to 1947. The story moves through three acts:


Act 1 The Poona Pact Era (1932–39) — Communal Award → Gandhi-Ambedkar clash → Poona Pact → Congress sessions → Forward Bloc.


Act 2 War & Revolt (1939–45) — WWII → Congress resignations → August Offer → Cripps Mission → Quit India Movement (9 Aug 1942) → INA under Bose → INA Trials.


Act 3 Transfer of Power (1944–47) — CR Formula → Desai-Liaquat Pact → Wavell Plan → Cabinet Mission → Direct Action Day → Mountbatten Plan → Independence.


The entire arc is: British refuse real freedom → Congress escalates → World War weakens Britain → India forces their hand → Freedom 1947.

Section 02
🧠 Chapter Memory Palace
🏛 The Freedom Train — A Journey from Pune Jail to Delhi's Red Fort

Imagine you are riding a train through India, stopping at key stations. Each station = one major event.

1
Pune Jail (Yerawada) — Gandhi is locked inside. Outside, MacDonald delivers the Communal Award (1932). Gandhi fasts. Ambedkar visits. They shake hands → Poona Pact. Malviya signs for Gandhi.
2
Lucknow Station (1936) — A huge Congress crowd. Two new clubs are born: All India Kisan Sabha (Sahjanand Saraswati) and Congress Socialist Party (JP Narayan, Lohia, Narayan Dev, Masani).
3
Haripura Station (1938) — Subhash Chandra Bose steps off as Congress President. He immediately forms National Planning Committee. Chairman? Nehru.
4
Tripuri Station (1939) — Bose wins the presidential election beating Gandhi's candidate Pattabhi Sitaramayya. Tension rises. Bose resigns → forms Forward Bloc.
5
Bombay — Gowalia Tank (Aug 1942) — The train halts. Gandhi gives the "Do or Die" speech. 9th August 1942 — Quit India Movement launches. All leaders arrested on Day 1.
6
Underground Network — Aruna Asaf Ali hoists the flag. Usha Mehta runs secret radio in Bombay. Chittu Pandey leads Ballia. Parallel govts: Jatiya Sarkar (Tamluk, Bengal) & Prati Sarkar (Satara, Maharashtra).
7
Singapore → Delhi Red Fort — Bose forms INA. Three officers tried at Red Fort (1945–46): Sahgal, Shahnawaz Khan, Dhillon. Nehru defends them in court. British back off.
8
Final Stop — Delhi (1947) — Mountbatten arrives. Partition Plan. Indian Independence Act 1947. India & Pakistan are born. Gandhi is in Noakhali, not Delhi.
Section 03
📋 Complete Revision Notes
1932
Communal Award 🔥 Must Know
Given by Ramsay MacDonald (PM of Britain). Extended separate electorate to Depressed Classes (Dalits), women, and Marathi community.

Background: Separate electorates were first introduced in Indian Councils Act 1909 (Morley-Minto Reforms). Expanded in Montagu-Chelmsford Reforms 1919 to Anglo-Indians & Christians.

Gandhi's stance: Opposed — feared permanent separation of Dalits from Hindu society.
Ambedkar's stance: Supported — believed Dalits could not be protected within Hindu community.
1932
Poona Pact 🔥 Must Know
Gandhi (in Yerawada Jail, Pune) fasted unto death against Communal Award. Ambedkar compromised.

Deal: Separate electorates for Depressed Classes given up. In return: increased reserved seats in legislatures.

Signed on Gandhi's behalf: Madan Mohan Malviya.

Results of Poona Pact era:
  • All India Anti-Untouchability League formed (later became Harijan Sevak Sangh, 1932)
  • Harijan Weekly published by Gandhi
  • Bahishkrit Hitkarini Sabha — formed by Ambedkar in 1924 (earlier, for depressed classes)
  • Ambedkar hated the term "Harijan" — called it patronizing
1934–1939
Key Congress Sessions & Formations ⭐ Important
1934 Congress Socialist Party — JP Narayan, Ram Manohar Lohia, Acharya Narayan Dev, Minoo Masani
1936 Congress session in Lucknow. All India Kisan Sabha formed by Shahjanand Saraswati
1937 Congress session in Faizpur, Maharashtra — FIRST session in a village
1938 Congress session in Haripura, Gujarat. Bose = President. National Planning Committee formed, Chairman = Nehru
1939 Bose beats Gandhi's candidate Pattabhi Sitaramayya. Resigns due to pressure. Forms Forward Bloc
1939–1940
World War II Begins — Congress Responds 🔥 Must Know
Allied Powers: Britain, USA, Russia  |  Axis Powers: Germany, Italy, Japan

British declared India's participation in WWII without consulting Congress. Congress resigned from all ministries as protest (Viceroy at this time: Linlithgow / Lingo).

August Offer 1940 (Linlithgow): Offered Dominion Status + expanded Viceroy's Executive Council with Indian majority. Rejected — Congress wanted Purna Swaraj now.

Response to August Offer: Individual Satyagraha
1st Satyagrahi → Acharya Vinoba Bhave
2nd → Jawaharlal Nehru
3rd → Brahmadatta
1942 (March)
Cripps Mission 🔥 Must Know
Sent by: British PM Churchill. Who: Stafford Cripps.

Proposals: Constituent Assembly, Indian Union with Dominion Status (not Purna Swaraj), new Constitution.

Gandhi's verdict: "A post-dated cheque on a failing bank"
Result: Rejected. Led directly to → Quit India Movement
9 August 1942
🔥 QUIT INDIA MOVEMENT (August Revolt) 🔥 Must Know
Approved at: Gowalia Tank, Bombay (also called August Kranti Maidan today)
Gandhi's slogan: "Do or Die"
Why August Revolt? Started on 9th August.

Day 1: All major leaders arrested → became a leaderless movement

Key Regional Leaders:
  • Aruna Asaf Ali — hoisted Congress flag at Gowalia Tank, led Congress Working Committee
  • Usha Mehta — ran underground radio from Bombay
  • Chittu Pandey — led from Ballia (UP)
  • Jatiya Sarkar — parallel govt at Tamluk, West Bengal
  • Prati Sarkar — parallel govt at Satara, Maharashtra

Congress Working Committee: A 15-member body formed after Nagpur session 1920.
1941–1945
Indian National Army (INA) — Subhash Chandra Bose 🔥 Must Know
INA Leadership:
  1. First: Mohan Singh (POW who organized Indian prisoners)
  2. Then: Ras Bihari Bose
  3. Finally: Subhash Chandra Bose (1943)

Bose's earlier activity (1941): Went to Germany, met Hitler. Formed Free India Legion / Tiger Legion. His alias: Orlando Mazzota.

Women's Regiment: Named after Rani Lakshmibai. Led by Lakshmi Sahgal (Captain Lakshmi).

INA Trials (1945–46): Conducted at Red Fort, Delhi.
Three officers tried: Prem Kumar Sahgal (Hindu) + Shahnawaz Khan (Muslim) + Gurbakhsh Singh Dhillon (Sikh).
Defence lawyers: Jawaharlal Nehru, Bhulabhai Desai.
Result: British released them — didn't want to look bad while leaving.
1946
Royal Indian Navy Mutiny ⭐ Important
Ship: HMIS Talwar. Sailors wrote "Britishers Quit India" & "Quit India" on the ship instead of celebrating British victory over Japan. This was the Royal Indian Navy Mutiny (1946).
1944–1947
Plans, Pacts & Partition 🔥 Must Know
1944 CR Formula (C Rajagopalachari). Also called Rajaji Formula. Failed.
1945 Desai-Liaquat Pact (Bhulabhai Desai + Liaquat Ali Khan). Failed.
1945 Wavell Plan / Shimla Conference — Viceroy Wavell called all leaders to Shimla. Failed due to Jinnah's Pakistan demand.
1946 Cabinet Mission Plan — PM: Clement Attlee (Labour). Members: Stafford Cripps, AV Alexander, Pethick Lawrence. Chairman: Pethick Lawrence. Proposed Grouping: Group A, B, C. Both Congress & ML initially accepted, but Nehru rejected compulsory grouping → ML withdrew → Direct Action Day: 16 Aug 1946 → Noakhali riots.
1947 Mountbatten Plan = Partition Plan → basis of Indian Independence Act 1947. India & Pakistan independent. East Pakistan → Bangladesh in 1971.

Clement Attlee facts: Labour Party leader. Announced British departure by 30 June 1948 (brought forward to Aug 1947). He was ALSO a member of Simon Commission 1928.
Section 04
🪝 Smart Memory Hooks
Hook — Poona Pact Signatories

Gandhi is in jail fasting. He can't sign. So Madan Mohan Malviya signs FOR him. Picture Malviya as Gandhi's "pen hand from outside the jail bars." → Malviya = Gandhi's proxy.

Hook — Individual Satyagraha Order

Remember: VJB — Vinoba Bhave (1st) → Jawaharlal Nehru (2nd) → Brahmadatta (3rd). Think: "Vinoba was the Very Junior Beginner who went first."

Hook — INA Order of Leadership

Picture a relay race baton passing: Mohan Singh (starts, captured) passes to Ras Bihari Bose (senior, sets up), who passes to Subhash Bose (the star runner). MRS — Mohan, Ras Bihari, Subhash.

Hook — INA Trial Officers (One Hindu, One Muslim, One Sikh)

The British accidentally created India's most powerful unity symbol. S-K-D: Sahgal (Hindu) + shahnawaz Khan (Muslim) + Dhillon (Sikh). India united to defend all three → British retreated.

Hook — Cabinet Mission Members

SCALP: Stafford Cripps + AV Alexander + Pethick Lawrence (chairman). Cabinet mission "scalped" the two-nation theory initially but couldn't hold.

Hook — Clement Attlee's Double Role

Attlee came to India in 1928 as Simon Commission member (hated by Indians). He came back in 1946 as PM to give India freedom. Full circle — the man Indians burnt in effigy in 1928 is the man who freed them in 1947.

Hook — Parallel Govts Location

Tamluk = Bengal (Jatiya Sarkar — "Jatio" sounds Bengali). Satara = Maharashtra (Prati Sarkar — "Prati" sounds like protest in Marathi culture). East = Tamluk, West = Satara.

Hook — Bose's alias in Germany

In Germany, Bose called himself Orlando Mazzota — an Italian-sounding name to avoid detection. Picture Bose in a trench coat with an Italian coffee, trying to sound European.

📌 Key Difference: Dominion Status vs Purna Swaraj

Dominion Status: Self-rule BUT under British Crown authority. (Like Canada, Australia)

Purna Swaraj: Complete independence. No British Crown. Full sovereign nation.

Congress demanded Purna Swaraj from 1929 (Lahore session). Every British offer of Dominion Status was therefore rejected.

Section 06
⚠️ Exam Traps
Trap 1 — Stafford Cripps appeared TWICE

Cripps Mission (1942) → came alone with Dominion Status offer. Cabinet Mission (1946) → came as ONE of THREE members. Don't confuse the two. In 1946, Cripps ≠ chairman (Lawrence was).

Trap 2 — Bose vs Bose

Subhash Chandra Bose → Forward Bloc (1939), INA (1943), Free India Legion in Germany. Ras Bihari Bose → led INA BEFORE Subhash. Don't mix them up in leadership sequence questions.

Trap 3 — Bahishkrit Hitkarini Sabha vs Harijan Sevak Sangh

Bahishkrit Hitkarini Sabha → formed by Ambedkar in 1924. Harijan Sevak Sangh → formed by Gandhi in 1932 (grew from Anti-Untouchability League). Two different organisations, two different founders.

Trap 4 — CR Formula ≠ "Clement Reed Formula"

CR Formula = C. Rajagopalachari formula (1944). Some students confuse "CR" with Clement Reed. CR stands for Chakravarti Rajagopalachari only.

Trap 5 — INA Trials Location

INA Trials were held at Red Fort, Delhi. NOT in Pune, NOT in Bombay. Red Fort = where INA officers were tried AND where India's independence was later declared.

Trap 6 — Gandhi's Location on 15th August 1947

Gandhi was NOT at Red Fort for Independence celebrations. He was in Noakhali (now Bangladesh), trying to stop the communal violence triggered by Direct Action Day.

Trap 7 — Faizpur Session (1937)

Faizpur is in Maharashtra, NOT some northern city. It's famous for being the FIRST Congress session held in a village. Don't confuse with Faizabad (UP).

Trap 8 — Viceroy during QIM

During Quit India Movement (1942), Viceroy was Linlithgow (Lingo). Wavell came later. Irwin was much earlier (Gandhi-Irwin Pact 1931).

Trap 9 — Clement Attlee's Simon Commission link

Attlee was a member of Simon Commission (1928) — not its chairman. Chairman was John Simon. Attlee later became PM and announced India's independence.

Trap 10 — National Planning Committee Chairman

Bose formed the National Planning Committee in 1938 at Haripura, but the chairman was Nehru, not Bose. Bose was the Congress President who created it.

1. Who gave the Communal Award (1932) and what did it do?
Ramsay MacDonald (British PM). Gave separate electorates to Depressed Classes, women, Marathi community.
2. Who signed the Poona Pact on behalf of Gandhi?
Madan Mohan Malviya
3. Which Congress session was the first held in a village? When and where?
Faizpur Session, 1937, Maharashtra.
4. Who was Pattabhi Sitaramayya and why is he important?
Gandhi's candidate for Congress President in 1939. Bose defeated him, causing a rift. Gandhi said Sitaramayya's defeat was his own defeat.
5. What was Gandhi's famous quote about the Cripps Mission proposals?
"A post-dated cheque on a failing bank."
6. Name the three officers tried at INA Trials. One Hindu, one Muslim, one Sikh.
Prem Kumar Sahgal (Hindu) | Shahnawaz Khan (Muslim) | Gurbakhsh Singh Dhillon (Sikh)
7. Who led the INA before Subhash Chandra Bose? In correct order.
1. Mohan Singh → 2. Ras Bihari Bose → 3. Subhash Chandra Bose
8. Who ran an underground radio station during QIM, and from where?
Usha Mehta, from Bombay.
9. What is Direct Action Day? Date and who called it?
16 August 1946. Called by Muhammad Ali Jinnah (Muslim League) after Nehru rejected compulsory grouping in Cabinet Mission Plan. Led to massive communal violence (Noakhali).
10. Fill in the blank: Bose's alias in Germany was ________ and his regiment there was called ________ or ________.
Orlando Mazzota | Free India Legion | Tiger Legion
11. Where was Gandhi on the night of 14–15 August 1947?
Noakhali (not Delhi). He was trying to stop communal violence.
12. Who formed Bahishkrit Hitkarini Sabha? When?
B.R. Ambedkar, 1924.
